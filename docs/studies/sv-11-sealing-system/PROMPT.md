# Bible Study: The Sealing System of Daniel's Vision

## Question
What do satham (H5640) and chatham (H2856) mean? What four mechanisms unseal the vision? Trace the vocabulary chain from Dan 8:26 through Rev 22:10. What was actually sealed — apply four criteria: not explained by Gabriel, Daniel had comprehension to be custodian, must be unsealable, must genuinely need unsealing.

## Prior Research Summary

### From Prior Series Studies (sv-01 through sv-10)
- **sv-06 (Dan 8 verse by verse):** Dan 8:26 is the proof text for the chazon/mar'eh distinction. The mar'eh is declared true; the chazon is sealed. Gabriel was sent to explain the mar'eh (8:16) but explained the chazon instead (8:20-25). The mar'eh remains unexplained — "none understood the mar'eh" (8:27). Two hapax constructions in v.14 (erev boqer, nitsdaq Niphal) mark the temporal element as encoded.
- **sv-10 (evening-morning unit):** The vocabulary choice of erev boqer is provably deliberate — Daniel uses yamim freely in 12:11-12. Dan 8:26 confirms the compound is a single unit (singular pronoun hu, anaphoric articles). The sealing command (8:26, 12:4) provides the motive for the unusual unit — it is the visible fingerprint of the encoding act.
- **sv-05 (literary architecture):** The chazon/mar'eh two-track system binds Dan 8-12 as one continuous narrative. The biyn understanding chain tracks eight stages. The sealed-to-unsealed arc: Dan 8:26 → Dan 9:24 → Dan 12:4 → Rev 22:10.
