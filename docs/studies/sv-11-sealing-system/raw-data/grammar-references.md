### 1. seal-command-syntax-daniel.md (CRITICAL)
Full comparative overview of four Daniel sealing constructions:
- Dan 8:26: Single imperative (satham) + definite object + no explicit terminus
- Dan 12:4: Dual imperative pair (satham + chatham) + two objects + explicit terminus (ad eth qets)
- Dan 12:9: Dual passive participle pair (setumim + chatumim) + definite subject + same terminus
- Dan 9:24: Infinitive of purpose (la-chatom) + double object (chazon + nabi)

Includes semantic distinction table (satham = blocks access; chatham = authenticates + closes), the eth qets terminus analysis, and contrast with Isa 8:16 and Isa 29:11.

### 6. dan-12-7-11-12.md

### 7. asyndetic-noun-pair.md
Dan 8:14's erev boqer = compound unit (two absolute-state nouns without conjunction, article, or construct). Dan 8:26's ha-erev we-ha-boqer = anaphoric title (articles + conjunction break the compound fusion). Dan 9:24's shavuim shivim = cognate-pluralized counter.

### 8. dan-10-1.md
Resolution of the biyn chain: u-biyn et ha-dabar (Qal.Perf.3ms — completed understanding). Niphal niglah = divine passive ("a thing was revealed"). The word emeth recurs: we-emeth ha-dabar ("and truth [was] the word").

### 9. imperative-mood.md
Hebrew imperative: 2nd person only, positive only. No negative imperative — prohibitions use lo + imperfect (categorical) or al + imperfect (situational). Rev 22:10's me sphragises maps to the al + imperfect pattern (immediate prohibition), not the lo + imperfect pattern (categorical). The Greek aorist subjunctive prohibition is the functional equivalent.

### 10. apocalyptic-time-unit-grammar.md
Counted-unit displacement: ordinary yamim replaced by non-standard substitutes (erev boqer, shavuim, iddan/mo'ed). The lexical choice signals symbolic register. Dan 12:11-12 DO use yamim but embedded in full apocalyptic register (sealing frame sustains symbolic context).
