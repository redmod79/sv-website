## Genesis 26:15 — satham physical usage (well-stopping)
Hebrew: וְכָל־הַבְּאֵרֹת אֲשֶׁר חָפְרוּ עַבְדֵי אָבִיו בִּימֵי אַבְרָהָם אָבִיו סִתְּמוּם פְּלִשְׁתִּים וַיְמַלְאוּם עָפָר

| Word | Lemma | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| סִתְּמוּם | סתם | Verb.Piel.Perf.3p.+3mp | stopped them up |
| פְּלִשְׁתִּים | פלשׁתי | Adj.mp.Abs | Philistines |
| וַיְמַלְאוּם | מלא | Verb.Piel.Wayq.3mp.+3mp | and filled them |
| עָפָר | עפר | Noun.ms.Abs | dust/earth |

Key: satham in Piel (intensive) — the Philistines aggressively stopped up the wells AND filled them with earth. Two actions: block the opening + fill the cavity. The prototype for figurative usage: making knowledge inaccessible by plugging the source.

## Isaiah 8:16 — sealing vocabulary comparison
Hebrew: צוֹר תְּעוּדָה חֲתוֹם תּוֹרָה בְּלִמֻּדָי

| Word | Lemma | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| צוֹר | צרר | Verb.Qal.Impv.2ms | bind up / wrap |
| תְּעוּדָה | תעודה | Noun.fs.Abs | testimony / attestation |
| חֲתוֹם | חתם | Verb.Qal.Impv.2ms | seal |
| תּוֹרָה | תורה | Noun.fs.Abs | instruction / law |
| בְּלִמֻּדָי | למד | Noun.mp.Abs.+1cs | among my disciples |

Key comparison with Dan 12:4:
- Both: paired Qal imperatives (verb1 X verb2 Y)
- Isaiah: tsuwr (bind/wrap) + chatham (seal) — two different verbs
- Daniel: satham (stop up) + chatham (seal) — two different verbs
- Isaiah: NO temporal boundary — sealed "among my disciples" (addressable group)
- Daniel: ad eth qets — sealed "until time of the end" (calendrical horizon)
- Isaiah: chatham torah — seal the instruction
- Daniel: chatham ha-sepher — seal the book/scroll

## Dan 8:26 Grammar Summary (from grammar-reference library)
- Clause 1 (NmCl): u-mar'eh ha-erev we-ha-boqer asher ne'emar emeth hu — authentication formula
- Clause 3 (Impv): we-attah setom he-chazon — sealing command (satham only)
- Clause 4 (NmCl): ki le-yamim rabbim — verbless causal clause (durational)

Three ki-clauses in Gabriel's address (vv.17, 19, 26):
- v.17: ki le-eth-qets he-chazon — terminus
- v.19: ki le-mo'ed qets — terminus
- v.26: ki le-yamim rabbim — duration

## Dan 12:4 Grammar Summary (from grammar-reference library)
Five clauses: WXIm (satham command) / Voct (Daniel) / WIm0 (chatham command + temporal boundary) / ZYqX (running to and fro) / WYqX (knowledge increases)

Dual imperative: satham ha-debarim (conceal the words) + wa-chatom ha-sepher (seal the book)
- Different verbs on different objects — comprehensive closure of both signified and signifier
- Temporal boundary: ad eth qets (preposition ad = terminus; state terminates after)

## Dan 12:9 Grammar Summary (from grammar-reference library)
Passive participle restatement: setumim wa-chatumim ha-debarim ad eth qets
- Shift from active command (12:4) to settled state (12:9)
- Agency suppressed — the words ARE sealed, not "you must seal"
- The ki-clause is causal ground for the dismissal lek ("go thy way")

## Dan 9:24 Grammar Summary (from grammar-reference library)
Six le + infinitive construct purpose-clauses under nechtakh ("are determined"):
le-kalle' (finish transgression) / le-hatem (make an end of sins) / le-kapper (atone) / le-habi' (bring in righteousness) / la-chatom (seal up vision and prophecy) / li-mshoach (anoint most holy)

la-chatom chazon we-nabi: Qal infinitive construct of chatham (H2856). Object: chazon AND nabi (vision AND prophet). This is authentication through fulfillment — the 70 weeks' prophetic content coming to pass validates ("seals") the prophetic office and its visions.
