## Focus Areas

### 1. venitsdaq Grammar: What Does the Niphal Perfect Mark?
**WHAT:** Parse venitsdaq (Niphal Perfect 3ms of tsadaq with waw-consecutive) and determine what the prophetic perfect construction marks -- certainty of outcome, speed of fulfillment, or inception of process.
**WHY:** If the prophetic perfect marks certainty (not speed), the grammar is compatible with processual vindication. If it marks instantaneous completion, the processual reading is grammatically excluded.
**HOW:** Read grammar entry dan-8-14, read stem-niphal entry, read word study H6663-tsadaq. Compare with other waw-consecutive Niphal Perfects in Daniel. Parse Dan 8:14 clause structure (WQtX type). Identify whether the prophetic perfect ever describes events that unfold over time in other OT passages.

### 2. Dan 8:13's ad-matay Question: What Does the 2300 Answer?
**WHAT:** Analyze the Hebrew question structure in Dan 8:13 -- does "how long" (ad-matay) measure the duration of the PROBLEM (trampling, removing tamid, giving sanctuary to trampling) or the SOLUTION (vindication)?
**WHY:** If the 2300 measures the problem's duration, then vindication BEGINS at the terminus. If it measures the solution's duration, vindication is completed by the terminus.
**HOW:** Read grammar entry dan-8-13-14-dialogue and ad-matay-interrogative. Parse the syntactic lock-and-key between v.13 question and v.14 answer. Examine the semantic inversion: qodesh as object of trampling (v.13) becoming subject of vindication (v.14). Compare with Isa 6:11 ad-matay / ad parallel.

### 3. Dan 7:9-14 as Sequential Process
**WHAT:** Map the verbal sequence of the judgment scene in Dan 7:9-14 and determine how many discrete sequential steps occur between the court convening and the kingdom being received.
**WHY:** If the antitypical DOA scene in Dan 7 is processual (thrones -> seated -> court -> books -> examination -> Son of Man -> kingdom), this establishes that the judgment the 2300 inaugurates is not instantaneous.
**HOW:** Read grammar entry dan-7-9-10 and dan-7-13. Trace the four tense-phases identified in sv-17: (1) completed actions, (2) static description, (3) ongoing service, (4) judicial acts. Map the temporal progression. Note Dan 7:11's temporal co-existence (horn speaking DURING judgment).

### 4. Dan 7:21-22's ad Structure: Beginning or Completion?
**WHAT:** Analyze the ad ("until") preposition in Dan 7:21-22 and determine whether it marks the completion of judgment or its beginning.
**WHY:** If ad marks a transition point (horn prevailing UNTIL judgment begins), this parallels the ad in Dan 8:14 (trampling UNTIL vindication begins). Both mark beginnings, not completions.
**HOW:** Read Strong's H5704 lexical data. Compare Dan 7:21-22 (horn prevailed UNTIL Ancient of Days came and judgment was GIVEN to saints) with Dan 8:14 (UNTIL 2300, then vindicated). Parse the verb sequence AFTER ad in both passages -- what unfolds after the transition point?

### 5. DOA Typology: A Day-Long Multi-Stage Ceremony
**WHAT:** Document the earthly DOA ritual as a multi-stage, day-long ceremony (Lev 16:12-22) and examine what this implies for the antitypical vindication.
**WHY:** If the type (earthly DOA) was processual, the antitype (heavenly vindication) should be processual. An instantaneous antitype would break the typological correspondence that sv-17 established.
**HOW:** Reference sv-04 findings on the twelve discrete steps, chiastic structure, kalah boundary, and three-verb chain (kaphar -> taher Piel -> taher Qal). Reference sv-04b on the Tishri calendar arc (Trumpets -> DOA -> Jubilee -> Tabernacles). Use word studies H3722-kaphar, H2891-taher.

### 6. Five Beneficiaries Requiring Staged Examination

**WHY:** sv-18 identified five beneficiaries. This study asks whether five different beneficiaries with five different audiences and five different types of evidence can be vindicated simultaneously or must be vindicated sequentially.
**HOW:** Reference sv-18 findings. For each beneficiary, identify what evidence vindicates them, who the audience is, and whether simultaneous vindication is logically coherent. Map to DOA stages where possible.

### 7. Rev 14:7's elthen: Arrival or Completion?
**WHAT:** Parse elthen (aorist indicative active of erchomai) in Rev 14:7 ("the hour of his judgment IS COME") and determine whether the aorist marks the arrival/beginning of judgment or its completion.
**WHY:** Rev 14:7 announces the judgment. If elthen marks arrival ("has come"), the judgment begins at that announcement. If it marks completion, the judgment is already finished.
**HOW:** Read word study G2064-erchomai. Survey other "hour has come" (elthen + hora) constructions in the NT: John 12:23; 13:1; 16:21,32; 17:1; Rev 14:7,15. Determine whether "the hour has come" ever means "the hour is finished." Trace the Revelation progression: Rev 14:7 (announced) -> 15:3 (saints declare) -> 16:7 (altar speaks) -> 19:1-2 (completed).

### 8. Processual Reading Strengthens the DOA Parallel
**WHAT:** Demonstrate how the processual reading of vindication strengthens (rather than weakens) the judgment-DOA parallel established in sv-17.
**WHY:** If vindication is instantaneous, the parallel with a day-long ceremony is merely lexical. If vindication is processual with multiple stages, the parallel is structural (same multi-stage, ordered-sequence pattern).

**HOW:** Reference sv-18 and sv-25 findings on the three-phase model. Then isolate the judgment phase and examine its internal structure using Dan 7:9-14, DOA typology, five beneficiaries, and Revelation progression.

### 10. The Blessing of Dan 12:12 and Sustained Adherence
