# Verses

## Question
What is the literary structure of Daniel's prophetic chapters? Map the Aramaic/Hebrew language division, Aramaic chiastic structure, four parallel vision cycles, chazon/mar'eh vocabulary distinction, and biyn chain. How does Daniel 7 function as a hinge?

## Primary Verses — Full Chapter Context

### Daniel 1 (Hebrew — Prologue)
> Daniel 1:1 In the third year of the reign of Jehoiakim king of Judah came Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon unto Jerusalem, and besieged it.
> Daniel 1:2 And the Lord gave Jehoiakim king of Judah into his hand, with part of the vessels of the house of God: which he carried into the land of Shinar to the house of his god; and he brought the vessels into the treasure house of his god.
> Daniel 1:3 And the king spake unto Ashpenaz the master of his eunuchs, that he should bring [certain] of the children of Israel, and of the king's seed, and of the princes;
> Daniel 1:4 Children in whom [was] no blemish, but well favoured, and skilful in all wisdom, and cunning in knowledge, and **understanding science** [mebiyney madda — Hiphil participle of biyn], and such as [had] ability in them to stand in the king's palace, and whom they might teach the learning and the tongue of the Chaldeans.
> Daniel 1:5-16 [Diet test narrative]
> Daniel 1:17 As for these four children, God gave them knowledge and skill [haskel — Hiphil infinitive absolute of sakal] in all learning and wisdom: and **Daniel had understanding [hebiyn — Hiphil perfect of biyn] in all visions [chazon] and dreams [chalomoth].**
> Daniel 1:18-20 [Examination before the king]
> Daniel 1:21 And Daniel continued [even] unto the first year of king Cyrus.

**BIYN CHAIN STAGE 1 — CAPACITY:** Dan 1:4 uses biyn (Hiphil ptcp) for Daniel's selection criteria; Dan 1:17 uses biyn (Hiphil perf) for Daniel's God-given gift. Both chazon and chalomoth appear — vision AND dream capacity.

### Daniel 2 (Aramaic — First Vision Cycle, Chiasm Element A)
> Daniel 2:1 And in the second year of the reign of Nebuchadnezzar Nebuchadnezzar dreamed dreams, wherewith his spirit was troubled, and his sleep brake from him.
> Daniel 2:4 Then spake the Chaldeans to the king **in Syriack** [Aramaic language transition — MARKED], O king, live for ever: tell thy servants the dream, and we will shew the interpretation.
> Daniel 2:19 Then was the secret revealed unto Daniel in a **night vision** [chezev di-leylaya — Aramaic cognate of chazon].
> Daniel 2:28 But there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets, and maketh known to the king Nebuchadnezzar **what shall be in the latter days** [be'acharith yomayya — Aramaic cognate of acharith]. Thy dream, and the visions of thy head upon thy bed, are these;
> Daniel 2:31-35 [Image description: gold head, silver arms, bronze belly, iron legs, iron-clay feet, stone]
> Daniel 2:36-43 [Interpretation: four kingdoms]
> Daniel 2:44 And in the days of these kings shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed: and the kingdom shall not be left to other people, [but] it shall break in pieces and consume all these kingdoms, and it shall stand for ever.
> Daniel 2:45 Forasmuch as thou sawest that the stone was cut out of the mountain without hands, and that it brake in pieces the iron, the brass, the clay, the silver, and the gold; the great God hath made known to the king what shall come to pass hereafter: and the dream [is] certain, and the interpretation thereof sure.
> Daniel 2:46-49 [Daniel promoted]

**ACHARITH INCLUSIO OPENING:** Dan 2:28 contains the Aramaic form of acharith (H319/A320) — "in the latter days." This opens the frame that closes at Dan 10:14.

### Daniel 3 (Aramaic — Chiasm Element B: Faithful Under Persecution)
> Daniel 3:1 Nebuchadnezzar the king made an image of gold, whose height [was] threescore cubits, [and] the breadth thereof six cubits: he set it up in the plain of Dura, in the province of Babylon.
> Daniel 3:4 Then an herald cried aloud, To you it is commanded, O **people, nations, and languages** [formula repeated in Dan 4:1; 5:19; 6:25; 7:14],
> Daniel 3:5-6 [Command to worship image or burn]
> Daniel 3:8-12 [Accusation of Shadrach, Meshach, Abednego]
> Daniel 3:14-15 [Nebuchadnezzar's threat: "who is that God that shall deliver you?"]
> Daniel 3:16-18 "If it be [so], our God whom we serve is able to deliver us from the burning fiery furnace, and he will deliver [us] out of thine hand, O king. But if not, be it known unto thee, O king, that we will not serve thy gods, nor worship the golden image which thou hast set up."
> Daniel 3:24-25 [Four men in furnace, "form of the fourth is like the Son of God"]
> Daniel 3:26-28 [Deliverance, king's confession]
> Daniel 3:29 Therefore I make a decree, That every **people, nation, and language**, which speak any thing amiss against the God of Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego, shall be cut in pieces...
> Daniel 3:30 Then the king promoted Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego, in the province of Babylon.

**B/B' CHIASTIC PAIR — Seven-Element Shared Skeleton with Dan 6:**
1. Faith of God's servants established
2. Decree targeting faithful worship
3. Accusation by jealous officials
4. King confronts the faithful
5. Punishment carried out (furnace / lions)
6. Divine deliverance (angel / "his angel")
7. King's confession and decree honoring God

### Daniel 7 (Aramaic — HINGE: Chiasm Element A' + Second Vision Cycle)
> Daniel 7:1 In the first year of Belshazzar king of Babylon **Daniel had a dream and visions [chezev] of his head upon his bed**: then he wrote the dream, [and] told the sum of the matters.
> Daniel 7:2-8 [Four beasts: lion, bear, leopard, fourth dreadful beast with ten horns, little horn]
> Daniel 7:9-10 I beheld till **the thrones were cast down**, and **the Ancient of days did sit**, whose garment [was] white as snow, and the hair of his head like the pure wool: his throne [was like] the fiery flame, [and] his wheels [as] burning fire. A fiery stream issued and came forth from before him: thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him: **the judgment was set, and the books were opened.**
> Daniel 7:13-14 I saw in the night visions, and, behold, [one] like **the Son of man** came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of days, and they brought him near before him. And there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all **people, nations, and languages**, should **serve [pelach]** him: his dominion [is] an everlasting dominion, which shall not pass away, and his kingdom [that] which shall not be destroyed.
> Daniel 7:15-16 I Daniel was grieved in my spirit in the midst of [my] body, and the visions of my head troubled me. I came near unto one of them that stood by, and asked him the truth of all this. So he told me, and made me know the interpretation of the things.
> Daniel 7:17-18 These great beasts, which are four, [are] four kings... But the saints of the most High shall take the kingdom, and possess the kingdom for ever, even for ever and ever.
> Daniel 7:21-22 I beheld, and the same horn made war with the saints, and prevailed against them; Until the Ancient of days came, and **judgment was given to the saints** of the most High; and the time came that **the saints possessed the kingdom.**
> Daniel 7:25 And he shall speak [great] words against the most High, and shall wear out the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws: and they shall be given into his hand **until a time and times and the dividing of time.**
> Daniel 7:26-27 But the judgment shall sit, and they shall take away his dominion, to consume and to destroy [it] unto the end. And **the kingdom and dominion**, and the greatness of the kingdom under the whole heaven, **shall be given to the people of the saints of the most High**, whose kingdom [is] an everlasting kingdom, and all dominions shall **serve [pelach] and obey him.**
> Daniel 7:28 **Hitherto [is] the end of the matter.** As for me Daniel, my cogitations much troubled me, and my countenance changed in me: but I kept the matter in my heart.

**HINGE CHAPTER EVIDENCE:**
- **Language:** Written in Aramaic (pairs with Dan 2 as A')
- **Genre:** First time Daniel himself receives prophetic revelation (not interpreting someone else's dream)
- **Content:** Four kingdoms + everlasting kingdom (parallels Dan 2)
- **Judgment:** First appearance of the judgment scene (7:9-10), which becomes central to Dan 8-12
- **Physical response:** "grieved in spirit... troubled" (7:15,28) — beginning of the intensification pattern

### Daniel 8 (Hebrew — Third Vision Cycle, Language Transition Back to Hebrew)
> Daniel 8:1 In the third year of the reign of king Belshazzar **a vision [chazon] appeared unto me**, [even unto] me Daniel, after that which appeared unto me at the first.
> Daniel 8:2 And I saw in a **vision [chazon]**; and it came to pass, when I saw, that I [was] at Shushan [in] the palace...
> Daniel 8:5 And as I was **considering [biyn, Hiphil ptcp]**, behold, an he goat came from the west...
> Daniel 8:8-9 [Great horn broken, four horns, little horn]
> Daniel 8:10-12 And it waxed great, [even] to the host of heaven... Yea, he magnified [himself] even to the prince of the host, and by him **the daily [tamid]** [sacrifice] was taken away, and the place of his sanctuary was cast down. And an host was given [him] against **the daily [tamid]** by reason of transgression...
> Daniel 8:13 Then I heard one saint speaking, and another saint said unto that certain [saint] which spake, How long [shall be] **the vision [chazon] [concerning] the daily [tamid]**, and the transgression of desolation, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot?
> Daniel 8:14 And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed [nitsdaq — Niphal perfect of tsadaq].
> Daniel 8:15 And it came to pass, when I, [even] I Daniel, had seen **the vision [chazon]**, and sought for **the meaning [biynah]**, then, behold, there stood before me as **the appearance [mar'eh]** of a man.
> Daniel 8:16 And I heard a man's voice between [the banks of] Ulai, which called, and said, **Gabriel, make this [man] to understand [habiyn — Hiphil imperative of biyn] the vision [mar'eh].**
> Daniel 8:17 So he came near where I stood: and when he came, I was afraid, and fell upon my face: but he said unto me, **Understand [biyn]**, O son of man: for **at the time of the end [eth qets]** [shall be] **the vision [chazon].**
> Daniel 8:19 Behold, I will make thee know what shall be **in the last end [acharith]** of the indignation: for **at the time appointed [moed] the end [qets]** [shall be].
> Daniel 8:20-25 [Interpretation: ram = Medo-Persia, goat = Greece, horn = fierce king]
> Daniel 8:26 And **the vision [mar'eh] of the evening and the morning** which was told [is] true: wherefore **shut thou up the vision [chazon]**; for it [shall be] for many days.
> Daniel 8:27 And I Daniel fainted, and was sick [certain] days; afterward I rose up, and did the king's business; and I was astonished at **the vision [mar'eh]**, but **none understood [mebiyn — Hiphil participle of biyn] [it].**

**CHAZON/MAR'EH PROOF TEXT (Dan 8:26):** Both terms appear in the SAME VERSE with different treatments:
- mar'eh of the evening and morning → declared TRUE (authentication)
- chazon → commanded to be SEALED ("shut up the vision; for it shall be for many days")

**BIYN CHAIN STAGES 2-3:**
- Stage 2 (COMMAND): Dan 8:16 — Gabriel commanded: "habiyn... the mar'eh" (Hiphil imperative)
- Stage 3 (FAILURE): Dan 8:27 — "none understood" (mebiyn — Hiphil participle + negation ein)

### Daniel 9:1-27 (Hebrew — Biyn Chain Stages 4-5)
> Daniel 9:1 In the first year of Darius the son of Ahasuerus, of the seed of the Medes, which was made king over the realm of the Chaldeans;
> Daniel 9:2 In the first year of his reign **I Daniel understood [biynoti — Qal perfect of biyn] by books** the number of the years, whereof the word of the LORD came to Jeremiah the prophet, that he would accomplish seventy years in the desolations of Jerusalem.
> Daniel 9:3-19 [Daniel's prayer of confession]
> Daniel 9:20-21 And whiles I [was] speaking, and praying... even the man **Gabriel**, whom I had seen **in the vision [chazon]** at the beginning, being caused to fly swiftly, touched me about the time of the evening oblation.
> Daniel 9:22 And he **informed [yabiyn — Hiphil wayyiqtol of biyn]** [me], and talked with me, and said, O Daniel, I am now come forth **to give thee skill [lehaskilkha — Hiphil infinitive of sakal] and understanding [biynah].**
> Daniel 9:23 At the beginning of thy supplications the commandment came forth, and I am come to shew [thee]; for thou [art] greatly beloved: therefore **understand [biyn — Qal imperative] the matter [dabar], and consider [habiyn — Hiphil imperative of biyn] the vision [mar'eh].**
> Daniel 9:24 Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city, to finish the transgression, and to make an end of sins, and to make reconciliation for iniquity, and to bring in everlasting righteousness, and **to seal up [chatham] the vision [chazon] and prophecy**, and to anoint the most Holy.
> Daniel 9:25-27 [Seventy weeks prophecy: Messiah, prince, desolation]

**BIYN CHAIN STAGES 4-5:**
- Stage 4 (QUEST): Dan 9:2 — biynoti (Qal perf) "I understood by books" — Daniel reads Jeremiah, seeks understanding
- Stage 5 (RESUMPTION): Dan 9:22-23 — Gabriel returns: yabiyn (Hiphil wayq) "he informed me" + "to give thee sakal and biynah" + double imperative "biyn the dabar, habiyn the mar'eh"

**GABRIEL LINK:** Dan 9:21 explicitly names Gabriel and references "the vision (chazon) at the beginning" — verbal link to Dan 8:16.

**BIYN CHAIN STAGE 6 — RESOLUTION:** Dan 10:1 is the resolution: "he understood (biyn, Qal perf) the thing, and had understanding (biynah) of the vision (mar'eh)." The mar'eh that was not understood in 8:27 is now understood.

**ACHARITH INCLUSIO CLOSING:** Dan 10:14 contains "in the latter days" (be'acharith hayyamim) — closing the frame opened at Dan 2:28.

## Sealed-to-Unsealed Arc

> Daniel 9:24 — "to **seal up [chatham]** the vision [chazon] and prophecy"
> Daniel 12:4 — "**shut up the words, and seal [chatham] the book**, even to the time of the end [eth qets]"
> Daniel 12:9 — "the words are closed up and **sealed [chatham]** till the time of the end [eth qets]"
> Revelation 22:10 — "And he saith unto me, **Seal NOT** the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand."

The arc runs: seal up (Dan 9:24) → seal the book (Dan 12:4) → sealed till the time of the end (Dan 12:9) → DO NOT SEAL (Rev 22:10). The reversal in Revelation signals that the "time of the end" has arrived.
