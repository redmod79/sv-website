### Central Spine: kaphar-to-tsadaq Progression

#### kaphar — H3722
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md`
- **Definition:** Primitive root; to cover, expiate, atone, placate
- **Occurrences:** 102 in OT; Piel dominates (92x = 90%)
- **Distribution:** Leviticus 67 (66%), Numbers 19; Pentateuchal ritual = 83%
- **Key collocations:** chattaah (sin offering, 38x), kohen (priest, 27x), salach (forgive, 12x), taher (cleanse, 12x)
- **LXX mapping:** hilaskomai (G2433, PMI 6.47), aphiemi (G863, 14x), katharizo (G2511, 14x)
- **Word family:** kopher (ransom, H3724), kippur (atonement, H3725), kapporeth (mercy seat, H3727)
- **Critical finding:** Daily formula = kaphar + salach (atonement → forgiveness). Annual formula = kaphar + taher (atonement → cleansing). salach NEVER in Lev 16; taher NEVER in Lev 4-5.

#### tsadaq — H6663
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`
- **Definition:** Be right, be just, be vindicated, justify, declare righteous
- **Occurrences:** 41 in OT across 5 stems
- **Stem distribution:** Qal 22 (be righteous), Hiphil 12 (declare righteous), Piel 5 (prove right), **Niphal 1** (be vindicated — Dan 8:14 ONLY), Hithpael 1
- **Critical finding:** The Niphal occurs exactly ONCE in the entire OT — Dan 8:14. This is the only passive "be vindicated" form. tsadaq has ZERO occurrences in Leviticus; kaphar has 67. Dan 9:24 is the ONLY verse where both vocabulary systems meet.
- **LXX mapping:** dikaioo (G1344, 21x), dikaios (G1342, 22x)

#### tsedeq — H6664
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6664-tsedeq.md`
- **Definition:** Righteousness, justice, the right (standard/norm)
- **Occurrences:** 119 tokens across 12 books
- **Distribution:** Psalms 49 (44%), Isaiah 25 (22%); wisdom and prophets = 97%
- **Key collocation:** mishpat (H4941) appears with tsedeq in 30 verses (27%)
- **Dan 9:24:** "tsedeq olamim" — everlasting righteousness, the ONLY verse pairing kaphar and tsedeq
- **LXX mapping:** dikaiosyne (G1343, PMI 26.71 — extremely strong bridge)

#### hilasterion — G2435
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2435-hilasterion.md`
- **Definition:** Expiatory (place or thing); mercy seat; propitiatory
- **Occurrences:** 2 in NT (Rom 3:25, Heb 9:5)
- **Rom 3:25:** Christ IS the hilasterion — both the place where divine righteousness is publicly displayed and the means of expiation through blood
- **Heb 9:5:** The mercy seat of the OT tabernacle — same word
- **Hebrew source:** kapporeth (H3727, PMI 9.75) — the LXX chose hilasterion over transliteration, foregrounding FUNCTION
- **Bridge role:** Connects OT kapporeth (mercy seat where kaphar happens) to NT Christological fulfillment

#### TR: tsadaq → dikaioo
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md`
- Hebrew tsadaq (41x) → Greek dikaioo (40x NT)
- The LXX maps Hiphil tsadaq to dikaioo 21x (forensic declaration)
- **Semantic shift:** Hebrew covers 5 stems (be righteous, declare righteous, prove right, be vindicated, clear self); Greek narrows to primarily forensic declaration
- **Critical gap:** The OT Niphal (Dan 8:14 — vindication of sanctuary) has NO direct NT parallel via dikaioo; the concept takes different vocabulary in the NT (the dikaios+alethinos pair in Revelation)

### Defilement-to-Cleansing Arc

#### tame — H2930
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2930-tame.md`
- **Occurrences:** 194 tokens across 13 books; Leviticus = 50% (71 verses)
- **Stems:** Qal 72 (become unclean), Piel 43 (defile/pronounce unclean), Niphal 16 (be defiled), Hithpael 12 (defile self)
- **LXX:** miaino (G3392, PMI 31.76 primary), akathartos (G169, PMI 24.39)
- **7 semantic categories:** ritual contact, bodily, priestly declaration, sexual, idolatry, sacred space, deliberate desecration

#### taher — H2891
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2891-taher.md`
- **Occurrences:** 94 in OT; Leviticus = 46% (53x)
- **Stems:** Piel 37 (declare/pronounce clean), Qal 34 (be clean), Hithpael 18 (cleanse self)
- **Key collocation:** kaphar (H3722, 12x) — "make atonement... and be clean"
- **LXX:** katharizo (G2511, 61x primary)
- **Critical passage:** Lev 16:30 — kaphar (Piel) + taher (Piel infinitive + Qal imperfect)

#### TR: taher → katharizo
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-taher-katharizo.md`
- **Continuity:** Blood remains primary instrument (Lev 16 → Heb 9); God remains ultimate agent (Eze 36:25 → 1 Jn 1:9)
- **Shifts:** Ritual → moral/spiritual; priest-mediated → direct; external → internal; repeated → once-for-all
- **Heb 9:23 uses katharizo** for heavenly purification; **Dan 8:14 uses tsadaq** for the same event. These describe different aspects: cleansing happens; vindication is what it all means.

### Daniel Vocabulary Chain

#### biyn — H995
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H995-biyn.md`
- **Occurrences:** 189 tokens across 18 books; **Daniel density = 5.3 per 100 verses** (highest)
- **Stems:** Qal 45 (understand), Hiphil 30 (cause to understand), Hithpael 22 (consider), Niphal 21 (be discerning)
- **LXX:** syniemi (G4920, 63x, PMI 27.04)
- **The biyn chain in Daniel (19 occurrences):**
  - Dan 1:4,17 — capacity given
  - Dan 8:5 — Daniel considering (Hithpael)
  - **Dan 8:16 — havin (Hiphil imperative): "make this man understand the mar'eh"**
  - Dan 8:17 — biyn (Qal imperative): "Understand, O son of man"
  - **Dan 8:27 — mevin (Hiphil participle + negative): "none understood"**
  - Dan 9:2 — binoti (Qal): "I Daniel understood by books"
  - **Dan 9:22 — lehavinekha (Hiphil): "to give thee skill and understanding"**
  - Dan 9:23 — biyn (Qal imperative + Hithpael imperative): "understand the matter and consider the vision"
  - Dan 9:25 — teda vetaskil (know + sakal, not biyn, but paired)
  - **Dan 10:1 — biyn (Qal): "he understood the thing, and had understanding [binah] of the vision [mar'eh]"** — RESOLUTION
  - Dan 10:11 — havin (Hiphil): "understand the words that I speak"
  - Dan 10:12 — lehitbonen (Hithpael): "thou didst set thine heart to understand"
  - Dan 10:14 — lehavinkha (Hiphil): "to make thee understand"
  - Dan 11:33 — maskiley (sakal, not biyn; but contextually parallel)
  - **Dan 12:8 — lo vinoti (Qal negative): "I understood not"** — SECOND FAILURE
  - **Dan 12:10 — yavinu (Qal x2): "none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand"** — ESCHATOLOGICAL TRANSFER

#### mar'eh — H4758
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md`

- **In Daniel:** Specifically the visual element — "the vision of the evening and morning" (Dan 8:26)

#### satham — H5640
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5640-catham.md`
- **Occurrences:** 17 tokens; Daniel 3 (0.84/100 verses)
- **Definition:** Stop up, block access; figuratively conceal
- **Physical sense:** Stop wells (Gen 26:15); repair breaches (Neh 4:7)
- **Prophetic sealing:** Dan 8:26, 12:4, 12:9 — always paired with chatham in Daniel
- **LXX:** sphragizo (G4972) — collapses Hebrew distinction between satham and chatham

#### chatham — H2856
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md`
- **Occurrences:** 26 tokens; Qal dominates (22x)
- **Definition:** Close up, seal — specifically with signet ring; legal authentication
- **Key distinction from satham:** chatham = seal for authentication/permanent closure (signet); satham = block access/obstruct
- **Dan 9:24:** "to seal up [chatham] the vision and prophecy" — authentication sense
- **Dan 12:4:** satham + chatham paired — maximum sealing

### Sealing-Unsealing Arc (Greek)

#### katasphragizo — G2696
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2696-katasphragizo.md`
- **Occurrences:** 1 (Rev 5:1 — HAPAX in NT)
- **Definition:** Intensive seal — kata (completely) + sphragizo (seal)
- **Rev 5:1 parsing:** katesphragismenon — Perfect Passive Participle, Accusative Singular Neuter — the book HAS BEEN sealed and REMAINS sealed
- **Maximum sealing point** in the arc — seven seals with intensive prefix

#### bibliaridion — G974
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G974-bibliaridion.md`
- **Occurrences:** 4 (all in Rev 10:2,8,9,10)
- **Definition:** Double diminutive of biblos → biblion → bibliaridion (little book)
- **Rev 10:2:** eneogmenon (perfect passive participle of anoigo G455) — PERMANENTLY OPEN
- **The contrast:** Rev 5:1 katesphragismenon (sealed) → Rev 10:2 eneogmenon (opened). Same perfect passive participle construction, opposite states.

#### apokalypsis — G602
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G602-apokalypsis.md`
- **Occurrences:** 18 in NT
- **Definition:** Disclosure, uncovering — apo (away from) + kalypto (to cover/veil)
- **The trajectory:** What was sealed (Dan 8:26 satham) → sealed maximum (Rev 5:1 katasphragizo) → uncovered (apokalypsis, Rev 1:1)

### Judgment and Three Angels

#### krisis — G2920
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2920-krisis.md`
- **Occurrences:** 47 in NT; Matthew 12, John 11
- **Hebrew source:** mishpat (H4941, 132 co-occ in LXX), riyb (H7379, 29x), diyn (H1779, 9x)
- **Rev 14:7:** "the hour of his judgment [krisis] IS COME [elthen, aorist — punctiliar, decisive]"
- **Key collocation:** dikaios (G1342) + krisis = "righteous judgment" (5x)
- **Louw-Nida domain:** Court of Justice (056.001); Judge, Condemn, Acquit (056.005)

#### alethinos — G228
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md`
- **Occurrences:** 27 tokens; Revelation 10 (40%), John 8 (32%); Johannine = 80%
- **Definition:** Genuine, the real thing (vs. counterfeit/copy/shadow)
- **Hebrew source:** emeth (H571, PMI 16.05)
- **Key pairing with dikaios (G1342):** Rev 15:3 (dikaiai kai alethinai), Rev 16:7 (alethinai kai dikaiai), Rev 19:2 (alethinai kai dikaiai)
- **Antitype declarations:** Heb 8:2 "true tabernacle" (alethinos), Heb 9:24 "figures of the true" (alethinos)
- **The emeth→alethinos restoration:** Horn casts emeth to ground (Dan 8:12) → God's ways declared alethinai (Rev 15:3, 16:7, 19:2)

#### tereo — G5083
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5083-tereo.md`
- **Occurrences:** 75 tokens; Johannine = 56%
- **Definition:** Guard, watch over, keep (from loss); by extension, obey (a command)
- **Rev 14:12:** terountes (Present Active Participle, Nominative Plural Masculine) — ONGOING, HABITUAL keeping
- **The chain:** nitsdaq (Niphal — sanctuary receives vindication) → matsdiqey (Hiphil — maskilim cause righteousness) → terountes (present active participle — remnant keeps commandments). Same tsadaq root in Hebrew; same obedience field in Greek.

#### diyn (Aramaic) — A1780
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A1780-diyn.md`
- **Occurrences:** 5 (Daniel 4, Ezra 1)
- **Dan 7:10:** dina yetiv — "the judgment was set" — the Aramaic courtroom opens
- **Dan 7:22:** dina yehiv — "judgment was given to the saints"
- **Dan 7:26:** dina yitiv — "the judgment shall sit"
