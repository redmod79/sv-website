## Prior Studies

### From Series (sv-01 through sv-05)

**sv-01-biblical-righteousness:**
- Established the tsadaq word family (530+ OT uses) and Greek dik- family (234+ NT uses) across three dimensions: forensic (courtroom verdict), relational (covenant faithfulness), character (conduct).
- The verb tsadaq appears in five stems: Qal (22x, state of being righteous), Hiphil (12x, declarative verdict), Piel (5x, comparison), Hithpael (1x, self-clearing), Niphal (1x, Dan 8:14 only — receiving passive vindication).
- The LXX translation mapping tsadaq→dikaioo has PMI 26.99 — one of the tightest Hebrew-Greek mappings in Scripture.
- The tame→tahor→tsadaq progression is the series arc: defilement→cleansing→vindication.
- Dan 8:14's nitsdaq is a judicial term carrying all three dimensions. KJV "cleansed" comes from Theodotion/Vulgate, not from the Hebrew root.

**sv-02-biblical-defilement:**
- Established the two-axis framework of Lev 10:10: qodesh/chol (holy/common) and tahor/tame (clean/unclean) are independent axes.
- Seven categories of defilement exist in the Pentateuch; three mechanisms transmit defilement to the sanctuary: blood application, priestly eating, ambient contamination at a distance (Lev 15:31, Num 19:13).
- The third mechanism (ambient defilement) fills the tumot category in Lev 16:16 that external commentators consistently miss.
- The Haggai 2:12-13 asymmetry: impurity transmits by secondary contact; holiness does not.
- Tumah and divine presence cannot coexist (Lev 15:31, Lev 16:2).

**sv-03-tamid-doa-connection:**
- The daily and annual services are two halves of one system: daily puts sin IN through three channels; annual takes sin OUT.
- The vocabulary distinction is absolute: kaphar+salach (daily, 13x) vs. kaphar+taher (annual, Lev 16). salach never in Lev 16; taher never in Lev 4-5.
- The nasa verb connects both: priest bears IN (Lev 10:17); scapegoat bears OUT (Lev 16:22).
- The tamid never stops — even on the DOA itself (Num 29:11). Every special occasion uses "beside the continual burnt offering."
- H8548 tamid is 100% positive across 104 OT occurrences. Never describes paganism.
- The chain: Horn removes tamid → tamid must be restored → tamid feeds the DOA → restoring tamid restores the system whose endpoint is DOA → nitsdaq declares that system vindicated.

**sv-04-doa-ritual-sequence:**
- Mapped the complete twelve-step DOA sequence with chiastic structure.
- Blood moves from the innermost point outward, reversing the daily direction (inward).
- Two goats = one chattat in two phases: LORD's goat (blood atonement) + scapegoat (sin removal). kalah (v.20) separates the phases.
- Three-term formula Lev 16:16: tumot + pesha + chattat covers everything. pesha has no daily provision — only the DOA addresses willful rebellion.
- "Afflict your souls" (Lev 23:29) makes the DOA a sorting event: participate or be cut off.
- The kaphar→taher formula (Lev 16:30) is a three-verb chain: kaphar (act) → taher Piel (process) → taher Qal (state).

**sv-05-daniel-literary-architecture:**
- Daniel has four overlapping structural systems: Aramaic/Hebrew division, Aramaic chiasm (A-B-C-C'-B'-A'), four parallel vision cycles, vocabulary chains binding Dan 8-12.
- The chazon/mar'eh vocabulary distinction is the primary analytical lens: chazon = broad panoramic vision; mar'eh = specific time element within the vision.
- Dan 8:26 proof text: mar'eh authenticated, chazon sealed. Two words, two referents, two treatments.
- The biyn chain (8 stages across Daniel) is the narrative engine: capacity→command→failure→quest→resumption→resolution→reversal→eschatological promise.
- Dan 7 is the hinge chapter: closes the Aramaic chiasm, opens the prophetic sequence, introduces the judgment scene (7:9-10) and Son of Man (7:13).
- Six vocabulary chains cross chapter boundaries: chazon/mar'eh, biyn, eth qets, tamid/shiqquts, chatham, acharith.
- The sealed-to-unsealed arc: Dan 8:26→Dan 12:4→Rev 22:10.

**mekhon-informs-miqdasho-dan-8-11:**
- mekhon (H4349) adds the sense of establishedness, foundedness, and dwelling-site to miqdasho.
- Exo 15:17 is the prototype: makown leshivteka... miqdash — God establishing his heavenly dwelling.
- Solomon's dedication texts (1Ki 8:13; 2Ch 6:2) confirm mekhon functioning as "established dwelling-place" in a sanctuary setting.
- The phrase in Dan 8:11 means "the established place, founded site, or settled dwelling-place of his sanctuary."
- The 3ms suffix cannot refer to the 3fs horn (qeren is feminine throughout Dan 8).

**dan-8-12-truth-cast-down:**
- emeth (H571) in Dan 8:12 encompasses God's entire faithful order — covenant reliability, revelation, law, and justice.
- The triple "cast to ground" pattern (8:7, 10, 12) with directional suffix -ah on erets creates a heaven-to-earth degradation motif.
- asah (H6213) in 8:12 is generic Qal "she did" — KJV "practised" is interpretive; but context supports sustained action.
- tsalach Hiphil (H6743) — genuine but temporary success. The 8:12→11:36 bracket: "prosper till the indignation be accomplished."
- The divine passive tinnaten (Niphal of nathan) — God permits the horn's activity "by reason of transgression" (pesha).
- The forensic answer nitsdaq encompasses all four attacks simultaneously.

**trodden-under-foot-dan-8-13:**
- mirmas (H4823) is a result-state noun in miqtal pattern: "a trampled thing" — sustained condition, not single event.
- All seven OT occurrences of mirmas are in prophetic contexts. Technical vocabulary for covenant-consequence.
- Both sanctuary AND host are trampled (compound direct object under one predicate).
- The agent is the little horn, though the grammar masks it through passives.
- The sanctuary is heavenly: no earthly Jerusalem temple in 551 BC, 3ms suffix on miqdasho binds to Prince.
- LXX bridge: ramas→katapateo (G2662), highest-confidence translation relationship (PMI 16.69).
- Luke 21:24, Rev 11:2 carry the time-bounded trampling motif into NT.

**dan-8-14-vindication-meaning:** (score: 0.438)
- Vindication answers the four-fold attack: people trampled→vindicated (Rom 8:33-34), character challenged→"just and true" (Rev 15:3), ministry obscured→"minister of the sanctuary" (Heb 8:1-2), truth attacked→"everlasting righteousness" (Dan 9:24).
- The kaphar→tsadaq progression: Lev 16:30 (kaphar→taher) → Dan 9:24 (kaphar→tsedeq olamim) → Isa 53:11 (bearing→justifying) → Rom 3:25-26 (hilasterion→dikaios kai dikaiou̱nta) → Dan 8:14 (nitsdaq verdict).
- The tinnaten/nitsdaq divine-passive pairing: both are Niphal forms. What God gave over, God will set right.
- Five beneficiaries of vindication: God's people, God's character, God's ministry, God's truth/law, the watching universe.

**dan-8-14-evening-mornings:** (score: 0.634)
- erev boqer is an asyndetic anarthrous singular compound — unique in the Hebrew Bible.
- Dan 8:26 restates as ha-erev ve-ha-boqer (with articles and conjunction), proving 8:14's compact form is deliberate authorial compression.
- The tamid connection: the question (8:13) asks about ha-tamid; the answer counts in the tamid's own units (evening-morning cycles).
- Daniel uses yamim freely elsewhere (12:11 "1290 yamim"), proving he deliberately chose not to use it here.
- The Genesis 1 echo: erev boqer invokes the creation-day formula but transforms narrative into counting unit.
- The order (evening-then-morning) matches Genesis day-definition order, not the tamid's service order (morning-then-evening).
