### tsadaq — H6663
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`
- **Original:** צָדַק
- **Transliteration:** tsadaq
- **POS:** Verb
- **Occurrences:** 41x (some sources count 40-42)
- **Definition:** to be just, righteous, vindicated
- **Stems:** Qal (22x, state of being righteous), Hiphil (12x, declarative verdict), Piel (5x, comparison), Hithpael (1x, self-clearing), **Niphal (1x — Dan 8:14 ONLY)**
- **Dan 8:14 significance:** The only Niphal of tsadaq in the entire OT. The Niphal is a receiving-passive: the sanctuary RECEIVES vindication. This is a judicial term, not a ritual cleansing term (taher H2891 would be the cleansing word; kaphar H3722 would be the atonement word).
- **KJV translation issue:** "cleansed" comes from Theodotion/LXX (katharisthesetai) and the Vulgate (mundabitur), not from the Hebrew root tsadaq.
- **LXX mapping:** tsadaq→dikaioo has PMI 26.99 — one of the tightest Hebrew-Greek mappings.

**TR entry:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md`

### tamid — H8548
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md`
- **Original:** תָּמִיד
- **POS:** Noun (functions as substantive adjective)
- **Occurrences:** 104x in OT
- **Definition:** continual, perpetual, regular (the continual service/offering)
- **Dan 8 occurrences:** v.11 (removed), v.12 (host given against it), v.13 (question includes it)
- **Key finding (sv-03):** 100% positive across all 104 OT occurrences. Never describes paganism.
- **Significance for Dan 8:** The question (v.13) asks about ha-tamid; the answer (v.14) counts in the tamid's own units (erev boqer = one tamid cycle). Restoring the tamid restores the system whose endpoint is the DOA. The chain: horn removes tamid → tamid must be restored → tamid feeds the DOA → nitsdaq declares the system vindicated.

### miqdash — H4720
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4720-miqdash.md`
- **Original:** מִקְדָּשׁ
- **POS:** Noun, masculine singular
- **Definition:** sanctuary, holy place (mem-preformative noun of √qdsh, "place of sanctification")
- **Dan 8:11 usage:** mekhon miqdasho — "the established place of his sanctuary" — with 3ms suffix binding to the Prince, not the horn.
- **Lexical contrast with qodesh (H6944):** miqdash is the spatial locus (the consecrated precinct); qodesh is the broader holiness-sphere. Dan 8 shifts from miqdash (v.11, the attacked place) to qodesh (vv.13-14, the vindicated domain). The shift enlarges scope at the verdict.

### makon — H4349
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4349-makon.md`
- **Original:** מָכוֹן
- **POS:** Noun, masculine singular
- **Definition:** fixed-place, established-site, foundation, dwelling-base
- **Occurrences:** ~17x, predominantly of YHWH's heavenly/Zion-enthronement site
- **Key collocations:** Exo 15:17 (mekhon le-shivtekha — "place for thy dwelling"), 1Ki 8:13/2Ch 6:2 (mekhon le-shivtekha olamim), Psa 89:15/97:2 (tsedeq u-mishpat mekhon kisekha — "righteousness and judgment are the foundation of thy throne")
- **Significance:** The word NEVER denotes a pagan shrine or an ordinary earthly house. It belongs to the divine-dwelling/throne-base semantic register. Dan 8:11 chose the rarest, most theologically loaded option (not heykal "temple" or bayit "house").

### emeth — H571
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H571-emeth.md`
- **Original:** אֱמֶת
- **Definition:** truth, trustworthiness, faithfulness, stability
- **Dan 8:12 significance:** The horn casts "emeth" to the ground. This encompasses God's entire faithful order — covenant reliability, revelation, law, and justice. Not just "factual truth" in a narrow sense.
- **Triple casting pattern:** v.7 ram cast down, v.10 stars cast down, v.12 truth cast down — escalating from political to celestial to theological.

**TR entry:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-emeth-aletheia.md`

### chazon — H2377
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2377-chazon.md`
- **Original:** חָזוֹן
- **Definition:** vision, prophetic revelation (exclusively prophetic)
- **Dan 8 distribution:** vv.1,2,13,15,17,26 — consistently labels the WHOLE vision-prophecy complex
- **Distinguished from mareh (H4758):** In Dan 8, chazon = macro-revelation; mareh = specific visible element. V.26 proves two referents: mareh authenticated, chazon sealed.

### mareh — H4758
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md`
- **Original:** מַרְאֶה
- **Definition:** sight, appearance, vision (broader range: physical beauty, priestly inspection, theophanic appearances, AND prophetic visions)
- **Dan 8 distribution:** vv.15(simile),16(direct object of haven),26a(titular),27(unresolved)
- **The decisive v.16 usage:** Gabriel is commanded to explain ham-mareh (NOT he-chazon). V.26a retrospectively identifies mareh as "the evening-and-morning" oracle. V.27 records Daniel's astonishment at "the mareh" — still unresolved.

**TR entries:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-chazon-horasis.md`, `TR-mareh-opsis.md`

### biyn — H995
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H995-biyn.md`
- **Original:** בִּין
- **Definition:** understand, discern, perceive
- **The biyn chain (sv-05):** 8 stages across Daniel — capacity → command → failure → quest → resumption → resolution → reversal → eschatological promise
- **Dan 8 stages:** v.15 Daniel seeks vinah (Piel.Wayq), v.16 voice commands haven (Hiphil.Impv), v.17 Gabriel says haven (Hiphil.Impv to Daniel), v.27 ven mevin ("no understander" — chain FAILS here), Dan 9:22 Gabriel returns to supply understanding.

### tsaba — H6635
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6635-tsaba.md`
- **Original:** צָבָא
- **Definition:** host, army, service (military and cultic)
- **Dan 8 occurrences:** v.10 (tseva ha-shamayim — host of the heavens), v.11 (sar-hats-tsava — Prince of the host), v.12 (tsava tinnaten — host given over), v.13 (qodesh ve-tsava mirmas — sanctuary and host trampled)
- **The construct chain tseva ha-shamayim (v.10) LOCATES the host in heaven.** The ladder: host (v.10) → Prince of the host (v.11) → sanctuary of the Prince (v.11) — all anchored in the heavenly realm.

### gadal — H1431
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1431-gadal.md`
- **Original:** גָּדַל
- **Definition:** be great, grow great, magnify
- **Dan 8 escalation chain:**
  - v.8: higdil (Hiphil.Perf.3ms) — goat "magnified exceedingly"
  - v.9: tigdal-yeter (Qal.Wayq.3fs) — horn "grew with surplus"
  - v.10: tigdal (Qal.Wayq.3fs) — horn "grew up to" the host of heaven
  - v.11: higdil (Hiphil.Perf.3ms) — horn "magnified himself" up to the Prince
  - v.25: yagdil (Hiphil.Impf.3ms) — horn "shall magnify himself in his heart"
- **Stem distinction:** Qal = intransitive growth (size-increase). Hiphil without object = reflexive-intensive self-exaltation (morally loaded).

**TR entry:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-gadal-megalyno.md`

### mirmac — H4823
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4823-mirmac.md`
- **Definition:** trampling, thing trampled (result-state noun in miqtal pattern)
- **All 7 OT occurrences** in prophetic contexts — technical covenant-consequence vocabulary
- **Dan 8:13:** qodesh ve-tsava mirmas — compound direct object ("sanctuary and host — a trampled thing"). Both are trampled; the grammar makes it a sustained condition.

### shalak — H7993
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7993-shalak.md`
- **Definition:** throw, cast, hurl
- **The voice-chiasm root:** Appears three times across vv.10-12:
  - v.10: tappel (Hiphil naphal, synonymous "cast down") — ACTIVE
  - v.11: hushlak (Hophal.Perf.3ms of shalak) — PASSIVE (agent suppressed)
  - v.12: tashlekh (Hiphil.Impf.3fs of shalak) — ACTIVE (horn as named agent)
- **Same root, opposite voice** at the hinge of v.11 — the sharpest index of deliberate voice alternation.

### nathan — H5414
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5414-nathan.md`

- **The tinnaten/nitsdaq divine-passive pairing (from dan-8-14-vindication-meaning):** Both are Niphal forms. What God gave over (tinnaten), God will set right (nitsdaq).

### pesha — H6588
