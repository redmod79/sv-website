### Core Defilement Vocabulary

#### tame (verb) — H2930
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2930-tame.md`

- **Word:** טָמֵא (tame), primitive root verb
- **Occurrences:** 142 unique verses (194 tokens) across 13 books
- **Definition:** To be foul, especially in a ceremonial or moral sense; defile (self), pollute (self), be/make/pronounce unclean
- **Stem Distribution:**
  - Qal (72) — be/become unclean (involuntary state change)
  - Piel (43) — defile, make unclean, pronounce unclean (causative/declarative)
  - Niphal (16) — be defiled (passive)
  - Hithpael (12) — defile oneself (reflexive — voluntary self-contamination)
  - Hotpael (1) — be made unclean (Deut 24:4 only)
  - Pual (1) — be defiled (Ezek 4:14 only)
- **Distribution:** Leviticus 71 (50%), Ezekiel 28 (20%), Numbers 21
- **LXX:** μιαίνω G3392 (86x, primary verb), ἀκάθαρτος G169 (53x, adjective)
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Ritual impurity from contact, (2) Bodily discharges/skin conditions, (3) Priestly declaration (Piel), (4) Sexual defilement, (5) Idolatry/self-defilement (Hithpael), (6) Defilement of sacred space/land, (7) Josiah's deliberate desecration (Piel)
- **Key insight:** Stem distribution maps directly onto the ritual/moral distinction: Qal = involuntary, Piel = causative/declarative, Hithpael = deliberate self-defilement (almost exclusively idolatry in Ezekiel)

### Two-Axis Framework Vocabulary

#### qodesh (noun) — H6944
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md`

- **Word:** קֹדֶשׁ (qodesh), masculine noun
- **Occurrences:** 452 in OT across 31 books
- **Definition:** Sacred place or thing; sanctity. "Holy, holiness, sanctuary, most holy"
- **Distribution:** Leviticus 84, Exodus 70, Numbers 58, Ezekiel 51
- **LXX:** ἅγιος G40 (346x, ~77% — overwhelming dominance)
- **Semantic categories:** Sacred quality, sacred space (sanctuary), most holy (qodesh qodashim), abstract holiness, holy things, consecrated status
- **Key for this study:** Qodesh is one pole of the first axis (qodesh/chol). It is fundamentally a noun of separation — what is withdrawn from common use for God.

#### chol (adjective/noun) — H2455
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2455-chol.md`

- **Word:** חֹל (chol), masculine noun
- **Occurrences:** 7 unique verses across 3 books (Leviticus, 1 Samuel, Ezekiel)
- **Definition:** Properly "exposed"; profane, common, unholy. The opposite of qodesh.
- **Translation:** "profane" (43%), "common" (29%), "unholy" (14%)
- **LXX:** βέβηλος G952 (5x, PMI 20.42 — primary)
- **Key verses:** Lev 10:10 (foundational); Ezek 22:26, 44:23 (priestly failure/restoration)
- **Key insight:** The holy/common axis is independent of the clean/unclean axis. Something can be common (chol) and clean, or common and unclean. The dangerous intersection is when something holy (qodesh) becomes unclean (tame).

### Translation Relationships (LXX Bridges)

#### TR: tame → akathartos
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tame-akathartos.md`
Chain: H2931 tame (78x OT) → G169 akathartos (69x LXX, PMI 31.22) → G169 akathartos (29x NT)
**Key shift:** OT applies primarily to animals, diseased persons, dead bodies; NT applies primarily to unclean spirits (25 of 29 occurrences). Acts 10-11 explicitly transitions the word's application.

#### TR: tame → miaino
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tame-miaino.md`
Chain: H2930 tame verb (142x OT) → G3392 miaino (86x LXX, PMI 31.76) → G3392 miaino (4x NT)
**Key shift:** OT overwhelmingly bodily/ceremonial impurity; NT 3 of 4 uses are moral/conscience defilement (Titus 1:15, Heb 12:15, Jude 1:8). Only John 18:28 preserves ritual sense. Perfect tenses in Titus 1:15 suggest settled state, not temporary condition.

#### TR: tumah → akatharsia
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tumah-akatharsia.md`
Chain: H2932 tumah (31x OT) → G167 akatharsia (26x LXX, PMI 28.97) → G167 akatharsia (10x NT)
**Key shift:** OT overwhelmingly ritual-cultic; NT overwhelmingly moral-ethical (sexual sin, greed, hypocrisy). Ezekiel 36:17,25 forms the transitional bridge. Matt 23:27 applies classic corpse-contact impurity imagery to moral hypocrisy. 1 Thess 4:7: "God hath not called us unto uncleanness, but unto holiness" — akatharsia opposes hagiasmos.

#### TR: taher → katharizo
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-taher-katharizo.md`
Chain: H2891 taher (94x OT) → G2511 katharizo (61x LXX) → G2511 katharizo (30x NT)
**Key continuity:** Blood remains primary instrument (Lev 16 → Heb 9); God remains ultimate agent (Ezek 36:25 → 1 John 1:9); priestly declaration pattern persists (Lev 13 → Jesus in Matt 8).
**Key shift:** From external ritual to internal spiritual; from priest-mediated to direct (Jesus declares "be thou clean"); from repeated to once-for-all (Heb 10:2).

#### TR: qodesh → hagios
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-qodesh-hagios.md`
Chain: H6944 qodesh (452x OT) → G40 hagios (346x LXX) → G40 hagios (219x NT)
Remarkably little shift. The command "be ye holy, for I am holy" transfers directly (Lev 19:2 → 1 Pet 1:16). The spatial holiness gradient preserved in Heb 9. "Saints" (hagioi) becomes standard term for believers.

#### TR: chol → bebelos
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-chol-bebelos.md`
Chain: H2455 chol (7x OT) → G952 bebelos (5x LXX, PMI 20.42) → G952 bebelos (5x NT)
The Lev 10:10 framework persists into NT, with both bebelos and koinos occupying the "common" slot.
**Key NT passage:** Heb 12:16 — Esau as paradigm bebelos person (treating the sacred as common).
**Parallel axis:** NT develops koinos G2839 as a second, less formal word for "common" — used in Acts 10-11 Gentile inclusion debate.

### DOA/Sin Vocabulary

#### kaphar (verb) — H3722
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md`

- **Occurrences:** 102 in OT
- **Stem:** Piel 92 (90%), Pual 7
- **Translation:** "make atonement" (72%), "purge" (8%), "reconcile" (7%)

- **Key verse for this study:** Lev 16:16 (kaphar on the qodesh because of tumot, peshaim, chattat); Lev 16:30 (kaphar to taher)

#### pesha (noun) — H6588
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6588-pesha.md`

- **Translation:** "transgression" (76%)

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2403-chattaah.md`

#### salach (verb) — H5545

### Hebrew Piel Stem
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-piel.md`
- Relevant for tame Piel = priestly declaration of uncleanness (Lev 13:3,8,11,25 etc.)

## Hebrew/Greek Parsing Results (Summary)

### Lev 10:10 — The Two-Axis Framework
**Structure:** ule-havdil beyn haqqodesh uveyn hachol uveyn hattame uveyn hattahor
- havdil = Hiphil infinitive construct of badal "to separate/distinguish"
- Four terms: haqqodesh (the holy), hachol (the profane), hattame (the unclean), hattahor (the pure)
- Two independent pairs joined by repeated beyn...uveyn construction
- Confirms two axes, not one spectrum

### Lev 15:31 — Ambient Defilement Clause
- wehizzartem (Hiphil perf 2mp of nazar "dedicate/separate") — separate the children of Israel
- mittum'atam (from their tumah) — from their uncleanness
- betam'am (Piel infinitive construct + 3mp of tame) — "when they defile" (causative: their defilement pollutes)
- et-mishkani (my dwelling-place) — the object of defilement is God's dwelling
- **Critical finding:** The Piel infinitive construct of tame shows causative action — the uncleanness actively defiles the tabernacle at a distance

### Lev 16:16 — The Three-Category Formula
- vekhipper (Piel perfect 3ms of kaphar) — "and he shall atone"
- al-haqqodesh (upon the holy place) — the object of atonement is the sanctuary itself
- mittum'ot (from the tumot — plural of tumah) — first category: uncleannesses
- umipish'eihem (and from their peshaim) — second category: rebellions/transgressions
- lekhol-chatto'tam (for all their chattat) — third category: sins
- **Structure:** Three min-prepositions (mi-/u-mi-/le-) introduce three distinct categories
- **Final clause:** hashshokhen ittam betokh tum'otam — "that dwells with them in the midst of their uncleannesses"

### Lev 16:30 — The kaphar-taher Pairing
- yekhapper (Piel imperfect 3ms of kaphar) — atonement
- letaher (Piel infinitive construct of taher) — purpose: "to cleanse you"
- mikol chatto'teikhem (from all your sins)
- titharu (Qal imperfect 2mp of taher) — "you shall be clean"
- **Key:** kaphar produces taher, not tsadaq. The DOA moves from tame to tahor, not directly to tsadaq.

### Num 19:13 — Ambient Defilement Penalty
- tame (Piel perfect 3ms of tame) — "he has defiled" — causative: the unpurified person actively defiles
- et-mishkan YHWH — "the dwelling-place of the LORD" — same object as Lev 15:31
- venikhretah (Niphal perfect 3fs of karat) — "shall be cut off" — capital/covenantal penalty
- mei niddah lo-zoraq alav — "water of separation was not sprinkled upon him"
- **Confirms ambient mechanism:** The person need not physically enter the tabernacle. Failing to purify = defiling the sanctuary at a distance.

### Num 35:33 — Land Defilement (chaneph root)
- tachaniphu (Hiphil imperfect 2mp of chaneph) — "do not pollute" — different root from tame
- yachanif (Hiphil imperfect 3ms of chaneph) — "blood pollutes the land"
- lo-yekhuppar (Pual imperfect 3ms of kaphar) — "cannot be atoned for" except by blood of the shedder
- **Confirms distinct vocabulary:** chaneph (Hiphil) for land-pollution by bloodshed, tame for ritual/moral impurity

### Hag 2:12-13 — The Asymmetry Test
- **v.12:** If holy flesh (besar-qodesh) touches food via garment — hayiqdash? (Qal impf 3ms: "shall it be holy?") — Answer: No
- **v.13:** If one unclean by a dead body (teme-nephesh) touches food — hayitma? (Qal impf 3ms: "shall it be unclean?") — Answer: Yes (yitma)
- **Asymmetry confirmed:** Holiness does not transfer by secondary contact; uncleanness does. The purity system is inherently defensive.

### Ezek 22:26 — Priestly Failure
- yechalelu (Piel wayyiqtol 3mp of chalal) — "they profaned" (my holy things)
- lo hivdilu (Hiphil perfect 3mp of badal) — "they did not distinguish" (between holy and profane)
- lo hodi'u (Hiphil perfect 3mp of yada) — "they did not make known" (between unclean and clean)
- va'echal (Niphal wayyiqtol 1cs of chalal) — "and I am profaned" among them
- **Two failures:** (1) Not distinguishing qodesh from chol, (2) Not teaching tame from tahor

### Lev 18:24 — Moral Defilement (Hithpael)
- tittamme'u (Hithpael imperfect 2mp of tame) — "do not defile yourselves" — reflexive: voluntary self-defilement
- nitme'u (Niphal perfect 3p of tame) — "the nations were defiled" — passive: they were defiled by these practices
- **Confirms:** Hithpael tame in moral/sexual context, not ritual

### Mark 7:15 — Greek Parsing
- koinosai (Aorist Active Infinitive of koinoo G2840) — "to defile/make common"
- koinounta (Present Active Participle of koinoo G2840) — "the things that defile"
- **Jesus uses koinoo, not miaino.** He addresses the holy/common distinction (the qodesh/chol axis), not the clean/unclean distinction (the tahor/tame axis). The word choice is significant.

### Heb 9:13-14 — Blood Purification
- **v.13:** kekoinomenous (Perfect Middle/Passive Participle of koinoo G2840) — "those who have been defiled" — the settled state of defilement
- hagiazo (Present Active Indicative of G37) — "sanctifies" — the Levitical blood sanctifies toward fleshly purity (sarkos katharoteta)
- **v.14:** kathariei (Future Active Indicative of katharizo G2511) — "will cleanse" — Christ's blood cleanses the conscience (syneidesis)
- **Key contrast:** Levitical system → purity of flesh; Christ's blood → purity of conscience. The NT internalizes the defilement/cleansing dynamic.
