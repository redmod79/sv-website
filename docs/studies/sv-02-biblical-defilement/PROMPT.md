# Bible Study: Biblical Defilement — Tumah, Tame, and the Sanctuary

## Question
What does the Bible mean by defilement/uncleanness (tumah H2932, tame H2930)? What are all the mechanisms by which defilement occurs? How does defilement relate to the sanctuary — why is tumah incompatible with God's holy presence? Cover the Leviticus 10:10 two-axis framework (qodesh/chol + tahor/tame).

## Series Context
This is **Study 2 of 23** in the "Daniel 8:14 and the Sanctuary Vindication" series. Study 1 (sv-01-biblical-righteousness) established the tsadaq endpoint — forensic, relational, and character dimensions of righteousness. This study establishes the opposite pole: tame (defilement) as incompatibility with God's holy presence. Together, Studies 1 and 2 define the vindication trajectory: tame → tahor → tsadaq. Study 3 will build on this by showing how defilement enters and accumulates in the sanctuary through the daily service.

5. **sanc-09-day-of-atonement-ritual** (score: 0.519) — Lev 16:16 three-term formula: tumot, pesha, chattat. Primary object of atonement is the sanctuary itself. Outward movement (Most Holy → Holy → altar).

6. **sanc-01-why-a-sanctuary** (score: 0.480) — Core purpose: "that I may dwell among them" (Exo 25:8). Eden as proto-sanctuary. The sanctuary exists to resolve the tension between a holy God dwelling among unholy people.
