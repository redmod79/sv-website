### mar'eh (appearance/vision) — H4758
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md`

### tsaba (host/warfare) — H6635
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6635-tsaba.md`

### sakal (be wise/prudent) — H7919
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7919-sakal.md`

- **Occurrences:** 63 unique verses (70 tokens), 16 books
- **Daniel density:** 2.5 per 100 verses (highest)
- **Stem:** Hiphil dominates (~60 of 70)
- **LXX:** G4920 syniemi (31x, PMI 23.84) — same Greek word as biyn!
- **The maskilim (Hiphil participle):**
  - Dan 11:33 — "they that understand among the people shall instruct many"
  - Dan 11:35 — "some of them of understanding shall fall"
  - Dan 12:3 — "they that be wise shall shine"
  - Dan 12:10 — "the wise shall understand"
- **Key link:** Both biyn and sakal map to syniemi in LXX, but Dan 12:10 uses BOTH: maskilim (sakal) yavinu (biyn). The wise act with circumspect insight AND discern/comprehend.

### tsadaq (be righteous/vindicate) — H6663

- **Occurrences:** 41 in OT
- **Stem distribution:** Qal (22), Hiphil (12), Piel (5), Niphal (1), Hithpael (1)

### 'amad (stand) — H5975
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5975-amad.md`

- **Occurrences:** 597 tokens, 497 verses, 36 books
- **Daniel density:** 10.6 per 100 verses — by far the highest of any book
- **Daniel uses span:** standing before (service), political accession ("stand up"), withstanding, taking a stand
- **Key Daniel 12 uses:**
  - 12:1 ya'amod — Qal.Impf.3ms: "Michael shall stand up" (political/protective rising)
  - 12:1 ha-'omed — Qal.Ptcp.ms: "which standeth for" (ongoing protective stance)
  - 12:13 ta'amod — Qal.Impf.2ms: "thou shalt stand" (resurrection rising to allotted portion)
- **Note:** Same verb, different functions: Michael "stands up" as protector-deliverer; Daniel "shall stand" as resurrected recipient of his inheritance.

### nuach (rest/settle) — H5117
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5117-nuwach.md` (newly generated)

- **Occurrences:** 67 unique verses (108 tokens), 23 books
- **Stem distribution:** Hiphil (73.2%), Qal (23.2%), Hophal (3.5%)
- **Dan 12:13 form:** Qal.Impf.2ms tanuach — "thou shalt rest"
- **Semantic categories relevant to Dan 12:13:**
  - Personal/existential rest: Job 3:13 "then had I been at rest"; Job 3:17 "there the weary be at rest"
  - God giving rest from enemies: Deu 3:20; Jos 21:44; 2Sa 7:1
  - Sabbath rest: Exo 20:11; 23:12
- **LXX:** anapauo G373 (24x) — "to repose, refresh"; katapauo G2664 (18x) — "to settle down, cease"
- **Key collocations:** YHWH (29x), erets (16x), ruach (9x)
- **Dan 12:13 significance:** tanuach (you shall rest) = death-rest, settling into the sleep of death. Combined with ta'amod (you shall stand) and goralekha (your lot) = death, resurrection, inheritance. The verb carries the Deuteronomic promise of rest fulfilled eschatologically.

### goral (lot/portion) — H1486
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1486-gowral.md` (newly generated)

- **Occurrences:** 67 unique verses (90 tokens), 18 books
- **Joshua dominates (24/67 = 36%)** — lot-based land allotment
- **Dan 12:13 form:** goralekha — "thy lot" (with 2ms suffix)
- **Semantic range:**
  - Casting lots to determine divine will (Lev 16:8; Jon 1:7; Pro 16:33)
  - Land allotment to tribes (Jos 14:2; 18:10)
  - Figurative: allotted portion/destiny (Psa 16:5; Psa 125:3; Dan 12:13; Isa 17:14)
- **Key collocations:** nachalah H5159 "inheritance" (9x) — the lot determines the inheritance
- **LXX:** kleros G2819 (56x, PMI 7.36) — preserves full range: literal lot, allotted land, portion/destiny
- **Dan 12:13 significance:** Daniel's goral is his divinely allotted portion at the end of days. Combined with 'amad (stand), it means: "you will stand [rise] to receive your allotted inheritance." The word bridges the literal land-lot tradition (Joshua) to eschatological destiny.

### chamad (desire/delight) — H2530
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2530-chamad.md` (newly generated)

- **Occurrences:** 27 unique verses (33 tokens); 67 total root-family occurrences
- **Daniel density highest:** 6 occurrences (all noun forms chamudoth/chemdah)
- **Semantic range:**
  - Coveting/prohibited desire (Exo 20:17; Deu 5:21)
  - Aesthetic delight/desirability (Gen 2:9; Psa 19:10)
  - Greatly beloved/precious person — **unique to Daniel** (Dan 9:23; 10:11; 10:19)
  - Precious/valuable things (Dan 11:38,43)
- **The chamudoth address:** Daniel addressed as "ish chamudoth" ("man of desirables/preciousnesses") three times — 9:23, 10:11, 10:19. No other prophet receives this address. The triple repetition parallels the triple strengthening (10:10, 10:16, 10:18) and triple "fear not" (10:12, 10:19 x2).
- **LXX bridge:** epithymia/epithymeo G1939/G1937 (12 combined co-occ) — Jesus echoes chamad in Matt 5:28

## Grammar Reference Data

### Dan 10:1 Grammar (from library)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-10-1.md`
- u-vin (Qal.Perf.3ms with conjunctive waw) — completed understanding
- biynah lo ba-mar'eh — nominal clause expressing stative possession
- niglah (Niphal.Perf.3ms of galah) — divine passive: "was revealed"
- tsaba gadol — "great warfare" (not merely "long time")
- emeth ha-dabar — nominal clause with fronted predicate: truth-character of the word emphasized

### Dan 12:4 Sealing Grammar (from library)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-12-4-sealing.md`
- Dual Qal imperative pair: setom (satham) + chatom (chatham) — two different objects
- satham → ha-devarim (the words); chatham → ha-sepher (the book)
- Temporal boundary: 'ad-'eth qets — preposition 'ad + construct chain
- Post-boundary coda: two Yiqtol imperfects (yeshotetu + tirbeh) — future activity after terminus

### Dan 12:9 Sealing Grammar (from library)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-12-9-sealing.md`
- v.4 active imperatives → v.9 passive participles (setumim + chatumim)
- Reframing: what Daniel was commanded to DO is now described as a STATE
- Same temporal boundary: 'ad-'eth qets (verbatim)
- Qal passive participle (qetul pattern) — not active participle

### Dan 12:7,11-12 Time Period Grammar (from library)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-12-7-11-12.md`
- 12:7: mo'ed mo'adim va-chetsi — "time, times, and a half"
- mo'adim is masculine plural (not dual) — morphologically indefinite as to count
- 12:11: 1,290 yamim — from husar ha-tamid (removal of the continual)
- 12:12: 1,335 yamim — ashrey (blessed) + mechakkeh (the one who waits)
- The beatitude on the 1,335 is unique in Daniel — no other period gets a blessing

### Dan 8:15-16 Grammar (from library)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-15-16.md`
- 8:15: va-avaqshah biynah — "and I sought understanding"

### Dan 8:26 Grammar (from library)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-26.md`
- "mar'eh of the evening-morning" is declared emeth (true)
