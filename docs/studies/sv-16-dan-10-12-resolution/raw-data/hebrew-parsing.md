**Key findings:** lo avin = Qal.Impf.1s of biyn — "I do not understand." NOT the Qal.Perfect (completed) form used in 10:1. This is imperfective aspect — the NOT-understanding is ongoing/attempted. Daniel understood the mar'eh (10:1) but does NOT understand these additional time elements. acharith elleh = "the latter end/outcome of these" — not just "what do they mean?" but "what is the outcome/culmination?"

## Daniel 12:10

```
וְהִרְשִׁיעוּ   רשׁע         Verb.Hiphil.Perf.3p       and the wicked have acted wickedly
רְשָׁעִים       רשׁע         Adj.mp.Abs                wicked
וְלֹא יָבִינוּ  בין          Verb.Qal.Impf.3mp         and shall not understand
כָּל רְשָׁעִים   כל/רשׁע      Noun.ms.Cst/Adj.mp        all the wicked
וְהַמַּשְׂכִּלִים שׂכל       Verb.Hiphil.Ptcp.mp       and the wise ones
יָבִינוּ        בין          Verb.Qal.Impf.3mp         shall understand
```

**Key findings:**
1. Three purification verbs: yitbararu (Hithpael — reflexive purifying), yitlabbenu (Hithpael — reflexive whitening), yitsarfu (Niphal — passive refining). SAME three roots as Dan 11:35 but different stems — 11:35 uses active/Piel; 12:10 uses reflexive/passive.
2. hirshi'u (Hiphil.Perf.3p of rasha) — "have acted wickedly" — perfective aspect: the wicked's condition is established.
3. The biyn chain CONCLUSION: lo yavinu (Qal.Impf.3mp) = "shall not understand" — wicked; yavinu (Qal.Impf.3mp) = "shall understand" — maskilim. The verb biyn is the discriminating marker. Understanding is not merely intellectual — it is character-dependent.
4. maskilim (Hiphil.Ptcp.mp of sakal) — the SAME word used in 11:33, 11:35, 12:3 — a consistent eschatological category.

## Daniel 12:13
```
Hebrew          Lemma        Parsing                   Gloss
וְאַתָּה        אתה          PersPron.2ms              and you
לֵךְ            הלך          Verb.Qal.Impv.2ms         go! walk!
לַקֵּץ          קץ           Prep+Art+Noun.ms.Abs      to the end
וְתָנוּחַ       נוח          Verb.Qal.Impf.2ms         and you shall rest
וְתַעֲמֹד       עמד          Verb.Qal.Impf.2ms         and you shall stand
לְגֹרָלְךָ      גורל         Noun.ms.Abs.+2ms          to your lot
לְקֵץ הַיָּמִין  קץ/יום      Noun.ms.Cst/Noun.mp.Abs   at the end of the days
```

**Key findings:**
1. Three imperatives/imperfects forming a sequence: lekh (go) → tanuach (rest) → ta'amod (stand)
2. tanuach = Qal.Impf.2ms of nuach — "you shall rest/settle" — death-sleep
3. ta'amod = Qal.Impf.2ms of 'amad — "you shall stand" — resurrection
4. le-goralekha = "to/for your lot" (goral + 2ms suffix + le preposition) — your allotted portion/inheritance
5. le-qets ha-yamin = "at the end of the days" — the eschatological terminus
6. The pastoral sequence: go your way → die and rest → rise to your inheritance at the end
