## Grammar Library Entries Read

### 1. Dan 10:1 Grammar (`dan-10-1.md`)
Full morphological analysis of the verse. Key features:
- niglah = Niphal.Perf.3ms of galah — divine passive ("a word was revealed")
- u-vin = Qal.Perf.3ms of biyn — completed understanding
- biynah lo ba-mar'eh = nominal clause: "understanding [was] to him in the mar'eh"
- ve-emeth ha-dabar = fronted predicate nominal: truth precedes understanding
- tsaba gadol = "great warfare" (not "long time")
- The biyn chain resolution: 8:15 (noun/desire) → 8:16 (Hiphil impv/commission) → 8:27 (negated ptcp/failure) → 9:23 (dual impv/renewed commission) → 10:1 (Qal perf + stative noun = completed)

### 2. Dan 12:4 Sealing Grammar (`dan-12-4-sealing.md`)
- Dual Qal imperative pair: setom (satham) ha-devarim + chatom (chatham) ha-sepher
- Two sealing verbs, two different objects (words vs. book/scroll)
- Temporal boundary: 'ad-'eth qets (until the time of end)
- 'ad specifies a BOUNDARY after which the sealed state terminates (stronger than le in 8:26)
- Post-boundary coda: yeshotetu rabbim ve-tirbeh ha-da'ath — asyndetically joined to command
- Five-clause structure: command triad (1-3) + prediction pair (4-5)
- Asyndeton between command and prediction leaves causal/consequential relationship open

### 3. Dan 12:9 Sealing Grammar (`dan-12-9-sealing.md`)
- Active imperatives (v.4) → passive participles (v.9): setom/chatom → setumim/chatumim
- Qal passive participle (qetul pattern): CeCuCim — distinct from active participle CoCeCim
- Same temporal boundary: 'ad-'eth qets (verbatim match with v.4)
- The reframing: what Daniel was commanded to DO is now stated as a settled STATE
- lekh = Qal.Impv.2ms of halak — "go (your way)" — commission/release formula

### 4. Dan 12:7,11-12 Time Period Grammar (`dan-12-7-11-12.md`)
- 12:7: le-mo'ed mo'adim va-chetsi — "for a time, times, and a half"
  - mo'adim = masculine plural (NOT dual — dual would be mo'adayim)
  - The tradition reads "2 times" but morphology allows any number ≥ 2
  - The oath formula: raised both hands, swore by "the living of eternity"
  - ukhekalloth nappets yad-'am-qodesh = "when the shattering of the hand of the holy people is completed"
  - tikhleynah kol-elleh = "all these shall be finished" (Qal.Impf.3fp — feminine plural subject = "all these")
- 12:11: yamim eleph matayim ve-tish'im = 1,290 days
  - From husar ha-tamid = "the removal of the tamid" (Hophal inf con of sur)
- 12:12: ashrey ha-mechakkeh = "blessed is the one who waits" (Piel ptcp of chakah)
  - yamim eleph shelosh me'oth sheloshim va-chamishah = 1,335 days
  - The only beatitude in Daniel — unique blessing attached to this period

### 5. Dan 9:22-23 Grammar (`dan-9-22-23.md`)
- vayyaskileni = Hiphil.Wayq.3ms of sakal — "he gave me insight"
- biynah = understanding (noun) — Gabriel came to give biynah
- 9:23: u-vin ba-dabar ve-haven ba-mar'eh = dual imperatives
  - biyn = Qal.Impv.2ms — "understand the word"
  - haven = Hiphil.Impv.2ms — "gain insight into the vision"
  - The mar'eh and the dabar are BOTH objects of understanding

### 6. Dan 8:15-16 Grammar (`dan-8-15-16.md`)
- 8:15: va-avaqeshah biynah = "and I sought understanding" (biynah noun)
- 8:16: haven et-hallaz ha-mar'eh = "cause this one to understand the mar'eh"
  - haven = Hiphil.Impv.2ms — the commission origin
  - The command is to Gabriel: make Daniel understand the mar'eh specifically

### 7. Dan 8:26 Grammar (`dan-8-26.md`)
- mar'eh ha-erev veha-boqer = "the vision of the evening and the morning" — declared emeth (true)
- ha-chazon = "the vision" — commanded to be sealed (satham)
- The grammatical split: mar'eh gets a truth-declaration; chazon gets a concealment-command
- Two different words, two different directives in the same verse

### 8. Seal Command Syntax (`seal-command-syntax-daniel.md`)
- Imperatives: Dan 8:26 (satham), Dan 12:4 (satham + chatham)
- Passive participles: Dan 12:9 (setumim + chatumim)
- Infinitives: Dan 9:24 (chatham — "to seal up vision and prophecy")
- The sealing vocabulary progresses: single command (8:26) → dual command (12:4) → dual stative (12:9)

### 9. Apocalyptic Time Unit Grammar (`apocalyptic-time-unit-grammar.md`)
- yamim = standard day-unit (used freely in Dan 12:11,12)
- erev boqer = unique to Dan 8:14 — deliberately unusual counting unit
- mo'ed = appointed time (Dan 8:19; 12:7) — not a counting unit but a duration marker
- 'eth qets = "time of the end" — technical construct chain, anarthrous but definite by genre
