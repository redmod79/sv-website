# Verse Analysis

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Daniel 8:15
**Context:** Daniel has just seen the chazon — the ram, goat, horn sequence. The question "how long?" has been asked and answered with "2300 evening-mornings." Now Daniel seeks understanding.
**Direct statement:** "I, even I Daniel, had seen the vision, and sought for the meaning." The word "meaning" is biynah (H998), the noun form of biyn. This is the chain's starting point — Daniel's desire to understand.
**Original language:** va-avaqeshah biynah — the verb is Piel of baqash, intensified seeking. This is not casual curiosity. Daniel is actively pursuing comprehension of the mar'eh.
**Cross-references:** This desire-to-understand is the seed of everything that follows across Dan 8-12. The biyn chain begins with a human wanting to know.
**Relationship to other evidence:** 8:15 establishes what 10:1 resolves. The noun biynah appears in both verses — bookending the gap between desire and possession.

### Daniel 8:16
**Context:** A voice between the banks of the Ulai commands Gabriel to act.
**Direct statement:** "Gabriel, make this man to understand the vision [mar'eh]." The commission is specific: the mar'eh, not the chazon broadly.
**Original language:** haven (Hiphil.Impv.2ms of biyn) — causative: "cause him to understand." et-hallaz ha-mar'eh — "this vision/appearance." The grammar reference (dan-8-15-16.md) confirms the Hiphil imperative as the mission origin.
**Cross-references:** This commission drives Gabriel's actions through 9:22-23 and into 10:14. He was told to make Daniel understand. He does not stop until that happens.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The Hiphil form (causative) means the understanding is something Gabriel must produce in Daniel, not something Daniel achieves on his own. When 10:1 uses Qal perfect (Daniel simply "understood"), the shift from Hiphil to Qal shows the causative mission succeeded — Daniel now possesses understanding without needing ongoing instruction.

### Daniel 8:17
**Context:** Gabriel approaches Daniel, who falls on his face in fear.
**Direct statement:** "Understand, O son of man: for at the time of the end shall be the vision." Gabriel's first word to Daniel is biyn (Hiphil.Impv.2ms) — "understand!"
**Original language:** The Hiphil imperative addresses Daniel directly — the same causative form used in 8:16 and 10:11. The phrase le-'eth-qets ha-chazon anchors the chazon to the eschatological end, not Daniel's near future.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the first hint that full understanding may be beyond Daniel's lifetime. The chazon extends to 'eth qets — a period Daniel will not live to see.

### Daniel 8:26-27
**Context:** Gabriel has explained the symbols (ram = Medo-Persia, goat = Greece, horn = the persecuting power) but has NOT explained the 2300 evening-mornings. He now gives two different instructions for two different vision elements.
**Direct statement:** "The vision [mar'eh] of the evening and the morning which was told is true: wherefore shut thou up the vision [chazon]; for it shall be for many days." Then: "none understood it [the mar'eh]."
**Original language:** The grammar reference (dan-8-26.md) confirms the split: mar'eh gets a truth-declaration (emeth); chazon gets a concealment-command (satham). These are two different words with two different fates. The final clause ein mevin (Hiphil.Ptcp.ms, negated) = "no one understanding" — mission failure. Gabriel was told to make Daniel understand the mar'eh (8:16). Daniel does not understand (8:27). The mission remains incomplete.
**Cross-references:** sv-12 established six lines of evidence connecting this failure to Gabriel's return in Dan 9. sv-11 showed that the sealed content is the temporal element (2300 erev boqer).
**Relationship to other evidence:** 8:27 creates the tension that drives everything through 10:1. The biyn chain has failed at its first attempt.

### Daniel 9:2
**Context:** Years later, in the first year of Darius (likely ca. 539/538 BC), Daniel is studying Jeremiah's 70-year prophecy.
**Direct statement:** "I Daniel understood by books the number of the years."
**Original language:** biyn (Qal.Perf.1s) — Daniel understood. But the object of understanding here is Jeremiah's prophecy, not the mar'eh. Daniel can understand prophetic time periods from Scripture (70 years). This shows his capacity — and makes the failure to understand the 2300 more pointed. It is not that Daniel lacks ability. It is that the mar'eh was deliberately sealed.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This verse shows Daniel's own initiative in the biyn chain. He does not wait passively for Gabriel. He studies.

### Daniel 9:20-23
**Context:** Daniel is praying and confessing sin for his nation. Gabriel returns.
**Direct statement:** "I am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding" (9:22). "Therefore understand the matter, and consider the vision" (9:23).
**Original language:** 9:22 uses lehaskiylekha (Hiphil.InfCon + 2ms suffix of sakal) — "to give thee skill" — and biynah (noun). The wayyiqtol verb in this verse (vayyaven) is from biyn, not sakal. Gabriel came specifically to deliver understanding. 9:23 uses the dual imperative: biyn (Qal.Impv.2ms) + haven (Hiphil.Impv.2ms) — "understand the word AND gain insight into the mar'eh." The grammar reference (dan-9-22-23.md) confirms: both the dabar and the mar'eh are objects of understanding.
**Cross-references:** The chamudoth address first appears here (9:23): "thou art greatly beloved." This is the first of three times Daniel hears this address. sv-12 traced the six lines connecting this return to 8:27.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Gabriel resumes the mission from 8:16. The commission was to make Daniel understand the mar'eh. Now he adds the 70-week revelation, which provides the starting point the mar'eh lacked. But does Daniel actually understand yet? Not until 10:1.

### Daniel 9:24-27
**Context:** Gabriel delivers the 70-weeks prophecy.
**Direct statement:** "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people..." The six purposes of 9:24 include "to bring in everlasting righteousness" and "to seal up the vision and prophecy."
**Original language:** sv-14 established that chathak (hapax, "cut") proves the 70 weeks are "cut from" a larger period — the 2300. The kaphar-to-tsedeq bridge (sv-14) is the only place in Scripture where DOA vocabulary and vindication vocabulary meet.
**Cross-references:** sv-13 confirmed the 457 BC starting point and AD 27 fulfillment of the 483-year sub-period. sv-15 extended the calculation to 1844.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This section gives Daniel the KEY to understanding the mar'eh — the starting point. But the text does not say Daniel understood at this point. That comes in 10:1.

### Daniel 10:1
**Context:** Third year of Cyrus (ca. 536/535 BC). This is the prologue to the final vision.
**Direct statement:** "A thing was revealed unto Daniel... and the thing was true, but the time appointed was long: and he understood the thing, and had understanding of the vision [mar'eh]."
**Original language:** The grammar reference (dan-10-1.md) is decisive. u-vin (Qal.Perf.3ms) = completed understanding. biynah lo ba-mar'eh = nominal clause expressing settled possession — "understanding was to him in the mar'eh." The mar'eh track that opened at 8:16 and failed at 8:27 and resumed at 9:23 now CLOSES. Daniel possesses understanding.
**Key phrase:** tsaba gadol. The KJV renders this "the time appointed was long," but the canonical word study (H6635-tsaba.md) shows tsaba means host/warfare/service in virtually every occurrence. The phrase means "great warfare." Daniel now understands not just the timing but the SCOPE of the conflict — centuries of attack on God's system.
**Cross-references:** Exo 33:14 — "My presence shall go with thee, and I will give thee rest (nuach)." The same verb that appears in 12:13. The divine-presence-plus-rest pattern bookends the final vision.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This verse is the pivot. Everything before 10:1 in the biyn chain was building toward this moment. Everything after it builds toward the chain's conclusion at 12:10.

### Daniel 10:2-3
**Context:** Immediately after the statement of understanding (10:1). Daniel mourns for three full weeks.
**Direct statement:** "In those days I Daniel was mourning three full weeks. I ate no pleasant bread, neither came flesh nor wine in my mouth, neither did I anoint myself at all."
**Original language:** The parsing shows: lechem chamudoth = "bread of desirables/delicacies" (same chamad root as the chamudoth address). The mourning vocabulary includes hitabel (Hithpael — reflexive mourning), and the fasting is described in terms of what Daniel DOES NOT DO. lehit'annoth (10:12) confirms this was self-affliction before God.
**Cross-references:** Dan 9:3 — Daniel also fasted and mourned before Gabriel's return in ch. 9. But that mourning was driven by the captivity and Israel's sin. This mourning is different. The text says Daniel ALREADY understood (10:1). He mourns because of what he understood — the tsaba gadol, the great warfare stretching across centuries.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The mourning is not confusion. It is grief. Daniel grasps the 2300 years and what they mean — prolonged conflict, the horn's attack on God's system, the suffering of God's people. Understanding brought sorrow, not relief.

### Daniel 10:4-9
**Context:** After the three weeks, Daniel is beside the Tigris (Hiddekel). He sees a figure described in extraordinary terms — linen garments, gold, beryl body, lightning face, fire eyes, brass limbs, multitude-voice.
**Direct statement:** Daniel alone sees the vision (10:7). His companions flee in terror. He is left without strength, falls into a deep sleep face-down.
**Original language:** mar'eh haggedolah hazzoth — "this great vision/appearance" (10:8). The word is mar'eh again — a visual experience. hodiy nehpak = "my comeliness was turned to corruption" — Daniel's physical reaction mirrors 8:27 (where he fainted and was sick).
**Cross-references:** Rev 1:13-17 — John's vision of the glorified Christ uses strikingly similar imagery (white garments, golden sash, fire eyes, bronze feet, great voice). John also falls "as dead."
**Relationship to other evidence:** The physical collapse is important structurally. It sets up the triple strengthening pattern that follows (10:10, 10:16, 10:18). Heaven does not just deliver information — it prepares the recipient to bear it.

### Daniel 10:10-11
**Context:** A hand touches Daniel, setting him on his knees and palms. Then the speaker addresses him.
**Direct statement:** "O Daniel, a man greatly beloved, understand the words that I speak unto thee, and stand upright: for unto thee am I now sent."
**Original language:** ish chamudoth (man of desirables/preciousnesses) — the second use of this address (first was 9:23, third will be 10:19). haven (Hiphil.Impv.2ms of biyn) — the biyn chain continues: "understand!" This is the same Hiphil imperative Gabriel used in the original commission (8:16). va'amod (Qal.Impv.2ms of 'amad) — "and stand." Daniel is told to STAND — the same verb that appears in 12:1 (Michael stands) and 12:13 (Daniel shall stand). The word 'amad threads through these chapters linking physical posture, protective action, and resurrection.
**Cross-references:** The "I am sent" language (shulachti — Pual.Perf.1s of shalach, divine passive) echoes Isa 6:8 — prophetic commissioning. The speaker was SENT, not self-appointed.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The haven command here is not the same commission as 8:16. There, Gabriel was told to make Daniel understand the mar'eh (already resolved at 10:1). Here, the instruction is to understand "the words" (devarim) — the coming prophecy of Dan 11-12.

### Daniel 10:12
**Context:** The speaker explains why he came.
**Direct statement:** "From the first day that thou didst set thine heart to understand, and to chasten thyself before thy God, thy words were heard, and I am come for thy words."
**Original language:** lehavin (Hiphil.InfCon of biyn) — "to understand." Daniel set his heart to understand. This biyn-link shows Daniel's pursuit was active and deliberate. lehit'annoth (Hithpael.InfCon of 'anah) — "to humble yourself / afflict yourself" — the same verb used for the Day of Atonement's "afflict your souls" (Lev 16:29,31). nishme'u (Niphal.Perf.3p of shama) — "were heard" (divine passive). Daniel's words reached heaven on day one.
**Cross-references:** Lev 16:29,31 — the Day of Atonement connection. Daniel's self-affliction parallels the DOA requirement. sv-04 showed the DOA's "afflict your souls" creates a sorting event — those who participate are cleansed, those who refuse are cut off. Dan 12:10's purification/understanding divide echoes this same pattern.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The 21-day gap between Daniel's prayer (day 1) and the messenger's arrival is explained in the next verse.

### Daniel 10:13
**Context:** The reason for the 21-day delay.
**Direct statement:** "The prince of the kingdom of Persia withstood me one and twenty days: but, lo, Michael, one of the chief princes, came to help me."
**Original language:** echad ha-sarim ha-rishonim — grammatically ambiguous. sv-13-chief-princes showed echad in construct can be cardinal ("one of") or ordinal ("first/foremost of"). The 21-day period matches Daniel's mourning exactly (10:2-3).
**Cross-references:** The cosmic-conflict dimension. Daniel mourns and prays on earth. In heaven, a battle rages for 21 days. Michael intervenes. This is the only passage in Scripture that gives a detailed glimpse of spiritual warfare behind earthly prayer.
**Relationship to other evidence:** Michael's appearance here is the first of three in Dan 10-12: helper in cosmic conflict (10:13,21), "your prince" (10:21), and the great prince who stands for God's people at the eschatological crisis (12:1). The title progression shows increasing revelation of Michael's role.

### Daniel 10:14
**Context:** The speaker states his purpose.
**Direct statement:** "Now I am come to make thee understand what shall befall thy people in the latter days: for yet the vision [chazon] is for many days."
**Original language:** lehavinekha (Hiphil.InfCon + 2ms suffix of biyn) — "to cause you to understand." be-acharith ha-yamim = "in the latter days / end of days." Two critical terms here: (1) biyn continues the chain — the speaker came to deliver understanding. (2) chazon — not mar'eh. The mar'eh was resolved at 10:1. The chazon remains open. It "is for many days" — it extends into the distant future.
**Cross-references:** This directly confirms the chazon/mar'eh tracking system (sv-05, sv-12, Insight #7). The mar'eh (2300 time prophecy) is understood. The chazon (the broad prophetic panorama) still has content to unfold. Dan 11-12 is that unfolding.
**Relationship to other evidence:** 10:14 is the hinge between resolution (mar'eh understood) and continuation (chazon still coming). It explains WHY Dan 11-12 exists: the chazon has more to reveal.

### Daniel 10:15-17
**Context:** Daniel collapses again — face to the ground, unable to speak, no breath in him.
**Direct statement:** "By the vision my sorrows are turned upon me, and I have retained no strength... neither is there breath left in me."
**Original language:** ruach (H7307) in 10:17 — "neither is there breath/spirit left in me." This is the same word used for the Spirit of God. Daniel is physically emptied. The vision's weight has drained him completely.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This second collapse triggers the second touch (10:16 — lips touched, ability to speak restored) and sets up the final strengthening.

### Daniel 10:18-19
**Context:** The third touch and the third chamudoth address.
**Direct statement:** "One like the appearance of a man... strengthened me, And said, O man greatly beloved, fear not: peace be unto thee, be strong, yea, be strong."
**Original language:** vayechazqeni (Piel.Wayq.3ms of chazaq) — "he strengthened me." Then the double imperative: chazaq chazaq — "be strong, be strong!" The repetition signals urgency and affection. shalom lekha = "peace to you." hitchazaqti (Hithpael.Perf.1s) — "I was strengthened / I strengthened myself" — the reflexive shows Daniel receiving and internalizing the strength.
**Cross-references:** Deu 31:6 — "Be strong and of a good courage" — God's charge to Israel before entering the promised land. Same verb chazaq. The pastoral tone is unmistakable.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The triple pattern is now complete: three touches (10:10, 10:16, 10:18), three chamudoth addresses (9:23, 10:11, 10:19), three "fear not" moments (10:12, 10:19 x2). Heaven did not just dump information on Daniel. It progressively strengthened him to bear it. This is pastoral care at the structural level.

### Daniel 10:20-21
**Context:** The speaker previews what is coming.
**Direct statement:** "Now will I return to fight with the prince of Persia: and when I am gone forth, lo, the prince of Grecia shall come. But I will shew thee that which is noted in the scripture of truth: and there is none that holdeth with me in these things, but Michael your prince."
**Original language:** sar-yevan — "prince of Greece." The cosmic conflict continues — Persia's prince, then Greece's prince. kitav emeth = "writing/scripture of truth." Michael is called sarekhem — "your prince" (2mp suffix — Daniel's people's prince). This is the second Michael title: up from echad ha-sarim ha-rishonim (10:13) to "your prince."
**Relationship to other evidence:** The mention of Persia and Greece directly connects to Dan 8's sequence (ram = Persia, goat = Greece). This confirms Bohr's claim that Dan 11 explains the Dan 8 chazon — same starting point, same sequence. The cosmic conflict behind national history is real, and Michael is Israel's defender in it.

### Daniel 11:31-35 (the maskilim section)
**Context:** The horn's activity at its peak.
**Direct statement:** 11:31 — the tamid is taken away and the abomination set up. 11:32 — "the people that do know their God shall be strong." 11:33 — "they that understand (maskilim) among the people shall instruct many" but suffer. 11:35 — "some of them of understanding shall fall, to try them, and to purge, and to make them white."
**Original language:** 11:33 maskilim = Hiphil.Ptcp.mp of sakal (H7919). Same word as 12:3 and 12:10. yavinu (from biyn) also appears in 11:33 (from the full occurrences list). 11:35 uses the purification triad for the FIRST time: tsaraph + barar + laban. 12:10 will recycle this exact triad. The purpose clause in 11:35 is litsrof bahem ulevarrer ulelabben — "to refine among them, and to purify, and to make white" — identical roots to 12:10.
**Cross-references:** Mal 3:2-3 — God as refiner's fire, purifying the sons of Levi. Same concept: refining produces purity in those who endure it.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The deliberate recycling of the purification triad from 11:35 to 12:10 ties the maskilim's historical experience (suffering, persecution) to their eschatological identity (purified, understanding). The same process that tested them in history prepares them for the end.

### Daniel 12:1
**Context:** The transition from Dan 11's historical sweep to the eschatological finale. "And at that time" connects 12:1 to the end of Dan 11's prophecy.
**Direct statement:** "Michael stand up, the great prince which standeth for the children of thy people: and there shall be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation... and at that time thy people shall be delivered, every one that shall be found written in the book."
**Original language:** ya'amod (Qal.Impf.3ms of 'amad) = "shall stand up" — a specific future action. ha-'omed (Qal.Ptcp.ms of 'amad) = "the one standing" — ongoing, habitual protection. Two aspects of 'amad in one verse: Michael will RISE at a specific moment AND is CONTINUOUSLY standing guard. Michael's third title: ha-sar ha-gadol — "the great prince." The progression: "one/first of chief princes" (10:13) → "your prince" (10:21) → "the great prince" (12:1).
**Cross-references:** Jer 30:7 — "the time of Jacob's trouble; but he shall be saved out of it." Same vocabulary (distress, great, time). The deliverance condition: found written in the book. Exo 32:32-33 — the book of God, from which sinners can be blotted. Rev 20:12 — the book of life opened at judgment.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the first layer of divine response: deliverance. The maskilim from 11:33-35 are among those delivered. The book parallels the judgment of Dan 7:10 (books opened). sv-04 showed the DOA has a sorting function; 12:1 shows the eschatological sorting — written in the book or not.

### Daniel 12:2
**Context:** After the time of trouble and deliverance.
**Direct statement:** "Many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt."
**Original language:** yeshene admat-'aphar = "sleepers of the soil of dust" — death described as sleep. yaqitsu (Hiphil.Impf.3mp of quts) = "shall awake" — causative: God causes them to wake. der'aon (H1860) = "abhorrence" — appears only here and Isa 66:24. The dual outcome is absolute: eternal life or eternal contempt. No middle ground.
**Cross-references:** John 5:28-29 — "all that are in the graves shall hear his voice, And shall come forth; they that have done good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have done evil, unto the resurrection of damnation." This is the clearest NT echo of Dan 12:2. Isa 26:19 — "Thy dead men shall live... Awake and sing, ye that dwell in dust." Job 19:25-27 — "I know that my redeemer liveth, and that he shall stand at the latter day upon the earth."
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the second layer of divine response: resurrection. Dan 12:2 is the clearest OT statement of individual bodily resurrection with dual outcomes. Earlier resurrection texts (Isa 26:19, Ezk 37:1-14) focus on national restoration or single-outcome hope. Dan 12:2 adds the negative outcome and makes it individual.

### Daniel 12:3
**Context:** The reward of the wise — immediately following the resurrection.
**Direct statement:** "They that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars for ever and ever."
**Original language:** ha-maskilim (Hiphil.Ptcp.mp of sakal) = "the wise ones." yazihru (Hiphil.Impf.3mp of zahar) = "shall shine / give light" — causative: they emit brightness. matsdiqey ha-rabbim (Hiphil.Ptcp.mp.Cst of tsadaq + "the many") = "those causing the many to be righteous." This is the matsdiqey-nitsdaq connection (Insight #20). The sanctuary is vindicated (nitsdaq, Niphal, 8:14). The maskilim turn many to righteousness (matsdiqey, Hiphil, 12:3). Same root (tsadaq, H6663). Different stems. Complementary roles. The Niphal receives vindication from God. The Hiphil causes others to participate in it.
**Cross-references:** Matt 13:43 — "Then shall the righteous shine forth as the sun in the kingdom of their Father." Jesus directly alludes to Dan 12:3. Phil 2:15 — "ye shine as lights in the world." The maskilim concept extends into the NT as the faithful who both understand and teach.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The maskilim are defined by TWO activities: they understand (12:10) AND they teach (12:3, 11:33). They are not passive recipients of truth. They are active agents who help the deceived understand "which system was real" — turning many to righteousness. This is the human dimension of vindication. God declares the sanctuary vindicated (8:14). The maskilim bring people into that vindication (12:3).

### Daniel 12:4
**Context:** The sealing command.
**Direct statement:** "Shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end: many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased."
**Original language:** The grammar reference (dan-12-4-sealing.md) shows: setom (Qal.Impv.2ms of satham) ha-devarim = "shut up the words"; chatom (Qal.Impv.2ms of chatham) ha-sepher = "seal the book." Two verbs, two objects. satham blocks access to CONTENT (the words). chatham secures the CONTAINER (the book/scroll). The temporal boundary 'ad-'eth qets = "until the time of the end" — the preposition 'ad marks a boundary after which the sealed state terminates. Post-boundary: yeshotetu rabbim = "many shall run to and fro"; tirbeh ha-da'ath = "knowledge shall be increased."
**Cross-references:** Rev 22:10 — "Seal NOT the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand." This explicitly reverses Dan 12:4's sealing command. sv-11 traced the full sealing/unsealing vocabulary chain.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The sealed content identified by sv-11 (four criteria) is the temporal element of Dan 8:14 — the 2300 erev boqer. The "book" in 12:4 contains the sealed prophecy, and it will be unsealed when 'eth qets arrives.

### Daniel 12:5-7
**Context:** The epilogue scene. Two others stand on either side of the river. One asks the figure in linen: "How long?"
**Direct statement:** "How long shall it be to the end of these wonders?" The man in linen raises both hands to heaven and swears by "him that liveth for ever" that it shall be "a time, times, and an half."
**Original language:** The grammar reference (dan-12-7-11-12.md) confirms: mo'ed mo'adim va-chetsi = "time, times, and a half." mo'adim is masculine plural, not dual — morphologically indefinite as to count. The oath formula is unique in Daniel: both hands raised (not one), invoking the eternal God. "When he shall have accomplished to scatter the power of the holy people" — ukhekalloth nappets yad-'am-qodesh = "when the shattering of the hand of the holy people is completed."
**Cross-references:** Dan 7:25 — "a time and times and the dividing of time." Same period, Aramaic version. Rev 12:14 quotes the LXX of Dan 7:25 directly. sv-15's time-times-half-time study showed seven expressions across Daniel-Revelation all describe the same 1260-year period.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The 1260 becomes the baseline from which the 1290 and 1335 extend.

### Daniel 12:8
**Context:** Daniel has just heard the time, times, and half answer plus the condition "when the shattering of the holy people's power is completed."
**Direct statement:** "And I heard, but I understood not: then said I, O my Lord, what shall be the end of these things?"
**Original language:** lo avin (Qal.Impf.1s of biyn) — "I do not understand." This is NOT the Qal.Perfect form used in 10:1. The imperfective aspect signals ongoing, unresolved not-understanding. The antecedent of "these things" (elleh) is the time periods just given (12:7) and about to be given (12:11-12). acharith elleh = "the latter end / outcome of these" — Daniel is asking: what do these time periods lead to?
**Cross-references:** Compare with 8:27 ein mevin — "no one understanding." Same root biyn. Same failure. But a crucial difference: 8:27's failure is about the mar'eh (the 2300). 12:8's failure is about NEW content — the 1260/1290/1335 time periods. The mar'eh has been resolved (10:1). These are additional sealed elements Daniel was not given to understand.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is the biyn chain's second failure point. The first failure (8:27) was resolved at 10:1. This second failure (12:8) will NOT be resolved in Daniel's lifetime. It is resolved at 12:10 — but for the maskilim, not for Daniel personally.

### Daniel 12:9
**Context:** The response to Daniel's question.
**Direct statement:** "Go thy way, Daniel: for the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end."
**Original language:** lekh (Qal.Impv.2ms of halak) — "go." setumim ve-chatumim (passive participles of satham and chatham) — "closed up and sealed." What 12:4 commanded actively, 12:9 states passively. The words ARE sealed (settled state). The same temporal boundary: 'ad-'eth qets. The grammar reference (dan-12-9-sealing.md) notes the shift from command to stative: Daniel is no longer the actor who seals; he is the recipient told the state exists.
**Cross-references:** Dan 8:26 — the first sealing command used only satham. Dan 12:4 uses both satham and chatham. Dan 12:9 confirms both as passive participles. The sealing vocabulary progresses from single (8:26) to dual (12:4) to dual-stative (12:9).
**Relationship to other evidence:** The answer to Daniel's question ("what is the outcome?") is: you will not know. The words are sealed. This is not punishment — it is structural. The content is designed for the maskilim of 'eth qets, not for Daniel.

### Daniel 12:10
**Context:** The discriminating conclusion of the biyn chain.
**Direct statement:** "Many shall be purified, and made white, and tried; but the wicked shall do wickedly: and none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand."
**Original language:** Three purification verbs: yitbararu (Hithpael.Impf.3mp of barar — reflexive purifying), yitlabbenu (Hithpael.Impf.3mp of laban — reflexive whitening), yitsarfu (Niphal.Impf.3mp of tsaraph — passive refining). Then the discrimination: hirshi'u resha'im (Hiphil.Perf.3p + Adj.mp of rasha) — "the wicked have acted wickedly" (perfective — their condition is established). lo yavinu kol-resha'im — "none of the wicked shall understand" (biyn, Qal.Impf.3mp, negated). ve-ha-maskilim yavinu — "but the wise shall understand" (biyn, Qal.Impf.3mp, affirmed).
**Cross-references:** Dan 11:35 — same three-verb purification triad (tsaraph + barar + laban), same maskilim. The vocabulary recycling is deliberate. The historical experience of the maskilim (11:35, persecution and refining) prefigures their eschatological identity (12:10, purified and understanding). Matt 13:19 — the parable of the sower: the one who hears and does not understand. The discrimination between understanding and not-understanding is a theme Jesus develops.
**Relationship to other evidence:** This is where the biyn chain arrives. From 8:15 (Daniel desires understanding) through 8:27 (failure) through 10:1 (resolution of the mar'eh) through 12:8 (new failure with new content) to 12:10 (the maskilim WILL understand). The chain's destination is not Daniel. It is the maskilim of the time of the end. Understanding requires not just intellectual capacity but moral purification. The wicked CANNOT understand. Their character blocks access.

### Daniel 12:11
**Context:** Two additional time periods, given after Daniel's question and the sealing response.
**Direct statement:** "And from the time that the daily sacrifice shall be taken away, and the abomination that maketh desolate set up, there shall be a thousand two hundred and ninety days."
**Original language:** husar ha-tamid (Hophal.Perf.3ms of sur — "the tamid was removed") — passive: the tamid is removed (by whom? Agent suppressed). ve-latheth shiqquts meshomem = "and to set up the abomination of desolation." 1,290 yamim — note: Daniel uses yamim (days) here, the standard unit. He did NOT use yamim in 8:14, where he chose erev boqer instead. This confirms the erev boqer in 8:14 was a deliberate encoding choice, not a language limitation (sv-10).
**Cross-references:** Dan 8:11 — the tamid is taken away (same verb sur in different stem). Dan 11:31 — "they shall take away the daily sacrifice, and they shall place the abomination that maketh desolate." Same language, same sequence. sv-03 traced the tamid's role in the daily-to-annual system.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The 1290 extends 30 days beyond the 1260 baseline. Counted from AD 508 (if using the traditional Clovis/papal ascendancy reckoning), it reaches 1798. Counted from the tamid's removal, +1290 yields a date 30 years beyond the 1260's endpoint. sv-15 placed this at 1828.

### Daniel 12:12
**Context:** The final time period, with a unique blessing.
**Direct statement:** "Blessed is he that waiteth, and cometh to the thousand three hundred and five and thirty days."
**Original language:** ashrey ha-mechakkeh (Piel.Ptcp of chakah) — "blessed is the one who waits." 1,335 yamim. The grammar reference (dan-12-7-11-12.md) notes: this is the ONLY beatitude in Daniel. No other time period in the book gets a blessing.
**Cross-references:** sv-15 showed the 1335 extends 75 days beyond the 1290 (and 75 + 1260 = 1335). Counted from the same starting point, it converges with the 2300's terminus at 1843/1844 (Insight #17). Two independent time systems — counted from different starting points — arrive at the same date. The blessing marks the convergence point.
**Relationship to other evidence:** The 1290 and 1335 are part of what Daniel does NOT understand in 12:8. They are sealed content designed for the maskilim. The 1335's convergence with the 2300 terminus functions as a verification: when two independent calculations reach the same date, the system is confirmed.

### Daniel 12:13
**Context:** The final verse of Daniel. The speaker's last words to the prophet.
**Direct statement:** "But go thou thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days."
**Original language:** The parsing shows three key terms in sequence: lekh la-qets = "go to the end." tanuach (Qal.Impf.2ms of nuach) = "you shall rest" — death-rest. The canonical word study (H5117-nuwach.md) shows nuach carries the Deuteronomic promise of rest: Job 3:13,17 — the rest of the grave; Exo 20:11 — God's own rest; Exo 33:14 — "I will give thee rest." ta'amod (Qal.Impf.2ms of 'amad) = "you shall stand" — resurrection. le-goralekha (goral + 2ms suffix) = "to/for your lot." The canonical word study (H1486-gowral.md) shows goral bridges the literal land-lot tradition (Joshua's land allotment) to eschatological destiny. Combined with 'amad, it means: "you will rise to receive your allotted inheritance." le-qets ha-yamin = "at the end of the days."
**Cross-references:** Exo 33:14 — "I will give thee rest (nuach)." The strongest OT parallel (confirmed by cross-testament parallels tool). Psa 16:5 — the lot of the righteous (though the Hebrew there is manah, not goral, the concept parallels). Job 3:13,17 — death as rest. Eph 6:13 — "having done all, to stand."
**Relationship to other evidence:** The pastoral sequence is: go → rest → rise → receive your inheritance. Daniel will not live to see the unsealing. He will not be among the maskilim of the time of the end. But he will rest in death, and he will rise to receive his allotted portion. The promise is personal, specific, and complete. It addresses Daniel as a man, not just as a prophet.

### Isaiah 26:19
**Context:** Isaiah's apocalyptic vision of final deliverance.
**Direct statement:** "Thy dead men shall live, together with my dead body shall they arise. Awake and sing, ye that dwell in dust."
**Cross-references:** The vocabulary parallels Dan 12:2: "dust" (aphar), "awake" (same concept as yaqitsu), living from death. But Isa 26:19 is single-outcome (restoration), while Dan 12:2 adds the negative outcome (shame and everlasting contempt).

**Cross-references:** The verb 'amad appears: "he shall stand." The same verb used for Daniel's resurrection in 12:13. Job's hope is bodily — "in my flesh shall I see God." This is the earliest clear statement of personal bodily resurrection hope.

## Patterns Identified

- **Pattern 1: The biyn chain as narrative spine.** The verb biyn (H995) appears 19 times in Daniel. It creates an unbroken thread: desire (8:15) → commission (8:16) → first failure (8:27) → self-study (9:2) → resumed commission (9:22-23) → resolution of the mar'eh (10:1) → continued instruction (10:11,12,14) → second failure on new content (12:8) → discriminating conclusion (12:10). Supported by: Dan 8:15, 8:16, 8:27, 9:2, 9:22, 9:23, 10:1, 10:11, 10:12, 10:14, 12:8, 12:10. This is not a collection of unrelated uses. It is a deliberately constructed narrative arc about the process of understanding itself.

- **Pattern 2: Triple repetition as pastoral care.** Heaven addresses Daniel in threes: three chamudoth addresses (9:23, 10:11, 10:19), three touches/strengthening episodes (10:10, 10:16, 10:18), three "fear not" messages (10:12, 10:19 x2). Supported by: Dan 9:23, 10:10, 10:11, 10:12, 10:16, 10:18, 10:19. The triple structure shows that revelation is not mere data transfer. Heaven progressively prepares the human recipient — physically, emotionally, spiritually — to bear the weight of what is disclosed.

- **Pattern 3: Purification as prerequisite for understanding.** Dan 11:35 and 12:10 use the identical three-verb triad (barar + laban + tsaraph). In 11:35, the maskilim are refined through persecution. In 12:10, the "many" are purified AND "the wise shall understand." The triad appears nowhere else in Scripture in this combination. Supported by: Dan 11:35, 12:10, Mal 3:2-3, Isa 1:18. Understanding is not merely intellectual. It requires moral purification. The wicked "shall not understand" — not because the content is too complex, but because their character blocks access.

- **Pattern 4: The 'amad thread.** The verb 'amad (H5975) threads through Dan 10-12 with different functions for different subjects: Daniel told to "stand upright" (10:11), the speaker "stood" before Daniel (10:11), Michael "shall stand up" as protector (12:1), Michael "standeth" as ongoing guardian (12:1), Daniel "shall stand" in his lot (12:13). Same verb. Four functions: physical posture, presence, protective rising, resurrection. Supported by: Dan 10:11, 12:1 (x2), 12:13.

- **Pattern 5: Two tracks, two resolutions.** The chazon/mar'eh tracking system creates two parallel narrative lines with different destinations. The mar'eh (2300 time prophecy) resolves at 10:1 — Daniel understands. The chazon (the broad prophetic panorama) continues through 10:14, 11-12, and into the time of the end (12:4). Supported by: Dan 8:16, 8:26, 10:1, 10:14, 12:4. This is not a minor literary feature. It is the structural backbone of Dan 8-12. Without it, chapters 10-12 seem like a separate vision. With it, they are the continuation and completion of what began in chapter 8.

## Word Study Integration

The original language data transforms the English reading of Dan 10-12 at several points.

First, the KJV of 10:1 renders tsaba gadol as "the time appointed was long." But tsaba means warfare/military service in virtually every one of its 492 OT occurrences (canonical H6635 study). The phrase means "great warfare." Daniel understood that the vision involved centuries of conflict — not merely that the timeline was extended. This changes the emotional register. Daniel does not mourn because the waiting is long. He mourns because the suffering is vast.

Second, the English "I understood not" (12:8) and "none understood" (8:27) look identical. But the Hebrew forms are different. 8:27 uses ein mevin (Hiphil.Ptcp, negated) — participial: "no one understanding." 12:8 uses lo avin (Qal.Impf.1s) — imperfective: "I do not understand." The first is a statement about the state of affairs. The second is Daniel's personal confession of an ongoing inability. And crucially, what Daniel does not understand in 12:8 is NOT the mar'eh (resolved at 10:1) but the NEW time periods (1260, 1290, 1335).

Third, the matsdiqey-nitsdaq connection (canonical H6663 study) links 12:3 and 8:14 through the tsadaq root. nitsdaq (Niphal — the sanctuary receives vindication) and matsdiqey (Hiphil — the maskilim cause many to be righteous) are complementary stems of the same root. The English obscures this: "cleansed" (8:14 KJV) and "turn to righteousness" (12:3 KJV) do not look related. In Hebrew, they share a root and work together: God vindicates the sanctuary; the maskilim bring people into that vindication.

Fourth, the purification triad (barar + laban + tsaraph) appears in both 11:35 and 12:10, but with shifted stems. 11:35 uses active stems (Qal/Piel/Hiphil — tsaraph is Qal.InfCon, barar is Piel.InfCon, laban is Hiphil.InfCon) — external forces refine the maskilim. 12:10 uses Hithpael/Niphal (reflexive/passive — the "many" purify themselves and are refined). The shift from passive suffering to active self-purification suggests growth: those refined by persecution become agents of their own ongoing purification.

Fifth, the LXX flattens the biyn/sakal distinction. Both Hebrew verbs map to syniemi in Greek (canonical TR-biyn-syniemi and TR-sakal-syniemi). A Greek reader of Dan 12:10 cannot distinguish between the biyn chain and the maskilim identity. In Hebrew, 12:10 deliberately uses BOTH: maskilim (sakal) yavinu (biyn). The wise act with practical wisdom AND discern/comprehend. The two words together are richer than either alone.

## Cross-Testament Connections

Dan 12:2 and John 5:28-29 form the tightest OT-NT resurrection parallel. Daniel gives dual-outcome bodily resurrection: "some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt." Jesus expands: "they that have done good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have done evil, unto the resurrection of damnation." Daniel says "many." Jesus says "all." The scope broadens; the structure is identical.

Dan 12:3 and Matt 13:43 connect directly. "The wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament" becomes "the righteous shall shine forth as the sun in the kingdom of their Father." Jesus validates Daniel's maskilim concept and extends it to all the righteous.

Dan 12:4's sealing command and Rev 22:10's reversal bracket the NT: "Seal up the book" (Dan 12:4) → "Seal NOT the sayings of the prophecy" (Rev 22:10). The sealed state of Daniel's prophecy has a terminus: the time of the end. Revelation's explicit reversal confirms the terminus has arrived.

Dan 12:1's "time of trouble, such as never was" connects to Jer 30:7 ("the time of Jacob's trouble") and through Jesus to Matt 24:21 ("great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world"). The concept of a climactic crisis runs from OT prophecy through Jesus' teaching to Revelation.

The 'amad verb in Dan 12:13 ("thou shalt stand") and Eph 6:13 ("having done all, to stand") share vocabulary but not context. Paul's "stand" is about spiritual warfare endurance. Daniel's "stand" is about resurrection. The parallel is lexical, not conceptual — an example of same word, different meaning in different genres.

## Difficult or Complicating Passages

**Dan 10:1 — when did Daniel understand?** The verse says Daniel "understood the thing, and had understanding of the mar'eh." But the grammar reference shows u-vin as Qal.Perfect with conjunctive waw. This COULD be waw-consecutive (implying temporal sequence: the thing was revealed, THEN he understood). Or it could be simple conjunction (the thing was revealed AND he understood — simultaneously). If waw-consecutive, Daniel's understanding may have come after the three-week mourning and the vision of Dan 10-12. If simple conjunction, he understood before/during the mourning. The prior study (dan10-mourning-dan11-response) argued for the "understood before mourning" reading based on the verse's prologue position, but the grammar allows both.

**Dan 12:2 — "many" not "all."** The text says "many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake." Not ALL. This is often read as a general resurrection, but the Hebrew says rabbim ("many"), not kol ("all"). Does this limit the resurrection to a subset? Or is rabbim being used in the Semitic sense of "the great multitude" (inclusive)? John 5:28 says "all that are in the graves." The tension between "many" and "all" is real. One possibility: Dan 12:2 describes a specific resurrection event at the end of the time of trouble (a limited, pre-advent resurrection), not the universal resurrection. Another: rabbim is simply a Semitic way of saying "the masses." The text does not resolve this fully.

**Dan 12:8 — what exactly doesn't Daniel understand?** The antecedent of "these things" (elleh) is debated. The most recent content before 12:8 is the time-times-half answer in 12:7 and the condition about shattering the holy people's power. The additional time periods (1290, 1335) come AFTER 12:8, in verses 11-12. So what Daniel heard and did not understand is primarily the 1260 and the shattering condition — not the 1290/1335, which haven't been given yet. His question "what shall be the end of these things?" (mah acharith elleh) prompts the further revelation of 12:9-13, including the 1290 and 1335. This complicates the simple reading that Daniel "didn't understand the 1290/1335." He didn't understand what he had already heard, and the answer included even more sealed content.

**Dan 12:13 — personal resurrection or prophetic legacy?** EGW reads Dan 12:13's "stand in thy lot" as including Daniel's prophetic testimony going to the world in the time of the end — not only personal resurrection. The Hebrew allows this: 'amad can mean "stand" in the sense of "take one's position" or "be established." goral can mean "allotted portion / destiny" beyond personal inheritance. But the most natural reading, given the death-rest-resurrection sequence (go → rest → stand), is personal resurrection followed by receiving one's inheritance. The dual reading is possible but not demanded by the text.

## Preliminary Synthesis

The evidence points in a clear direction. Dan 10-12 is not a new vision. It is the completion of the vision that began in Dan 8. The chazon/mar'eh tracking system (sv-05, sv-12) demonstrates this: the mar'eh resolves at 10:1; the chazon continues through 10:14 and into chapters 11-12. The biyn chain (19 occurrences across Dan 8-12) is the structural backbone binding these chapters together — from Daniel's desire to understand (8:15) through failure (8:27) through resolution (10:1) to a second failure on new content (12:8) and finally to the discriminating promise that the maskilim will understand (12:10).

Daniel's mourning (10:2-3) is best read as a theological response to understanding what the 2300 years entail: centuries of warfare, the horn's attack on God's system, the suffering of God's people. He understands and is devastated. Heaven responds with the most detailed prophecy in Scripture (Dan 11) — showing God's sovereign foreknowledge over every ruler and event — plus four layers of comfort: Michael's deliverance (12:1), resurrection hope (12:2), eternal reward for the maskilim (12:3), and personal assurance (12:13).

The 1290 and 1335 are sealed content Daniel cannot grasp (12:8-9). They are signposts designed for the maskilim of the time of the end. The 1335's convergence with the 2300 terminus at 1844 (sv-15) functions as a verification: two independent time systems reaching the same date confirms the prophetic framework.

Dan 12:10 is the biyn chain's destination. Understanding is not purely intellectual — it requires moral purification. The purification triad (barar + laban + tsaraph) appears identically in 11:35 and 12:10, linking historical refinement through persecution to eschatological preparedness. The wicked cannot understand. The maskilim, purified through trial, will understand AND will turn many to righteousness (12:3), participating in the vindication that 8:14 announced.

Dan 12:13 closes the book with pastoral tenderness: go, rest, rise, receive your inheritance. Daniel will die. He will sleep. He will awake. He will stand in his allotted portion at the end of days. The promise is personal, specific, and complete. It addresses Daniel the man, not just Daniel the prophet.
