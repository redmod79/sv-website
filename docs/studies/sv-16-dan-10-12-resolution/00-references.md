## Prior Studies

### From Study Plan (Integrate list)

**dan10-mourning-dan11-response:**
- Question: "Is Daniel mourning because of Daniel 8 and 9? Is Daniel 11 God's response?"
- **biyn chain established:** 24+ occurrences of biyn (H995) across Dan 8-12 create an unbroken lexical thread: Gabriel commissioned (8:16, haven Hiphil imperative), failure (8:27, ein mevin — Hiphil participle negated), Daniel's initiative (9:2, binoti Qal perfect), Gabriel returns (9:22-23, va-yaven + binah + haven), resolution (10:1, u-vin + binah + ba-mar'eh)
- **10:1 as prologue, not retrospective:** waw-consecutive perfect u-vin and positioning as opening verse favor reading Daniel's understanding as preceding/accompanying the mourning. Daniel mourns BECAUSE he understands, not because he is confused
- **tsaba gadol = "great warfare":** H6635 tsaba means host/army/warfare in virtually every occurrence. The "time appointed was long" (KJV) obscures the Hebrew. Daniel understood the vision involves massive extended conflict
- **Four layers of divine response:** (1) detailed prophetic history showing divine sovereignty (ch. 11), (2) Michael's deliverance (12:1), (3) resurrection hope (12:2-3), (4) personal assurance to Daniel (12:13)
- **Pastoral care elements:** triple chamudoth address (9:23, 10:11, 10:19), triple strengthening (10:10, 10:16, 10:18), "fear not" formula (10:12, 10:19)
- **21-day correspondence:** Daniel's mourning and the cosmic conflict were concurrent; prayer triggered the heavenly mission
- **12:8 "I understood not" (lo vinoti):** Daniel understood the mar'eh (10:1) but NOT the additional time periods (1260, 1290, 1335). Full understanding reserved for maskilim: "the wise shall understand" (12:10, yavinu — biyn future tense)
- **12:13 triple promise:** nuach (rest/death), 'amad (stand/arise), goral (lot/inheritance) — death, resurrection, and eternal destiny

**time-times-half-time:**
- Seven prophetic time expressions across Daniel-Revelation all describe the same 3½-year/1260-day period
- Aramaic 'iddan means "year" (proven by Dan 4)
- Rev 12:14 directly quotes LXX of Dan 7:25 — proves John understood Daniel's time prophecy
- Day-year principle consistency: 70 weeks = 490 years (proven), 2300 = 2300 years, 1260 = 1260 years
- Extended periods: 1290 (+30 from 1260 baseline → 1828) and 1335 (+75 → 1843/1844)
- 1335 converges with 2300 terminus (457 BC + 2300 = 1844)
- Beatitude unique to 1335: "Blessed is he that waiteth and cometh to the 1335 days"

**dan10-13-chief-princes:**
- Hebrew phrase "echad ha-sarim ha-rishonim" is grammatically ambiguous: echad in construct can be cardinal ("one of") or ordinal ("first/foremost of")
- rishon (H7223) means "first" 98/185x but "chief" only 3x — minority KJV rendering
- Daniel's title progression for Michael: "one/first of chief princes" (10:13) → "your prince" (10:21) → "the great prince" (12:1)
- Five converging lines of evidence for Michael's preeminence: title progression, singular definite "THE archangel" (Jude 1:9), resurrection voice convergence (1 Thess 4:16 / John 5:25), Zech 3:2/Jude 1:9 rebuke formula, and biblical "firstborn" pattern
- Relevant to sv-16: Michael's role in Dan 10 cosmic conflict and Dan 12:1 deliverance

**michael-the-archangel:** (from semantic search, referenced in plan)
- Comprehensive study of Michael's characteristics and functions across Scripture
- Michael contends with Satan (Jude 1:9), fights the dragon (Rev 12:7), stands for God's people (Dan 12:1)
- Archangel title: singular definite in Jude 1:9, plural never appears in Scripture

**daniel-qets-the-end:** (referenced in plan but not found in semantic search — may exist under different slug)
