# Reference Gathering: The Little Horn's Counterfeit System

## Question
What specifically does the little horn replace the tamid with in Daniel 8:12? The horn doesn't just remove -- Dan 11:31 and 12:11 use explicit "take away AND place/set up" language. What is the counterfeit system? Map the four-fold attack to four counterfeits. How does the Dan 2:21/7:25 contrast (same verb shna, different forms) encode the gap between sovereignty and counterfeit? What does the divine passive tinnaten ("was given," 8:12) reveal about God's role?

## Study Plan Context
This is Study 9 of 23 in the "Daniel 8:14 and the Sanctuary Vindication" series. Per the series plan:

**Depends on:** Studies 3 (tamid-DOA connection) and 6 (Dan 8 verse-by-verse).

**Series function:** What the vindication must answer — the specific attacks that nitsdaq reverses.

**Key insights from series-insights.md:**
- Insight #13 (substitution not just removal): Dan 11:31 and 12:11 use "take away AND place/set up." Four attacks → four counterfeits.
- Insight #16 (standing/sitting contrast): Heb 10:11-12 — earthly priest STANDS (incomplete), Christ SAT DOWN (finished). Reinstalling standing priests reverses Christ's completed work.
- Insight #4 (divine passive frame): tinnaten (Dan 8:12, Niphal "was given") → nitsdaq (Dan 8:14, Niphal "shall be vindicated"). Both divine passives. What God permitted, God will set right.
- The Dan 2:21/7:25 shna contrast: Same Aramaic verb (Haphel), different forms — God ACTUALLY changes times (participle); the horn merely INTENDS to change (infinitive). Grammar encodes the gap.
- emeth = dath: Dan 8:12 (emeth cast down) and Dan 7:25 (dath changed) target the same thing. Psa 119:142 equates God's law with truth.
