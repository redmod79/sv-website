# Grammar References — Library Entries Read + Notes

## Library Entries Read

### 1. Niphal Stem (hebrew/stem-niphal.md)
- The N-stem: primarily simple passive, reflexive, or tolerative/middle
- Perfect: ni- prefix (niqtal); Imperfect: dagesh in first root letter (yiqqatel)
- Dan 8:14 nitsdaq = Niphal.Perf.3ms of tsadaq — the ONLY Niphal of tsadaq in the OT
- Function 1 (Simple Passive): subject receives action from external agent
- Dan 8:12 tinnaten = Niphal.Impf.3fs of nathan — "was given" (divine passive)

### 2. Hophal Stem (hebrew/stem-hophal.md)
- Passive counterpart of Hiphil — the causative passive
- ~440 occurrences in BH (vs. ~9,000 Hiphil) — rare but theologically heavy
- Agent-suppression is the defining feature: "was caused to do Z" — causer unnamed
- Dan 8:11 huram (Hophal.Perf.3ms of rum) — "was taken away" (tamid)
- Dan 8:11 hushlak (Hophal.Perf.3ms of shalak) — "was cast down" (sanctuary)
- Dan 12:11 husar (Hophal.Perf.3ms of sur) — "shall be taken away" (tamid)
- Legal passive formula: "shall be put to death" (mot yumat, ~17x) — agent unnamed

### 3. Hiphil Stem (hebrew/stem-hiphil.md)
- Causative active — the agent names who causes the action
- Dan 8:10 tappel (Hiphil.Wayq.3fs of naphal) — horn "cast down" the host (active, named agent)
- Dan 8:11 higdil (Hiphil.Perf.3ms of gadal) — horn "magnified himself" (active, named)
- Dan 8:12 tashlekh (Hiphil.Impf.3fs of shalak) — horn "cast down" truth (active, named)
- Dan 11:31 hesirou (Hiphil.Perf.3p of sur) — they "took away" the tamid (active, named)

### 4. Infinitive Construct of nathan — Predicative Complement (hebrew/infinitive-construct-natan-predicative.md)
- Dan 8:13 teth weqodesh wetsaba mirmac — "to give [both] sanctuary and host [to be] a trampling"
- The infinitive governs coordinated nominal object + predicative complement
- Dan 12:11 welatheth shiqquts shomem — "and for the purpose of placing the abomination that maketh desolate"
- 1 Ki 5:17 syntactic parallel: same InfCon teth + object + subjugation-predicate

### 5. Dan 8:14 Passage Analysis (passages/dan-8-14.md)
- wenitsdaq qodesh: Niphal.Perf.3ms of tsadaq with waw = prophetic perfect (future certainty)
- qodesh without article but definite from context (the sanctuary under discussion)
- erev boqer: asyndetic noun pair = compound time unit
- Clause is WQtX type (weqatal-X) — main prediction within quoted speech
- KJV "cleansed" is mistranslation; the verb root tsadaq = "be righteous/just/vindicated"

### 6. Divine Passive (greek/divine-passive.md)
- Passive verb whose unexpressed agent is God — Semitic reverential convention
- Apocalyptic edothe pattern in Revelation: Aorist Passive Indicative 3S of didomi
- Rev 13:5 edothe (2x): mouth given + authority given for 42 months
- Rev 13:7 edothe (2x): authority to make war with saints + authority over every tribe
- Rev 6:2,4,8; 9:1,3,5; 11:1; 16:8; 19:8; 20:4 — same pattern throughout Revelation
- The construction is structurally identical for positive and negative recipients

### 7. Aorist Passive Indicative (greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md)
- Rev 13:5,7 edothe forms confirmed as Aorist Passive Indicative 3S
- Includes the authorization-pattern analysis for Rev 13

### 8. Dan 8:12 asah parsing (hebrew/asah-qal-perfect-3fs.md)
