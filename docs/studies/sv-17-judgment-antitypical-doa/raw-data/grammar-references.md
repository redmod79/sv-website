### 1. Dan 7:9-10 Aramaic Judgment Scene
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-7-9-10.md`

**Key grammatical features:**
1. Periphrastic imperfective: chazeh haweit ("I was seeing") — Aramaic vision-introduction formula
2. remiyu (Peil Perfect 3mp of rmah): "thrones were placed" — NOT "cast down" (KJV). Court context favors placement, not overthrow. Peil = Aramaic passive.
3. yetib (Peal Perfect 3ms of ytb): used twice — v.9 "Ancient of Days sat" (enthronement); v.10 "the judgment sat" (court convened). Same verb, different subjects, marks transition from person to proceeding.
4. Four-phase verbal tense sequence:
   - Phase 1: Perfects (completed actions — thrones placed, Ancient sat)
   - Phase 2: Nominal clauses (static description — garment, hair, throne, wheels)
   - Phase 3: Participles + Imperfects (ongoing — fire flowing, serving, standing)
   - Phase 4: Perfects (judicial acts — judgment convened, books opened)
5. petiychu (Peil Perfect 3mp of ptch): "were opened" — divine passive. Creates inclusio with remiyu: both Peil passives frame the scene (thrones placed... books opened), with agent suppressed in both.
6. yeshammeshunneh (Pael Impf 3mp + 3ms suffix of shmsh): "were serving him" — only Pael (intensive) verb in the passage; imperfect = ongoing durative service.

### 2. Leviticus 16:30 — Kaphar-Taher Formula
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/lev-16-30.md`

**Three-verb formula (unique to this verse):**
1. ykhapper (Piel Impf 3ms of kaphar): priestly atoning action
2. letaher (Piel Inf.Con. of taher): purpose clause — "to cleanse you"
3. tithharu (Qal Impf 2mp of taher): resulting state — "you shall be clean"

**Stem shift:** Piel taher (transitive, causative) → Qal taher (intransitive, stative). Grammar encodes that cleanness is both externally conferred and internally realized.

**Formula comparison:**
- Daily: kaphar + salach (Niphal divine passive = forgiveness) — Lev 4:20, 4:26, etc.
- Annual: kaphar + taher (Qal stative = cleansing) — Lev 16:30 only in full form
- Daily addresses individual; annual addresses corporate ("all your sins," 2mp)
- salach NEVER in Lev 16; taher NEVER in Lev 4-5

### 3. Daniel 9:24-27 — Six Infinitival Purposes
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-9-24-27.md`

**Six telic infinitive construct clauses:**
1. lekhalle (Piel of klh): "to finish the transgression" (pesha)
2. ulehatem (Hiphil of tmm): "to make an end of sins" (chattat)
3. ulekhapper (Piel of kphr): "to make reconciliation for iniquity" (avon)
4. ulehabi (Hiphil of bw): "to bring in everlasting righteousness" (tsedeq olamim)
5. welachtom (Qal of chtm): "to seal up the vision and prophecy"
6. welimshoch (Qal of mshch): "to anoint the most Holy" (qodesh qodashim)

**3+3 structure:** First triad removes sin (pesha, chattat, avon = exact Lev 16:21 triad). Second triad installs positive results (tsedeq olamim, sealed prophecy, anointed sanctuary).

**nechtak (Niphal Perfect 3ms of chathak, H2852):** HAPAX — the only occurrence in the OT. "Seventy weeks are cut off/decreed." The unique cutting verb marks this as a deliberate allotment.

### 4. Leviticus 16:14-16 — Blood Application Sequence
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/lev-16-14-16.md`

- v.14: Blood of bull sprinkled on mercy seat (kapporeth) eastward, then before the mercy seat 7 times with finger
- v.15: Goat blood treated identically ("do with that blood as he did with the blood of the bullock")
- v.16: The summary statement — kaphar for the holy place because of tumot, pish'eihem, chatotam (three sin categories)
- Grammar: Hiphil of nazah (spatter) used twice in v.14, shifting from perfect (wehizzah, completed act) to imperfect (yazzeh, prescriptive instruction)

### 5. Leviticus 16:20-22 — Live-Goat Ritual
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/lev-16-20-22.md`

- v.20: kalah (Piel Perf 3ms of klh) — "when he hath made an end of reconciling" — structural boundary marker; blood atonement complete before scapegoat
- v.21: Three sin terms in coordinated construct: avonot (iniquities) + pish'eihem (their transgressions) + chatotam (their sins) — exact match to Lev 16:16
- v.21: hithvaddah (Hithpael of ydh) — "confess" — reflexive, Aaron confesses on behalf of the people
- v.22: nasa (Qal Perf 3ms of nsa) — "the goat shall bear" — the goat carries the sins away

### 6. Dan 8:14 Grammar Analysis
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-14.md`

- nitsdaq (Niphal Perfect 3ms of tsadaq): the only Niphal of tsadaq in the OT
- Asyndetic erev boqer: no conjunction between evening and morning; unique counting unit
- Two hapax constructions in one verse mark the temporal element as encoded

### 7. Dan 7:13 — kebar enash
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-7-13.md`

- "one like the Son of man came with the clouds [ananey] of heaven"
- The Aramaic anan (A6050) is hapax — one use in the entire Bible
- The figure approaches the Ancient of Days (movement toward the throne, not away from it)

### 8. Hebrew Stem Niphal
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-niphal.md`

- Three main functions: simple passive, reflexive/middle, tolerative
- Dan 8:14 nitsdaq = passive ("shall be vindicated") — sanctuary receives vindication
- Dan 9:24 nechtak = passive ("is decreed/cut off")
- The Niphal as "receiving vindication" puts the sanctuary as the beneficiary, not the agent

### 9. Dan 8:11-12 Voice Alternation
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-11-12-voice-alternation.md`

- Active-passive-active chiasm: horn acts → sanctuary affected (passive) → horn acts again
- When sanctuary is the target, grammar shifts to passive with agent suppressed
- Horn cannot directly touch heavenly sanctuary — grammar encodes the limitation
