# External Corpus Claims — Biblical Verification Data

## Claim 1: EGW — Sins transferred to heavenly sanctuary and blotted out (4SP 266.1)
**Claim:** "our sins are, in fact, transferred to the heavenly sanctuary by the blood of Christ" and cleansing = "removal, or blotting out, of the sins which are there recorded"

**Biblical data gathered:**
- Heb 9:23: "the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these" — uses katharizo (cleanse), supports heavenly purification concept
- Heb 9:24: Christ entered "heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us" — confirms heavenly ministry
- Rev 20:12: "judged out of those things which were written in the books, according to their works" — records exist in heaven; content = "works"
- Mal 3:16: "a book of remembrance was written before him" — records kept in heaven for the righteous
- Dan 7:10: "the books were opened" — Aramaic siphryn
- The texts confirm heavenly records exist and are examined. Whether "sins" are "transferred" or "recorded" involves theological inference. The texts say "works" (Rev 20:12) and "remembrance" (Mal 3:16).

## Claim 2: EGW — DOA high priest white linen → Christ's ministry (1BC 1111.5)
**Claim:** Direct typological line from DOA white linen garments to Christ's ministry

**Biblical data gathered:**
The linen/white chain:
- Lev 16:4: bad (H906) — Hebrew, "holy linen coat"
- Ezek 9:2: bad (H906) — Hebrew, "clothed with linen" (angel with inkhorn)
- Dan 10:5: bad (H906) — Hebrew, "clothed in linen" (heavenly figure)
- Dan 7:9: lebus (A3831) + chivvar (H2358) — Aramaic, different root, "garment white as snow"
- Rev 1:14: leukos (G3022) — Greek, "white like wool, white as snow"

**Finding:** The chain is THEMATIC, not lexical. bad (b-d root) → lebus (l-b-sh root) is a root break. Hebrew → Aramaic → Greek further separates the vocabulary. The imagery of white/linen persists but the vocabulary changes at each transition.

## Claim 3: Bohr — Dan 8 / Lev 1-16 parallel structure (GPOT2V1 p.451)
**Claim:** Dan 8 shifts from daily-type imagery (8:11 tamid) to annual-type imagery (8:14 nitsdaq), paralleling Lev 1-15 → Lev 16.

**Biblical data gathered:**
- Dan 8:11: tamid (H8548) — "the daily sacrifice" removed by the horn. Tamid = the daily continual service.
- Dan 8:14: nitsdaq — the terminal event, using vindication vocabulary unique in the OT.
- The structural shift from tamid (8:11, daily) to nitsdaq (8:14, annual/judgment) is present in the text. Whether this deliberately mirrors the Leviticus 1-15 → 16 structure is a structural observation, not a lexical proof. The text does shift from daily-type to annual-type vocabulary.

## Claim 4: Bohr — tsadaq is "not connected with the ritual of the sanctuary" (GPOT2V1 p.450)
**Claim:** tsadaq is a legal term, not a sanctuary-ritual term.

**Biblical data gathered:**
- H6663 tsadaq: 0 occurrences in Leviticus, 0 in Numbers
- All 41 verbal uses distributed across Job (23), Isaiah (8), Psalms (5), Daniel (4), Genesis (3), Ezekiel (3), Exodus (2, both courtroom), Deuteronomy (1, courtroom)
- The two Pentateuchal uses (Exo 23:7 and Deu 25:1) are both in judicial/courtroom settings

**Finding: CONFIRMED.** tsadaq never appears in any sanctuary/ritual context. It is exclusively a forensic/legal/wisdom term. The vocabulary gap between tsadaq (courtroom) and kaphar/taher (sanctuary) is real. Dan 9:24 bridges the gap by placing kaphar and tsedeq in the same verse.

## Claim 5: Smith — Dan 7:10 "books opened" = DOA cleansing (STTHD 260.2)
**Claim:** Direct equation between books being opened and DOA cleansing.

**Biblical data gathered:**
- Dan 7:10: "the books were opened" (siphryn petiychu)
- Lev 16:16: "make an atonement for the holy place, because of the uncleanness... their transgressions in all their sins"
- No text explicitly connects the books being opened (Dan 7:10) with the DOA cleansing process
- The connection is mediated through: (a) the structural parallel (both involve comprehensive examination/resolution of accumulated records/sins), (b) Dan 9:24 as the bridge verse, (c) the tamid link (the system whose removal prompts the judgment and whose restoration is declared in the vindication)

**Finding:** The equation is a theological inference, not a textual equation. The texts do not explicitly say "the books opened = the DOA cleansing." The connection is valid but mediated, not direct.

## Claim 6: Smith — "every individual of every generation" passes in review (STTHD 260.2)
**Claim:** Universal scope of the pre-advent judgment.

**Biblical data gathered:**
- Dan 7:22: "judgment was given TO the saints" — this could mean either judgment on behalf of saints or judgment authority given to saints
- Dan 7:10: "thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him" — universal angelic attendance, but does not specify who is judged
- Rev 20:12: "the dead, small and great, stand before God... judged according to their works" — universal scope, but this is the post-millennial scene
- Ecc 12:14: "God shall bring every work into judgment" — universal scope
- Mat 12:36: "every idle word" — universal scope
- 2 Cor 5:10: "we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ" — universal scope for believers
- Rev 14:7: "the hour of his judgment is come" — announcement, scope not specified in verse

**Finding:** The GENERAL judgment is universal in scope. Whether the PRE-ADVENT phase (Dan 7:9-10) covers "every individual of every generation" or focuses on the saints is not explicitly stated in Dan 7. Dan 7:22 says judgment given to/for the saints. Dan 12:1 says "thy people shall be delivered, every one that shall be found written in the book" — implying the book is checked for who IS written, which could support an investigative/pre-advent review.
