**Key vocabulary:**
- dikaion (G1342) = "just" — God himself IS righteous

- This is the NT synthesis of the kaphar→tsedeq bridge: propitiation demonstrates that God is both righteous and the one who makes righteous.


**Key vocabulary:**
- katharizesthai (G2511) = "to be cleansed/purified" — Present Passive Infinitive of katharizo. This is the LXX's standard rendering of taher (H2891).
- ta epourania (G2032) = "the heavenly things" — distinct from the earthly "patterns" (hypodeigmata). The heavenly realities needed their own (better) purification.
- The earthly DOA formula: kaphar → taher (Lev 16:30)
- The heavenly equivalent: better sacrifices → katharizesthai (Heb 9:23)
- The Dan 9:24 elevation: kaphar → tsedeq (beyond cleansing to vindication)

**Key vocabulary:**
- echrisen (G5548) = "anointed" — Aorist Active Indicative 3S of chrio. God is the subject who anoints.
- dikaiosynen (G1343) = "righteousness" — what the anointed one loved. Same word as in Rom 3:25-26.
- anomian (G458) = "lawlessness" — what was hated. LXX rendering of pesha and avon.
- Quoting Psalm 45:7. The anointing follows moral qualification: righteousness loved → then anointed.
- This connects to Dan 9:24: "everlasting righteousness" (tsedeq→dikaiosyne) + "anoint the most holy" (mashach→chrio). Both vocabulary streams appear in Heb 1:9.
