## From Word Studies Library

### kaphar — H3722
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md`
- **Root verb** of the atonement concept. 102 OT occurrences. Primitive root meaning "to cover."
- **Stem distribution:** Piel dominates (92x, 90%) = "make atonement, expiate, purge." Qal 1x = physical "cover" (Gen 6:14).
- **KJV translation:** "make atonement" ~73x (72%); "purge" 8x; "reconcile" 7x; "forgive" 5x.
- **Book distribution:** Leviticus 67x (66%), Numbers 19x. Pentateuch ritual = 83% of all uses. Prophetic usage shifts toward divine forgiveness. Daniel has exactly 1 occurrence: Dan 9:24.
- **Key collocations:** chattaah (38x), kohen (27x), mizbeach (17x), dam (12x), salach (12x), taher (12x), avon (10x).
- **Critical formula distinction:** Daily = kaphar + salach (Lev 4-5); Annual DOA = kaphar + taher (Lev 16:30). Dan 9:24 = kaphar + tsedeq (the ONLY co-occurrence in all of Scripture).
- **LXX mapping:** hilaskomai G2433 (highest PMI 6.47); aphiemi G863 (14x); katharizo G2511 (14x).
- **Word family:** kopher H3724 (ransom); kippur H3725 (expiation/DOA); kapporeth H3727 (mercy seat).

### tsedeq — H6664
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6664-tsedeq.md`
- **Masculine noun.** 112 unique verses, 119 tokens across 12 books.
- **Primary meaning:** "the right" (natural, moral, legal); abstract equity or prosperity.
- **KJV translation:** "righteousness" ~65 (58%); "just/justice" ~12 (11%).
- **Distribution:** Psalms 49 (44%), Isaiah 25 (22%). Sparse in Pentateuch (7 occurrences). Absent from narrative books.
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Equitable legal standard; (2) God's righteous character; (3) Personal moral uprightness; (4) God's commandments/Torah as righteousness; (5) tsedeq-mishpat couplet; (6) Messianic/eschatological righteousness.
- **Dan 9:24 usage:** tsedeq olamim = "everlasting righteousness" — the ONLY place kaphar and tsedeq appear in the same verse.
- **Key collocations:** YHWH (33x), mishpat (30x), shaphat (19x), emeth (8x), emunah (6x).
- **LXX:** dikaiosyne G1343 (81x, PMI 26.71) — primary, extremely strong bridge.

### tsadaq — H6663
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`
- **Root verb.** 41 occurrences. "To be right, to be just."
- **Stem distribution:** Qal 22 (be righteous); Hiphil 12 (justify, declare righteous); Piel 5 (prove right); **Niphal 1** (Dan 8:14 — the ONLY Niphal, "be vindicated"); Hithpael 1.
- **Key Niphal:** Dan 8:14 nitsdaq = "shall be vindicated/cleansed" — the forensic verdict on the sanctuary.
- **Key Hiphil:** Isa 53:11 yatsdiq = "shall justify many" — the righteous servant's causative action.
- **Dan 12:3 connection:** matsdiqey = Hiphil participle, "those who turn many to righteousness" — same root as nitsdaq.

### pesha — H6588
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6588-pesha.md`
- **Masculine noun.** 90 unique verses. Root = pasha H6586, "to break away from authority, rebel."
- **Core meaning:** Active defection from legitimate authority, not merely moral failure. The strongest sin-word.
- **Dan 9:24 role:** Purpose 1 — "to finish the transgression [ha-pesha]." The article "the" marks it as a known, specific transgression.
- **Lev 16:16,21 match:** pesha appears in both DOA formula verses alongside tumot and chattat. Dan 9:24 mirrors this triad.
- **LXX:** Spread across asebeia G763, anomia G458, hamartia G266 — no single Greek equivalent captures the rebellion sense.

### chattaah — H2403
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2403-chattaah.md`
- **Feminine noun.** 270 unique verses. Dual meaning: (1) the sin itself; (2) the sin offering that addresses it.
- **Dan 9:24 role:** Purpose 2 — Kethiv "to seal up sins [chatam chattaot]"; Qere "to make an end of sins [hatam chattaot]."
- **Lev 16:16,21 match:** chatotam = "their sins" (plural + 3mp suffix). The corporate/plural form confirms DOA scope.
- **LXX:** hamartia G266 (242x, PMI 31.95) — overwhelmingly dominant.

### avon — H5771
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5771-avon.md`
- **Masculine noun.** 213 unique verses. Three distinct senses: (1) iniquity/crookedness; (2) guilt; (3) punishment.
- **Dan 9:24 role:** Purpose 3 — "to make reconciliation [kaphar] for iniquity [avon]."
- **Lev 16:21 match:** avonot = "all the iniquities of the children of Israel." Corporate plural.
- **Isa 53 connection:** avon appears in 53:5, 53:6, 53:11 — the LORD laid avon on the Servant; the Servant bears avonot.
- **nasa avon formula:** 37x — bearing iniquity is priestly/vicarious.

### chathak — H2852 (HAPAX)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2852-chathak.md`
- **HAPAX LEGOMENON.** Occurs ONCE in entire OT: Dan 9:24.
- **Root meaning:** "to cut off." The figurative extension to "decree" follows the same path as other Hebrew "cut" verbs.
- **Verb form:** Niphal Perfect 3ms (nechtak) — divine passive: "has been cut off / decreed."
- **KJV:** "are determined" — captures the decree sense but obscures the cutting sense.
- **Theodotion LXX:** synetmethesan (from syntemno, "to cut short") — PRESERVES the cutting metaphor.
- **OG LXX:** ekrithesan (from krino, "to judge/decide") — renders the decree sense.
- **Cutting-verb family comparison:**
  - charats H2782 "determined/decreed" (12x) — Dan 9:26,27; 11:36
  - gazar H1504 "cut off/divide/decree" (13x) — Isa 53:8 (nigzar = "cut off")
  - karath H3772 "cut off" (288x) — Dan 9:26 (Messiah "cut off")
  - **chathak H2852 "cut off" (1x) — Dan 9:24 (70 weeks "cut off")**
- **The severance argument:** If chathak means "cut off," the 70 weeks are a portion severed from a larger whole. The only candidate in context is the 2300 of Dan 8:14. Gabriel was explaining the mar'eh; the 70 weeks are the first segment of that explanation.

### chatham — H2856
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md`
- **Verb.** 26 tokens, 24 verses. "To close up; to seal."
- **Dan 9:24 roles:** (1) Purpose 2 Kethiv: "to seal up [chatham] sins"; (2) Purpose 5: "to seal [chatham] the vision and prophecy."
- **Two senses of chatham in Daniel:** Concealment (Dan 12:4 "seal the book") vs. Authentication (Dan 9:24 "seal the vision" = stamp as genuine). The 70 weeks' historical fulfillment authenticates the entire prophetic time system.
- **LXX:** sphragizo G4972 (15x, PMI 9.53) — primary.

### mashach — H4886
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4886-mashach.md`
- **Verb.** 66 verses. "To rub with oil, anoint, consecrate."
- **Dan 9:24 role:** Purpose 6 — "to anoint [limshoch] the most Holy [qodesh qodashim]."
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Anointing sacred objects (Exo 40:9); (2) Anointing priests (Lev 8:12); (3) Anointing kings (1 Sam 16:13); (4) YHWH as direct agent (Isa 61:1, Ps 45:7); (5) Eschatological (Dan 9:24).
- **Key collocation with qadash:** mashach + qadash = 11x (17%). "Anoint and sanctify" as paired cultic sequence.
- **LXX:** chrio G5548 (51x, PMI 8.21) — near-exclusive for official anointing.

### qodesh — H6944
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md`
- **Masculine noun.** 452 occurrences. "A sacred place or thing; holiness."
- **qodesh qodashim ("most Holy"):** The doubled construct marks the highest sanctity. Used 40+ times in OT. NEVER refers to a person — always: (a) the Most Holy Place (Exo 26:33-34); (b) the most sacred objects (Exo 30:29); (c) the most sacred offerings (Lev 2:3, 10; 6:17; 7:1; 10:12; 24:9; Num 18:9); (d) devoted things (Lev 27:28); (e) the altar (Exo 29:37; 40:10); (f) Ezekiel's temple boundary (Eze 43:12; 48:12).
- **Dan 9:24 significance:** "to anoint qodesh qodashim" — since the phrase never refers to a person, the object of anointing is a place/sanctuary, not the Messiah personally. Combined with mashach's use for consecrating the tabernacle (Exo 40:9), this points to heavenly sanctuary inauguration.
- **Verification of qodesh qodashim pattern:** search_strongs.py --verses H6944 "most holy" returns 7 verses: Lev 10:12, 10:17, 14:13, 24:9, 27:28, 1Ch 6:49, Eze 43:12. All designate places, offerings, or objects. Zero refer to a person.

### tamam — H8552 (Purpose 2 Qere reading)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8552-tamam.md` (newly generated)
- **Verb.** 61 unique verses. "To complete, be finished, be consumed."
- **Stem distribution:** Qal 55 (87%) = "be complete, be finished, be consumed"; Hiphil 6 (10%) = "bring to completion."
- **Dan 9:24 role:** The Qere reading of Purpose 2 is ulhattem (Hiphil InfCon of tamam) = "to make an end of sins." The Hiphil causative sense means bringing sins to their definitive termination.
- **Dan 8:23 connection:** "when the transgressors are come to the full [tamam]" — transgression reaching its fullness, setting the stage for judgment.
- **Near-synonym kalah H3615:** tamam emphasizes reaching wholeness/totality; kalah emphasizes cessation/ending.
- **Moral sense:** Hithpael (2x) = "show oneself upright" (2 Sam 22:26, Psa 18:25). The completeness/wholeness concept overlaps with moral integrity.

### kala — H3607 (Purpose 1 alternative reading)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3607-kala.md` (newly generated)
- **Verb.** 17 unique verses. "To restrict, hold back, restrain, shut up."
- **Dan 9:24 role:** Some read Purpose 1 as lekale (Piel InfCon of kala) = "to restrain/shut up the transgression" rather than lekhalle from kalah H3615 "to finish."
- **BHSA parser confirms:** The grammar reference for Dan 9:24-27 parses the form as Piel InfCon from kalah H3615 "be complete." But the consonantal text kl' is ambiguous between the two roots.
- **Semantic distinction:** If kala (H3607): transgression is restrained/confined. If kalah (H3615): transgression is finished/completed. Both senses are theologically meaningful.

### chrio — G5548 (NT anointing verb)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5548-chrio.md` (newly generated)
- **Greek verb.** 5 NT occurrences. "To smear/rub with oil; to consecrate."
- **All aorist active:** Each anointing is a completed, punctiliar act — not a process.
- **Key passages:** Luke 4:18 (Spirit anointing for ministry); Acts 4:27 (divine appointment); Acts 10:38 (Spirit + power); Heb 1:9 (moral qualification → anointing); 2 Cor 1:21 (believers anointed).
- **Heb 1:9 significance:** "Thou hast loved righteousness [dikaiosyne] and hated iniquity [anomia]; therefore God... hath anointed [echrisen] thee." Righteousness + anointing vocabulary together — connecting to Dan 9:24's pairing of tsedeq olamim + mashach qodesh qodashim.
- **Hebrew source via LXX:** mashach H4886 (51 co-occurrences, PMI 32.44) — dominant. chrio IS the Greek mashach.

## Word Groups (from Library)

### WG-atonement.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-atonement.md`
- **Core cluster:** kaphar (action) → kopher (price) → kippur (event) → kapporeth (place)
- **Supporting:** chattaah (offering), dam (instrument), kohen (agent), mizbeach (location), salach (result-divine), taher (result-ritual), avon (problem)
- **Key co-occurrence:** kaphar + chattaah (38v), kaphar + salach (12v), kaphar + taher (12v)
- **Daily formula:** kaphar → salach (atonement → forgiveness)
- **Annual formula:** kaphar → taher (atonement → cleansing)
- **Dan 9:24 innovation:** kaphar → tsedeq (atonement → everlasting righteousness) — a vocabulary escalation from type to antitype

### WG-sin.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-sin.md`
- **The triad:** pesha (rebellion, 90v) + avon (iniquity/guilt, 213v) + chattaah (sin/sin-offering, 270v)
- **Key convergence passages:** Exo 34:7, Lev 16:21, Dan 9:24, Psa 32:5, Isa 59:12
- **LXX collapse:** Greek conflates all four Hebrew sin-terms into hamartia G266 — the NT reader loses the nuanced distinctions.

### WG-hebrew-righteousness.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-hebrew-righteousness.md`
- **Full family:** tsadaq (verb, 41x) + tsaddiyq (adj, 206x) + tsedeq (masc noun, 119x) + tsedaqah (fem noun, 157x) = 523 total
- **Key pair:** tsaddiyq vs rasha (81 shared verses) — the righteous vs. wicked antithesis
- **Key co-occurrence:** tsedaqah + mishpat (46 shared verses) — the justice hendiadys

### WG-sealing.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-sealing.md`
- **OT core:** chatham H2856 (verb, 23x) + chowtham H2368 (signet, 13x) + satham H5640 (shut up, 14x)
- **NT core:** sphragizo G4972 (verb, 25x) + sphragis G4973 (seal, 16x)
- **Dan 9:24 significance:** chatham appears in both the Kethiv of Purpose 2 ("seal up sins") and Purpose 5 ("seal the vision"). Two different senses of the same verb: concealment vs. authentication.

### WG-anointed.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-anointed.md`
- **Core:** mashiyach H4899 (anointed one, 38x) + mashach H4886 (anoint, 66x) + melek (king) + kohen (priest) + nagiyd (prince)
- **Dan 9:25-26:** mashiyach nagiyd = "Anointed Prince" (two absolutes in apposition)
