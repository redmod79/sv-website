## Prior Series Studies

### sv-03-tamid-doa-connection (Study 3: The Daily-to-Annual System)
**Relevant findings:**
- The daily (tamid) and annual (DOA) services are two phases of one integrated system. The daily puts sin IN; the annual takes sin OUT.
- The vocabulary distinction is absolute: kaphar+salach (daily, forgiveness) vs. kaphar+taher (annual, cleansing). salach never appears in Lev 16; taher never appears in Lev 4-5.
- The tamid never stops -- even on the DOA itself (Num 29:11, millebad formula). This is the base layer of the worship calendar.
- The nasa verb connects both phases: priest bears sin IN (Lev 10:17); scapegoat bears sin OUT (Lev 16:22). Same verb, opposite directions.
- The DOA's three-category formula (tumot, pesha, chattat) covers more than the daily service handles -- pesha (willful rebellion) has no daily provision.
- **Key for sv-04b:** The daily-to-annual system establishes the mechanics that the Jubilee DECLARES resolved. The Jubilee trumpet sounds on the DOA because the DOA is when the accumulated defilement is removed. Liberty follows cleansing.

### sv-04-doa-ritual-sequence (Study 4: The Day of Atonement Ritual)
**Relevant findings:**
- The DOA follows a chiastic structure: inward movement to the Most Holy Place (pivot), then outward. Five lines of grammatical evidence confirm this (nominal clauses, WXYq center, kapporeth distribution, nazah symmetry, kalah closure).
- The three-verb chain in Lev 16:30 traces the complete arc: kaphar (act) -> taher Piel (process) -> taher Qal (state). This is the DOA's explicit result: cleansing before the LORD.
- "Afflict your souls" (Lev 23:29) makes the DOA a sorting event. Those who participate are cleansed; those who refuse are cut off (karath Niphal -- divine passive). No other festival carries this penalty.
- The Pual of anah in the penalty clause (v.29) implies the affliction involves something done TO the soul -- a divine process, not merely a human act.
- The divine-knowledge function is built into the DOA: only God can verify inner compliance with "afflict your souls." This is not a new feature layered onto the type.
- **Key for sv-04b:** The DOA's binary outcome (cleansed OR cut off, Lev 16:30/23:29) maps to Isaiah 61:2's binary proclamation (ratson/favor + naqam/vengeance). The sorting function of the DOA is the mechanism that enables the Jubilee's liberty declaration -- only the cleansed receive it.

### sanc-15-jubilee-day-of-atonement (Score: 0.577)
**Title:** Jubilee: Liberty Proclaimed on the Day of Atonement
**Relevant findings:**
- The Jubilee's three interlocking components: personal liberty (deror), return to possession (shuv to land), return to family (shuv to kinship). All three are declared on the DOA.
- Theological foundation: "the land is mine" (Lev 25:23) -- the Jubilee asserts divine sovereignty. No human transaction can permanently alienate what belongs to God.
- The deror (H1865) -> aphesis (G859) LXX mapping creates a demonstrable vocabulary bridge from the Jubilee institution to the NT concept of forgiveness. Every NT proclamation of aphesis is linguistically a Jubilee proclamation.
- The Tishri sequence (Trumpets -> DOA -> Jubilee -> Tabernacles) encodes: warning -> judgment/liberation -> celebration.
- Jeremiah 34:15-17 demonstrates the Jubilee's two-edged nature: deror that liberates can also judge. God "proclaims a liberty to the sword" for those who revoke their liberty proclamation.
- The three-stage "already/not yet" pattern: Jubilee proclaimed (Luke 4:18-21), Spirit given as deposit (Eph 1:13-14), full redemption awaits (Rom 8:23).
- **Specific for sv-04b:** This study's primary weakness is that it did not trace the Isaiah 61 vocabulary chain verse by verse through the deror -> tsedeq -> tsedaqah progression. That is exactly what sv-04b must do.

### sanc-16-feast-of-tabernacles (Score: 0.613)
**Title:** Feast of Tabernacles: God Dwelling with Man
**Relevant findings:**
- The sukkah -> skene LXX bridge (PMI 16.97) and the exclusive NT use of skenoo (5x, all Johannine) establish the vocabulary spine of the dwelling theology.
- The fall feast sequence encodes: warning (Trumpets) -> judgment/purification (DOA) -> permanent dwelling/celebration (Tabernacles). The five-day gap between DOA and Tabernacles represents the theological principle that atonement must be complete before God can dwell fully.
- The emotional trajectory: DOA commands "afflict your souls" (Lev 23:27); five days later Tabernacles commands "rejoice before the LORD" (Lev 23:40). Affliction to joy is structurally mandated.
- Isaiah 4:4-6 follows the DOA-to-Tabernacles sequence exactly: purification by "spirit of judgment and burning" (v.4), THEN God creates a sukkah for shelter (v.6). The feast's own vocabulary in the feast's own sequence.
- Rev 7:14-16 repeats: washed robes in blood (DOA atonement) -> he shall skenosei over them (Tabernacles sheltering).
- **Key for sv-04b:** Tabernacles is the third element of the Tishri sequence. Its commanded joy is the endpoint of the arc that begins with Trumpets' alarm and passes through the DOA's judgment/Jubilee.

**Lead 1: Tabernacles joy follows DOA cleansing (PP 542.1)**
EGW claims: "The people of Israel praised God at the Feast of Tabernacles, as they called to mind His mercy in their deliverance from the bondage of Egypt... But when the ransomed of the Lord shall have been safely gathered into the heavenly Canaan, forever delivered from the bondage of the curse... they will rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory. Christ's great work of atonement for men will then have been completed, and their sins will have been forever blotted out."
**Verify against:** Lev 23:27 (DOA affliction) vs. Lev 23:40 (Tabernacles rejoicing) -- does the text itself mandate this joy-after-judgment sequence? Check whether the five-day gap (Tishri 10 to Tishri 15) structurally encodes the principle that atonement enables celebration. Cross-reference Rom 8:22 and the eschatological trajectory.

**Lead 2: Tabernacles celebration after DOA (EP 388.6)**
EGW claims: "This feast was an occasion of rejoicing. It occurred just after the great Day of Atonement, when assurance had been given that their iniquity should be remembered no more. At peace with God, the labors of the harvest ended and the toils of the new year not yet begun, the people could give themselves up to the sacred, joyous influences of the hour."
**Verify against:** Lev 23:34-43 -- does the text give a rationale for the joy? **Lead 3: Zechariah 3 garment change as DOA-context vindication (PK 591.1)**

## Key Vocabulary to Investigate

The research agent should perform independent word studies or verify these vocabulary items:

1. **deror (H1865)** -- "liberty/release." Appears in Lev 25:10; Isa 61:1; Jer 34:8,15,17; Ezek 46:17. The word study should verify the full distribution and whether deror is exclusively Jubilee vocabulary.

2. **ratson (H7522)** -- "favor/acceptance/will." Appears in Isa 61:2 ("acceptable year of the LORD"). Check whether ratson connects to the DOA vocabulary. Lev 1:3 uses lirtsono ("for his acceptance") for burnt offerings. Does Isa 61:2's "year of ratson" echo the sacrificial acceptance formula?

3. **naqam (H5358/H5359)** -- "vengeance/avenge." Appears in Isa 61:2 ("day of vengeance of our God"). The binary ratson + naqam in Isa 61:2 may map to the DOA's binary outcome: cleansed (Lev 16:30) or cut off (Lev 23:29). Verify whether the pairing is parallel to the DOA sorting function.

4. **tsedeq (H6664) / tsedaqah (H6666)** -- The chain in Isaiah 61: tsedeq (v.3, "oaks of righteousness"), mishpat (v.8, "judgment"), tsedaqah (v.10, "robe of righteousness"), tsedaqah (v.11, "righteousness before all nations"). All from the tsadaq root -- same as nitsdaq in Dan 8:14.

5. **yashar (H3477)** -- "right/upright." Jer 34:15 uses yashar in connection with deror. Verify whether yashar and tsadaq overlap or are distinct semantic fields.

6. **machalatsot (H4254)** -- "festival garments" in Zech 3:4. Compare with me'il tsedaqah in Isa 61:10. Are these the same type of garment, or different? The Zech 3/Isa 61:10 parallel depends on whether both texts describe a post-judgment conferral of righteous standing through garment imagery.
