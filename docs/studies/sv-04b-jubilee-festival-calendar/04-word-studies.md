# Word Studies — sv-04b Jubilee, Isaiah 61, and the Festival Calendar

## Core Vocabulary

### H1865 deror (liberty/release)
**Source:** `word-studies/H1865-drowr.md`
- 9 tokens across 7 verses in 5 books (Exodus, Leviticus, Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel)
- KJV: "liberty" (71%), "pure" (14%)
- Root = unimpeded movement/flow
- 6 of 7 liberty-sense occurrences use collocation *qara deror* ("proclaim liberty") — always PROCLAIMED, never merely enacted
- 4 semantic categories: Jubilee institutional release (Lev 25:10, Eze 46:17), slave release in covenant context (Jer 34:8,15,17), messianic/prophetic liberation (Isa 61:1), "pure" = free-flowing myrrh (Exo 30:23)
- Jer 34:17: God's ironic counter-proclamation — "I proclaim a deror FOR YOU to sword, pestilence, famine"
- LXX bridge: G859 aphesis (6 co-occ, PMI 8.96) — forgiveness/release

### H3104 yobel (jubilee/ram's horn)
**Source:** `word-studies/H3104-yowbel.md`
- 25 verses/35 tokens across 4 books. Leviticus dominates (72%)
- KJV: "jubile" (84%), "rams' horns" (20%)
- Two semantic poles: (1) instrument — physical ram's horn (Jos 6, Exo 19); (2) institution — fiftieth-year cycle (Lev 25, 27, Num 36)
- Instrument names the institution — connection is the prolonged blast (teruah) that inaugurates the year
- LXX translators chose aphesis (release/forgiveness) rather than transliterating, capturing the core meaning

### H6663 tsadaq (be righteous/justify)
**Source:** `word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`
- 41 occurrences. Qal (22), Hiphil (12), Piel (5), Niphal (1 = Dan 8:14 only), Hithpael (1)
- ZERO occurrences in Leviticus — courtroom/forensic vocabulary, not ritual
- Dan 8:14 nitsdaq is the sole Niphal — passive receipt of vindication
- Collocations: mishpat (30x), rasha (27x), emeth (8x) — judicial context

### H6664 tsedeq (righteousness, masc.)
**Source:** `word-studies/H6664-tsedeq.md`

- KJV: "righteousness" (~58%), "just/justice" (~11%)
- Isa 61:3: "oaks of THE tsedeq" (trees of righteousness)
- Dan 9:24: tsedeq olamim (everlasting righteousness) — the bridge verse

### H7522 ratsown (pleasure/favor/acceptance)
**Source:** `word-studies/H7522-ratsown.md`

- KJV: "acceptable" (16%), "favour" (16%), "will" (16%)
- Isa 61:2: shnat ratson — "year of favor/acceptance" (the positive side of the DOA binary)
- Levitical usage: sacrifice accepted = ratson (Lev 1:3, 19:5, 22:19-21)

### H5358 naqam (avenge, verb)
**Source:** `word-studies/H5358-naqam.md`
- 31 verses/44 tokens. 5 stems: Qal (13), Niphal (12), Hithpael (5), Hophal (3), Piel (2)

- LXX bridge: G1556 ekdikeo (22x, PMI 8.04) — judicial vindication

## Bridge Vocabulary

### H5674 avar (pass over/through)
**Source:** `word-studies/H5674-abar.md`
- 493 verses/690 tokens. Qal (85%), Hiphil (13%)
- **CRITICAL BRIDGE:** Lev 25:9 uses Hiphil of avar twice (העברת, תעבירו) for "cause the trumpet to pass through" = trumpet sounding. Zec 3:4 uses Hiphil of avar (העברתי) for "I have caused your iniquity to pass from you" = iniquity removal.
- Same verb form connects Jubilee proclamation and iniquity removal

### H3444 yeshuah (salvation/deliverance)
**Source:** `word-studies/H3444-yshuwah.md`
- 76 verses/77 tokens. "salvation" (82%). Psalms (59%), Isaiah (24%)
- Isa 61:10: bigdey yesha — "garments of salvation" paired with "robe of tsedaqah"
- LXX: G4991 soteria (38 co-occ) — but also rendered as dikaiosyne 8x in prophetic contexts (translators recognized yeshuah-tsedaqah pairing as near-synonymous)

### H4254 machalatsah (festival garments)
**Source:** `word-studies/H4254-machalatsah.md`
- Only 2 OT occurrences: Isa 3:22 and Zec 3:4
- Root = chalats (arm for battle / draw out)
- In Zec 3:4: the garments God gives Joshua after removing iniquity

## Key Parsing Results

### Isa 61:10 — Priestly Investiture Language
- הִלְבִּישַׁנִי (Hiphil of labash) — "He clothed me" — divine causative
- כֶּחָתָן יְכַהֵן פְּאֵר — "as bridegroom ACTS AS PRIEST with headdress" (Piel of kahan!)
- Priestly function in a vindication/garment context

### Isa 61:8 — Everlasting Covenant
- בְרִית עוֹלָם אֶכְרוֹת — "everlasting covenant I will CUT" (karath = same verb as Lev 23:29 penalty)
- karath in Lev 23:29 = cut off (penalty). karath in Isa 61:8 = cut a covenant (blessing). Same root, opposite functions — judgment and restoration.

### Jer 34:15/17 — The Deror Binary
- v.15: deror proclamation = hayashar in God's eyes (righteousness)
- v.17: refusal to proclaim deror → God proclaims deror "to sword, pestilence, famine"
- Same word (deror), opposite outcomes — identical to DOA binary (Lev 23:29-30)
