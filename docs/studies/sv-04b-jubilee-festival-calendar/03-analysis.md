# Analysis — sv-04b: The Jubilee, Isaiah 61, and the Festival Calendar

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Leviticus 25:9-10 — The Jubilee Proclaimed on the Day of Atonement

Lev 25:9 uses the Hiphil of avar (H5674) twice: ha'avarta ("you shall cause to pass") and ta'aviru ("you shall cause to pass through"). The object is showphar teruah — "horn of shouting." The Hiphil is causative: someone causes the trumpet-sound to pass through the land. The date is explicit: beyom hakippurim, "on the Day of Atonement," the tenth day of the seventh month.

Lev 25:10 follows immediately: uqratem deror, "and you shall proclaim liberty." The verb is Qal of qara (proclaim), and the direct object is deror (H1865). This is followed by a double shub: "you shall return every man to his possession, and you shall return every man to his family." Three restorations in two verses: personal freedom (deror), property return (shub to land), family reunion (shub to kinship).

The placement is not incidental. The Jubilee is proclaimed on the DOA — not the day after, not the week after, but on the same day the high priest completes the atonement ritual. Liberty follows cleansing. The DOA resolves accumulated defilement (sv-03 established the kaphar+taher formula of Lev 16:30). The Jubilee declares its social consequences: those cleansed are also restored.

The Hiphil of avar in Lev 25:9 will become critical when we reach Zechariah 3:4.

### Isaiah 61:1 — Deror: The Jubilee Word in the Messianic Announcement

Isa 61:1 opens with anointing language (mashach, the verb from which "messiah" derives). The anointed one's commission includes: preaching good tidings to the meek, binding up the brokenhearted, and — critically — liqro... deror, "to proclaim liberty to the captives."

The word deror (H1865) appears only 7 times in the Old Testament in the liberty sense. Six of those seven use the exact collocation qara deror — "proclaim liberty." This is formulaic. Deror is always proclaimed, never merely enacted. And the formula traces directly to Lev 25:10, the Jubilee institution. When Isaiah 61:1 uses qara deror, it is invoking the Jubilee by its own vocabulary.

This is not interpretation. It is word distribution. The same phrase, in the same form, anchored in the same institution.

### Isaiah 61:2 — The DOA Binary: Ratson and Naqam

Isa 61:2 pairs two proclamations: shnat ratson YHWH ("the acceptable year of the LORD") and yom naqam eloheynu ("the day of vengeance of our God").

Ratson (H7522) has deep roots in sacrificial vocabulary. In Lev 1:3, the burnt offering is brought lirtsono — "for his acceptance." Ratson is the word the priestly system uses to describe what happens when a sacrifice is accepted. Isa 61:2 declares an entire year of ratson — a year of divine acceptance.

Naqam (H5359) is the opposite pole. The noun appears 19 times in the OT, concentrated in Isaiah (6 occurrences). The formulaic "day of vengeance" (yom naqam) appears in Isa 34:8, 61:2, and 63:4. The LXX translates the naqam verb family primarily with ekdikeo (G1556, PMI 8.04) — judicial vindication, not merely emotional rage.

The pairing is deliberate. One proclamation contains both favor and judgment. This matches the DOA's binary outcome established in sv-04: Lev 16:30 declares cleansing for those who participate; Lev 23:29 declares cutting off (karath Niphal, divine passive) for those who refuse. The DOA is the only festival with this double outcome. Isaiah 61:2 encodes the same binary: ratson for those who respond to the deror proclamation, naqam for those who refuse.

The strength of this parallel is structural. The text does not quote Leviticus 23:29. But the binary shape — acceptance or judgment, with no middle ground — is identical.

### Isaiah 61:3 — Tsedeq: The Pivot to Vindication Vocabulary

The recipients of the deror proclamation are renamed: eiley hatsedeq, "oaks of THE tsedeq" (H6664). The definite article matters. They are not merely righteous trees. They are trees of THE righteousness — a specific, known quality.

Tsedeq is the masculine form of the tsadaq root. This is the same root as nitsdaq in Dan 8:14. The chain has now moved in three verses from Jubilee vocabulary (deror, v.1) through DOA-shaped binary language (ratson/naqam, v.2) to the vindication root itself (tsedeq, v.3).

### Isaiah 61:8 — Mishpat: God's Judicial Character

Verse 8 introduces mishpat (H4941): "I the LORD love mishpat." The participial form (ohev, "loving") marks this as a permanent attribute, not a one-time action. Mishpat collocates with tsedaqah 51 times across the OT. It is the most frequent companion of the tsadaq word family.

The verse also contains an everlasting covenant: berit olam ekhrot — "everlasting covenant I will cut." The verb karath (H3772) is the same verb used in Lev 23:29 for the penalty of non-participation in the DOA. In Lev 23:29, karath cuts off the disobedient. In Isa 61:8, karath cuts a covenant with the faithful. Same root, opposite directions — one destroys relationship, the other establishes it permanently.

### Isaiah 61:10 — The Tsedaqah Garment and Priestly Investiture

Verse 10 reaches the garment climax: "He has clothed me (hilbishani, Hiphil of labash — divine causative) with the garments of yeshuah (H3444, salvation), he has covered me with the robe of tsedaqah (meil tsedaqah, H6666)."

Three features demand attention:

First, the garment vocabulary. Meil tsedaqah — "robe of righteousness." This is the feminine form of the tsadaq root, appearing now as a garment. Righteousness has moved from an attribute (v.3, trees of tsedeq) to a covering (v.10, robe of tsedaqah). What was interior has become visible.

Second, the divine agent. Hilbishani is Hiphil — God does the clothing. The recipient does not dress himself. This matches Zech 3:4-5, where the divine command produces the garment change.

Third, the hidden priestly language. The Hebrew reads kechatan yekahen pe'er — "as a bridegroom acts as priest (Piel of kahan!) with a headdress." The Piel of kahan means "to act as priest." In a verse about garments, salvation, and righteousness, the bridegroom is described as performing a priestly function. This is not garment metaphor alone — it is investiture language drawn from the sanctuary.

### Isaiah 61:11 — Tsedaqah Before All Nations

The chapter concludes: "The Lord GOD will cause tsedaqah and praise to sprout (yatsmiyach, Hiphil of tsamach) before all the nations." The Hiphil is again causative — God causes righteousness to grow, like vegetation. But the audience has expanded. In v.3 the tsedeq was for the mourners in Zion. In v.10 the tsedaqah was a personal garment. Now in v.11 tsedaqah stands before all the nations.

The progression is complete:
- v.1: deror (Jubilee liberty)
- v.2: ratson + naqam (DOA-shaped binary)
- v.3: tsedeq (righteousness — the vindication root)
- v.8: mishpat (judgment — tsedaqah's constant companion)
- v.10: tsedaqah as garment (visible righteousness, priestly investiture)
- v.11: tsedaqah before all nations (public vindication)

This is one chapter. The vocabulary moves from Jubilee to vindication without a break. Every link is verifiable by Strong's number.

### Jeremiah 34:15 — Deror Equals Yashar

Jer 34:15 provides independent confirmation from a different author. The text reads: vata'asu et hayashar be'einay, "you did the right thing (yashar, H3477) in my eyes, in proclaiming liberty (liqro deror)."

Yashar means "right, upright, straight." God explicitly identifies the proclamation of deror as yashar — as the righteous thing. This is not a sophisticated theological inference. It is a direct statement: proclaiming liberty = doing what is right in God's sight.

Yashar and tsadaq are not identical words, but they share significant semantic territory. Both describe conformity to God's standard. The TR-yashar-euthys translation study shows that both map into the domain of moral/judicial uprightness. The overlap is real, even if the words are not synonyms.

### Jeremiah 34:17 — The Anti-Jubilee

Jer 34:17 turns the deror against those who revoked it: "Behold, I proclaim a deror for you — to the sword, to the pestilence, and to the famine." God uses the exact same vocabulary. The same word that means liberty now means destruction. The anti-Jubilee mirrors the DOA binary: the same event that cleanses the obedient cuts off the disobedient.

Deror in Jer 34:8,15,17 appears three times with three results: covenant-obedience (v.8), divine approval (v.15), and divine judgment when the covenant is broken (v.17). The word itself is neutral — what determines its outcome is the human response.

### Zechariah 3:4 — The Hiphil-of-Avar Bridge

Zech 3:4 contains the key grammatical bridge. The angel declares to Joshua the high priest: "Behold, I have caused your iniquity to pass from you (he'evarti avonikha, Hiphil Perfect 1s of avar) and I will clothe you (vehalbbesh, Hiphil InfAbs of labash) with festival garments (machalatsot, H4254)."

The Hiphil of avar (H5674) here is the same verb form as Lev 25:9: ha'avarta showphar teruah. In Lev 25:9, the Hiphil of avar causes the trumpet to pass through the land — the Jubilee announcement. In Zech 3:4, the Hiphil of avar causes iniquity to pass from Joshua — the cleansing declaration.

Same verb. Same stem. Same causative function. Different objects: the trumpet passes through the land; the iniquity passes from the person. But the grammatical structure is identical. The verb that announces liberty is the verb that removes iniquity.

This is not a theological inference. It is a grammatical fact verifiable in any Hebrew concordance.

### Zechariah 3:5 — DOA Vocabulary in the Garment Scene

Zech 3:5 adds the turban: tsaniyf tahor — "pure turban." The adjective tahor (H2891) is the DOA's result word. Lev 16:30 uses taher to describe what the DOA produces: "that you may be clean (tahor) from all your sins before the LORD." The same root appears on Joshua's turban after his iniquity is removed. The garment scene uses DOA vocabulary.

### Leviticus 23:24-25 — The Feast of Trumpets

Trumpets opens the Tishri sequence on the first day of the seventh month. The text prescribes zikron teruah — "a memorial of blowing." The construct chain means the memorial consists of the trumpet blast. Num 10:9 provides the function: blow the alarm (teruah) → "be remembered before the LORD your God" → "be saved from your enemies." The alarm brings Israel before God's attention.

### Leviticus 23:39-40 — Tabernacles Rejoicing

Tabernacles comes five days after the DOA (Tishri 15). The text commands: usamachtem — "you shall rejoice" (Qal Perfect 2mp of samach) before the LORD for seven days. Palm branches, willows, and thick-tree boughs are taken up. The DOA commands affliction (anah). Five days later, Tabernacles commands joy (samach). The calendar itself encodes the emotional progression: affliction gives way to celebration. The intervening event — the Jubilee proclaimed on the DOA — is the hinge.

### Luke 4:18-19 — Jesus Stops Mid-Verse

Jesus reads Isaiah 61:1-2 in the Nazareth synagogue. He reads through "the acceptable year of the Lord" (ratson) and stops. He does not read "the day of vengeance of our God" (naqam). He closes the book and declares: "Today this scripture is fulfilled in your ears."

The mid-verse stop separates the two elements of the DOA binary across time. At his first coming, Jesus proclaims the ratson — the acceptable year, the Jubilee liberation. The naqam — the judgment-vengeance — belongs to a later fulfillment. The binary is real, but its two halves are not simultaneous.

The Greek text of Luke 4:18 uses aphesis (G859), the LXX translation of deror. The vocabulary bridge from Jubilee to NT forgiveness passes through this exact word: deror → aphesis → the forgiveness Jesus proclaims.

## Patterns and Connections

### The Vocabulary Chain

The central finding of this study is the Isaiah 61 vocabulary chain. It moves from Jubilee language to vindication language within a single chapter:

| Verse | Hebrew | Strong's | Category |
|-------|--------|----------|----------|
| 61:1 | deror | H1865 | Jubilee liberty |
| 61:2a | ratson | H7522 | Sacrificial acceptance / DOA favor |
| 61:2b | naqam | H5359 | Judgment / DOA penalty |
| 61:3 | tsedeq | H6664 | Righteousness (= nitsdaq root) |
| 61:8 | mishpat | H4941 | Judgment (tsedaqah's partner) |
| 61:10 | tsedaqah | H6666 | Righteousness as garment |
| 61:11 | tsedaqah | H6666 | Righteousness before nations |

Every entry is verifiable by its Strong's number. The progression is not imposed — it is read from the text in order.

### The Hiphil-of-Avar Bridge

The Hiphil of avar (H5674) connects two texts:

| Text | Form | Object | Result |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| Lev 25:9 | Hiphil Perf 2ms (ha'avarta) | showphar teruah (trumpet) | Jubilee announced |
| Zech 3:4 | Hiphil Perf 1s (he'evarti) | avon (iniquity) | Iniquity removed |

Same verb, same stem, same causative meaning. The verb that proclaims liberty is the verb that removes sin. This is a grammatical bridge, not a theological inference.

### The DOA Binary Parallels

Three texts encode the same binary structure:

| Text | Positive Outcome | Negative Outcome |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Lev 16:30 / 23:29 | Cleansed (taher) | Cut off (karath Niphal) |
| Isa 61:2 | Year of favor (ratson) | Day of vengeance (naqam) |
| Jer 34:15 / 17 | Deror = yashar (right) | Deror to sword, pestilence, famine |

In each case: one event, two outcomes, determined by human response.

### The Deror-Yashar Equation

Jer 34:15 states explicitly: proclaiming deror = doing hayashar in God's sight. Liberty is righteous. If deror maps to the tsadaq family through Isaiah 61's vocabulary chain, and Jeremiah 34 independently identifies deror as yashar (right/upright), then both biblical authors — Isaiah and Jeremiah — connect Jubilee liberty to the righteousness vocabulary.

## Word Study Integration

### Deror (H1865)
The word study reveals that 6 of 7 liberty-sense occurrences use the formulaic qara deror. This is exclusively proclaimed vocabulary. The LXX bridge to aphesis (G859, PMI 8.96) means that every NT use of aphesis — including Luke 4:18 and the forgiveness proclamations — carries Jubilee resonance. The word is rare (7 liberty occurrences) and precise.

### Yobel (H3104)
The instrument names the institution. The ram's horn that sounds at Jericho (Josh 6) is the same word as the fiftieth-year cycle (Lev 25). The connection is the prolonged blast (teruah) that inaugurates the year. The LXX chose aphesis rather than a transliteration — the translators understood the institution's core meaning as release.

### Naqam (H5358/H5359)
The LXX translates the verb family primarily with ekdikeo (G1556) — judicial vindication, not merely personal vengeance. This matters. The "day of vengeance" in Isa 61:2 is not about divine rage. It is about judicial resolution. God as righteous judge executing the verdict. This aligns with the DOA's character as established in sv-04: the DOA is a sorting event, not a punishment event.

### Tsadaq Family (H6663/H6664/H6666)
The word study for tsadaq (H6663) confirms zero occurrences in Leviticus — this is courtroom vocabulary, not ritual vocabulary. The sole Niphal (Dan 8:14) is the passive receipt of vindication. Isaiah 61 uses the family three times (tsedeq v.3, tsedaqah vv.10,11), progressively expanding from internal quality to visible garment to public display before nations.

### Ratson (H7522)
The word study confirms the sacrificial acceptance usage (Lev 1:3, lirtsono). When Isa 61:2 declares a "year of ratson," it is using the priestly vocabulary of sacrifice-acceptance. The year itself is treated as an offering accepted by God.

### Avar (H5674)
The word study confirms the Hiphil distribution: 13% of 690 tokens. The Hiphil of avar meaning "cause to pass" appears in both Lev 25:9 (trumpet) and Zech 3:4 (iniquity). The bridge is textually verified.

### Kaphar (H3722)
With 102 occurrences, 67 in Leviticus (Piel dominant at 90%), kaphar is the sanctuary word. The DOA formula is kaphar + taher. The daily formula is kaphar + salach. The distinction is absolute (sv-03). The Jubilee follows the kaphar+taher result — liberty after cleansing.

### Yeshuah (H3444)
The word study reveals that the LXX renders yeshuah as dikaiosyne (righteousness) 8 times in prophetic contexts. The translators recognized that in Isaiah's vocabulary, salvation and righteousness overlap. Isa 61:10 pairs bigdey yesha with meil tsedaqah — garments of salvation with robe of righteousness — as near-synonyms, confirming what the LXX data shows.

### Machalatsah (H4254)
Only 2 OT occurrences: Isa 3:22 (luxury garments) and Zech 3:4 (garments given by God after iniquity removal). The rarity makes the Zech 3:4 usage striking — these are festival garments, appropriate for celebration after judgment, not ordinary clothing.

## Cross-Testament Connections

### Luke 4:18-19 — The First Advent Jubilee

Jesus reads Isaiah 61 and stops at the ratson half of the binary. He proclaims the acceptable year. He does not proclaim the day of vengeance. The Greek aphesis (G859) translates the LXX's rendering of deror. The vocabulary chain is intact: Hebrew deror → LXX aphesis → Luke's aphesis.

The mid-verse stop has theological weight. At the first coming, the Jubilee is proclaimed. The liberty is real. But the judgment side — the naqam — is deferred. This matches the "already/not yet" pattern visible across the NT: the proclamation has begun but the full resolution awaits.

### Acts 3:21 — Restoration of All Things

Peter speaks of the "restoration of all things" (apokatastasis panton). The parallel search links this to Lev 25:10's double shub — return to land, return to family. The Jubilee's three restorations (liberty, property, family) find their eschatological counterpart in the "all things" Peter describes.

### Revelation 7:9-17 — Tabernacles Imagery

The great multitude holds palm branches (v.9) — the same elements commanded in Lev 23:40 for the Feast of Tabernacles. They stand before the Lamb. The Lamb "shall dwell among them" (v.15, skenosei — the Tabernacles verb from skene, G4637). They have washed their robes in the blood of the Lamb (v.14) — a cleansing that precedes the dwelling. The DOA-to-Tabernacles sequence is visible: cleansing first, then the Lamb tabernacles over them.

### Revelation 21:3 — The Eschatological Tabernacle

Rev 21:3 uses the double sken- root: skene (tabernacle, noun) and skenosei (dwell/tabernacle, verb) in the same verse. This is the only NT verse where both noun and verb of this root co-occur. The deliberateness of the vocabulary marks this as Tabernacles fulfillment.

The placement is significant. Rev 20 records the judgment. Rev 21 records the new heaven and new earth where God dwells with humanity. The calendar sequence — judgment (DOA) then dwelling (Tabernacles) — is preserved in the Apocalypse's own structure.

### Zechariah 14:16-19 — Nations Keep Tabernacles

Tabernacles is the only Israelite festival projected into the eschatological age. Zech 14:16 requires all surviving nations to observe it. Verses 17-18 attach a penalty for non-observance — no rain. The binary appears again: participate and be blessed, refuse and be judged.

## Difficult Passages

### Strongest Evidence

**The Isaiah 61 vocabulary chain** is textually verifiable. Each word has a Strong's number. The progression from deror to tsedaqah is readable in the Hebrew text without interpretation. This is the study's strongest finding.

**The Hiphil-of-avar bridge** (Lev 25:9 = Zech 3:4) is a grammatical fact. Same verb, same stem, verified by parsing. No inference required.

**The Jubilee-on-DOA placement** (Lev 25:9-10) is explicitly stated. The text says beyom hakippurim. This is not inferred.

**The deror-yashar equation** (Jer 34:15) is a direct statement. God says proclaiming deror is hayashar. This requires no inference beyond reading the verse.

### Moderate Evidence

**The DOA binary mapping** (Isa 61:2 ratson/naqam = Lev 16:30/23:29 cleansed/cut-off) is a structural parallel. The texts do not quote each other. But both present a single event with a double outcome determined by human response. The parallel is reasonable but not lexically proven — ratson is not taher, and naqam is not karath. The structure matches; the vocabulary does not overlap.

**The garment parallel** (Zech 3:4 machalatsot = Isa 61:10 meil tsedaqah) is suggestive. Both texts describe post-judgment investiture by divine action (Hiphil of labash in both). But the garment words are different. Machalatsot are "festival garments." Meil tsedaqah is "robe of righteousness." The concept parallels; the vocabulary does not match precisely. The strongest link is the Hiphil of labash — God clothes the recipient in both cases.

**The tahor in Zech 3:5** (tsaniyf tahor, "pure turban") uses DOA vocabulary. Tahor is the DOA's result word (Lev 16:30). Its presence in the garment scene links the scene to the DOA's cleansing function. But tahor appears widely — it is not exclusively DOA vocabulary. The connection is suggestive, not unique.

### Weakest Evidence

**The eschatological Tabernacles mapping** (Rev 21:3 = eternal Tabernacles) rests on vocabulary (skenoo/skene) and structural placement (post-judgment). The vocabulary is strong — the double sken- root in Rev 21:3 is deliberate. But the text does not explicitly say "this is the Feast of Tabernacles fulfilled." The connection is made through vocabulary and sequence, not through direct statement.

**The Isa 61:10 priestly reading** (Piel of kahan = "acts as priest") is grammatically present. But the verse is metaphorical — "as a bridegroom acts as priest with his headdress." Whether this indicates literal priestly investiture or merely uses priestly imagery for comparison is debatable. The grammar is clear; the interpretive weight is uncertain.

## Preliminary Synthesis

The Tishri calendar encodes a single arc: alarm (Trumpets) → judgment/cleansing (DOA) → liberty/restoration (Jubilee, proclaimed on the DOA) → celebration/dwelling (Tabernacles). Each stage has commanded emotional content — blowing, affliction, proclamation, rejoicing. The calendar is a narrative, not merely a schedule.

The Jubilee sits on the DOA because liberty follows cleansing. Lev 25:9-10 makes this explicit. The defilement must be resolved (kaphar+taher, Lev 16:30) before the restoration can be declared (uqratem deror, Lev 25:10). This is the same sequence the series has traced from sv-03 forward: the daily puts sin in, the annual takes it out, and the Jubilee declares the social consequences.

Isaiah 61 compresses this entire arc into one chapter. The vocabulary moves from deror (Jubilee, v.1) through the DOA binary (ratson/naqam, v.2) to the vindication root (tsedeq/tsedaqah, vv.3,10,11). The chapter bridges Jubilee vocabulary to the tsadaq family — the same root used in Dan 8:14's nitsdaq. This provides a second vocabulary bridge alongside Dan 9:24 (which sv-14 will examine).

The Hiphil-of-avar bridge strengthens the connection grammatically: the verb that announces the Jubilee (Lev 25:9) is the verb that removes iniquity (Zech 3:4). And Zechariah 3's garment scene uses DOA vocabulary (tahor in v.5) while conferring the post-judgment garments that parallel Isaiah 61:10's robe of tsedaqah.

Jeremiah 34:15 provides independent confirmation: deror = yashar. Liberty is righteousness. The connection Isaiah builds through a chapter-long vocabulary chain, Jeremiah states in one verse.

The eschatological projection is consistent. Tabernacles is the only feast projected into the age to come (Zech 14:16). Rev 21:3 uses Tabernacles vocabulary for the final state of God dwelling with humanity. The arc that begins at the DOA does not end at liberty — it ends at dwelling. Vindication is not the destination. It is the gate through which humanity passes on the way to the destination: God among His people, with no veil, no barrier, no defilement.
