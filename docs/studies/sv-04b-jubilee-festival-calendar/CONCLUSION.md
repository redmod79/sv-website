# The Jubilee, Isaiah 61, and the Festival Calendar

## Question
What happens after the Day of Atonement in the festival calendar? How does the Tishri sequence (Trumpets, DOA, Jubilee, Tabernacles) map the full arc from alarm to judgment to restoration to celebration? How does Isaiah 61 bridge Jubilee vocabulary (deror) to vindication vocabulary (tsedeq/tsedaqah, the tsadaq root of nitsdaq in Dan 8:14)?

## Summary Answer

The Jubilee is proclaimed on the Day of Atonement (Lev 25:9-10). Not after it. On it. The trumpet sounds on the tenth day of the seventh month — the same day the high priest completes the atonement ritual. Liberty follows cleansing. The Tishri calendar encodes a single continuous arc: Trumpets sounds the alarm (Tishri 1), the DOA cleanses and sorts (Tishri 10), the Jubilee declares restoration on that same day, and Tabernacles commands rejoicing five days later (Tishri 15-22). Each stage has mandated content — blowing, affliction, proclamation, joy. The calendar is a narrative.

Isaiah 61 compresses this entire arc into one chapter. It opens with Jubilee vocabulary (deror, v.1), moves through a DOA-shaped binary (ratson/naqam, v.2), and arrives at the vindication root itself (tsedeq v.3, tsedaqah vv.10-11). Every word is traceable by its Strong's number. The chapter bridges the Jubilee institution to the tsadaq word family — the same root used in Daniel 8:14's nitsdaq. Jeremiah 34:15 confirms the connection independently: God calls the proclamation of deror "the right thing" (yashar) in His sight. Liberty is righteousness.

The Hiphil of avar (H5674) provides a grammatical bridge between two critical texts. In Lev 25:9 it causes the trumpet to pass through the land (Jubilee announcement). In Zech 3:4 it causes iniquity to pass from the high priest (cleansing declaration). Same verb, same stem, same causative function. The verb that announces liberty is the verb that removes sin.

## Key Verses

> **Leviticus 25:9-10** "Then shalt thou cause the trumpet of the jubile to sound on the tenth day of the seventh month, in the day of atonement shall ye make the trumpet sound throughout all your land. And ye shall hallow the fiftieth year, and proclaim liberty throughout all the land unto all the inhabitants thereof."

> **Isaiah 61:1-2** "The Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me; because the LORD hath anointed me to preach good tidings unto the meek; he hath sent me to bind up the brokenhearted, to proclaim liberty to the captives, and the opening of the prison to them that are bound; to proclaim the acceptable year of the LORD, and the day of vengeance of our God."

> **Isaiah 61:10** "I will greatly rejoice in the LORD, my soul shall be joyful in my God; for he hath clothed me with the garments of salvation, he hath covered me with the robe of righteousness, as a bridegroom decketh himself with ornaments, and as a bride adorneth herself with her jewels."

> **Jeremiah 34:15** "And ye were now turned, and had done right in my sight, in proclaiming liberty every man to his neighbour; and ye had made a covenant before me in the house that is called by my name."

> **Zechariah 3:4** "And he answered and spake unto those that stood before him, saying, Take away the filthy garments from him. And unto him he said, Behold, I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee, and I will clothe thee with change of raiment."

> **Leviticus 16:30** "For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you, that ye may be clean from all your sins before the LORD."

## Analysis

### The Tishri Festival Calendar: One Arc, Four Stages

Leviticus 23 places three observances in the seventh month (Tishri). Each has commanded content that builds on what precedes it.

**Trumpets (Tishri 1, Lev 23:24-25).** The text prescribes zikron teruah — "a memorial of blowing." The construct chain means the memorial consists of the trumpet blast. Numbers 10:9 supplies the function: blow the alarm, be remembered before the LORD, be saved from your enemies. Trumpets brings Israel before God's attention. It is the wake-up call.

**Day of Atonement (Tishri 10, Lev 23:27-32).** Nine days later, the DOA commands two things: afflict your souls (anah Piel + nephesh) and do no work. The affliction requirement is internal and invisible — only God can verify whether someone genuinely participated (sv-04, series insight #26). The enforcement is severe: whoever does not participate is cut off (nikhretah, Niphal of karath — Lev 23:29) and destroyed (Hiphil of abad — Lev 23:30, God as explicit agent). No other festival carries this penalty. The DOA is a sorting event.

**Jubilee — Proclaimed on the DOA (Lev 25:9-10).** On the fiftieth year, the Jubilee trumpet sounds on the same day: beyom hakippurim, "on the Day of Atonement." The text is explicit. The Hiphil of avar causes the showphar teruah to pass through all the land. Then comes the proclamation: uqratem deror — "you shall proclaim liberty." Three restorations follow: personal freedom (deror), property return (shub to land), family reunion (shub to kinship). The placement is the key. Liberty is declared on the day cleansing is completed. Not before. Not after. The same day.

**Tabernacles (Tishri 15-22, Lev 23:34-43).** Five days after the DOA, Tabernacles commands joy: usamachtem — "you shall rejoice before the LORD your God seven days" (Lev 23:40). Palm branches, willows, and thick-tree boughs are taken up. The people dwell in booths for seven days to remember the Exodus (v.43). The emotional trajectory is mandated by the calendar: the DOA commands affliction (anah); Tabernacles commands rejoicing (samach). Judgment gives way to celebration. The intervening Jubilee is the hinge — the moment when the DOA's cleansing becomes the people's liberty.

The arc is: alarm → judgment → liberty → celebration. Or, in vocabulary: teruah → kaphar/taher → deror → samach.

### The Jubilee Proclaimed on the Day of Atonement

The placement of the Jubilee on the DOA (Lev 25:9-10) is the structural foundation of this study. The daily-to-annual system established in sv-03 shows that the daily service transfers sin into the sanctuary (kaphar + salach = forgiveness for the individual), while the annual service removes accumulated sin from the sanctuary (kaphar + taher = cleansing of the sanctuary and people). The DOA is the resolution day. Only after the accumulated defilement has been resolved can the trumpet of liberty sound.

This answers a structural question: why does the Jubilee sit on the DOA rather than on Trumpets or Tabernacles? Because the Jubilee declares the social consequences of what the DOA has accomplished ritually. Cleansing is the prerequisite. Liberty is the proclamation. Celebration is the response.

The Jubilee's three restorations — freedom, land, family — are not random acts of generosity. They are reversals. Every debt, every servitude, every land sale is undone. The people return to their original state. The word deror (H1865) carries this force. Its root means "unimpeded flow" — the removal of whatever blocks free movement. In the Jubilee context, it removes the blockages that sin, poverty, and circumstance have created. It restores what was always intended.

### The Isaiah 61 Vocabulary Chain

Isaiah 61 is the study's primary evidence for bridging Jubilee vocabulary to vindication vocabulary. The chain moves through six stages:

**Stage 1 — Deror (v.1).** The anointed one proclaims liqro deror — "liberty to the captives." The word deror appears only 7 times in the OT with the liberty sense. Six of those seven use the exact phrase qara deror — "proclaim liberty" — tracing directly to the Jubilee formula of Lev 25:10. Isaiah 61:1 opens with Jubilee vocabulary, deliberately and precisely.

**Stage 2 — Ratson and Naqam (v.2).** The proclamation continues with two objects: shnat ratson YHWH, "the acceptable year of the LORD," and yom naqam eloheynu, "the day of vengeance of our God." Ratson (H7522) is sacrificial vocabulary. Lev 1:3 uses lirtsono for the burnt offering brought "for his acceptance." The year of ratson is a year of divine acceptance — the positive side of the DOA result. Naqam (H5359) is the judgment side. Its LXX translation family (ekdikeo, G1556) carries the sense of judicial vindication, not mere retaliation. Together, ratson and naqam encode the DOA binary: acceptance for the participants, judgment for the refusers (Lev 16:30/23:29).

This mapping is structural, not lexical. Isaiah does not quote Leviticus 23:29. But the binary shape — one event, two outcomes, determined by response — is identical.

**Stage 3 — Tsedeq (v.3).** The recipients of the Jubilee proclamation are renamed eiley hatsedeq — "oaks of THE righteousness." Tsedeq (H6664) is the masculine form of the tsadaq root. This is the root of nitsdaq in Dan 8:14. In three verses, Isaiah has moved from Jubilee vocabulary (deror) through DOA-shaped binary language (ratson/naqam) to the vindication root itself.

**Stage 4 — Mishpat (v.8).** God declares: "I love mishpat" (judgment, H4941). The participial form ohev ("loving") marks this as a permanent attribute. Mishpat collocates with tsedaqah 51 times in the OT — it is the constant companion of the tsadaq family. The verse also uses karath to cut an everlasting covenant — the same verb that cuts off the disobedient in Lev 23:29. One verb, two directions: karath destroys (DOA penalty) or karath establishes (everlasting covenant).

**Stage 5 — Tsedaqah as Garment (v.10).** God clothes the speaker with bigdey yesha ("garments of salvation," H3444) and meil tsedaqah ("robe of righteousness," H6666). The clothing is divine — hilbishani (Hiphil of labash), God as causative agent. The recipient does not dress himself. Righteousness has moved from an attribute (trees of tsedeq, v.3) to a visible covering (robe of tsedaqah, v.10). What was interior has become external.

The verse also contains hidden priestly language: kechatan yekahen pe'er — "as a bridegroom acts as priest (Piel of kahan) with a headdress." The Piel of kahan means to function as a priest. In a garment-investiture context, this is sanctuary language.

**Stage 6 — Tsedaqah Before Nations (v.11).** God will cause tsedaqah and praise to sprout (Hiphil of tsamach) before all the nations. The audience has expanded from Zion's mourners (v.3) to the entire world. Righteousness is now public. It stands before every nation as visible evidence.

The LXX data adds a further connection. The word study for yeshuah (H3444) reveals that the LXX translates yeshuah as dikaiosyne (righteousness) eight times in prophetic contexts. The translators recognized that in Isaiah's vocabulary, salvation and righteousness overlap. Isa 61:10 pairs them — garments of yeshuah and robe of tsedaqah — as near-synonyms.

### The Hiphil-of-Avar Bridge

The Hiphil of avar (H5674) creates a grammatical bridge between the Jubilee proclamation and the cleansing declaration.

In Lev 25:9, ha'avarta showphar teruah means "you shall cause the trumpet of shouting to pass through" the land. The Hiphil is causative. Someone causes the trumpet-sound to traverse the territory. This is the Jubilee announcement.

In Zech 3:4, he'evarti avonikha means "I have caused your iniquity to pass from you." The Hiphil is causative. God causes the iniquity to depart from Joshua the high priest. This is the cleansing declaration.

Same verb. Same stem. Same causative function. Different objects: trumpet passes through the land; iniquity passes from the person. The grammatical structure is identical.

This is a verifiable fact, not a theological inference. Any Hebrew concordance confirms that both verses use the Hiphil of avar (H5674). The verb that announces the Jubilee is the verb that removes iniquity.

Zechariah 3:5 deepens the connection. The turban placed on Joshua's head is tsaniyf tahor — "pure turban." Tahor (H2891) is the DOA's result word. Lev 16:30: "that you may be clean (tahor) from all your sins before the LORD." The garment scene in Zechariah 3 uses DOA vocabulary — the cleansing root appears on the restored priest's turban.

### The Deror-Yashar Equation

Jeremiah 34:15 provides independent confirmation from a different prophetic author. God declares: vata'asu et hayashar be'einay — "you did the right thing (yashar, H3477) in my eyes" — liqro deror — "in proclaiming liberty."

This is a direct divine statement. God identifies the proclamation of deror as yashar — as the right, upright thing. Yashar and tsadaq are not identical words. But they share significant semantic territory in the moral-judicial domain. Both describe conformity to God's standard. The TR-yashar-euthys translation study confirms their overlap.

What Isaiah builds through a chapter-long vocabulary chain (deror → tsedeq → tsedaqah), Jeremiah states in one clause: deror is yashar. Liberty is righteousness. A different prophet, a different context, the same connection.

Jer 34:17 completes the picture. When the people revoke their deror proclamation, God responds with an ironic counter-deror: "I proclaim a deror for you — to the sword, to the pestilence, and to the famine." The same word that meant freedom now means destruction. The anti-Jubilee mirrors the DOA binary: one word, two outcomes. Participate in the deror and it liberates. Refuse, and the deror turns against you.

### The DOA Binary in Isaiah 61

The ratson/naqam pair in Isa 61:2 maps to the DOA's binary outcome:

- Ratson (favor/acceptance) corresponds to Lev 16:30's cleansing. Those who participate in the DOA — who afflict their souls — receive cleansing before the LORD.
- Naqam (vengeance/judicial resolution) corresponds to Lev 23:29's karath penalty. Those who refuse to participate are cut off.

The parallel is structural. Both texts present a single occasion with a double result determined by the human response. Both texts are set in the context of the Tishri calendar (Lev 23:27-29) or its vocabulary (Isa 61:1's deror from Lev 25:10). Both texts present no middle ground — it is either favor or judgment.

The limitation must be stated honestly. Ratson is not taher. Naqam is not karath. The vocabulary does not overlap word-for-word. The parallel is in the binary structure, not in the specific terms. This makes it a reasonable inference, not a lexically proven identity.

### The Garment Change

Two texts describe divine investiture after judgment:

**Zechariah 3:4-5.** After the courtroom scene (Satan resisting, filthy garments), God removes iniquity (Hiphil of avar) and clothes Joshua with machalatsot (H4254, festival garments) and a tsaniyf tahor (pure turban).

**Isaiah 61:10.** After the deror proclamation and DOA binary, God clothes the speaker with bigdey yesha (garments of salvation) and meil tsedaqah (robe of righteousness).

Both use the Hiphil of labash — God as the agent who dresses the recipient. Both occur in post-judgment contexts. Both involve garments that signify restored standing. But the garment words themselves differ: machalatsot (festival garments) is not meil tsedaqah (robe of righteousness). The concept parallels. The vocabulary does not match exactly. The strongest connection is the shared divine-causative clothing verb and the post-judgment context, not the garment names.

### Eschatological Tabernacles

Tabernacles is the only Israelite festival projected into the eschatological age. Three texts converge:

**Zechariah 14:16-19.** After the eschatological battle, all surviving nations go up yearly to keep the Feast of Tabernacles. Those who refuse receive no rain. The binary reappears: participate or face consequences.

**Revelation 7:9-17.** A great multitude holds palm branches (Lev 23:40, Tabernacles imagery) before the Lamb. They have washed their robes (v.14, cleansing = DOA). The Lamb shall "dwell among them" (v.15, skenosei — the Tabernacles verb).

**Revelation 21:3.** The tabernacle (skene) of God is with men, and he will dwell (skenosei) with them. The double sken- root — both noun and verb in one verse — occurs nowhere else in the NT. This is deliberate Tabernacles vocabulary placed at the climax of Revelation, after the judgment of chapter 20.

The sequence of the Tishri calendar is preserved: judgment completes (DOA/Rev 20) → God dwells with humanity (Tabernacles/Rev 21). The calendar arc extends from type to antitype.

The evidence here must be graded honestly. The vocabulary (skene/skenoo) is strong. The structural placement (post-judgment) is consistent. But none of these texts explicitly state "this is the fulfillment of the Feast of Tabernacles." The connection is made through vocabulary and sequence, not direct declaration. It is the most persuasive type of circumstantial evidence — multiple independent indicators pointing the same direction — but it remains inference, not statement.

### Series Significance: A Second Vocabulary Bridge

This study's primary contribution to the series is the identification of a second vocabulary bridge from DOA-context language to vindication language.

The first bridge is Dan 9:24, where the six purposes of the seventy weeks include both kaphar (atonement — DOA vocabulary) and tsedeq olamim (everlasting righteousness — tsadaq vocabulary). Study sv-14 will examine this bridge in detail.

The second bridge is Isaiah 61, where deror (Jubilee, proclaimed on the DOA) connects through a chapter-long chain to tsedeq and tsedaqah (the vindication root of nitsdaq in Dan 8:14). The bridge is independently confirmed by Jer 34:15 (deror = yashar) and structurally supported by the Hiphil-of-avar connection (Lev 25:9 = Zech 3:4).

The series has now traced the following progression: the daily service transfers sin into the sanctuary (sv-03). The DOA removes it (sv-03, sv-04). The DOA sorts participants: cleansed or cut off (sv-04). The Jubilee declares the social consequences of the DOA's resolution: liberty, restoration, return (this study). And the vocabulary that describes this process — from deror to tsedeq — connects directly to the vindication language of Dan 8:14.

## Word Studies

The following word studies shaped the analysis:

**Deror (H1865)** — Only 7 liberty-sense occurrences, 6 using the formula qara deror. Exclusively proclaimed vocabulary, tracing to the Jubilee institution. LXX bridge: aphesis (G859, PMI 8.96), connecting to NT forgiveness language.

**Avar (H5674)** — The Hiphil appears in both Lev 25:9 (trumpet passing through the land) and Zech 3:4 (iniquity passing from the priest). The grammatical bridge connecting Jubilee announcement to cleansing declaration.

**Ratson (H7522)** — Sacrificial acceptance vocabulary (Lev 1:3, lirtsono). Isaiah 61:2's "year of ratson" uses the priestly acceptance formula for an entire year. The positive side of the DOA binary.

**Naqam (H5358/H5359)** — The LXX renders the verb family primarily as ekdikeo (G1556) — judicial vindication. The "day of vengeance" is a day of judicial resolution, not rage.

**Tsadaq family (H6663/H6664/H6666)** — Zero occurrences of tsadaq in Leviticus. Courtroom vocabulary. The sole Niphal (Dan 8:14) is the passive receipt of vindication. Isaiah 61 uses the family three times (vv.3,10,11), expanding from quality to garment to public display.

**Yashar (H3477)** — "Right/upright." Jer 34:15 explicitly equates proclaiming deror with doing hayashar in God's sight. Independent confirmation of the deror-righteousness connection.

**Yeshuah (H3444)** — LXX renders as dikaiosyne 8 times in prophetic contexts. Isa 61:10 pairs yeshuah and tsedaqah as near-synonyms.

**Machalatsah (H4254)** — Only 2 OT occurrences. In Zech 3:4, festival garments conferred after divine removal of iniquity.

**Kaphar (H3722)** — The DOA formula (kaphar + taher) is the ritual foundation on which the Jubilee declaration stands. Liberty is proclaimed on the day kaphar is complete.

## Difficult Passages

### Where Evidence Is Strongest

The Isaiah 61 vocabulary chain is verifiable by Strong's numbers without any interpretation. Each word is parseable, each link is readable in order. The chain from deror (v.1) to tsedaqah (v.11) is the study's most secure finding.

The Hiphil-of-avar bridge (Lev 25:9 and Zech 3:4) is a grammatical fact confirmed by Hebrew parsing. Same verb, same stem. No inference required.

The Jubilee's placement on the DOA (Lev 25:9, beyom hakippurim) is an explicit date statement.

The deror-yashar equation (Jer 34:15) is a direct divine declaration, not an inference.

### Where Evidence Is Moderate

The DOA binary mapping (ratson/naqam in Isa 61:2 = cleansed/cut-off in Lev 16:30/23:29) is a structural parallel. The binary shape matches, but the specific vocabulary does not overlap. The texts do not quote each other. This is a reasonable inference, not a proven identity.

The garment parallel between Zech 3:4-5 and Isa 61:10 rests on shared context (post-judgment) and shared verb (Hiphil of labash) rather than shared garment terminology. The concept aligns; the vocabulary partially diverges.

The tahor in Zech 3:5 (tsaniyf tahor) connects to DOA language, but tahor appears widely in the OT. The connection is suggestive, not exclusive.

### Where Evidence Requires Most Inference

The eschatological Tabernacles mapping (Rev 21:3 as eternal Tabernacles fulfillment) rests on vocabulary (skene/skenoo) and structural placement (after judgment). No text explicitly declares "this is the Feast of Tabernacles fulfilled." The evidence is strong circumstantially — multiple independent indicators converge — but it remains inference.

The Isa 61:10 priestly reading (Piel of kahan in a garment context) is grammatically present but metaphorical. Whether it indicates literal priestly investiture or uses priestly imagery for comparison cannot be resolved from grammar alone.

The yashar-tsadaq semantic overlap is real but not identity. Both words describe moral/judicial uprightness, but they are distinct lexemes with different distribution patterns. Jer 34:15 connects deror to yashar, not directly to tsadaq. The additional step from yashar to tsadaq is supported by semantic analysis but not by an explicit biblical equation.

## Conclusion

This study establishes that the Tishri festival calendar encodes a single theological arc: alarm, judgment, liberty, celebration. The Jubilee sits on the Day of Atonement because liberty is the social proclamation of what cleansing has accomplished ritually. Isaiah 61 traces this connection in vocabulary, moving from the Jubilee word deror through the DOA-shaped binary of ratson/naqam to the vindication root tsedeq/tsedaqah — the same root as nitsdaq in Daniel 8:14.

The Hiphil-of-avar bridge provides grammatical evidence that the Jubilee announcement and the removal of iniquity use the same verb form. Jeremiah 34:15 provides independent prophetic confirmation that proclaiming liberty is "the right thing" in God's sight. The Zechariah 3 garment scene uses DOA vocabulary (tahor) while depicting the post-judgment investiture that parallels Isaiah 61:10's robe of tsedaqah.

For the series, this study provides a second vocabulary bridge — alongside Dan 9:24 — connecting Day of Atonement language to vindication language. The Jubilee is not merely a social institution. It is a theological declaration: cleansing produces liberty, liberty is righteousness, and righteousness will stand before all nations. The arc does not end at vindication. It ends at Tabernacles — God dwelling with His people, the commanded joy that follows the completed judgment.
