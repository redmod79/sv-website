**Key observations:**
- **Peplērōtai** = Perfect Passive Indicative 3S of plēroō. The PERFECT tense signals completed action with ongoing relevance — "the time HAS BEEN fulfilled" (and remains fulfilled). Not aorist (point action) but perfect (completed state).
- **ho kairos** = THE appointed time (with article). kairos (not chronos) = the divinely appointed moment, not mere duration.
- **ēggiken** = Perfect Active Indicative of eggizō ("draw near"). Also perfect tense — the kingdom HAS drawn near and remains near.
- Both key verbs in perfect tense: the time HAS BEEN fulfilled AND the kingdom HAS drawn near. Two completed states declared simultaneously.

**Key observations:**
- **to plērōma tou chronou** = "the fullness of THE time" — plērōma (G4138, noun: fullness/completion, Nom Sg Neut) + chronos (G5550, time/duration, Gen Sg Masc). Here Paul uses chronos (duration) not kairos (appointed moment). The "fullness" = the divine timetable reaching its appointed completion.
- **ēlthen** = Aorist Active Indicative 3S of erchomai — "came." Point-action: the fullness of time arrived definitively.
- **exapesteilen** = Aorist Active of exapostellō — "sent forth." God's action of sending the Son is presented as a definite historical act triggered by the arrival of the appointed time.
- Mark 1:15 uses kairos + peplērōtai (perfect passive). Gal 4:4 uses chronos + plērōma + ēlthen (aorist). Same concept, different vocabulary angles: the prophetic clock reached its destination.
