**Key structural observation:** Six infinitive construct clauses with prefixed le-, all dependent on nechtak. 3+3 pattern:
1. lekalle happesha' — "to finish the transgression" (Piel of kalah)
2. ulehatem chatta'ot — "to seal up sins" / "to make an end of sins" (Hiphil of tamam, Qere reading)
3. ulekapper 'avon — "to atone for iniquity" (Piel of kaphar)
4. ulehabi tsedeq 'olamim — "to bring in everlasting righteousness" (Hiphil of bo')
5. velachtom chazon venabi' — "to seal up vision and prophet" (Qal of chatham)
6. velimshoch qodesh qodashim — "to anoint holy of holies" (Qal of mashach)

Note from grammar reference dan-9-24-27.md: The Kethib/Qere variant at #2 — Kethib reads velachtom (chatham, "to seal") while Qere reads ulehatem (tamam, "to complete"). Both end in the sealing/completing semantic field.
