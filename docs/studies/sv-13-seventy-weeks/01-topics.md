## Topics

### SEVENTY
- The senate of the Israelites composed of seventy elders (Exo 24:1,9; Num 11:16,24,25)
- The seventy disciples sent forth by Jesus (though the best Greek mss. have "seventy-two") (Luk 10:1-17)
- The Jews in captivity in Babylon for seventy years (Jer 25:11,12; 29:10; Dan 9:2; Zec 1:12; 7:5)
- 70 Weeks in the vision of Daniel (Dan 9:24)

**Key observation:** The number 70 recurs in Israel's governance (70 elders), mission (70 disciples), judgment (70-year exile), and prophecy (70 weeks). The 70-year exile is the immediate context for Daniel's prayer in Dan 9:2, which triggers Gabriel's 70-weeks revelation.

### ARTAXERXES
1. A Persian king probably identical with Ahasuerus
   - Prohibits the rebuilding of Jerusalem (Ezr 4:7-24)
2. King of Persia. Decree of, in behalf of the Jews (Ezr 7; Neh 2; 5:14)

**Key observation:** Artaxerxes issues the decree in Ezra 7 (457 BC) that is the starting point of the 70-weeks and 2300-day prophecies. His later interaction with Nehemiah (Neh 2, 444 BC) involves only wall-building, not the full "restore and build" mandate.

### ATONEMENT
- For tabernacle and furniture (Lev 16:15-20,33)
- Day of: Time of (Exo 30:10; Lev 23:27; 25:9; Num 29:7)
- How observed (Exo 30:10; Lev 16:2-34; 23:27-32; Num 29:7-11; Act 27:9; Heb 5:3; 9:7,19,22)
- Made by animal sacrifices (Exo 29:36; Lev 1:4; 4:20; 16:6-34; 17:11; Num 15:22-28; Heb 9:22)
- Made by Jesus: Divinely ordained (Luk 2:30,31; Gal 4:4,5; Eph 1:3-12; Col 1:19,20; 1Pe 1:20; Rev 13:8)
- Made but once (Heb 7:27; 9:24-28; 10:10,12,14; 1Pe 3:18)
- Unclassified scriptures: Dan 9:24-27 listed alongside Isa 53:4-12, Zec 13:1, Rom 3:24-26, Heb 9:12-15

**Key observation:** Nave's explicitly includes Dan 9:24-27 under ATONEMENT, placing the 70 weeks prophecy in the atonement category. The entry connects Dan 9:24's "make reconciliation for iniquity" (kaphar) with the DOA and Christ's once-for-all sacrifice.

### SABBATIC YEAR (from Nave's semantic search)
- Land rest every seventh year (Exo 23:9-11; Lev 25)
- Penalty for violation: the land compensated during exile (Lev 26:34,35; 2 Chr 36:21)
- Related to release of servants and debts (Deu 15:1-6,12-18)
- Jeremiah's 70 years (Jer 25:11,12; 29:10; Dan 9:2)

**Key observation:** The 70-year exile compensated for 490 missed sabbatical years (one every 7 years for ~490 years of monarchy). Gabriel's 70-weeks response (70 x 7 = 490 years) mirrors this exact number. The punishment and the remedy use the same numeric framework.

## Related Topics Discovered

### The 490/70 Numeric Pattern
The number 490 (70 x 7) connects three key texts:
1. **Lev 26:34-35** — The land compensates for missed sabbatical years
2. **2 Chr 36:21** — The 70-year exile fulfills this compensation
3. **Dan 9:24** — Gabriel announces 70 weeks (490 years) upon Daniel's people

### Three Decrees Pattern
Three Persian decrees relate to Jerusalem:
1. **Cyrus' decree** (538 BC) — Build the temple (Ezr 1:1-4; 2 Chr 36:22-23)
2. **Darius' decree** (520 BC) — Confirm Cyrus' decree (Ezr 6:1-12)
3. **Artaxerxes' decree** (457 BC) — Restore and build the city, with full judicial authority (Ezr 7:11-26)

Ezra 6:14 groups all three under one "commandment": "according to the commandment of the God of Israel, and according to the commandment of Cyrus, and Darius, and Artaxerxes king of Persia."
