# Word Studies

## From Word Studies Library

### tsadaq — H6663
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`

- **Word:** צָדַק (tsadaq) — verb, primitive root
- **Occurrences:** 41 in OT (54 KJV translation entries)
- **Definition:** To be (causatively, make) right in a moral or forensic sense
- **Stem Distribution:**
  - Qal (22): be righteous, be in the right
  - Hiphil (12): justify, declare righteous, acquit
  - Piel (5): prove right, show to be righteous
  - **Niphal (1): be vindicated — ONLY Dan 8:14**
  - Hithpael (1): clear oneself
- **Key Semantic Categories:**
  1. Being righteous before God (Job 4:17; 9:2; 25:4; Psa 143:2)
  2. Declaring righteous / judicial acquittal (Exo 23:7; Deu 25:1; Pro 17:15)
  3. Proving oneself right (Gen 44:16; Eze 16:51-52)
  4. Being vindicated (Dan 8:14 — the ONLY Niphal)
  5. Causing righteousness in others (Isa 53:11; Dan 12:3)
  6. God's righteousness affirmed (Psa 51:4; 19:9)
- **LXX:** dikaioo (G1344) 21x, dikaios (G1342) 22x, krino (G2919) 8x
- **Common collocations:** mishpat (8x), shaphat (8x), tsaddiyq (7x), rasha (6x)
- **Book distribution:** Job dominates (56%), wisdom + prophetic = 90%

### qodesh — H6944
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md`

- **Word:** קֹדֶשׁ (qodesh) — masculine noun
- **Occurrences:** 452 in OT
- **Definition:** A sacred place or thing; holiness, sanctuary
- **Translation:** holy (53%), sanctuary (15%), most holy (10%), holiness (7%)
- **Distribution:** Leviticus (84), Exodus (70), Numbers (58), Ezekiel (51), Daniel (12)
- **Semantic categories:** sacred quality (modifier), sacred space (sanctuary), superlative (qodesh qodashim), abstract holiness, holy things, consecrated status
- **LXX:** hagios (G40) 346x — one of the tightest Hebrew-Greek mappings
- **In Dan 8:14:** qodesh appears without article (anarthrous) but is contextually definite from vv.11-13

### nathan — H5414
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5414-nathan.md`

- **Word:** נָתַן (nathan) — verb, primitive root
- **Occurrences:** 1,814 unique verses (2,285 tokens) — one of the most frequent OT verbs
- **Stem Distribution:** Qal 1,920 (active transfer), **Niphal 82 (be given, be delivered)**, Hophal 8
- **In Dan 8:12:** tinnaten = Niphal.Impf.3fs — "was given" — simple passive (divine passive register). The host is given over against the tamid by pesha (transgression). Agent suppressed.
- **Key semantic category for Dan 8:** "Delivering enemies/persons into another's hand" (Jdg 1:2; Dan 1:2; Jer 20:4) — natan + beyad pattern. Can be punitive (Israel given over to exile).

### kaphar — H3722
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md`

- **Word:** כָּפַר (kaphar) — verb, primitive root
- **Occurrences:** 102 in OT
- **Definition:** To cover; figuratively, to expiate, condone, placate
- **Stem Distribution:** Piel 92 (make atonement), Pual 7, Qal 1
- **Distribution:** Leviticus 67 (66%), Numbers 19, Exodus 10
- **Collocations:** chattaah (38x), kohen (27x), mizbeach (17x), dam (12x), salach (12x), taher (12x)
- **The kaphar+taher formula:** Atonement → cleansing (Lev 16:30). This is the DOA result in the type.
- **The kaphar+salach formula:** Atonement → forgiveness (Lev 4). This is the daily result.
- **In Dan 9:24:** ulekapper avon — "to make reconciliation for iniquity." The ONLY verse where kaphar and tsedeq appear together.
- **LXX:** hilaskomai (G2433, propitiation) PMI 6.47; aphiemi (G863, forgive) 14x; katharizo (G2511, cleanse) 14x

### taher — H2891
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2891-taher.md`

- **Word:** טָהֵר (taher) — verb, primitive root
- **Occurrences:** 94 in OT
- **Definition:** To be bright; by implication, to be pure (physically, ritually, morally)
- **Stem Distribution:** Piel 37 (declare/cleanse), Qal 34 (be clean), Hithpael 18 (cleanse oneself)
- **Distribution:** Leviticus 53 (46%), Ezekiel 15, Numbers 11
- **Key distinction from tsadaq:**
  - taher = ritual/physical purity — removing what is wrong
  - tsadaq = moral/forensic rightness — declaring what is right
  - taher describes the state (clean); tsadaq describes the verdict (righteous/vindicated)
- **In Lev 16:30:** kaphar → taher — the DOA produces cleansing (taher), NOT vindication (tsadaq)
- **Leviticus 16 never uses tsadaq.** The LXX of Lev 16 also avoids dikaioo (justify) — uses katharizo (cleanse). The gap is consistent across both languages.
- **Whether taher is exclusively cultic:** No — it appears in moral/spiritual contexts (Jer 33:8; Eze 36:25; Mal 3:3; Job 4:17; Pro 20:9). But its range is narrower than tsadaq. taher deals with purity status; tsadaq deals with legal standing, moral character, AND relational rightness.

### mirmah — H4820 (Newly Generated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4820-mirmah.md`

- **Word:** מִרְמָה (mirmah) — feminine noun
- **Occurrences:** 38 unique verses in OT
- **Definition:** Deceit, fraud, craft, treachery (from ramah, "to deceive")
- **Translation:** deceit (70%), false, guile, craft, treachery
- **Distribution:** Psalms (14), Proverbs (8), Daniel (2), Jeremiah (3)
- **In Dan 8:25:** "through his policy also he shall cause craft [mirmah] to prosper" — one of the horn's weapons. Mirmah is the antithesis of tsedeq (righteousness) and mishpat (justice).
- **In Isa 53:9:** "neither was any deceit [mirmah] in his mouth" — the Suffering Servant has NO mirmah, directly opposing the horn's mirmah-filled method.
- **Semantic categories:** deceit of tongue/speech, fraudulent commerce, inner character/counsel, political/military treachery, absence of deceit (righteous)
- **LXX:** dolos (G1388, guile) 21x, dolios (G1386, guileful) 10x — together 82% of renderings
- **Relevance:** mirmah (the horn's method) is one of three injustice-words answered by nitsdaq. The horn operates by deceit; the verdict operates by truth.

### dikaios — G1342
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1342-dikaios.md`

- **Word:** δίκαιος (dikaios) — adjective
- **Occurrences:** 76 unique verses (81 tokens) in NT
- **Definition:** Equitable in character or act; innocent, holy; just, righteous
- **Translation:** righteous (39.5%), just (35.5%), right (6.6%)
- **Distribution:** Matthew (16), Luke (11), Romans (7), Revelation (5)
- **Key Revelation occurrences:**
  - Rev 15:3 — dikaiai kai alēthinai hai hodoi sou ("just and true are thy ways")
  - Rev 16:5 — dikaios ei, ho ōn kai ho ēn ("thou art righteous, O Lord")
  - Rev 16:7 — alēthinai kai dikaiai hai kriseis sou ("true and righteous are thy judgments")
  - Rev 19:2 — alēthinai kai dikaiai hai kriseis autou ("true and righteous are his judgments")
  - Rev 19:11 — en dikaiosynē krinei ("in righteousness he judges")
- **Hebrew source:** tsaddiyq (H6662) 182x PMI 32.40 — the dominant LXX bridge
- **Collocations:** theos (16x), kyrios (10x), hagios (7x), krinō (6x)

### alethinos — G228
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md`

- **Word:** ἀληθινός (alethinos) — adjective
- **Occurrences:** 27 tokens in 25 verses (6 books)
- **Definition:** Truthful; genuine, real (the authentic thing vs. copy/counterfeit)
- **Translation:** "true" in virtually every instance (88%)
- **Distribution:** Revelation (10), John (8), Hebrews (3), 1 John (2)
- **Semantic domains:** genuine vs. copy (Heb 8:2; 9:24), true God vs. idols (Jhn 17:3; 1Th 1:9), faithful and true (Rev 3:7; 19:11), true judgments (Rev 15:3; 16:7; 19:2)
- **Hebrew source:** emeth (H571, truth/faithfulness) PMI 16.05 — primary Hebrew equivalent
- **Key pairing:** dikaios + alethinos = tsedeq + emeth — this vocabulary pair runs through all four Revelation vindication declarations. The horn cast emeth to the ground (Dan 8:12); the verdict declares God's ways dikaios kai alethinos.

### dikaioma — G1345 (Newly Generated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1345-dikaioma.md`

- **Word:** δικαίωμα (dikaiōma) — neuter noun
- **Occurrences:** 10 in NT
- **Definition:** An equitable deed; by implication, a statute or decision
- **Semantic senses:** ordinance/regulation (Luk 1:6; Heb 9:1,10), righteous requirement of law (Rom 2:26; 8:4), judicial decree/sentence (Rom 1:32; **Rev 15:4**), righteous act (Rom 5:16,18), righteous deeds of believers (Rev 19:8)
- **In Rev 15:4:** "thy judgments [dikaiōmata] are made manifest" — God's righteous decrees/ordinances are brought to light
- **In Rev 19:8:** "the fine linen is the righteousness [dikaiōmata] of saints" — the vindication produces visible righteous living

## Translation Relationship Studies (from Library)

### TR-tsadaq-dikaioo (tsadaq → dikaioo)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md`
- tsadaq (H6663) maps to dikaioo (G1344) in the LXX with PMI 26.99 — the strongest cross-testament verb bridge in the righteousness family
- The LXX of Dan 8:14 uses dikaiothēsetai (future passive of dikaioo) — showing the translators understood nitsdaq as "shall be justified/vindicated"

### TR-tsedeq-dikaiosyne (tsedeq → dikaiosyne)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tsedeq-dikaiosyne.md`
- tsedeq (H6664) maps to dikaiosyne (G1343) — the standard noun bridge

### TR-taher-katharizo (taher → katharizo)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-taher-katharizo.md`
- taher (H2891) maps to katharizo (G2511) — the cleansing bridge
- This is what the LXX used in Lev 16 — katharizo (cleansing), NOT dikaioo (justification)
- The LXX translators kept the DOA vocabulary in the cleansing register and the vindication vocabulary in the forensic register

### TR-emeth-aletheia (emeth → aletheia)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-emeth-aletheia.md`
- emeth (H571) maps to aletheia (G225) and alethinos (G228) — the truth bridge
- In Rev vindication declarations: alethinos (from emeth) + dikaios (from tsaddiyq) = emeth + tsedeq
- The horn cast emeth to the ground (Dan 8:12); the verdict uses alethinos to declare restoration

### TR-qodesh-hagios (qodesh → hagios)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-qodesh-hagios.md`
- qodesh maps to hagios 346x — one of the tightest LXX mappings
- Rev 15:4: "thou only art holy [hagios]" — in the vindication declaration context

### TR-mishpat-krima (mishpat → krima)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-mishpat-krima.md`
- mishpat (H4941) maps to krima (G2917) — the judgment bridge
- mishpat is tsadaq's primary forensic partner (8x collocations)
