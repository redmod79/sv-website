## Prior Studies

### From Series (sv-01 through sv-17)

**sv-01-biblical-righteousness:**
- Established the tsadaq word family (530+ OT uses) operates across three dimensions: forensic (courtroom verdict), relational (covenant faithfulness), and character (actual conduct).
- The Niphal of tsadaq appears only once in the entire OT — Dan 8:14 — meaning "to be vindicated." The sanctuary receives a passive verdict.
- The verb's collocations (mishpat 8x, shaphat 8x, rasha 6x) are all courtroom words. LXX maps tsadaq→dikaioo with PMI 26.99.
- Righteousness received through faith by imputation (Gen 15:6, Rom 4:3-5), maintained through walking in Spirit (Rom 8:1-4), lost through deliberate sustained turning (Eze 18:24, Heb 10:26-29).
- Key bridge texts: Isa 53:11 (three tsadaq forms in one verse), Rom 3:25-26 (God is "just AND the justifier"), Dan 9:24 (kaphar→tsedeq olamim).

**sv-02-biblical-defilement:**
- Established the tame/tumah starting point — defilement incompatible with God's presence.
- Two-axis framework from Lev 10:10: qodesh/chol and tahor/tame are independent axes.
- Three transmission mechanisms: blood application, priestly eating, ambient defilement (Lev 15:31, Num 19:13).
- DOA three-category formula (Lev 16:16): tumot, pesha, chattat — covers everything.
- The tame→tahor→tsadaq progression: this study defines the starting point.

**sv-03-tamid-doa-connection:**
- Daily and annual services are two halves of one integrated system. Daily puts sin in; annual takes sin out.
- Absolute vocabulary distinction: kaphar+salach (daily, 13x) vs kaphar+taher (annual, Lev 16:30). salach never in Lev 16; taher never in Lev 4-5.
- nasa connects both: priest bears sin IN (Lev 10:17); scapegoat bears sin OUT (Lev 16:22).
- The tamid never stops — even on the DOA itself (Num 29:11). H8548 tamid is 100% positive across 104 occurrences.
- The tamid is the textual link to the DOA: horn removes tamid (Dan 8:11) → tamid must be restored → tamid feeds the DOA → nitsdaq declares system vindicated.

**sv-04-doa-ritual-sequence:**
- DOA follows a chiastic structure: inward to mercy seat (pivot), then outward in reverse order.
- Blood movement reverses daily direction: during the year sin flows inward; on DOA cleansing flows outward.
- Two goats = one chattat (singular, Lev 16:5). LORD's goat provides blood atonement; scapegoat provides sin removal (after kalah, v.20).
- "Afflict your souls" (Lev 23:29) creates a sorting event: participate or be cut off. No other festival has this penalty.
- Three-verb chain in Lev 16:30: kaphar (act) → taher Piel (process) → taher Qal (state). DOA produces tahor, not tsaddiyq.

**sv-05-daniel-literary-architecture:**
- Four overlapping structural systems: Aramaic/Hebrew division, Aramaic chiasm (A-B-C-C'-B'-A'), four vision cycles, vocabulary chains.
- chazon (H2377) = broad prophetic panorama; mar'eh (H4758) = specific time element. Two independent tracking systems.
- biyn chain: 19 occurrences across Dan 8-12, tracking the process of understanding itself.
- Daniel 7 is the hinge chapter — belongs to Aramaic chiasm AND opens Hebrew prophetic section.

**sv-06-dan-8-verse-by-verse:**
- Four-fold attack mapped: host cast down (8:10), Prince challenged (8:11a), tamid removed/sanctuary cast down (8:11b), truth cast to ground (8:12).
- Active-passive-active voice chiasm across vv.10-12 encodes the horn's actual reach vs. pretensions. Same root shalak in active Hiphil (horn casts truth) and passive Hophal (sanctuary "was cast down").
- Two hapax constructions in v.14: erev boqer (counting unit) and nitsdaq (only Niphal of tsadaq).
- tinnaten (Niphal of nathan, 8:12) = divine passive — God permits the horn's activity. Cause: ba-pesha (willful rebellion). Bounded by 2300 erev boqer.
- Dan 8:13's ad matay = prophetic complaint formula. Three injustice-words (pesha, shamem, mirmac) answered by one justice-word (tsadaq).

**sv-07-dan-7-8-same-horn:**
- Dan 7 and Dan 8 horns are the same entity — convergence of shared characteristics, targets, timeline, destruction formulas.
- gadal/yether progression: ram→goat→horn, each exceeding the previous. yether requires surpassing both named empires.
- az paniym exclusive construct chain: Dan 8:23 / Deu 28:50 — only two OT occurrences. Embeds horn in Deuteronomic covenant-curse framework.
- be-efes yad (Dan 8:25) / di-la bidayin (Dan 2:34,45) — divine termination without human agency.
- Dan 7 provides the courtroom (judicial process). Dan 8 provides the sanctuary (verdict). Both needed.

**sv-08-sanctuary-earthly-or-heavenly:**
- The sanctuary in Dan 8:11-14 is heavenly. Multiple independent lines of evidence converge.
- 3ms suffix on miqdasho binds the sanctuary to masculine sar-ha-tsaba (not feminine qeren).
- mekon (H4349) belongs to heavenly-dwelling register (1 Ki 8:39,43,49; Exo 15:17; Psa 89:14).
- Daniel's own vocabulary stratification: heikal (Aramaic, earthly), bare miqdash (prayer, earthly), mekon miqdash (vision, heavenly).
- Voice alternation encodes horn's inability to directly touch heaven — it obscures access from earth.

**sv-09-counterfeit-system:**
- The horn substitutes, not just removes. Dan 11:31 and 12:11 use explicit "take away AND place" language.
- Four attacks → four counterfeits: persecution replaces protection, human authority replaces Prince's role, earthly priesthood replaces heavenly ministry, changed laws replace God's truth.
- Dan 2:21/7:25 shna contrast: God actually changes times (participle, sovereign act); horn merely intends to (infinitive, pretension). Grammar encodes the gap.
- tinnaten divine passive: God permits the counterfeit within bounded limits (2300 erev boqer). The same God who answers with nitsdaq.
- Heb 10:11-12 standing/sitting contrast: earthly priest stands (never finished); Christ sat down (finished). Reinstalling standing priests reverses Christ's "sat down."

**sv-10-evening-morning-unit:**
- erev boqer is a deliberately chosen, grammatically unique compound — nowhere else in the Hebrew Bible as a counting unit.
- Asyndetic bare noun pair: no conjunction, no article, no preposition, no verb. Structurally different from all comparators.
- Daniel knew yamim (used it in 12:11-12) but chose erev boqer. The unusual unit is the visible fingerprint of the sealing act.

**sv-11-sealing-system:**
- Two complementary verbs: satham (block access) and chatham (secure container). Four unsealing mechanisms: Christological, temporal, moral/spiritual, progressive.
- The sealed content is the temporal element of Dan 8:14: the 2300 erev boqer — a number without a starting point in an unusual unit.
- The event (vindication) is NOT sealed. Dan 8:26 seals the chazon but authenticates the mar'eh.

**sv-12-gabriel-return:**
- Dan 8 and 9 are one continuous narrative. Six independent textual evidences prove Gabriel returns to finish his Dan 8 mission.
- chazon/mar'eh two-track architecture: mar'eh resolves at 10:1; chazon extends to 12:4.
- Gabriel's 9:23 commission ("consider the mar'eh") resumes the 8:16 commission ("make this man understand the mar'eh").

**sv-13-seventy-weeks:**
- 70 weeks span 490 years from 457 BC to AD 34. chathak (hapax, "cut off") proves 70 weeks severed from 2300.
- Precise fulfillment: 457 BC + 483 = AD 27 (baptism); "midst of the week" = AD 31 (crucifixion); AD 34 (Stephen's stoning/gospel to Gentiles).
- The 70 weeks function as a mathematical verification key for the 2300 and the day-year principle.

**sv-14-dan-924-six-purposes:**
- Dan 9:24 is the ONLY verse where DOA vocabulary (kaphar) and vindication vocabulary (tsedeq) appear together.
- Six infinitival purposes in two triads: three resolve the sin problem (pesha, chattat, avon — same as Lev 16:21); three install positive realities (tsedeq olamim, authenticated prophecy, anointed sanctuary).
- qodesh qodashim never refers to a person in 40+ OT uses — points to heavenly sanctuary inauguration.

**sv-15-2300-calculation:**
- 457 BC + 2300 = 1844 AD. The chathak connection proves shared starting point with 70 weeks.
- 1335 independently converges on the same terminus from a different starting point (538 + 1335 = 1843/1844).
- The event at the terminus is nitsdaq qodesh — the antitypical DOA beginning.

**sv-16-dan-10-12-resolution:**
- biyn chain resolves at 10:1 (Daniel understood the mar'eh) but a second failure occurs at 12:8 (Daniel doesn't understand the 1290/1335).
- Dan 12:10: the maskilim will understand. Character determines access.
- Dan 12:3: matsdiqey (Hiphil of tsadaq) — same root as nitsdaq. The wise participate in vindication.
- Dan 12:13: triple promise to Daniel — rest, stand, lot (death, resurrection, inheritance).

**sv-17-judgment-antitypical-doa:**
- Dan 7:9-10 functions as the antitypical DOA at the level of structural correspondence, NOT lexical dependence.
- Five thematic parallels tested: white garments, fire, cloud, judicial examination, exclusion. Vocabulary differs at every transition point. Cloud cognate (anan) is the only shared root.
- Strongest connection: the kaphar-to-tsadaq linguistic progression anchored in Dan 9:24.
- Books contain "works" broadly (Rev 20:12, Mal 3:16) — larger than tamid-transferred sins. Antitype exceeds type.
- The judgment is pre-advent: Dan 7:11 shows the horn still speaking during judgment. Rev 22:12: "my reward is with me."
