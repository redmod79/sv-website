# Bible Study: What Does "The Sanctuary Shall Be Vindicated" Mean?

## Question
What does nitsdaq qodesh mean? Four-fold attack answered. Three-phase accomplishment. Five beneficiaries. tinnaten→nitsdaq frame.

## Prior Research Summary
Seventeen prior series studies (sv-01 through sv-17) provide the comprehensive foundation for this climax study:
- **sv-01** established the tsadaq word family (530+ OT uses) across three dimensions (forensic, relational, character). The Niphal of tsadaq appears only once in the entire OT — Dan 8:14. LXX maps tsadaq→dikaioo with PMI 26.99. Key bridge texts: Isa 53:11, Rom 3:25-26, Dan 9:24.
- **sv-02** established the defilement framework (tame/tumah), the two-axis system (qodesh/chol and tahor/tame), and the DOA three-category formula (Lev 16:16): tumot, pesha, chattat.
- **sv-03** showed daily and annual services are two halves of one integrated system. Absolute vocabulary distinction: kaphar+salach (daily) vs kaphar+taher (annual). nasa connects both halves. The tamid is the textual link to the DOA: horn removes tamid → nitsdaq declares system vindicated.
- **sv-04** established DOA chiastic structure, blood reversal, two-goats-as-one, "afflict your souls" sorting, and the three-verb chain (kaphar→taher Piel→taher Qal). DOA produces tahor, not tsaddiyq.
- **sv-06** mapped the four-fold attack: host cast down (8:10), Prince challenged (8:11a), tamid removed/sanctuary cast down (8:11b), truth cast to ground (8:12). Also identified tinnaten as Niphal divine passive — God permits the horn's activity, bounded by 2300 erev boqer.
- **sv-09** mapped four counterfeits matching the four attacks. tinnaten divine passive: God permits within bounded limits.
- **sv-14** established Dan 9:24 as the ONLY verse where DOA vocabulary (kaphar) and vindication vocabulary (tsedeq) appear together. Six infinitival purposes in two triads resolving sin (pesha, chattat, avon) and installing positive realities (tsedeq olamim, authenticated prophecy, anointed sanctuary).
- **sv-17** established the judgment (Dan 7:9-10) as antitypical DOA through structural correspondence. The kaphar-to-tsadaq linguistic progression anchored in Dan 9:24 is the strongest connection.

Four prior non-series studies (dan-8-14-vindication-meaning, sanc-25, sanc-19, sanctuary-vindication-meaning) also treated this exact question extensively, establishing: nitsdaq is forensic, the four-fold attack maps to four dimensions of vindication, the tinnaten→nitsdaq frame, three-phase accomplishment, five beneficiaries, the Zechariah 3 model, and the Revelation vindication declarations (14:7→15:3→16:7→19:2).

**External corpus leads** (Bohr/EGW) suggest: (1) nitsdaq encompasses both vindication and cleansing, (2) Daniel chose tsadaq over taher because taher confines meaning to earthly type, (3) the judgment answers the horn's work.

### Focus Areas

1. **The unique Niphal of tsadaq (nitsdaq) — what the grammar itself demands**: This is the ONLY Niphal occurrence of tsadaq in the entire OT (sv-01 established this). The Niphal stem functions as simple passive or divine passive. The sanctuary RECEIVES vindication — it does not vindicate itself. 2. **The tinnaten→nitsdaq narrative frame**: Both are Niphal (divine passive) forms. tinnaten (Niphal of nathan, 8:12) = God permits the horn's activity; nitsdaq (Niphal of tsadaq, 8:14) = God sets right what was permitted. The research agent should retrieve Dan 8:12 and 8:14 with full chapter context, run hebrew_parser.py on both verses, compare the Niphal forms, and READ `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5414-nathan.md`.
