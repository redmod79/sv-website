Key words:
- δίκαιαι (dikaios G1342) — Nom Pl F — "just/righteous" (predicate adjective)
- ἀληθιναὶ (alethinos G228) — Nom Pl F — "true/genuine" (predicate adjective)
- αἱ ὁδοί (hodos G3598) — Nom Pl F — "the ways" (subject)
- Βασιλεὺς τῶν ἐθνῶν — "King of the nations"

**Grammatical note:** δίκαιαι καὶ ἀληθιναὶ αἱ ὁδοί σου — predicate-first word order emphasizes the quality: "JUST and TRUE [are] thy ways." Both adjectives are nominative plural feminine matching ὁδοί.

## Revelation 16:7

Greek Text: Καὶ ἤκουσα τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου λέγοντος Ναί, Κύριε ὁ Θεός ὁ Παντοκράτωρ, ἀληθιναὶ καὶ δίκαιαι αἱ κρίσεις σου.

Key words:
- τοῦ θυσιαστηρίου λέγοντος (thysiastērion G2379 + legō G3004 PAP-GSM) — "the altar saying" — the altar itself speaks
- ἀληθιναὶ (alethinos G228) — Nom Pl F — "true"
- δίκαιαι (dikaios G1342) — Nom Pl F — "righteous"
- αἱ κρίσεις (krisis G2920) — Nom Pl F — "the judgments"

**Grammatical note:** Word order reverses from 15:3 — here ἀληθιναὶ precedes δίκαιαι (true and righteous). Subject is κρίσεις (judgments) rather than ὁδοί (ways). The altar (θυσιαστήριον, neuter) takes a masculine participle (λέγοντος) — personification of the altar as a speaking witness.

## Revelation 19:1-2

Key words:

- ἔκρινεν (krinō G2919) — Aorist Act Ind 3S — "he judged" (completed action)
- ἐξεδίκησεν (ekdikeō G1556) — Aorist Act Ind 3S — "he avenged" (completed action)

**Grammatical note:** Both verbs are aorist (completed action) — the judgment and avenging are presented as accomplished facts. This is the declaration of vindication complete.

## Romans 3:25-26

v.25: ὃν προέθετο ὁ Θεὸς ἱλαστήριον (hilastērion G2435 — propitiation/mercy seat) διὰ πίστεως ἐν τῷ αὐτοῦ αἵματι, εἰς ἔνδειξιν (endeixis G1732 — demonstration) τῆς δικαιοσύνης (dikaiosynē G1343 — righteousness) αὐτοῦ

v.26: εἰς τὸ εἶναι αὐτὸν δίκαιον (dikaios G1342 — just) καὶ δικαιοῦντα (dikaioō G1344 — PAP-ASM — "the one justifying") τὸν ἐκ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ.

**Critical grammatical note:** The purpose clause εἰς τὸ εἶναι αὐτὸν δίκαιον καὶ δικαιοῦντα uses an articular infinitive (εἰς τὸ εἶναι) expressing purpose/result: "in order that he might be..." Two predicate elements: (1) δίκαιον — adjective, "just" and (2) δικαιοῦντα — present active participle, "the one justifying." Both δικ- words from the same root. God is simultaneously JUST (dikaios) AND the JUSTIFIER (dikaiōn) — the syntactic tension is the theological point.

## Romans 8:33-34

v.33: τίς ἐγκαλέσει (engkaleō G1458 — FAI-3S — "shall bring a charge") κατὰ ἐκλεκτῶν (eklektos G1588 — "elect/chosen ones") Θεοῦ; Θεὸς ὁ δικαιῶν (dikaioō G1344 — PAP-NSM — "the one justifying")

**Grammatical note:** Rhetorical question with future indicative (ἐγκαλέσει — "who SHALL lay charge"). The answer is a verbless nominal clause: Θεὸς ὁ δικαιῶν — "God [is] the one justifying." The present participle δικαιῶν indicates ongoing action — God continually justifies.
