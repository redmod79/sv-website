# Topics Research

## Question
What is the "little book open" (biblaridion eneogmenon) in the angel's hand in Revelation 10? How does the Dan 12:5-7 oath scene structurally parallel Rev 10:5-6? How does the content inversion (duration to termination) identify the little book as Daniel's sealed prophecy? What does the sweet-then-bitter experience correspond to? What is the recommission of Rev 10:11?

### OATH
**Source:** Nave's Topical Dictionary — `naves_db.py --topic "OATH"`

**Definition:** A solemn qualification used in solemnizing covenants.

**Key subcategory — ATTRIBUTED TO GOD:**
GEN 22:16; PSA 89:35; 95:11; 105:9; 132:11; ISA 14:24; 45:23; JER 11:5; 22:5; 49:13; 51:14; LUK 1:73; HEB 3:11,18; 4:3; 6:13,14,17; 7:21,28; REV 10:6

EXO 20:7; 22:10,11; 23:1; LEV 6:2-5; 19:12; NUM 5:19-24; DEU 5:11; 6:13; 10:20; 1KI 8:31,32; PSA 15:1,2,4; ECC 8:2; ISA 48:1; JER 4:2; 5:2,7; 7:8,9; 12:16; DAN 9:11; **12:7**; HOS 4:15; MAT 5:33-37; 14:3-12; 23:18-22; 26:63; MRK 6:26; ACT 23:12-14; 2CO 1:23; GAL 1:20; HEB 6:16; JAS 5:12; **REV 10:5,6**

**Analysis note:** Nave's explicitly lists both DAN 12:7 and REV 10:5,6 together under the OATH topic, confirming these two passages belong to the same oath-scene category. Both appear under "Attributed to God" (divine oath) and "Unclassified Scriptures" (oath principles). This is the primary topical link connecting the Daniel and Revelation oath scenes.

**Selected covenant oath examples:**
- Between Abraham and Abimelech: GEN 21:22,23
- Rahab requires oath from spies: JOS 2:12-14; 6:22
- Jonathan and David confirm covenant by oath: 1SA 20:3,13-17

### MYSTERIES
**Source:** Nave's Topical Dictionary — `naves_db.py --topic "MYSTERIES"`

OF REDEMPTION: DEU 29:29; JOB 15:8; PSA 25:14; PRO 3:32; AMO 3:7; MAT 11:25; 13:11,35; MRK 4:11; LUK 8:10; JHN 3:8-12; ROM 16:25,26; 1CO 2:7-10; 2CO 3:12-18; EPH 1:9,10; 3:3-5,9,18,19; 6:19; COL 1:25-27; 2:2; 4:3,4; 2TH 2:7; 1TI 3:9,16; HEB 5:11; 1PE 1:10-12; **REV 10:7**

**Analysis note:** Rev 10:7 ("the mystery of God should be finished, as he hath declared to his servants the prophets") is the culminating entry in this mystery chain. The topic traces from DEU 29:29 ("the secret things belong unto the LORD our God") through the prophets' searching (1PE 1:10-12) to the eschatological finishing of the mystery. AMO 3:7 ("Surely the Lord GOD will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets") provides the hermeneutical key: mysteries are revealed to prophets, and the mystery of God finishes when the seventh angel sounds.

**Analysis note:** Dan 12:1 mentions "the book" in the context of Michael standing up and the time of trouble — the same chapter that contains the sealing command (12:4) and the oath scene (12:7). The book of life is distinct from the sealed prophecy, but both "book" concepts converge in Daniel 12.

### DANIEL
**Source:** Nave's Topical Dictionary — `naves_db.py --topic "DANIEL"`

1. A Jewish captive, also called BELTESHAZZAR:
   - Educated at king's court: DAN 1
   - Interprets visions: DAN 2; 4; 5
   - **Prophecies of: DAN 4:8,9; 7; 8; 9; 10; 11; 12; MAT 24:15**
   - Abstinence: DAN 1:8-16
   - Wisdom: DAN 1:17; EZK 28:3
   - Devoutness: DAN 2:18; 6; 9; 10; 12; EZK 14:14

**Analysis note:** Daniel's prophecies span chapters 7-12, with MAT 24:15 ("the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet") confirming Jesus' recognition of Daniel as a prophet. The sealed content specifically relates to the time-element of the vision (Dan 8:26, 12:4, 12:9).
