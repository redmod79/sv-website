### 1. participle-perfect-passive-state.md
**Path:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md`

Key points applied to this study:
- V-RPP describes STATE resulting from prior completed action
- Three functions: (1) attributive, (2) predicative/periphrastic, (3) circumstantial
- Central example: Rev 5:6 esphagmenon — Lamb carries enduring condition of having-been-slain
- Applied to Rev 10:2: eneogmenon — little book carries enduring condition of having-been-opened
- Applied to Rev 5:1: katesphragismenon — sealed scroll carries enduring condition of having-been-sealed
- The contrast: sealed (perf. pass.) → opened (perf. pass.) = one permanent state replaced by another

### 7. weqatal-apodosis-vow.md
**Path:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow.md`

Key points:
- Weqatal advances non-past discourse (future/modal/consequent)
- In oath contexts: stands in apodosis ("then...") committing speaker to future act
- Dan 12:7's oath uses wayyiqtol narrative (wayyishaba, wayyarem) rather than weqatal
- The oath content (moed moedim va-chetsi) is introduced by ki (that/because), not by weqatal
- The terminus clause (tikhleynah) uses Qal Imperfect 3fp — future completion
