## Rev 10:1 — Theophanic Description
- εἶδον (V-2AAI-1S): second aorist "I saw" — governs the entire verse
- καταβαίνοντα (V-PAP-ASM): present active participle — ongoing descent
- **περιβεβλημένον** (V-RPP-ASM): **perfect passive participle** — settled state of being clothed with a cloud. Same construction as Rev 12:1, 19:13.

## Rev 10:2 — Little Book Open
- ἔχων (V-PAP-NSM): present active participle — "having" (continuous possession)
- **ἠνεῳγμένον** (V-RPP-ASN): **perfect passive participle** of ἀνοίγω (G455) — permanently opened state. The book was opened at some prior point and now stands open. Passive: opened by an agent other than the holder.
- ἔθηκεν (V-AAI-3S): aorist — "he set" (single decisive act)

## Rev 10:3-4 — Seven Thunders Sealed
- ἔκραξεν (V-AAI-3S): aorist — "he cried" (completed act)
- μυκᾶται (V-PNI-3S): present middle/passive — "roars" (gnomic present, what a lion characteristically does)
- ἤμελλον γράφειν (V-IAI-1S + V-PAN): imperfect of μέλλω + present infinitive — "I was about to write" (conative imperfect: intention interrupted)
- **Σφράγισον** (V-AAM-2S): **aorist active imperative** of σφραγίζω — "seal up!" Single decisive act. The ONLY sealing command in Revelation's unsealing program.
- μὴ γράψῃς (PRT-N + V-AAS-2S): prohibitive subjunctive — "do not write these things"

## Rev 10:5-6 — The Oath
- **ἑστῶτα** (V-RAP-ASM): **perfect active participle** of ἵστημι — stative perfect, "standing." Not "having taken a stand" but "being in the posture of standing."
- ἦρεν (V-AAI-3S): aorist — "lifted" (single decisive act of raising the hand)
- **ὤμοσεν** (V-AAI-3S): **aorist active indicative** of ὀμνύω (G3660) — "he swore" (completed act)
- ἐν τῷ ζῶντι (PREP + T-DSM + V-PAP-DSM): "by the living one" — substantival present active participle
- ἔκτισεν (V-AAI-3S): constative aorist of κτίζω — entire creation as one act
- **χρόνος** (N-NSM): anarthrous nominative — qualitative "time/delay"
- **οὐκέτι** (ADV-N): "no longer" — presupposes prior state
- **ἔσται** (V-FDI-3S): **future** middle/deponent of εἰμί — prospective declaration

## Rev 10:7 — Mystery Finished
- ὅταν μέλλῃ σαλπίζειν (V-PAS-3S + V-PAN): present subjunctive + present infinitive — "whenever he is about to trumpet" (indefinite temporal)
- **ἐτελέσθη** (V-API-3S): **aorist passive indicative** of τελέω — **proleptic/prophetic aorist**: future event described as already completed. Divine passive: God finishes his mystery.
- εὐηγγέλισεν (V-AAI-3S): aorist of εὐαγγελίζω — "he declared good news" (single historical proclamation)

## Rev 10:8 — Take the Book
- τὸ βιβλίον τὸ ἠνεῳγμένον (T-ASN + N-ASN + T-ASN + V-RPP-ASN): shift from βιβλαρίδιον to βιβλίον. Same perfect passive participle in second-attributive position: "the book — the one having been opened."
- Ὕπαγε (V-PAM-2S): present imperative — "go" (process)
- λάβε (V-2AAM-2S): aorist imperative — "take" (punctiliar act)

## Rev 10:9 — Sweet and Bitter
- λέγει (V-PAI-3S): **historic present** — vivid narrative device
- κατάφαγε (V-2AAM-2S): aorist imperative of κατεσθίω — "devour completely" (κατά intensifies)
- πικρανεῖ (V-FAI-3S): **future active** — "it shall make bitter" (scroll is agent)
- ἔσται γλυκὺ ὡς μέλι (V-FDI-3S + A-NSN): **future** — "it shall be sweet as honey"

## Rev 10:10 — Experience
- ἔλαβον, κατέφαγον (V-2AAI-1S × 2): aorist — "I took, I devoured" (completed acts)
- ἦν γλυκύ (V-IAI-3S + A-NSN): **imperfect** — durative sweetness during eating
- **ἐπικράνθη** (V-API-3S): **aorist passive** — "was made bitter" (scroll's content causes bitterness; belly is patient)

## Rev 10:11 — The Recommission
- **λέγουσίν** (V-PAI-3P): present active indicative 3rd **PLURAL** — "they say" (not "he said"). Greek is unambiguously plural despite KJV singular.
- **Δεῖ** (V-PAI-3S): impersonal present — divine necessity currently operative
- **σε** (P-2AS): accusative subject of infinitive — John personally commissioned
- **πάλιν** (ADV): "again" — presupposes prior prophetic activity; modifies the infinitive not the finite verb
- **προφητεῦσαι** (V-AAN): **aorist active infinitive** — the ONLY aorist infinitive of προφητεύω in the entire NT. Treats the commission as a complete, bounded task (contrast present infinitive in 1 Cor 14:31, 14:39).
- **ἐπὶ λαοῖς καὶ ἔθνεσιν καὶ γλώσσαις καὶ βασιλεῦσιν πολλοῖς**: ἐπί + dative series — "concerning/over peoples, nations, tongues, and many kings." Four-fold universal scope formula.
