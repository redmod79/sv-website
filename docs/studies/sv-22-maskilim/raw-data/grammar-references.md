## Hebrew Stem References

### stem-hiphil.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-hiphil.md`
- Primary causative stem — subject causes object to do/become the Qal action
- Functions: (1) causative-factitive, (2) productive/internal, (3) declarative/estimative, (4) denominative
- Key for maskilim study: matsdiqey (Dan 12:3) is Hiphil participle of tsadaq = "those CAUSING to be righteous"
- yatsdiq (Isa 53:11) is Hiphil imperfect = "shall justify/cause to be righteous"
- haven (Dan 8:16) is Hiphil imperative of biyn = "CAUSE to understand"
- yavinu (Dan 11:33) is Hiphil imperfect of biyn = "they CAUSE to understand [the many]"

### stem-hithpael.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-hithpael.md`
- Reflexive-iterative stem — action subject performs on/for/within itself
- Functions: (1) reflexive, (2) reciprocal, (3) iterative/habitual, (4) middle voice, (5) estimative/pretend, (6) denominative
- Key for maskilim study: Dan 12:10 verbs yitbararu (Hithpael of barar) and yitlabbenu (Hithpael of laban)
- The Hithpael = "purify THEMSELVES," "make THEMSELVES white" — reflexive/self-directed
- Contrast with Dan 11:35 where external agents refine/purge/make white

### stem-niphal.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-niphal.md`
- Simple passive/reflexive stem
- Functions: (1) simple passive (subject receives action), (2) reflexive, (3) tolerative/middle
- Key for maskilim study: nitsdaq (Dan 8:14) = Niphal of tsadaq — sanctuary receives vindication
- yitsarfu (Dan 12:10) = Niphal of tsaraph — the many "are refined" (passive)

### stem-piel.md (referenced but not central)
- Intensive/factitive stem
- Dan 11:35: barar in Piel = intensive purging
