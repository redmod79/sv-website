## From Word Studies Library

### chathak — H2852 (The Lynchpin: "Cut Off")
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2852-chathak.md`

- **Original:** חָתַךְ | **Transliteration:** chathak | **POS:** Verb | **Occurrences:** 1 (HAPAX)
- **Definition:** Primitive root; properly, to cut off; figuratively, to decree/determine
- **Sole occurrence:** Dan 9:24 — Niphal Perfect 3ms (nechtak): "Seventy weeks are determined [cut off]"
- **The physical root meaning is CUTTING — severing a portion from a whole.** The figurative extension to "decree" follows the same path as other Hebrew cutting verbs. An authoritative decision *cuts off* a defined portion from an undefined whole.
- **Hapax status is critical:** Daniel had charats (H2782) available for "decree" (uses it in 9:26-27) but deliberately chose a unique, never-before-used verb. A word that exists nowhere else in Scripture, used for a one-time act of temporal severance.
- **LXX rendering:** Theodotion uses syntemno (cut short) — preserving the cutting metaphor. OG uses ekrithesan (were judged/decided).
- **Three cutting verbs in Dan 9:** chathak (cuts time, v.24), karath (cuts a person, v.26), charats (cuts fate, vv.26-27). Each cuts something different, creating a deliberate cutting motif.

### tsadaq — H6663 (The Terminus Event: "Be Vindicated")
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`

- **Original:** צָדַק | **Transliteration:** tsadaq | **POS:** Verb | **Occurrences:** 41 in OT
- **Definition:** To be (causatively, make) right in a moral or forensic sense
- **Stem Distribution:** Qal 22, Hiphil 12, Piel 5, **Niphal 1** (Dan 8:14 only), Hithpael 1
- **Dan 8:14 — the ONLY Niphal of tsadaq in the entire OT.** Nitsdaq qodesh = "the sanctuary shall be vindicated." The Niphal is passive: the sanctuary RECEIVES vindication.
- **KJV translates "cleansed" but the Hebrew root is judicial, not ritual.** The cleansing word would be taher (H2891). Tsadaq carries forensic force: be righteous, be vindicated, be declared right.
- **Dan 12:3 — matsdiqey (Hiphil participle):** "they that turn many to righteousness." Same root, different stem. The maskilim CAUSE many to be righteous. Complementary to nitsdaq: the sanctuary receives vindication (Niphal); the wise cause righteousness (Hiphil).
- **LXX mapping:** dikaioo (G1344) 21x, dikaios (G1342) 22x. The Theodotion of Dan 8:14 used katharisthesetai ("shall be cleansed") — mistranslating the root.

### tamid — H8548 (The Link: "The Continual")
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md`

- **Original:** תָּמִיד | **Transliteration:** tamid | **POS:** Masculine noun | **Occurrences:** 104 in OT
- **Definition:** Continuance; used attributively as "constant" or adverbially "continually"; elliptically, the regular daily sacrifice
- **100% positive across all 104 occurrences** — every use describes God's worship system (offerings, lamp, showbread, incense, praise). Never pagan, never negative.
- **In Daniel used substantively ("the tamid") without qualifier:** Dan 8:11, 8:13, 11:31, 12:11. KJV supplies [sacrifice] in italics. The Hebrew is elliptical — the referent is the entire continual ministry system, not just one offering.
- **The tamid in the 1290/1335 calculation:** Dan 12:11 starts from "the time that the daily shall be taken away." The starting point for the 1290 and 1335 is tied to the removal of the tamid.
- **Dan 11:31 ‖ Dan 12:11 — identical formula:** 11:31 uses Hiphil active (wehesiru, "they shall remove"), 12:11 uses Hophal passive (husar, "was removed"). Same event, different voice: active names the agent; passive focuses on the consequence.

### qodesh — H6944 (The Subject: "Sanctuary/Holiness")
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md`

- **Original:** קֹדֶשׁ | **Transliteration:** qodesh | **POS:** Masculine noun | **Occurrences:** 452 in OT
- **In Dan 8:14:** Anarthrous (no article) yet contextually definite — the sanctuary under discussion since 8:11-13. Subject of nitsdaq (Niphal passive): the sanctuary receives vindication.
- **Dan 9:24 — qodesh qodashim:** "most Holy" — never refers to a person in 40+ OT occurrences; always designates Most Holy Place or most sacred objects. Points to heavenly sanctuary inauguration.
- **Dan 8:13 — qodesh ve-tsaba:** "sanctuary and host" given to trampling. The question asks how long the trampling; the answer (8:14) gives the terminus when qodesh is vindicated.

### esher — H835 (The Beatitude: "Blessed/Happy")
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H835-esher.md` (newly generated)

- **Original:** אֶשֶׁר | **Transliteration:** esher | **POS:** Masculine noun | **Occurrences:** 42 verses / 51 tokens across 9 books
- **Definition:** Happiness; only in masculine plural construct as exclamatory formula: "how happy/blessed is the one who..."
- **Distribution:** Psalms 60%, Proverbs 19%, Isaiah 3 occurrences, Daniel 1 occurrence. Overwhelmingly Wisdom literature.
- **Dan 12:12 is the ONLY prophetic-apocalyptic beatitude in Daniel.** No other time period in Daniel receives a blessing.
- **Seven semantic categories identified:**
  1. Blessedness of Torah-keeping (Psa 1:1, 119:1-2)
  2. Blessedness of trusting God (Psa 2:12, 34:8, 84:12)
  3. Blessedness of forgiveness (Psa 32:1-2)
  4. Blessedness of divine correction (Job 5:17, Psa 94:12)
  5. Blessedness of proximity to God (Psa 65:4, 84:4-5)
  6. National blessedness (Deut 33:29, Psa 33:12)
  7. **Eschatological/prophetic blessedness** (Isa 30:18, Dan 12:12, Isa 56:2)
- **Isaiah 30:18 is the closest lexical parallel to Dan 12:12:** "blessed are all they that WAIT for him" (same root chakah for "wait")
- **barak vs. esher distinction:** barak = active blessing conferred by an agent; esher = declared state of happiness/blessedness. Esher introduces a predication about a subject; barak takes a direct object.
- **LXX mapping:** H835 → G3107 makarios in 38 of 42 verses (90%). One of the cleanest one-to-one Hebrew-Greek bridges.

### chakah — H2442 (The Waiting: "Wait/Tarry")
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2442-chakah.md` (newly generated)

- **Original:** חָכָה | **Transliteration:** chakah | **POS:** Verb (primitive root) | **Occurrences:** 13 verses / 16 tokens across 8 books
- **Definition:** Properly "to adhere to"; hence, to await. Root idea: piercing/adhering — a deliberate, sustained waiting.
- **Overwhelmingly Piel (93%)** — intensified sense: deliberate, sustained adherence. Not passive delay but active, persistent waiting.
- **Prophetic concentration:** 7 of 13 verses (54%) in prophetic books (Isaiah, Hosea, Habakkuk, Zephaniah, Daniel).
- **Key theological collocations:** YHWH 38% (theological waiting on God), mishpat 15% (waiting tied to divine justice), esher (H835) 15% (beatitude formula pairing).
- **Dan 12:12 — Piel participle hamechakkeh:** "the one waiting" — characteristic, sustained, adherent waiting. Not idle but active perseverance.
- **Hab 2:3 parallel:** "though it tarry (chakah), wait for it; because it will surely come" — the vision has an appointed time; waiting is for fulfillment of prophetic time.
- **Isa 30:18 — double chakah:** God waits (Piel) that he may be gracious; blessed are all who wait (Qal) for him. The only verse where God and humans both use chakah.
- **LXX mapping:** H2442 → G5278 hupomeno (7x, "endure/remain under") and G3306 meno (4x, "remain/abide"). LXX translators understood chakah as persistent remaining, not idle delay.
- **Synonym distinction:** qavah (H6960, 49x) = expectant tension; yachal (H3176, 46x) = patient hope; chakah = adherent clinging. Chakah is the rarest and most intimate of the "waiting" verbs.

### TR: esher → makarios (Blessedness Cross-Testament Trace)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-esher-makarios.md` (newly generated)

- **H835 esher (42x OT) → G3107 makarios (49x NT)** via LXX bridge of 38 co-occurrences (PMI 32.98)
- **Direct OT→NT quotation:** Rom 4:7-8 quotes Psalm 32:1-2 — Paul uses the Davidic forgiveness-beatitude for his justification-by-faith argument.
- **Continuity:** Beatitude formula structure preserved across testaments. Both apply to forgiveness, trust, and obedience.
- **NT expansion:** Paradoxical beatitudes (poor, mourning, persecuted in Matt 5:3-11) absent from OT esher usage. Eschatological beatitudes appear in both testaments (Isa 30:18, Dan 12:12 → Rev 14:13, 22:7).
- **Semantic shift minimal:** Both function as exclamatory markers of the fortunate state. Grammar shifts from construct-noun (Hebrew) to adjective (Greek) but function is identical.

### WG: Blessedness (Word Group)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-blessed.md` (newly generated)

Seven words form the blessedness cluster: esher (the declaration), ashar (the root verb), barak (active counterpart), YHWH (the source), derek (the path), halak (the conduct), shamar (the obedience). The concept forms a theological cluster: YHWH is the source, derek is the prescribed conduct, halak and shamar describe the living-out, and esher declares the resulting state.

## Grammar Reference Entries Read

### dan-8-14.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-14.md`
- Nitsdaq = Niphal Perfect 3ms of tsadaq. Prophetic perfect with waw = future certainty.
- Erev boqer = asyndetic noun pair functioning as single compound time unit.
- Clause 2 (time phrase) is elliptical (no verb); Clause 3 (WQtX) provides the main predication.

### dan-9-24-27.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-9-24-27.md`
- Nechtak = Niphal Perfect 3ms of chathak (HAPAX). Stative/resultative: settled divine decision.
- Six telic infinitive construct clauses: 3 removing sin + 3 installing positives.
- Three cutting verbs: chathak (v.24 — time), karath (v.26 — person), charats (vv.26-27 — fate).
- Dan 8-9 connection grammatically established through lexical resumption of biyn/sakal chain.

### dan-12-7-11-12.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-12-7-11-12.md`
- Dan 12:11: husar = Hophal Perfect 3ms of sur (agent-suppressing passive). Identical formula to Dan 11:31 but voice shifts from active (Hiphil) to passive (Hophal).
- Dan 12:12: ashrey hamechakkeh = beatitude formula (mp.Cst noun + Piel participle). Closest OT parallel: Isa 30:18.
- weyaggiya = Hiphil Imperfect 3ms of naga — "attain to" the 1335 days. Subject continuity from the participle.
- The shift from indeterminate "time, times, half" (v.7) to exact day-counts (vv.11-12) = deliberate rhetorical move from formulaic to calendrical.

### dan-8-26.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-26.md`
- Mareh ha-erev ve-ha-boqer = single titular unit (one construct head, anaphoric articles, singular predicate concord).
- Three ki + le + temporal-noun formulas bracket Gabriel's address: v.17 (et qets), v.19 (moed qets), v.26 (yamim rabbim).
- Dan 8:26 commands concealment (satham) but not sealing-with-authentication (chatham). The full pair appears in Dan 12:4.

### dan-8-13-14-dialogue.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-13-14-dialogue.md`
- The ad-matay/ad lock-and-key: question's ad-matay answered by answer's ad + terminus. Whatever the question asks about, the 2300 erev boqer terminates.
- Question scope favors whole-vision reading (b): ha-chazon = the entire Dan 8 vision, not just tamid sub-period. Supported by anaphoric linkage to vv.1-2 and Gabriel's framing in vv.17,26.
- Semantic inversion: question closes with qodesh as recipient of mirmas (trampling); answer closes with qodesh as recipient of nitsdaq (vindication). Same subject, reversed verdict.

### dan-9-22-23.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-9-22-23.md`
- Four biyn-root instances in two verses = highest concentration in the entire book.
- Gabriel's paired imperatives: ubin baddabar (Qal: understand the word) + wehaben bammareh (Hiphil: gain insight into the vision). Explicitly reconnects to unfinished Dan 8 business.
- The mareh of 9:23 reaches back to the mareh of 8:16, 26-27 that was left uninterpreted at 8:27.

### stem-niphal.md
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-niphal.md`
- Niphal: simple passive (subject receives action), reflexive, or tolerative/middle.
- Nitsdaq (Dan 8:14) = simple passive: the sanctuary receives vindication.
- Nechtak (Dan 9:24) = simple passive: the 70 weeks have been cut off (by divine agent).
