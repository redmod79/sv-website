### From Study Plan (Dependencies: sv-12, sv-13)

**sv-12-gabriel-return:**
- Question: How does Daniel 8 connect to Daniel 9?
- Six independent lines of evidence prove Dan 8-9 are one continuous narrative: Gabriel's back-reference (9:21), identical commission formula (8:16 = 9:23), biyn failure-resolution arc, "many days" echo (8:26 = 10:14), shared vocabulary network, and Daniel's Jeremiah study as bridge event
- The chazon/mar'eh two-track system: chazon = broad panorama (extends through Dan 12); mar'eh = the specific time element (2300 evening-mornings, resolves at 10:1)
- Dan 8:26 is the proof text: both words appear with different referents and different fates — mar'eh declared "true" (emet hu), chazon commanded to be "shut up"
- The biyn chain: commission (8:16, haben) → failure (8:27, ein mebiyn) → self-study (9:2, binoti) → resumption (9:22-23, wehaben bammar'eh) → resolution (10:1)
- chathak (H2852) as hapax legomenon in 9:24 — root meaning "cut off." Daniel used charats for "decree" in 9:26-27, making the unique word a deliberate choice. Theodotion preserves cutting sense (syntemno). If 70 weeks are "cut from" the 2300, they share a starting point.

**sv-13-seventy-weeks:**
- Question: What is the 70 weeks prophecy? Starting point, day-year principle, calculation, 70th week events, verification key function.
- Day-year principle rests on three pillars: declared (Num 14:34, Ezek 4:6), signaled (shabuim with yamim vs. without yamim), verified by history (457 BC + 483 = AD 27)
- Starting point: Artaxerxes' decree in Ezra 7 (457 BC) — the only decree authorizing both restoration of judicial authority AND building
- Calculation: 457 BC + 483 = AD 27. Jesus declares "the time is fulfilled" (Mark 1:15, peplerōtai ho kairos). Galatians 4:4 confirms.
- 70th week (AD 27-34): Messiah strengthens (gabar) existing covenant; mid-week crucifixion (spring AD 31); sacrifice ceases by fulfillment (Matt 27:51, veil torn); gospel extends to Gentiles by ~AD 34
- **Verification key function:** The 70 weeks are both a Messianic prophecy and a mathematical proof. If the arithmetic works on the testable 490-year subset, the same day-year conversion applied to the parent 2300-year period is authenticated.
- Three cutting verbs in Dan 9: chathak (cuts time, v.24), karath (cuts a person, v.26), charats (cuts fate, vv.26-27)

**sv-14-dan-924-six-purposes:**
