## Key Grammatical Findings for This Study

### The chathak-nitsdaq Chain
- Dan 9:24: nechtak (Niphal Perf 3ms of chathak) — HAPAX. 70 weeks "cut off." Passive = divine agent.
- Dan 8:14: nitsdaq (Niphal Perf 3ms of tsadaq) — ONLY Niphal of tsadaq. Sanctuary "vindicated." Passive = divine agent.
- Both are Niphal perfects with suppressed agents — divine passive constructions framing the beginning (cutting off the 70 weeks) and terminus (vindicating the sanctuary) of the prophetic period.

### The Tamid Removal Formula
- Dan 11:31: wehesiru hatamid (Hiphil Perf 3p of sur) — ACTIVE: "they shall remove the daily"
- Dan 12:11: husar hatamid (Hophal Perf 3ms of sur) — PASSIVE: "the daily was removed"
- Same event, voice shift: 11:31 names the agent (arms/forces), 12:11 suppresses the agent and focuses on the temporal consequence as a starting point for the 1290 count.

### The 12:12 Beatitude Structure
- ashrey (mp.Cst) + hamechakkeh (Art + Piel.Ptcp.ms) + weyaggiya (Conj + Hiphil.Impf.3ms) + leyamim (Prep + mp.Abs) + number
- Formula: "Blessed is the one persistently waiting and attaining to [exact count] days"
- Closest OT parallel: Isa 30:18 ashrey kol-chokhey lo ("blessed all who wait for him") — identical root chakah, identical ashrey formula
- The intensified Piel of chakah in Dan 12:12 (vs. Qal in Isa 30:18) adds emphasis: this is not casual waiting but sustained, adherent perseverance toward a specific temporal mark
