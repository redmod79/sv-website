## Key Grammar Findings for This Study

### 1. The kalah→teleo LXX Bridge
- Gen 2:1: kalah Pual wayyiqtol (וַיְכֻלּוּ) → LXX synteleo
- Gen 2:2: kalah Piel wayyiqtol (וַיְכַל) → LXX synteleo
- Lev 16:20: kalah Piel perf (וְכִלָּה) — "when he has finished atonement"
- Dan 9:24: kalah Piel InfCon (לְכַלֵּא) — "to finish the transgression"
- John 19:30: teleo Perfect Passive Indicative (Τετέλεσται) — "it is finished"
- The chain: H3615 kalah → G4931 synteleo (85x LXX) and G5055 teleo (5x LXX)

### 2. The kaphar→taher→tsedeq Progression
- Lev 16:30: kaphar (Piel) → taher (Piel InfCon → Qal Impf) = atonement → cleansing
- Dan 9:24: kaphar (Piel InfCon) + tsedeq olamim = atonement → everlasting righteousness
- Dan 8:14: nitsdaq (Niphal of tsadaq) = vindication declared
- Progression: atonement (ground) → cleansing (application) → vindication (declaration)

### 3. Isa 53:12 Tense Shift
- Three Perfects: הֶעֱרָה (poured out), נִמְנָה (was numbered), נָשָׂא (bore) = COMPLETED actions
- One Imperfect: יַפְגִּיעַ (makes intercession) = ONGOING action
- Grammar encodes two-phase structure: bearing done, intercession continues

### 4. Heb 10:14 Two-Tense Compression
- τετελείωκεν (Perfect Active Indicative of teleioo) = "has perfected" — completed, results hold
- ἁγιαζομένους (Present Passive Participle of hagiazo) = "those being sanctified" — ongoing process
- One sentence, two tenses = sacrifice complete, sanctification progressive
