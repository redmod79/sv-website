## Leviticus 16:15 — DOA sacrifice
```
שָׁחַט   שׁחט   Verb.Qal.Perf.3ms   "slaughter" — the killing action
הֵבִיא   בוא    Verb.Hiphil.Perf.3ms "bring" — causative, bringing blood inside
הִזָּה   נזה    Verb.Hiphil.Perf.3ms "sprinkle" — blood application onto kapporeth
```
Sequence: slaughter (courtyard) → bring blood inside (past the veil) → sprinkle on kapporeth.
Three distinct actions separated in space: killing = outside, blood = carried inside, application = at the mercy seat.

## Leviticus 16:30 — kaphar→taher formula
```
יְכַפֵּר   כפר   Verb.Piel.Impf.3ms   "make atonement" — priestly action
טַהֵר     טהר   Verb.Piel.InfCon     "to cleanse" — purpose/result
תִּטְהָרוּ טהר   Verb.Qal.Impf.2mp   "you shall be clean" — people's resulting state
```
Three-verb formula unique to this verse. Stem shift Piel→Qal encodes: cleanness is both externally conferred (Piel factitive) and internally realized (Qal stative).

## Isaiah 53:12 — tense shift (bearing + intercession)
```
הֶעֱרָה   ערה   Verb.Hiphil.Perf.3ms  "poured out" — COMPLETED (soul unto death)
נִמְנָה   מנה   Verb.Niphal.Perf.3ms  "was numbered" — COMPLETED (with transgressors)
נָשָׂא    נשׂא  Verb.Qal.Perf.3ms    "bore/carried" — COMPLETED (sin of many)
יַפְגִּיעַ פגע  Verb.Hiphil.Impf.3ms "makes intercession" — ONGOING (for transgressors)
```
CRITICAL: Three perfects (completed actions: poured out, numbered, bore) followed by one imperfect (ongoing action: makes intercession). The grammar itself encodes the two-phase structure: bearing IS done; intercession CONTINUES.
