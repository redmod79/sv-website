# Word Studies

## From Word Studies Library

### kaphar — H3722
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md`
- Verb (primitive root), 102 occurrences. Piel dominates (92/102 = 90%).
- Definition: To cover; figuratively to expiate, condone, placate, cancel.
- KJV: "make atonement" ~73x (72%), "purge" 8x, "reconcile" 7x.
- Distribution: 67% in Leviticus, 83% in Pentateuch.
- Core collocations: chattaah (38x), kohen (27x), dam (12x), salach (12x), taher (12x).
- Two result formulas: kaphar→salach (daily, 13x in Lev 4-5) vs. kaphar→taher (DOA, Lev 16:30).
- **Key for this study:** kaphar technically describes the blood-application action, not the killing. In Lev 16, kaphar appears in v.6, v.10, v.11, v.16, v.17, v.18, v.20, v.24, v.27, v.30, v.32, v.33, v.34 — always for what happens WITH the blood, never for the slaughter itself.

### teleo — G5055
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5055-teleo.md`
- Verb, 26 unique verses. Root: telos (G5056) = end, goal, terminus.
- Semantic categories: (1) Narrative closure "when Jesus had finished," (2) Fulfillment of Scripture, (3) "It is finished" — cross declaration, (4) Fulfilling the law, (5) Completing a mission, (6) Eschatological completion in Revelation.
- τετέλεσται (John 19:30) = Perfect Passive Indicative 3S. Perfect tense = completed action with ongoing results.
- Hebrew source: H3615 kalah (PMI 5.49, 5 co-occurrences). LXX bridge confirmed.

### telos — G5056
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5056-telos.md`
- Neuter noun, 41 unique verses. Root: tello = to set out for a definite point or goal.
- Primary sense: the conclusion of an act or state, OR the purpose toward which something tends.
- KJV: "end/the end" 83%.
- "Christ is the end (telos) of the law for righteousness" (Rom 10:4) — goal/culmination sense.
- Hebrew source via LXX: natsach (H5329, PMI 7.21) — "to oversee/be preeminent."

### kalah — H3615
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3615-kalah.md`
- Verb, 199 unique verses. Piel dominates (141/206 tokens).
- Definition: To end, complete, consume, destroy.
- Semantic categories: (1) Completion of a task (Gen 2:1-2), (2) Finishing speaking, (3) Divine judgment/consumption, (4) Wrath accomplished.
- **Key for this study:** Lev 16:20 uses kalah Piel perf 3ms (כִלָּה) + min + kaphar InfCon = "when he has finished from making atonement" — structural boundary marker between blood-application phase and scapegoat phase.
- LXX mapping: synteleo G4931 (85x, PMI 27.17), teleo G5055 (5x, PMI 9.84). The kalah→teleo bridge through Gen 2:1-2 connects creation completion to John 19:30.

### hilasterion — G2435
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2435-hilasterion.md`
- Neuter noun, 2 NT occurrences (Rom 3:25; Heb 9:5).
- Lexicon: An expiatory place or thing — mercy seat (Heb 9:5) or propitiatory sacrifice (Rom 3:25).
- Hebrew source: kapporeth H3727 (16x LXX, PMI 9.75).
- **Key for this study:** Rom 3:25 applies hilasterion to Christ himself. The typological chain: mercy seat (kapporeth) → Christ (hilasterion). What happened annually by blood-sprinkling at the kapporeth now happens once-for-all at the cross. Combined with dikaiosyne = DOA vocabulary + vindication vocabulary together.

### entygchano — G1793
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1793-entynchano.md`
- Verb, 5 occurrences. Root: en + tygchano = to hit upon, approach, appeal.
- Present tense dominates (4 of 5) — emphasizes ongoing, continuous intercession.
- Key verses: Rom 8:27 (Spirit intercedes), Rom 8:34 (Christ intercedes), Heb 7:25 ("ever liveth to make intercession").
- Hebrew background: H6293 paga (Isa 53:12 LXX).

### parakletos — G3875
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3875-parakletos.md`
- Masculine noun, 5 occurrences. Exclusively Johannine.
- KJV: "Comforter" 4x (Holy Spirit, John 14-16), "advocate" 1x (Christ, 1 John 2:1).
- 1 John 2:1: Christ as legal advocate before the Father — forensic/courtroom dimension.

### kathizo — G2523
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2523-kathizo.md`
- Verb, 46 unique verses. Both transitive ("cause to sit") and intransitive ("sit down").
- Semantic categories include: (1) Enthronement of Christ, (2) Thrones for the faithful, (3) Judicial seating, (4) Teaching posture.
- Hebrews enthronement uses: Heb 1:3, 8:1, 10:12, 12:2.
- **Key for this study:** Every Hebrews occurrence describes the ENTHRONED exalted Christ. Sitting = royal authority, not retirement. Kings sit to govern, not to cease activity.

### leitourgos — G3011
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3011-leitourgos.md`
- Masculine noun, 5 occurrences. From leitos ("public") + ergon ("work").
- Heb 8:2: Christ is leitourgos "of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle." This is the verse that explicitly combines sitting (v.1, kathizo) with active ministry (v.2, leitourgos).

### hagiazo — G37
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G37-hagiazo.md`
- Verb, sanctify/set apart. Used in Heb 10:10 (ephapax — once for all offering sanctifies) and 10:14 (present passive participle — "those being sanctified," ongoing process).

### katharizo — G2511
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2511-katharizo.md`
- Verb, 30 occurrences. LXX equivalent of taher H2891.
- 1 John 1:7: καθαρίζει = Present Active Indicative — "cleanses" (ongoing).
- Heb 9:23: καθαρίζεσθαι = Present Passive Infinitive — the heavenly things need to be purified.
- **Key for this study:** katharizo in present tense = ongoing cleansing. The blood's purifying work continues after the sacrifice is complete.

### Translation Relationships

#### TR: taher → katharizo
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-taher-katharizo.md`
- taher (H2891, 94x) → katharizo (G2511, 61x as LXX translation).
- Continuity: Blood remains the instrument; God remains the agent; priestly declaration pattern persists.
- Shifts: From ritual to moral/spiritual; from priest-mediated to direct; from repeated to once-for-all.

#### TR: tsadaq → dikaioo
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md`
- tsadaq (H6663, 41x) → dikaioo (G1344, 21x LXX, 40x NT).
- OT covers Qal (be righteous), Hiphil (declare righteous), Piel (prove right), Niphal (be vindicated).
- NT dikaioo primarily carries the Hiphil forensic-declaration sense.
- **Key:** The OT Niphal (Dan 8:14 — vindication) has no direct NT parallel via dikaioo — the concept takes different vocabulary in the NT.

## Newly Generated Studies (Phase 3.5)

### hapax — G530
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G530-hapax.md`
- Adverb, 15 occurrences across 6 books. Hebrews has 8 (53%).
- Two senses: (1) Numerical "once" (bare counting), (2) Unrepeatable sacrifice/offering.
- Semantic categories: numerical once (2 Cor 11:25), unrepeatable sacrifice (Heb 9:26,28; 1 Pet 3:18), unrepeatable human experience (Heb 9:27), past completed state (Heb 6:4; 10:2), eschatological finality (Heb 12:26-27).
- **Key finding:** hapax has broader range than ephapax. In sacrificial contexts (Heb 9:26,28), it modifies the sacrifice/appearing. It NEVER modifies intercession or ongoing ministry.
- Hebrew source via LXX: paam H6471 (16x, PMI 19.66), echad H259 (14x, PMI 10.70). Primary range is numerical.

### ephapax — G2178
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2178-ephapax.md`
- Adverb (compound: epi + hapax), 5 occurrences. Hebrews has 3 (60%).
- In NT, 4 of 5 uses = decisive unrepeatable sacrifice: Rom 6:10, Heb 7:27, 9:12, 10:10.
- 1 Cor 15:6 = "at once" (simultaneous occurrence — different sense).
- **CRITICAL VERIFICATION:** Every sacrificial use of ephapax modifies the sacrifice/offering/death:
  - Rom 6:10: "he died unto sin once" (modifies DIED)
  - Heb 7:27: "this he did once, when he offered up himself" (modifies DID/OFFERED)
  - Heb 9:12: "he entered in once... having obtained eternal redemption" (modifies ENTERED)
  - Heb 10:10: "through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once" (modifies OFFERING)
- ephapax NEVER modifies intercession, ministry, or ongoing priestly activity.

### histemi — G2476
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2476-histemi.md`
- Verb, 152 unique verses. Complex tense/voice system.
- Heb 10:11: ἕστηκεν (Perfect Active Indicative) = "stands" — ongoing state of standing.
- **Key for this study:** The standing-sitting contrast in Heb 10:11-12: Every priest STANDS (histemi, perfect = ongoing state) daily ministering... but THIS MAN sat down (kathizo, aorist = completed action). Standing = work never finished. Sitting = sacrifice complete.
