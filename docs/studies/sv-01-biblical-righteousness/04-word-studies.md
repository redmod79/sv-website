### Hebrew tsadaq Family (4 words, 523+ OT occurrences)

#### tsadaq (verb) — H6663
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H6663-tsadaq.md`
- **Occurrences:** 41 (54 translation entries)
- **Primary meaning:** to be righteous, to be in the right, to justify, to vindicate
- **Stem distribution:** Qal 22x (be righteous), Hiphil 12x (declare righteous/acquit), Piel 5x (prove right by comparison), Niphal 1x (Dan 8:14 — be vindicated), Hithpael 1x (Gen 44:16 — clear oneself)
- **Key translations (KJV):** "justify/justified" ~20x, "righteous/be righteous" ~11x, "just/be just" ~8x, "cleansed" 1x (Dan 8:14 — anomalous)
- **Book concentration:** Job dominates (56% of all uses, 23/41), reflecting sustained exploration of whether a mortal can be righteous before God
- **Collocations:** mishpat (H4941) 8x, shaphat (H8199) 8x, tsaddiyq (H6662) 7x, rasha (H7563) 6x
- **LXX mapping:** dikaioo (G1344) 21x, dikaios (G1342) 22x, krino (G2919) 8x

**All H6663 translations from search_strongs.py:** "be justified" (4x), "righteous" (2x), "justify" (2x), "just" (2x), "be cleansed" (1x — ONLY Dan 8:14), "to righteousness" (1x — Dan 12:3), plus 33 other unique phrase renderings all within the justify/righteous/just semantic range. **The "cleansed" translation in Dan 8:14 is unique among all 54 occurrences** — every other rendering stays within the righteous/justify/just domain.

#### tsedeq (noun, masculine) — H6664
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H6664-tsedeq.md`
- **Occurrences:** 112 unique verses (119 tokens) in 12 books
- **Primary meaning:** righteousness as objective standard/norm, justice, equity
- **Key translations:** "righteousness" ~58%, "just/justice" ~11%
- **Book concentration:** Psalms 44%, Isaiah 22%
- **Semantic categories:** Equitable legal standard (weights, measures, judicial process), God's character and throne, personal moral uprightness, God's commandments as righteousness, tsedeq-mishpat couplet, messianic/eschatological righteousness
- **Key verse:** Dan 9:24 — "to bring in everlasting righteousness (tsedeq olamim)"
- **LXX mapping:** dikaiosyne (G1343) 81x (PMI 26.71 — extremely strong), dikaios (G1342) 23x

#### tsedaqah (noun, feminine) — H6666
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H6666-tsedaqah.md`
- **Occurrences:** 150 unique verses (165 tokens) in 22 books
- **Primary meaning:** righteousness as personal quality or concrete act, justice
- **Key translations:** "righteousness" ~80%, "justice" ~8%
- **Distinction from tsedeq:** tsedaqah tends toward concrete righteous acts or personal quality; tsedeq is the abstract standard or norm
- **Key collocations:** mishpat (H4941) 46x hendiadys, asah (H6213) "do/execute righteousness" 42x
- **Notable:** Gen 15:6 uses tsedaqah — the first occurrence in the OT is in the imputation context
- **LXX mapping:** dikaiosyne (G1343) 129x (86%), eleemosyne (G1654) 10x (7% — source of later "almsgiving" meaning)

#### tsaddiyq (adjective) — H6662
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H6662-tsaddiyq.md`
- **Occurrences:** 197 unique verses (210 tokens) in 25 books
- **Primary meaning:** just, righteous (person)
- **Key translations:** "righteous" ~77%, "just" ~22%

### Greek dik- Family (8 words, 234+ NT occurrences)

**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1342-dikaios.md`
- **Occurrences:** 76 unique verses (81 tokens) in 20 NT books

#### dikaiosyne (noun) — G1343

#### dikaioo (verb) — G1344
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1344-dikaioo.md`
- **Occurrences:** 36 unique verses (48 tokens) in 10 books
- **Primary meaning:** to justify, declare righteous, acquit, vindicate
- **Louw-Nida domain:** 56.005 — Courts and Legal Procedures: Judge, Condemn, Acquit
- **Voice distribution:** Passive ~30x (divine passive — sinner receives verdict from God), Active ~9x (God or person as justifier)
- **Tense distribution:** Aorist ~17x (completed definitive act), Present ~12x (ongoing principle), Future ~5x (eschatological verdict)
- **All G1344 translations from search_strongs.py:** "justified" (9x, 18.8%), "is justified" (5x, 10.4%), "be justified" (3x, 6.2%), "are justified" (2x, 4.2%), "is freed" (1x — Rom 6:7 only), "let him be righteous" (1x — Rev 22:11)
- **Hebrew source:** tsadaq (H6663) PMI 8.73, shaphat (H8199) PMI 5.32, zakah (H2135) PMI 8.25

#### dikaiosis (noun) — G1347
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1347-dikaiosis.md`
- **Occurrences:** 2 (Rom 4:25 and Rom 5:18 ONLY)
- **Primary meaning:** justification, acquittal
- **Translation:** 100% "justification"
- **Key feature:** Always paired with paraptoma (transgression/offence). Antithetical to katakrima (G2631, condemnation) in Rom 5:18.
- **Distinctly Pauline:** Does not appear in LXX. A term Paul coined or borrowed for the specific act/process of being declared righteous.
- **-sis vs. -ma suffix:** dikaiosis = the act/process of justifying; dikaioma = the result/product (righteous deed, decree)

#### dikaioma (noun) — G1345
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\G1345-dikaioma.md`
- **Occurrences:** 10 in NT
- **Three senses:** (1) Righteous regulation/ordinance (Luk 1:6, Heb 9:1,10), (2) Righteous requirement of the law (Rom 2:26, 8:4), (3) Righteous act with justifying effect (Rom 5:16,18), (4) Righteous deeds of believers (Rev 19:8)
- **Critical for garment imagery:** Rev 19:8 uses the PLURAL dikaiomata = "righteous acts of the saints" — the fine linen is composed of actual deeds, not abstract status

### Covenant and Legal Vocabulary

#### checed (covenant loyalty) — H2617
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H2617-checed.md` (newly generated)
241 occurrences. Central to the relational dimension of righteousness. Dominant in Psalms (53%). Paired with emeth (H571, truth) 33x as hendiadys. God's covenant loyalty is the ground of righteous relationship. Key: Mic 6:8 — "love checed"; Hos 6:6 — "I desired checed, not sacrifice."

#### riyb (to contend/bring lawsuit) — H7378
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H7378-riyb.md` (newly generated)
59 unique verses. The courtroom mechanism behind God's vindication. Semantic categories: divine covenant lawsuit (God as plaintiff — Mic 6:1, Jer 2:9), God as advocate (Jer 50:34, Lam 3:58), petition for God to plead (Psa 35:1). LXX: krino (G2919) 23x, dike (G1349) 6x.

#### shaphat (to judge/govern) — H8199
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H8199-shaphat.md` (newly generated)
182 unique verses. The full judicial act — hearing, deliberating, pronouncing sentence. Distinct from riyb (bringing the case) and diyn (adjudicating). Key collocation: tsedeq (H6664) 19x — "judge with righteousness." LXX: krino (G2919) 105x, dikaioo (G1344) 9x — shaphat sometimes maps to dikaioo when judgment shades into vindication.

#### mishpat (judgment/verdict) — H4941
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\word-studies\H4941-mishpat.md`
406 unique verses. The derived noun from shaphat. Co-occurs with tsedaqah 46x and tsedeq 30x as hendiadys for comprehensive justice. The judicial process and its result — the standard against which all righteousness is measured.

### Grammar References

#### Niphal Stem
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\grammar-reference\hebrew\stem-niphal.md`
The N-stem: simple passive, reflexive, or tolerative. Dan 8:14's nitsdaq is Niphal Perfect 3ms — the sanctuary receives vindication passively. The only Niphal of tsadaq in the OT.

#### Daniel 8:14 Grammar Analysis
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\grammar-reference\passages\dan-8-14.md`
Word-by-word: venitsdaq qodesh = "and [the] holy place shall be vindicated." Niphal Perfect with waw = prophetic perfect (future certainty). KJV "cleansed" is from the Vulgate; the Hebrew root is tsadaq (righteous/vindicated), not taher (ritually clean).

#### Divine Passive
**Source:** `D:\bible\bible-studies\grammar-reference\greek\divine-passive.md`
Passive voice with God as implied agent. Directly relevant to dikaioo's passive voice distribution (~30 of 39 forms are passive): justification is something received from God, not achieved by the sinner. Rev 19:8's edothe ("it was granted") is the same divine passive pattern — the wedding garment of righteous acts is divinely granted.
