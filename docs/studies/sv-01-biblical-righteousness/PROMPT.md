# Bible Study: Biblical Righteousness — Vocabulary Foundation for the Vindication Verdict

## Question
What does the Bible mean by righteousness? What are the Hebrew words (tsadaq H6663, tsedeq H6664, tsedaqah H6666, tsaddiyq H6662) and Greek words (dikaios G1342, dikaiosyne G1343, dikaioo G1344, dikaiosis G1347)? How is righteousness received, maintained, and lost? Cover forensic (declared), relational (covenant faithfulness), and character (conduct) dimensions. This study establishes the vocabulary that culminates in Daniel 8:14's nitsdaq — the learner must understand that tsadaq is courtroom language before encountering it in the prophetic verdict.

## Prior Research Summary

**From prior study (biblical-righteousness):**
- The tsadaq root produces four major words: tsaddiyq (H6662, 210x), tsedaqah (H6666, 165x), tsedeq (H6664, 119x), tsadaq verb (H6663, 41x) — over 530 OT occurrences total
- Verb stem variations encode dimensions: Qal (be righteous, 22x), Hiphil (declare righteous/causative, 12x — includes Isa 53:11 and Exo 23:7), Piel (comparative, 5x), Niphal (Dan 8:14 only — passive verdict), Hithpael (Gen 44:16, justify oneself)
- Greek dik- family: dikaios (G1342, 81x), dikaiosyne (G1343, 92x), dikaioo (G1344, 40x), dikaioma (G1345, 10x), dikaiosis (G1347, 2x), dikaiokrisia (G1341, hapax Rom 2:5)
- LXX bridge: tsaddiyq → dikaios PMI 32.40 (extremely tight correspondence)
- dikaioo voice distribution: ~30 passive (divine passive), ~9 active — justification is received, not achieved
- Righteousness obtained by faith through imputation (Gen 15:6 → Rom 4:3). Maintained by walking in the Spirit (Rom 8:1-4), confession (1 John 1:7-9), faith working through love (Gal 5:6). Lost through deliberate, sustained turning (Eze 18:24, Heb 10:26-29, 2 Pet 2:20-22).
- Sanctuary bridge: Lev 16 = kaphar → taher; Dan 9:24 = kaphar → tsedeq olamim; Zech 3 = filthy garments removed + festival robes given; Rom 3:25-26 = hilasterion → dikaios kai dikaiunta
- Garment imagery: Job 29:14 (conduct), Isa 61:10 (God provides), Zech 3:3-5 (cleansing + investiture), Matt 22:11-14 (required), Rev 7:14 (washed), Rev 19:8 (dikaiomata = righteous acts, granted)

**From sanc-19 (vindication-courtroom):**
- Dan 8:14's nitsdaq uses courtroom vocabulary (tsadaq), not ritual vocabulary (taher/kaphar) — THE foundational lexical observation
- Four dimensions of vindication: God's Law, God's People, God's Plan, God's Character
- The riyb (H7378, lawsuit) pattern: God initiates His own trial (Mic 6:1-2), invites cross-examination (Isa 43:26), asks to be evaluated (Isa 5:3-4)
- Isa 53:11: three tsadaq forms converge — righteous servant IS righteous, DECLARES many righteous, BY BEARING iniquities

**From sanc-25 (Daniel 8:14 vindicated):**
- nitsdaq = Niphal Perfect of tsadaq (H6663) — the ONLY Niphal in the entire OT
- KJV's "cleansed" is unique among 54 occurrences — anomaly from Vulgate influence
- OG LXX uses dikaiothesatai (justified); Theodotion uses katharisthesetai (cleansed)
- kaphar → tsadaq progression across four texts: Lev 16:30 → Dan 9:24 → Isa 53:11 → Rom 3:25-26 → Dan 8:14

**From atone-16 (justification by faith):**
- dikaioo is courtroom vocabulary, Louw-Nida domain 56.005 — Courts: Judge, Condemn, Acquit
- Antonym pair katakrima/dikaiosis (G2631/G1347) in Rom 5:18
- Four atonement frameworks each contribute a dimension to justification

**External corpus leads (EGW, Bohr) — for biblical verification:**
- EGW claims imputed righteousness "means holiness, uprightness, purity" (TSB 134.1) and the righteousness garment = actual character, not just legal covering
- EGW connects Dan 9:24 "everlasting righteousness" to Christ's imputed righteousness clothing the believer (1SM 396.1)
- EGW distinguishes "active obedience of Christ" clothing the believer with righteousness meeting law's demands (SD 240.4)
- Bohr claims tsadaq is "not connected with the ritual of the sanctuary but rather is a legal term used in the context of a court of law" (GPOT2V1 p. 442)
- Bohr argues Dan 7:9-14 (judicial) and Dan 8:14 (sanctuary) describe the same event from different angles
