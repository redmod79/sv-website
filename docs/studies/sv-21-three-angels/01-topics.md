# Topics Research

## Question
What is the significance of the three angels' messages in Revelation 14:6-12? How does the first angel's "the hour of his judgment (krisis) is come (elthen, aorist)" connect to Daniel 8:14's vindication and Dan 7:9-10? Why does the first angel invoke creation language (Rev 14:7 quoting Exo 20:11)? How do the vindication declarations (Rev 15:3, 16:7, 19:2) trace the verdict? What does Rev 14:12 reveal about the remnant?

## Related Topics Discovered

### GLORIFYING GOD — for judgments
The connection between glorifying God and judgment (ISA 25:3; EZK 28:22; REV 14:7) places the first angel's command to "give glory to him" directly within the context of divine judgment. This is not generic praise but a response to the announcement that "the hour of his judgment is come."

### PRAISE — in heaven
The heavenly praise passages form a progression: Rev 4:8-11 (creation praise) → 5:9-14 (redemption praise) → 7:9-12 (victory praise) → 14:2,3 (new song) → 15:3,4 (vindication praise) → 19:1-7 (completed vindication praise). The vindication declarations sit within this heavenly worship trajectory.
