## Prior Studies

### From Series Integration List

**sv-15-2300-calculation:**
- Question: How does Daniel 8-9 establish the 2300/70-week relationship? 457 BC + 2300 = 1844.
- The hapax verb chathak (H2852) proves the 70 weeks are "cut from" the 2300 — sharing the same starting point (457 BC)
- 457 BC + 2300 = 1844 AD. The 1335 independently converges on the same terminus from a different starting point (508 + 1335 = 1843)
- The event at the terminus is nitsdaq qodesh — sanctuary vindicated — the beginning of the antitypical DOA
- The DOA calendar correspondence: 2300 years from autumn 457 BC end in autumn 1844, at the season of the DOA (10th of Tishri)
- Rev 14:7 announces this event: "the hour of his judgment is come" — aorist erchomai treating the arrival as accomplished fact
- Key verse chain: Dan 8:14 → Dan 7:9-10 → Rev 14:7

**sv-17-judgment-antitypical-doa:**
- Question: How does Dan 7:9-10 correspond to Lev 16? Five parallels tested.
- The parallel is structural, not lexical. Vocabulary differs at every transition point except the cloud cognate
- The kaphar-to-tsadaq linguistic progression is the strongest bridge: Lev 16:30 (kaphar→taher) → Dan 9:24 (kaphar→tsedeq) → Isa 53:11 (sin-bearing→yatsdiq) → Rom 3:25-26 (hilasterion→dikaios) → Dan 8:14 (nitsdaq)
- Dan 9:24 is the ONLY verse where DOA vocabulary (kaphar) and vindication vocabulary (tsedeq) appear together
- Three-phase judgment: Phase A pre-advent (Dan 7:9-10; Rev 14:7), Phase B millennial (Rev 20:4), Phase C post-millennial (Rev 20:11-15)
- The DOA parallels map specifically to Phase A — the pre-advent judgment that Rev 14:7 announces

**sv-18-vindication-meaning:**
- Question: What does nitsdaq qodesh mean? Climax of the series.
- nitsdaq qodesh = "the sanctuary shall be vindicated" — forensic verdict, not ritual cleansing
- The Niphal passive declares God Himself vindicates His sanctuary
- The tinnaten-nitsdaq divine passive frame holds Dan 8: what God permitted (tinnaten, 8:12), God sets right (nitsdaq, 8:14)
- Four-fold attack mapped to four-fold vindication:
  1. Host trampled → judgment given TO saints (Dan 7:22; Rom 8:33)
  2. Prince challenged → "just and true are thy ways" (Rev 15:3; 19:2)
  3. Tamid removed / sanctuary cast down → heavenly ministry recognized (Heb 8:1-2; Rev 16:7 — altar speaks)
  4. Truth cast down → everlasting righteousness brought in (Dan 9:24; Psa 119:142)
- Five beneficiaries: God's people, God's character, God's ministry, God's truth, the watching universe
- The Revelation vindication declarations (Rev 15:3, 16:7, 19:1-2) all use the dikaios + alethinos pair — Greek equivalents of tsedeq + emeth
- Rev 16:7: the altar itself speaks the verdict — closest NT parallel to nitsdaq qodesh
- Rev 19:1-2: both verbs aorist (ekrinen, exedikesen) — vindication completed
- The matsdiqey-nitsdaq connection: Dan 12:3's maskilim "turn many to righteousness" (Hiphil of tsadaq) — same root as nitsdaq (Niphal). The maskilim participate in vindication on earth.

**sv-20-rev-10-unsealing:**
- Question: What is the "little book open" in Revelation 10?
- Five converging evidence lines identify it as Daniel's sealed prophecy, now permanently opened
- The oath scene (Rev 10:5-6) replicates five elements of Dan 12:5-7 with one decisive content inversion: duration → termination
- chronos ouketi estai = "time shall be no longer" — the cessation of prophetic time periods
- The sweet-then-bitter experience: prophetic message received with joy whose implications prove painful
- Rev 10:11: "thou must prophesy again before many peoples, nations, tongues, and kings" — the recommission
- This recommission bridges directly into the three angels' messages as the CONTENT of that renewed prophecy
- The four-fold audience formula in Rev 10:11 uses Danielic vocabulary (Dan 7:14) and uniquely substitutes "kings" for "tribes"

**sv-09-counterfeit-system:**
- Question: What specifically does the little horn replace the tamid with?
- The horn does not merely remove — it substitutes. Dan 11:31 and 12:11 have explicit "take away AND place/set up" language
- Four attacks mapped to four counterfeits:
  1. Persecution replaces divine protection
  2. Human authority replaces the Prince's role (2 Thess 2:4)
  3. Earthly priesthood replaces Christ's heavenly ministry (Heb 10:11-12 standing vs. sitting)
  4. Changed times and laws replace God's truth (Dan 7:25)
- The Dan 2:21/7:25 shna contrast: God actually changes times (participle — ongoing reality); the horn merely intends to (infinitive — aspiration). Same verb, different grammatical form.
- The emeth-dath-torah equation: emeth (Dan 8:12) = dath (Dan 7:25) = torah (Psa 119:142)
- nitsdaq reverses each counterfeit: persecution→acquittal, usurped authority→God declared just, counterfeit priesthood→heavenly ministry recognized, changed law→truth restored
