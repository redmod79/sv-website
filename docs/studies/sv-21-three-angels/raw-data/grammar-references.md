## Key Grammatical Observations for This Study

### The Three Aorist Imperatives of Rev 14:7
phobethete + dote + proskynesate — three decisive commands forming the substance of the first angel's message. The aorist aspect treats each as a specific act of decision, not a gradual process. "Fear!" "Give!" "Worship!" — these demand response.

### The Aorist elthen as Announcement of Accomplished Fact
Rev 14:7 elthen (erchomai, aorist active indicative) — the judgment "has come." This is not a prediction ("will come") or a process ("is coming") but an announcement of accomplished reality. The judgment exists as an ongoing fact from the moment of this announcement.

### The Vindication Verb Tense Progression
| Passage | Verb | Tense | Aspect |
|---------|------|-------|--------|
| Rev 14:7 | elthen (come) | Aorist | Judgment has arrived |
| Rev 15:3 | — (nominal: just and true are thy ways) | Present | Ongoing character affirmation |
| Rev 15:4 | ephanerothesan (were manifested) | Aorist Passive | Judgments revealed as completed action |
| Rev 16:5 | ekrinas (you judged) | Aorist | Specific completed acts of judgment |
| Rev 16:7 | — (nominal: true and righteous thy judgments) | Present | Ongoing affirmation |
| Rev 19:2 | ekrinen (he judged) + exedikesen (he avenged) | Aorist + Aorist | Both judgment AND vindication completed |

The progression: announcement (aorist — arrived) → character affirmation (present — ongoing) → specific acts (aorist — completed) → full vindication (double aorist — both judged and avenged).

### The legon Parallel Structure
Rev 14:6-7: allon aggelon... legon... (another angel... saying...)
Rev 14:8: allos aggelos... legon... (another angel... saying...)
Rev 14:9: allos aggelos tritos... legon... (another angel, a third... saying...)

The repetition of legon (present active participle) unifies all three messages under the same grammatical structure. The present tense suggests ongoing proclamation — these are not one-time announcements but continuing messages.
