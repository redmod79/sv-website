# Word Studies

## From Word Studies Library

### עֶרֶב (ereb) — H6153 (evening)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6153-ereb.md`

- **Core:** Masculine noun, 125 unique verses (132 tokens), from H6150 arab "to grow dusky"
- **Definition:** Dusk — the transition period as daylight fades; marks the beginning of the new day in Hebrew reckoning
- **Translation:** ~97% rendered "even/evening" in KJV; 1 instance as "days" (Dan 8:14)
- **Key distribution:** Leviticus 29x (purity law), Genesis 13x (creation + patriarchal), Daniel 3x (8:14, 8:26 — erev boqer pair)
- **Collocations:** boqer (H1242) 44x = 35% of all ereb verses; tame (H2930) 30x = "unclean until the even"
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Creation day frame, (2) Ritual purity deadline, (3) Passover timing, (4) Tamid sacrifice, (5) Day's end narrative, (6) Figurative/poetic
- **LXX:** ἑσπέρα (hespera, G2073) is the primary equivalent (96 co-occurrences)
- **For this study:** Ereb is the standard sanctuary-register word for the onset of evening. Its pairing with boqer in Gen 1:5 establishes the day frame; its pairing in Dan 8:14 echoes that frame.

### בֹּקֶר (boqer) — H1242 (morning)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1242-boqer.md`

- **Core:** Masculine noun, 205 occurrences, from baqar "to seek out, inspect" — morning as the time of visibility/inspection
- **Translation:** 85%+ rendered "morning"/"the morning"/"in the morning"
- **Key distribution:** Exodus 35x (tamid regulations + manna), Genesis 19x (creation + patriarchal), Daniel 7x (including 8:14, 8:26)
- **Collocations:** ereb (H6153) ~60x — strongest collocation; shakam (H7925) ~40x "rose early in the morning"
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Creation day formula, (2) Temporal marker, (3) Tamid offering, (4) Divine deliverance/hope at dawn, (5) Judgment and justice at morning, (6) Prophetic time formulas, (7) Transience
- **Root insight:** From baqar "to inspect" — morning as when hidden things become visible. Connects to justice/judgment semantics (Jer 21:12 "Execute judgment in the morning"; Zeph 3:5 "every morning he brings his judgment to light").
- **LXX:** πρωΐ (proi, G4404) is primary equivalent (145 co-occurrences)
- **For this study:** Boqer is the sanctuary-register morning word. Its root connection to "inspection" resonates with the forensic/judicial context of Dan 8:14 (nitsdaq = vindication/judgment).

### יוֹם (yowm) — H3117 (day)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3117-yowm.md`

- **Core:** Masculine noun, 1926 unique verses (2742 tokens), from unused root "to be hot"
- **Translation:** ~47.6% "day/days," plus "this day," "in the day," etc.
- **Key distribution:** Universal across 39 OT books; Daniel 20x (5.6 per 100 verses)
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Literal calendar day, (2) Historical era, (3) Appointed/holy day, (4) Day of the LORD, (5) Lifetime span, (6) Adverbial idioms
- **LXX:** ἡμέρα (hemera, G2250) ~77% of yowm occurrences — strongest Hebrew→Greek bridge by volume
- **For this study:** Yowm is the STANDARD word for "day" that Daniel deliberately avoided in 8:14. He uses yamim freely in Dan 12:11 ("1290 yamim") and 12:12 ("1335 yamim"). The switch from yamim to erev boqer in 8:14 is a vocabulary choice, not a limitation.

### תָּמִיד (tamid) — H8548 (continual)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md`

- **Core:** Masculine noun (used attributively/adverbially), 104 occurrences
- **Definition:** Continuance as indefinite extension — regular, unbroken recurrence
- **Translation:** 50.5% "continually," 16.2% "the continual," 6.7% "the daily"
- **Key distribution:** Psalms 23x, Numbers 20x, Daniel 5x (used substantively as "the daily")
- **Daniel's unique usage:** In Daniel, tamid stands alone as a noun — "the tamid" (the continual service) — without qualifying noun. KJV supplies [sacrifice] in italics.
- **Collocations:** olah (H5930) 26x "continual burnt offering"; boqer (H1242) 7x "morning... continual burnt offering"
- **For this study:** The tamid is the explicit context of the question in Dan 8:13. The answer uses erev boqer — the same evening-morning cycle that defines one tamid period. The counting unit matches the subject of the question.

### צָדַק (tsadaq) — H6663 (to be just/vindicated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`

- **Core:** Verb, 41 occurrences across 5 stems (Qal 22, Hiphil 12, Piel 5, Niphal 1, Hithpael 1)
- **The Niphal hapax:** Dan 8:14 is the ONLY Niphal occurrence in the entire OT — "be vindicated/justified"
- **Stem distribution insight:** Qal = "be righteous" (intrinsic state); Hiphil = "declare righteous" (judicial act); Niphal = "be vindicated" (passive reception of the verdict)
- **Book concentration:** Job 23x (56% of all occurrences) — the sustained exploration of whether a mortal can be righteous before God
- **Key contrast words:** taher (H2891, "cleanse") — ritual/physical purity; naqah (H5352, "acquit") — innocence/exemption from punishment; tsadaq = moral/forensic rightness. KJV translates Dan 8:14's Niphal as "cleansed" but the Hebrew root is judicial, not ritual.
- **For this study:** Daniel chose tsadaq (vindication) over taher (cleansing). This is a vocabulary choice parallel to choosing erev boqer over yamim. Both choices are deliberate, non-standard, and theologically loaded.

### חָתַם (chatham) — H2856 (to seal)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md`

- **Core:** Verb, 26 tokens across 4 stems (Qal 22, Niphal 2, Piel 1, Hiphil 1)
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Legal/documentary sealing, (2) Divine sealing of hidden things, (3) Prophetic sealing of revelation, (4) Physical closure
- **Key verses:** Dan 9:24 "seal up the vision and prophecy"; Dan 12:4 "shut up the words, and seal the book"
- **Collocations:** sepher (H5612) 8x "seal a book/document"; kathab (H3789) 7x "write then seal"
- **For this study:** The sealing command in Dan 8:26 (satam) and 12:4 (satam + chatham) frames the encoding-act hypothesis. Erev boqer is the visible evidence that the sealing was executed — the temporal element was encoded in a non-standard unit.

### מוֹעֵד (moed) — H4150 (appointed time)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4150-moed.md`

- **Core:** 229 occurrences; dominant meaning "tabernacle of the congregation" (150x, 65.5%)
- **As appointed time:** Gen 1:14 "let them be for signs, and for seasons (moadim)"; Dan 8:19 "at the time appointed the end shall be"
- **For this study:** Gabriel uses moed in Dan 8:19 ("at the moed the end shall be") — linking the 2300 erev boqer to creation's appointed-time system (Gen 1:14 uses moed for the celestial timekeeping function). The erev boqer unit and the moed framing both echo Genesis 1.

### עֵת (eth) — H6256 (time)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6256-eth.md`

- **Core:** Feminine noun, 258 unique verses; from uncertain root related to Aramaic iddan
- **Eschatological usage:** Dan 8:17 "at the eth-qets shall be the vision"; Dan 12:1 "a eth of trouble"
- **For this study:** Gabriel uses eth ha-qets ("time of the end") in Dan 8:17,19 alongside the erev boqer prophecy. The prophetic time-system has its own vocabulary: eth for the eschatological moment, erev boqer for the counting unit, moed for the appointed-time framework.

### חָזוֹן (chazon) — H2377 (vision)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2377-chazon.md`

- **Core:** 37 occurrences; Daniel 12x (32% of all), from chazah "to perceive mentally"
- **For this study:** Dan 8:26 commands Daniel to "seal up the chazon" — the broad vision-prophecy. The mar'eh of the evening-and-morning is authenticated as truth (emet) while the chazon is sealed. Erev boqer belongs to the mar'eh that is certified; its encoding serves the sealing of the chazon.

### מַרְאֶה (mareh) — H4758 (appearance/vision)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md`

- **Core:** 82 unique verses; from ra'ah "to see" — the visual form of what is perceived
- **Daniel's usage:** 11 occurrences in Daniel; distinct from chazon — mar'eh = specific visible element; chazon = broad prophetic panorama
- **For this study:** Dan 8:26 uses BOTH words: "the mar'eh of the evening and the morning is true" (authentication of the specific time element) → "seal up the chazon" (concealment of the whole vision). The two-term distribution confirms that erev boqer is the named content of the mar'eh.

### שַׁחַר (shachar) — H7837 (dawn) — THE WORD DANIEL DID NOT CHOOSE FOR "MORNING"
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7837-shachar.md`

- **Core:** Masculine noun, 24 unique verses across 13 books
- **Definition:** Dawn — specifically the first light, the break of day; from root meaning "to seek early"
- **Translation:** Always appears in construct phrases — "breaking of the day," "wings of the morning," "son of the morning"
- **Key distribution:** Poetic and prophetic concentration — Psalms 4x, Isaiah 4x, Job 3x, Genesis 3x. Absent from Torah after Genesis.
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Literal dawn time-marker, (2) Dawn as boundary of endurance, (3) Cosmic/poetic dawn, (4) Simile for certainty, (5) Mythological title ("son of the morning" Isa 14:12)
- **vs. boqer:** Shachar = the specific moment of first light; boqer = the full morning period. Shachar is poetic/cosmic; boqer is cultic/liturgical.
- **For this study:** Daniel chose boqer (the sanctuary-register morning word) over shachar (the poetic/cosmic dawn word). If Daniel merely wanted to say "daybreak" poetically, shachar was available. The choice of boqer ties the unit to the tamid-service vocabulary, not to natural-world dawn imagery.

### לַיִל (layil) — H3915 (night) — THE WORD DANIEL DID NOT CHOOSE FOR "EVENING"
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3915-layil.md`

- **Core:** Masculine noun, 223 unique verses (241 tokens) across 30 books
- **Definition:** Night; properly "a twist away of the light"
- **Translation:** 98% rendered "night" in various prepositional forms
- **Key distribution:** Broadly distributed — Psalms 27x, Genesis 25x, Job 17x. **Absent from Daniel entirely** (only the Aramaic cognate leyleya appears in Dan 2:19, 5:30, 7:2, 7:7).
- **Semantic categories:** (1) Creation ordinance, (2) Day-and-night merism, (3) Divine guidance at night, (4) Military/covert activity, (5) Suffering/restlessness, (6) Night visions, (7) Figurative darkness
- **Collocations:** yowm (H3117) 56x (25%) — the "day and night" merism
- **vs. ereb:** Layil = the full darkness period; ereb = the onset of dusk only. Ereb is a transitional boundary-marker; layil is the extended darkness.
- **For this study:** Daniel chose ereb (the transitional sanctuary-register word) over layil (the general darkness-period word). Ereb is the word used in Lev 11:24 "unclean until the even" and Exo 29:39 "offer at even" — it is sanctuary vocabulary. Layil is natural-world vocabulary for the night period. The choice confirms that erev boqer operates in the cultic/liturgical register, not the natural/astronomical register.

### עָרַב (arab) — H6150 (to grow dark)
**Source:** Fresh lexicon lookup

- **Definition:** Primitive root (identical with through the idea of covering with a texture); to grow dusky at sundown
- **Occurrences:** 3 (Judges 19:9, 1 Samuel 17:16, Isaiah 24:11)
- **Significance:** The verbal root of ereb. Extremely rare (3x), all narrative — the evening as the light "growing dusky." The noun ereb (125x) vastly outnumbers its verbal root, confirming ereb has fully lexicalized as a standard time-term independent of the verb.

### טָהֵר (taher) — H2891 (to be clean/pure)
**Source:** Fresh lexicon lookup

- **Definition:** To be bright; by implication to be pure (physically sound, clear; Levitically uncontaminated; morally innocent or holy)
- **Occurrences:** 94 — concentrated in Leviticus (ritual cleansing), 2 Chronicles (temple cleansing), Ezekiel (land cleansing)
- **Key verse:** Lev 16:30 "on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to **cleanse** (taher) you" — the DOA purification verb
- **For this study:** This is the word Daniel DID NOT choose for Dan 8:14. The DOA vocabulary uses taher for the annual cleansing result. Daniel chose tsadaq (vindication) instead — elevating the vocabulary from ritual purification to forensic declaration.

### נָקָה (naqah) — H5352 (to be clean/innocent)
**Source:** Fresh lexicon lookup

- **Definition:** To be or make clean; by implication to be bare, extirpated
- **Occurrences:** 44 — concentrated in Proverbs, Jeremiah, Numbers
- **Key translations:** "acquit," "be blameless," "be guiltless," "be innocent," "be unpunished"
- **For this study:** Another cleaning/innocence verb Daniel did not choose. Naqah = declare innocent/exempt from punishment; tsadaq = declare positively righteous. Dan 8:14's verdict is not merely acquittal or innocence — it is active vindication.

## Grammatical Comparison Table: Evening-Morning Constructions

This table synthesizes the Hebrew parsing data from the grammar reference library and fresh parser runs.

| Feature | Dan 8:14 | Gen 1:5 | Lev 23:32 | Exo 27:21 | Exo 29:39 |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Hebrew** | עֶרֶב בֹּקֶר | וַיְהִי עֶרֶב וַיְהִי בֹקֶר | מֵעֶרֶב עַד עֶרֶב | מֵעֶרֶב עַד בֹּקֶר | בַבֹּקֶר...בֵּין הָעַרְבָּיִם |
| **Ereb state** | Noun.ms.Abs | Noun.ms.Abs | Noun.ms.Abs (×3) | Noun.ms.Abs | Noun.md.Abs (dual form) |
| **Boqer state** | Noun.ms.Abs | Noun.ms.Abs | (absent) | Noun.ms.Abs | Noun.ms.Abs |
| **Connection** | **Asyndetic** (no particle) | **Syndetic** (each has its own וַיְהִי) | **Prepositional** (מִן...עַד) | **Prepositional** (מִן...עַד) | **Prepositional** (בְּ...בֵּין) |
| **Articles** | None | None | הַ on third ereb only (בָּעֶרֶב) | None | הַ on boqer and arbayim |
| **Prepositions** | עַד governs compound | None on ereb/boqer | מִן, עַד | מִן, עַד | בְּ, בֵּין |

| **Word order** | evening → morning | evening → morning | evening → evening | evening → morning | morning → evening |
| **Function** | **Compound counting unit** | **Narrative day-boundary** | **Boundary markers** (from X to X) | **Duration** (from X to X) | **Time specification** (at X...at X) |
| **Type of construction** | UNIT (one thing counted) | FORMULA (two events narrated) | BOUNDARY (two endpoints of a span) | DURATION (two endpoints of a span) | SCHEDULE (two separate time-slots) |

**Key findings from the table:**

1. **Dan 8:14 is grammatically unique among all five constructions.** It is the only one that uses asyndetic juxtaposition — two bare nouns with no conjunction, no article, no preposition, no verb between them. Every other construction uses some grammatical particle to relate the nouns.

2. **Word order matches creation, not tamid.** Dan 8:14 says evening-morning (ereb boqer); Gen 1:5 says evening-morning (ereb... boqer). The tamid passages (Exo 29:39, Num 28:4) say morning-evening (boqer... arbayim). Daniel's word order echoes Genesis, not the daily sacrifice.

3. **The asyndetic compression transforms the pair from a description to a unit-noun.** In Genesis 1, "evening" and "morning" are subjects of two separate clauses. In Daniel 8:14, they are compressed into a single undivided compound that a cardinal number counts. This is a deliberate grammatical transformation.

4. **Dan 8:26 restates the compound in syndetic form** with articles: הָעֶרֶב וְהַבֹּקֶר. The shift from asyndetic/indefinite (8:14) to syndetic/definite (8:26) is the expected grammatical move from counting-unit to anaphoric reference.

Sources: Grammar reference entries `passages/dan-8-14.md`, `passages/dan-8-26.md`, `passages/gen-1-5-creation-day-formula.md`, `hebrew/asyndetic-noun-pair.md`; fresh parser output for all five comparison verses.
