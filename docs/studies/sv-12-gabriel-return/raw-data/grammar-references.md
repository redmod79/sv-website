## Passage Analyses Read

### dan-8-15-16.md (Gabriel Commissioned)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-15-16.md`

Key findings:
1. wayhî + bə-infinitive-construct temporal frame — canonical Danielic vision-framing pattern
2. Piel wayyiqtol 1cs with paragogic-ה — deliberate pursuit of understanding (not passive reception)
3. mar'eh/chazon lexical switch: 8:15 uses chazon (whole vision); 8:16 switches to mar'eh (specific element). Gabriel is commissioned for the mar'eh, not the chazon.
4. Hiphil imperative haben with demonstrative hallaz — "make this one understand the mar'eh"
5. The haben of 8:16 answers Daniel's biqqesha binah of 8:15 — the voice hears Daniel's seeking and dispatches an interpreter
6. The indefinite construct ke-mar'eh gaber (8:15) echoes Gabriel's name (geber + el)

### dan-9-22-23.md (Gabriel's Resumption)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-9-22-23.md`

Key findings:
1. wayyaben (Hiphil wayq 3ms of biyn) — Gabriel's first action is to CAUSE understanding (same root/stem as 8:16 commission)
2. lehaskilkha binah — Hiphil inf.con of sakal + 2ms suffix + noun binah: two understanding-roots (sakal + biyn) yoked together
3. chamudoth attah — Daniel is "greatly beloved" — the ground for receiving revelation
4. Paired imperatives ubin (Qal) + wehaben (Hiphil): "understand the word AND gain insight into the mar'eh"
5. The stem shift Qal→Hiphil in one breath: Qal = receptive (take in the word); Hiphil = productive (work the vision)
6. wehaben bammar'eh directly reconnects to 8:16's haben...et-hammar'eh and 8:27's ein mebiyn

### dan-9-2-jeremiah-reading.md (Daniel's Independent Study)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-9-2-jeremiah-reading.md`

Key findings:
1. binoti (Qal perf 1cs of biyn) — self-generated understanding via reading, NOT angel-mediated
2. basepharim — instrumental bet: "by means of the books" — text-mediated knowledge
3. The genre distinction: sepher (public letter) vs. chazon (sealed vision) determines hermeneutic
4. Dan 9:2 is the calibrating verse for day-year hermeneutics: Daniel reads Jeremiah's literal years as literal years
5. The reading (binoti, 9:2) triggers the prayer (9:3-19), which triggers Gabriel's return (9:20-23)

### dan-8-26.md (Dual Vision Terms)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-26.md`

Key findings:
1. mar'eh ha-ereb vehabboqer — construct chain with ARTICLED absolutes (anaphoric, referring back to 8:14)
2. Contrast with 8:14's ARTICLELESS ereb boqer — numeric counting unit vs. 8:26's titular reference
3. emet hu — verbless nominal clause: "truth is it" — AUTHENTICATION of the mar'eh
4. setom hechazon — imperative to SEAL the chazon (different word, different treatment)
5. Two verbless clauses bracket one imperative: authenticate mar'eh → seal chazon → reason (many days)
6. le-yamim rabbim — temporal prepositional phrase connecting to 10:14's layyamim

### dan-10-1.md (Resolution)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-10-1.md`

Key findings:
1. niglah (Niphal perf 3ms of galah) — "was revealed/uncovered" — divine passive
2. ubiyn et-haddabar — Qal perf 3ms of biyn: COMPLETED understanding (the biyn chain resolves)
3. ubiynah lo bammar'eh — nominal clause: "and understanding to him in the mar'eh"
4. The mar'eh question is ANSWERED at 10:1. The biyn commission from 8:16 is FULFILLED.
5. tsaba gadol ("great warfare") characterizes the content — Daniel understands but grieves

### dan-9-24-27.md (70 Weeks Prophecy)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-9-24-27.md`

Key findings:
1. nechtak (Niphal perf 3ms of chathak H2852) — HAPAX: "are cut off/decreed"
2. Six purpose infinitives: lekalle (Piel — finish transgression), lehatem (Hiphil — seal up sins), lekapper (Piel — atone for iniquity), lehabi (Hiphil — bring in righteousness), lachtom (Qal — seal vision), limshoch (Qal — anoint most holy)
3. tsedeq olamim — construct chain "righteousness of ages" — shares tsadaq root with nitsdaq (8:14)
4. lachtom chazon venabi — authentication mode of chatham, not concealment
5. qodesh qodashim — "holy of holies" — never refers to a person in OT; always the most holy place/objects
