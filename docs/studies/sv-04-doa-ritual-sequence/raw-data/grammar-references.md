### stem-piel.md (D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-piel.md)
- **Definition:** D-stem (doubled middle consonant). Core function: bringing about a state.
- **Functions:** (1) Factitive — causes a state; (2) Resultative — completed result; (3) Declarative-Estimative — declares a state; (4) Denominative — from a noun.
- **Key verbs in Lev 16:**
  - kaphar Piel — cultic-technical "make atonement" (listed as common verb)
  - taher Piel — "pronounce clean" (declarative) or "actively cleanse" (factitive)
  - qadash Piel — "consecrate, make holy" (factitive)
  - kalah Piel — "bring to an end, complete" (resultative)
  - shalach Piel — "send away, dismiss" (intensive of Qal "stretch out")
  - anah Piel — "afflict" (factitive — bring about the state of lowliness)
- **Piel vs Hiphil distinction:** Piel causes a STATE; Hiphil causes an EVENT/ACTION.

### stem-hithpael.md (D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-hithpael.md)
- **Definition:** tD-stem, reflexive of the Piel. Subject performs action on/for itself.
- **Functions:** (1) Direct reflexive; (2) Reciprocal; (3) Iterative/habitual; (4) Middle voice; (5) Estimative/pretend; (6) Denominative reflexive.
- **In Lev 16:21:** hitvaddah (yadah Hithpael) = "confess" — reflexive confession. The subject acknowledges sin over/upon itself (or on the goat's behalf).
- **Key diagnostic (from library):** 2 Chr 29:5 shows Hithpael hitqaddesh "sanctify YOURSELVES" vs Piel qaddesh "sanctify THE HOUSE" — same root, different direction.

### stem-niphal.md (D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-niphal.md)
- **Definition:** N-stem. Passive and reflexive of the Qal.
- **In Lev 23:29:** nikhretah (karath Niphal) = "shall be cut off" — passive, divine agency implied.
- **Theological significance:** The karat penalty formula consistently uses the Niphal, indicating God (not human courts) executes the cutting off.

## Key Grammatical Observations for Lev 16

### Nominal Clauses (verbless) — Chiastic Frame
- **v.4:** bigdei-qodesh hem — "garments of holiness they [are]" (classifying nominal clause)
- **v.31:** shabbat shabbathon hi — "a sabbath of sabbath-rest it [is]" (classifying nominal clause)
These two nominal clauses form an A/A' inclusio around the ritual, marking the beginning and end of the procedure.

### WXYq Construction (v.17)
- vekhol-adam lo yihyeh = W(conj) + X(noun kol-adam) + Y(neg lo) + q(yiqtol yihyeh)
- This is the most grammatically distinctive clause in the chapter.
- The noun-first, negated imperfect construction marks absolute prohibition.

### Piel Dominance
Nearly every major verb in the ritual sequence is Piel:
- kaphar (Piel) — make atonement (16x)
- taher (Piel) — cleanse (v.19, v.30)
- qadash (Piel) — consecrate (v.19)
- kalah (Piel) — complete (v.20)
- shalach (Piel) — send away (v.21,22)
- anah (Piel) — afflict (v.29,31)
The one exception: yadah (Hithpael) — confess (v.21) — the reflexive form.

### Stem Shift in the Penalty Clause (Lev 23:29)
- anah shifts from Piel (active command) to Pual (passive result)
- karath appears as Niphal (passive — divine agent)
Both passives in the same verse create a "divine passive binary": the soul that is not (passively) afflicted shall be (passively) cut off. God is the implied agent in both.
