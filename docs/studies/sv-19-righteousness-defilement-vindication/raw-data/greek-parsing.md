### Rom 3:26
- εἰς τὸ εἶναι αὐτὸν δίκαιον (A-ASM of δίκαιος G1342) — "that he might be just/righteous"
- καὶ δικαιοῦντα (V-PAP-ASM, Pres Act Ptcp of δικαιόω G1344) — "and the justifier/one-declaring-righteous"
- τὸν ἐκ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ — "the one [who is] out of faith of/in Jesus"

Key observation: ἱλαστήριον (hilasterion G2435, mercy seat / propitiation) appears in the SAME sentence as δίκαιον (just) + δικαιοῦντα (justifier). God is simultaneously the propitiation-provider AND the righteous-declarer. This is the NT equivalent of Dan 9:24's kaphar→tsedeq bridge.

δικαιοῦντα is a Present Active Participle (Accusative Singular Masculine) — describing an ongoing characteristic of God: "the one who is [continually] justifying."

## Revelation 19:8
- ἐδόθη (V-API-3S, Aor Pass Ind of δίδωμι G1325) — "it was granted" (divine passive — the giver is God)
- αὐτῇ (Dat Sg Fem) — "to her" (the bride)
- ἵνα περιβάληται (V-2AMS-3S, Aor Mid Subj of περιβάλλω G4016) — "that she might clothe herself"
- βύσσινον λαμπρὸν καθαρόν (A-ASN of βύσσινος G1039, λαμπρός G2986, καθαρός G2513) — "fine linen, bright, clean/pure"
- τὸ γὰρ βύσσινον τὰ δικαιώματα τῶν ἁγίων ἐστίν — "for the fine linen IS the righteous acts (δικαιώματα G1345) of the saints"

Key observation: ἐδόθη (edothe) is Aorist PASSIVE Indicative — divine passive. The fine linen was "granted" to her by God. Yet the fine linen IS (ἐστίν) the δικαιώματα (righteous acts/deeds) of the saints. Paradox: the garment is both divinely given AND constituted by the saints' righteous acts.

δικαιώματα (dikaiomata G1345) — Nominative Plural Neuter. Not δικαιοσύνη (abstract righteousness) but δικαιώματα (concrete righteous acts/ordinances). The plural form emphasizes the individual righteous deeds.

καθαρός (katharos G2513) — "clean/pure" — the LXX equivalent of tahor (H2889). The garment imagery combines BOTH cleansing (katharos) AND righteousness (dikaiomata) in a single image.

## Revelation 7:14
- ἔπλυναν (V-AAI-3P, Aor Act Ind of πλύνω G4150) — "they washed"
- τὰς στολὰς αὐτῶν (Acc Pl Fem of στολή G4749) — "their robes"
- ἐλεύκαναν (V-AAI-3P, Aor Act Ind of λευκαίνω G3021) — "they made white"
- αὐτὰς (Acc Pl Fem) — "them"
- ἐν τῷ αἵματι τοῦ Ἀρνίου (Dat Sg Neut of αἷμα G129) — "in the blood of the Lamb"

Key observation: Two aorist verbs in sequence — "washed" AND "made white." Both are Aorist Active Indicative — completed actions. The paradox: robes made white (λευκαίνω, "to whiten") IN blood (ἐν τῷ αἵματι). Blood normally stains; here it whitens. This inverts the natural order.

πλύνω (G4150) is the LXX equivalent of כבס (kabas, wash garments) — the standard Levitical purification act. λευκαίνω (G3021) adds the visual result — brilliance/purity.
