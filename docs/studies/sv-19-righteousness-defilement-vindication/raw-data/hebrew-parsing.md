### Key Observations: Verb Sequence in Zec 3:4
1. הָסִירוּ — Hiphil Imperative plural: "Remove!" (command to multiple attendants)
2. הֶעֱבַרְתִּי — Hiphil Perfect 1cs: "I have caused to pass" (God as speaker, 1st person)
3. וְהַלְבֵּשׁ — Hiphil Infinitive Absolute: "and clothing [thee]" (continuing action)

Three distinct actions by the Angel of YHWH:
- Step 1: Command to remove filthy garments (Hiphil of סור — "cause to turn aside")
- Step 2: Declaration that iniquity has been caused to pass (Hiphil of עבר — "cause to pass over/away")
- Step 3: Clothing with festival garments (Hiphil of לבשׁ — "cause to wear")

The Hebrew supports THREE steps, not two. The removal of garments and the removal of iniquity are grammatically distinct acts (different roots: סור vs. עבר). The third act (clothing) uses a different garment term (machalatsah H4254 = "festival/change of raiment") from the filthy garments (beged H899).

## Daniel 9:24
Six infinitive construct purposes:
1. לְכַלֵּא הַפֶּשַׁע — Piel.InfCon of כלה: "to bring to an end the transgression"
2. וּלְהָתֵם חַטָּאת — Hiphil.InfCon of תמם: "to seal up/complete sins"
3. וּלְכַפֵּר עָוֹן — Piel.InfCon of כפר: "to atone for iniquity"
4. וּלְהָבִיא צֶדֶק עֹלָמִים — Hiphil.InfCon of בוא: "to bring in everlasting righteousness"
5. וְלַחְתֹּם חָזוֹן וְנָבִיא — Qal.InfCon of חתם: "to seal vision and prophet"
6. וְלִמְשֹׁחַ קֹדֶשׁ קָדָשִׁים — Qal.InfCon of משׁח: "to anoint holy of holies"

First triad: remove sin (pesha, chattat, avon — same three DOA categories)
Second triad: install positive realities (tsedeq olamim, sealed prophecy, anointed sanctuary)
Bridge: #3 (kaphar, Piel) directly bridges to #4 (tsedeq). Same verse contains DOA vocabulary AND vindication vocabulary.
