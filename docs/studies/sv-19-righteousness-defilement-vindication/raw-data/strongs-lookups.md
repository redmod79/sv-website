## H4254 — מַחֲלָצָה (machalatsah)
- **Transliteration:** machălâtsâh
- **Pronunciation:** makh-al-aw-tsaw
- **Part of Speech:** Feminine noun
- **BLB Count:** 2
- **Definition:** From H2502 (chalats, "to strip off"); a mantle (as easily drawn off): changeable suit of apparel, change of raiment.
- **Occurrences:**
  - Isaiah 3:22 — "The changeable suits of apparel" (among luxury items of Jerusalem's women)
  - Zechariah 3:4 — "with change of raiment" (festival garments given to Joshua)
- **Translation distribution:**
  - 1x "The changeable suits of apparel" (Isa 3:22)
  - 1x "with change of raiment" (Zec 3:4)
- **Etymology:** Derived from chalats (H2502, "to strip, equip, arm"). The root idea is something easily stripped off/put on — hence a change of clothing, festival garments.
- **Significance:** Only 2 occurrences in OT. In Zech 3:4, the machalatsoth (plural) are the NEW garments that replace the filthy ones. This is not ordinary clothing (beged H899) but special festival/change garments — garments for a new occasion.

## H5674 — עָבַר (abar) — Relevant to Zech 3:4
- **Transliteration:** âbar
- **Pronunciation:** aw-bar
- **Part of Speech:** Verb
- **BLB Count:** 690 total occurrences
- **Definition:** A primitive root; to cross over; used very widely of any transition.
- **Key translations:** pass (19x), passed over (20x), go over (10x), went over (8x), transgress (4x)
- **Zech 3:4 usage:** הֶעֱבַרְתִּי (Hiphil.Perf.1cs) — "I have caused to pass [from thee]" — with iniquity (avon) as the object. The Hiphil = causative: God CAUSES the iniquity to pass away from Joshua.
- **Parallel usage:** Zec 13:2 also uses H5674 for removing: "I will cause... to pass out of the land."
- **Theological note:** In Zech 3:4, abar is used for iniquity-removal rather than taher (cleansing) or kaphar (covering). The metaphor is of sin PASSING AWAY / BEING TRANSFERRED — consistent with the DOA scapegoat imagery (Lev 16:21-22 where sins are transferred to the goat and sent away).

## H1777 — דִּין (diyn)
- **Transliteration:** dîyn
- **Pronunciation:** deen
- **Part of Speech:** Verb
- **BLB Count:** 24
- **Definition:** A primitive root; to rule; by implication to judge (as umpire); also to strive (as at law): contend, execute judgment, judge, minister judgment, plead the cause, strive.
- **Key translations:** judge (4x), shall judge (4x), execute/plead (various)
- **Key verses:**
  - Gen 15:14 — "that nation, whom they shall serve, will I judge"
  - Gen 49:16 — "Dan shall judge his people"
  - Deu 32:36 — "For the LORD shall judge his people"
  - Psa 50:4 — "He shall call to the heavens from above, and to the earth, that he may judge his people"
  - Psa 110:6 — "He shall judge among the heathen"
  - Zec 3:7 — "then thou shalt also judge my house" (uses diyn in the Zechariah 3 context!)
- **Significance:** H1777 appears in Zech 3:7 — the conditional promise to Joshua after the garment change. After vindication (garment change = tsadaq dimension), Joshua receives authority to JUDGE (diyn). This connects vindication to judicial authority.

## H3198 — יָכַח (yakach)
- **Transliteration:** yâkach
- **Pronunciation:** yaw-kahh
- **Part of Speech:** Verb
- **BLB Count:** 59
- **Definition:** A primitive root; to be right (i.e. correct); reciprocal, to argue; causatively, to decide, justify or convict: appoint, argue, chasten, convince, correct, daysman, dispute, judge, maintain, plead, reason together, rebuke, reprove.
- **Key translations:** reprove (4x), rebuke (2x+), reason together (1x)
- **Key verses:**
  - Isa 1:18 — "Come now, and let us reason together" (yakach)
  - Isa 11:3-4 — "He shall not judge... and reprove (yakach) with equity for the meek"
  - Job 9:33 — "Neither is there any daysman (yakach) betwixt us"
  - Job 23:7 — "There the righteous might dispute (yakach) with him"
  - Mic 6:2 — "the LORD hath a controversy... he will plead (yakach)"
- **Significance:** Courtroom/forensic argumentation verb. The "daysman" (mediator) usage in Job 9:33 is notable — one who argues/decides between parties. The verb covers the full spectrum of forensic proceedings: argue, decide, reprove, justify.
