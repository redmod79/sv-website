# Word Studies

## Question
How do righteousness and defilement converge in Dan 8:14's vindication? Why tsadaq not taher? tame→tahor→tsadaq progression. Zechariah 3 model. Sanctuary purpose requires both.

## From Word Studies Library

### Core Verb Triad: tame → taher → tsadaq

#### tsadaq — H6663
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`

- **Word:** צָדַק (tsadaq) — verb, primitive root
- **Occurrences:** 41 in OT (54 KJV translation entries)
- **Definition:** To be (causatively, make) right in a moral or forensic sense: cleanse, clear self, justify, be righteous
- **Stem distribution:**
  - Qal (22x): be righteous, be in the right — Job 4:17, 9:2, 25:4; Psa 143:2
  - Hiphil (12x): justify, declare righteous — Exo 23:7; Deu 25:1; 1 Kgs 8:32; Isa 53:11; Dan 12:3
  - Piel (5x): prove right, show to be righteous — Gen 44:16; Job 32:2; Jer 3:11; Eze 16:51-52
  - **Niphal (1x):** be vindicated — Dan 8:14 ONLY
  - Hithpael (1x): clear oneself — Gen 44:16
- **Distribution:** Job dominates (56%, 23/41). Wisdom and prophetic literature = 90%.
- **Top collocates:** mishpat (8x), shaphat (8x), rasha (6x) — all courtroom/forensic language
- **LXX mapping:** dikaioo (G1344, 21x), dikaios (G1342, 22x), krino (G2919, 8x)
- **Key for this study:** The only Niphal (Dan 8:14) is passive — the sanctuary RECEIVES vindication. The Hiphil (Isa 53:11, Dan 12:3) is causative — God/the Servant CAUSES righteousness. The forensic character is established by the collocates (judgment, judging, wicked/righteous pairs).

#### taher — H2891
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2891-taher.md`

- **Word:** טָהֵר (taher) — verb, primitive root ("to be bright → to be pure")
- **Occurrences:** 94 (115 KJV instances across 80 verses)
- **Definition:** To be pure (physically sound, clear; Levitically uncontaminated; morally innocent or holy): be clean, cleanse, purge, purify
- **Stem distribution:**
  - Piel (37x): declare/pronounce clean; actively cleanse
  - Qal (34x): be clean (stative/intransitive)
  - Hithpael (18x): cleanse oneself (reflexive)
  - Pual (1x): be cleansed (passive)
- **Distribution:** Leviticus 46% (53/115). Ezekiel 15x. Absent from most prophetic books except Malachi (2x).
- **Top collocates:** kohen/priest (25x), kabas/wash (13x), beged/garment (13x), kaphar (12x), mayim/water (10x)
- **LXX mapping:** katharizo (G2511, 61x), katharos (G2513, 23x)
- **Key for this study:** Taher is the DOA result verb (Lev 16:30). Its collocations are with priestly, ritual, and physical-purity vocabulary. It CAN extend to spiritual/eschatological contexts (Mal 3:3, Eze 36:25, Jer 33:8) but its semantic center is ritual cleanness. Critically, taher never appears in Dan 8:14 — Daniel chose tsadaq instead.

#### tame — H2930
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2930-tame.md`

- **Word:** טָמֵא (tame) — verb, primitive root ("to be foul")
- **Occurrences:** 142 verses (194 tokens) across 13 books
- **Definition:** To be foul, especially in ceremonial or moral sense: defile, pollute, be unclean
- **Stem distribution:**
  - Qal (72x): be/become unclean (stative)
  - Piel (43x): defile, make unclean, pronounce unclean
  - Niphal (16x): be defiled (passive)
  - Hithpael (12x): defile oneself (reflexive)
- **Distribution:** Leviticus 50% (71/142). Ezekiel 28x.
- **Categories:** Ritual impurity from contact, bodily states, priestly declaration, sexual defilement, idolatry, defilement of sacred space
- **LXX mapping:** miaino (G3392, 86x), akathartos (G169, 53x)
- **Key for this study:** Tame is the STARTING POINT of the progression. Defilement enters (tame) → must be removed (taher) → but the antitype goes beyond removal to declaration (tsadaq).

### machalatsah — H4254
**Source:** Fresh lookup (search_strongs.py --lookup H4254, --lexicon H4254)

- **Word:** מַחֲלָצָה
- **Transliteration:** machălâtsâh
- **Part of Speech:** Feminine noun
- **BLB Count:** 2
- **Definition:** From H2502 (chalats, "to strip off, equip"); a mantle (as easily drawn off): changeable suit of apparel, change of raiment
- **Occurrences:**
  - Zechariah 3:4 — "change of raiment" (the NEW garments replacing the filthy ones)
  - Isaiah 3:22 — "changeable suits of apparel" (luxury items of Jerusalem's women)
- **Etymology:** Root chalats (H2502) means "to strip, equip, arm" — hence a garment that is easily changed, festival clothing
- **Significance:** In Zech 3:4, the machalatsoth are distinct from ordinary beged (garments). They are SPECIAL festival/ceremonial garments — garments for a NEW occasion. The filthy garments (beged tsow) are replaced not with just clean garments but with FESTIVAL garments. This vocabulary choice suggests the vindication scene is not merely cleansing (returning to a previous state) but inauguration (entering a new status).

### abar — H5674 (in Zech 3:4)
**Source:** Fresh lookup (search_strongs.py --lookup H5674, --verses H5674 "to pass")

- **Word:** עָבַר
- **Transliteration:** âbar
- **Part of Speech:** Verb (primitive root)
- **BLB Count:** 690
- **Zech 3:4 form:** הֶעֱבַרְתִּי (Hiphil.Perf.1cs) — "I have caused to pass from thee"
- **Object:** עֲוֹנֶךָ (avon, "thine iniquity")
- **Parallel:** Zec 13:2 also uses H5674: "I will cause [the names of the idols] to pass out of the land"
- **Significance:** God uses the Hiphil (causative) — He actively CAUSES the iniquity to pass away. This is not passive cleansing but divine action of sin-removal. The Hiphil of abar for sin-removal is distinct from both taher (cleanse) and kaphar (cover/atone). It describes sin being TRANSFERRED AWAY — similar to the scapegoat concept (Lev 16:21-22) where sins are placed on the goat and sent to the wilderness.

## Stem Analysis Summary: The Three Stems of tsadaq

| Stem | Form | Meaning | Key Verse | Significance |
|------|------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Niphal** (passive) | נִצְדַּק (nitsdaq) | "be vindicated" | Dan 8:14 | The sanctuary RECEIVES vindication — 1 occurrence (hapax) |
| **Hiphil** (causative) | יַצְדִּיק / מַצְדִּיקֵי | "cause to be righteous / justify" | Isa 53:11; Dan 12:3; Deu 25:1 | God/Servant/judges CAUSE righteousness — 12 occurrences |
| **Qal** (simple) | צָדַק | "be righteous" | Job 4:17; Psa 143:2 | State of being right before God — 22 occurrences |

The Niphal (Dan 8:14) and Hiphil (Isa 53:11, Dan 12:3) are complementary:
- Niphal: the sanctuary receives the verdict (passive recipient)
- Hiphil: the Servant/wise ones produce the righteousness (active cause)
- Together: vindication has both a receiving end (the sanctuary/God's people) and a producing end (the Servant's work/the wise ones' ministry)

## Key Vocabulary Contrasts

### Why tsadaq not taher (vocabulary shift evidence)

| Feature | taher (H2891) | tsadaq (H6663) |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **Semantic domain** | Ritual/physical purity | Forensic/judicial rightness |
| **Primary setting** | Levitical system (46% in Leviticus) | Courtroom/wisdom (56% in Job) |
| **Top collocates** | priest, wash, garment, water | judgment, judge, wicked |
| **LXX equivalent** | katharizo (cleanse) | dikaioo (justify) |
| **DOA presence** | Central (Lev 16:19, 16:30) | Absent from Lev 16 entirely |
| **Dan 8:14 choice** | NOT used | USED (Niphal hapax) |
| **Heavenly usage** | Not used for heavenly sanctuary | Used for heavenly sanctuary verdict |

The shift from taher to tsadaq is a register elevation: from ritual (what happens to objects) to forensic (what happens in court). The type uses taher; the antitype uses tsadaq. Same function (restoration), different register (ritual → judicial).
