### dan-7-9-10.md — Judgment Scene Grammar
- Aramaic periphrastic construction: chazeh haveit (Ptcp + Perf.1s) = "I was seeing/beholding"
- karsavan remiyu (Peil passive) = "thrones were placed/set up" (not "cast down" — KJV misleads)
- attiq yomin (Adj.ms.Cst + Noun.mp) = "Ancient of days" — title construct
- yethib (Peal.Perf.3ms) = "sat down" — the court takes its seat
- diyna yethib (7:10) = "the judgment was set/sat" — the tribunal convenes
- siphrin petichi (7:10) = "books were opened" — formal judicial proceeding
- Three stages of diyn in Dan 7: SET (7:10), GIVEN to saints (7:22), ACTS against dominion (7:26)

### dan-8-8-9.md — Horn Emergence Grammar
Key points:
- min-ha-achat mehem (8:9): 3mp suffix on feminine antecedents = constructio ad sensum
- yatsa (Qal.Perf.3ms): masculine verb for feminine subject qeren — treats horn as the king
- mits'eirah (H4704): hapax feminine noun, compound min + tsa'ir = "from littleness"
- tigdal-yether: Qal wayyiqtol + adverbial noun = "grew exceedingly" — surpassing degree
- The gadal progression across 8:4, 8:8, 8:9: ram "became great" -> goat "very great" -> horn "exceeding great"

### dan-8-10-12.md — Four-Fold Attack Grammar
Key points:
- Voice chiasm: Active (higdil) -> Passive (huram, hushlakh) -> Passive (tinnatein) -> Active (tashlekh)
- sar ha-tsava construct chain (8:11): "Prince of the host" — a heavenly figure
- mekhon miqdash-o (8:11): 3ms suffix on miqdash = "HIS sanctuary" — Prince's, not horn's
- huram (Hophal of ruwm): "was lifted away" — agentless passive
- hushlakh (Hophal of shalak): "was cast down" — agentless passive
- tinnatein (Niphal of natan): "was given" — divine passive
- The passive cluster encodes divine or unnamed agency at the key action points

### dan-8-13-14-dialogue.md — Heavenly Dialogue Grammar
Key points:
- echad qadosh (8:13): "one holy one" — heavenly being
- ha-palmoni ha-medabber: "that certain one who speaks" — palmoni is likely a compound
- ad matay: "how long?" — standard prophetic complaint formula
- ha-chazon ha-tamid: "the vision [concerning] the daily" — construct or apposition
- ve-ha-pesha shomem: "and the transgression of desolation" — shomem Qal Ptcp (agent)
- nitsdaq (Niphal.Perf.3ms of tsadaq): sole Niphal in OT, forensic vindication

### dan-8-17-19.md — End-Time Framing Grammar
Key points:
- eth qets (8:17): "time of the end"
- acharit hazza'am (8:19): "the last end of the indignation"
- le-mo'ed qets (8:19): "at the time appointed the end" — mo'ed + qets compound
- Gabriel's triple horizon: three different temporal phrases all pointing to the same end

### dan-8-23-25.md — Horn Characteristics Grammar
Key points:
- az-paniym (8:23): exclusive construct chain — ONLY Dan 8:23 + Deu 28:50 in all OT
- mevin chidot (8:23): "understanding riddles/dark sentences" — biyn Hiphil Ptcp
- ve-atsam kocho ve-lo be-kocho (8:24): "his power shall be mighty but not by his own power"
- be-efes yad yisshaber (8:25): "without hand he shall be broken" — Niphal passive, divine agency
- sar sarim (8:25): "Prince of princes" — superlative construct (cf. 8:11 sar ha-tsava)

### 2thess-2-1-12.md — Man of Sin Grammar
Key points:
- ho antikemenos kai huperairomenos (2:4): "who opposes and exalts himself"
- eis ton naon tou theou kathisai (2:4): "in the temple of God to sit" — temple/sanctuary language
- ho anomos (2:8): "the lawless one"
- hon ho kyrios anelei to pneumati tou stomatos autou (2:8): "whom the Lord shall consume with the spirit of his mouth" — stoma echoes Dan 7:8; divine destruction echoes Dan 7:11,26 and Dan 8:25

### mat-24-15-mk-13-14-abomination.md — Abomination Grammar
Key points:
- to bdelygma tes eremoseos (Matt 24:15): "the abomination of desolation"
- to rhethen dia Daniel tou prophetou (Matt 24:15): "that which was spoken through Daniel the prophet"
- Jesus explicitly attributes the abomination to Daniel and tells readers to "understand" (noeo) — echoing Dan 8:16-17 where Gabriel tells Daniel to "understand" (biyn/sakal)
