### H5810 azaz — "to be stout, prevail"
- 12 occurrences (BLB count), 16 translation entries
- Primitive root: be stout, harden, prevail, strengthen
- Top translations: prevailed (2x), strengthen, strong is, impudent
- Occurrences: Jdg 3:10; 6:2; Psa 9:20; 52:9; 68:29; 89:14; Pro 7:13; 8:28; 21:29; Ecc 7:19; Isa 30:2; Dan 11:12
- Note: Dan 8:24 uses ATSAM (H6105), not azaz. Dan 8:23 uses AZ (H5794, adjective from azaz root) in construct chain az-paniym ("fierce countenance").

### H656 aphes — "to cease, disappear"
- 6 occurrences (verb form)
- Primitive root: to disappear, cease, be clean gone, fail
- Gen 47:15,16; Psa 77:9; Isa 16:4; 29:20
- The NOUN form efes (H657) = "end, nothing, cessation" — 45 occurrences
- Dan 8:25 be-EFES yad uses H657 (noun), not H656 (verb)
- Dan 8:25 construction: be (prep) + efes (H657, "cessation/nothing") + yad (H3027, "hand") = "in nothing of hand" = "without hand"

### H3027 yad — "hand" (Hebrew)
- 1,734 total occurrences — most common Hebrew noun
- Top translations: hand (114x), his hand (109x), into the hand (101x), by the hand (59x)
- Daniel uses: Dan 1:2,17; 2:38; 5:5,23,24; 8:4,7,25; 9:10,15; 10:4,10; 11:6,11,16,17,22,25,33,41,42; 12:7
