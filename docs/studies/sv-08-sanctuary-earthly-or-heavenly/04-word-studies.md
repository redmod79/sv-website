### makon — H4349
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4349-makon.md`

- **Word:** מָכוֹן (mâkôwn)
- **POS:** Masculine noun
- **Root:** כּוּן (kûn, H3559) — to be firm, established
- **Occurrences:** 17 unique verses (19 tokens) in 7 books
- **Definition:** A fixture, a basis; a place, especially as an abode — foundation, habitation, (dwelling-, settled) place
- **Key semantic distinction:** Not merely locative ("a spot") but stability-of-location — a site that has been fixed, founded, or settled. The root force of kûn carries through.

**Translation distribution:** "place" 35.3%, "habitation" 11.8%, "dwelling place" 11.8%, "settled place" 5.9%, "foundations" 5.9%

**Semantic categories relevant to this study:**
1. **The Sanctuary as YHWH's Established Site (programmatic):** Exo 15:17 — "in the Sanctuary, O Lord, which thy hands have established [makon]"; Dan 8:11 — "the place [makon] of his sanctuary was cast down"
2. **YHWH's Heavenly Dwelling Place (temple-dedication refrain):** 1 Ki 8:39,43,49; 2 Chr 6:30,33,39 — "hear thou in heaven thy dwelling place [makon]"
3. **The Earthly Temple as Settled/Founded Place:** 1 Ki 8:13 — "a settled place [makon] for thee to abide in for ever"
4. **Foundation of the Throne:** Psa 89:14; 97:2 — "righteousness and judgment are the habitation [makon] of his throne"

**Critical observation:** 8 of 17 occurrences cluster in Solomon's temple-dedication material (1 Ki 8; 2 Chr 6) where "thy dwelling place" (mekon shibteka) is a refrain. The phrase consistently refers to God's **heavenly** dwelling place — Solomon prays toward the earthly temple, but asks God to hear "in heaven thy dwelling place." The single Daniel occurrence (8:11) is the only prophetic-apocalyptic use tied to the sanctuary.

**Signature collocation:** H4349 + H3427 (yashab) — "the place of thy dwelling" (59% of occurrences). Fixed phrase marking YHWH's established residence.

**LXX:** Primary rendering is κατοικητήριον (G2732, "dwelling-place"); Exo 15:17 LXX = ἕτοιμον κατοικητήριόν σου ("your prepared dwelling-place"), preserving both foundedness and dwelling nuances.

### miqdash — H4720
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4720-miqdash.md`

- **Word:** מִקְדָּשׁ (miqdash)
- **POS:** Masculine noun
- **Root:** H6942 קָדַשׁ (qadash) — to be holy
- **Occurrences:** 71 unique verses (78 tokens) across 14 books
- **Definition:** A consecrated thing or place; sanctuary, holy place, chapel
- **Key distinction from qodesh:** miqdash always means a specific location. qodesh (H6944) is broader — quality, objects, space.
- **Ezekiel dominates:** 39% of all uses. Concentrated in prophetic texts dealing with defilement, judgment, and restoration.
- **Daniel's 3 uses:** Dan 8:11 (mekon miqdasho — "the place of his sanctuary"), Dan 11:31 (miqdash hamaoz — "the sanctuary of strength"), Dan 9:17 (prayer for the sanctuary)

**Semantic categories relevant to this study:**
1. **Divine Dwelling Place:** Exo 25:8 ("let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them")
2. **Profaning the Sanctuary:** Lev 20:3; Eze 5:11; 23:38; Dan 11:31
3. **Sanctuary Cast Down/Destroyed:** Psa 74:7; Lam 1:10; Dan 8:11
4. **God Himself as Sanctuary:** Isa 8:14; Eze 11:16

**LXX:** Primary rendering ἅγιος (hagios, G40) — 46 co-occurrences, PMI 18.54.

### qodesh — H6944
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md`

- **Word:** קֹדֶשׁ (qodesh) — holiness, holy thing, sanctuary
- **Occurrences:** 452 in OT (Daniel has 12, clustering in Dan 8-12 around sanctuary/holy city language)
- **Key for this study:** Dan 8:13-14 uses qodesh (not miqdash) at the verdict point. Dan 8:11 uses miqdash for the attacked sanctuary. The shift from miqdash (specific structure) to qodesh (the holiness domain) at the verdict broadens the referent from a physical precinct to the entire holy domain being vindicated.

### tsaba — H6635
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6635-tsaba.md`

- **Word:** צָבָא (tsaba) — host, army, service
- **Occurrences:** 462 unique verses, 27 books
- **Daniel cluster:** 5 occurrences, all in Dan 8:10-13. Four uses in vv.10-13, one in v.8.
- **Key pattern:** Dan 8:10 — "host of heaven" (tsaba ha-shamayim); Dan 8:11 — "prince of the host" (sar-ha-tsaba); Dan 8:12 — "a host was given"; Dan 8:13 — "the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot"
- **Collocation with sar (H8269):** 44 co-occurrences (10%), including Josh 5:14 "captain of the host of the LORD" and Dan 8:11 "prince of the host." This exact formula occurs in only these two places.

### sar — H8269
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8269-sar.md`

- **Word:** שַׂר (sar) — prince, captain, ruler
- **POS:** Masculine noun (CRITICAL — masculine, unlike qeren which is feminine)
- **Occurrences:** 368 unique verses
- **Daniel's cosmic use (category 4):** Dan 8:11 (prince of the host), Dan 10:13 (prince of kingdom of Persia), Dan 10:21 (Michael your prince), Dan 12:1 (Michael, the great prince). Daniel uniquely extends sar to angelic/cosmic beings governing nations.
- **Gender significance:** sar is masculine. The 3ms suffix on miqdasho ("his sanctuary") in Dan 8:11 can refer back to the masculine sar-ha-tsaba. It cannot refer to the feminine qeren (horn).

### heikal — H1965 (Aramaic)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1965-heikal.md`

- **Word:** הֵיכַל (heikal) — palace, temple (Aramaic)
- **Occurrences:** 15 (Aramaic portions only: Ezra 4-6, Daniel 2-7)
- **Daniel's usage:** Dan 5:2-3 uses heikal for the Jerusalem temple (vessels taken from the heikal). Dan 4:4,29; 6:18 use it for Nebuchadnezzar/Darius's palace.
- **KEY FOR THIS STUDY:** Daniel had heikal available as a word for "temple" — he used it in Dan 5:2-3 for the earthly temple in Jerusalem. But in Dan 8:11, he did NOT use heikal. He used mekon miqdash — a completely different register. This vocabulary choice is deliberate and marks a distinction between the earthly temple (heikal) and the sanctuary in the vision (mekon miqdash).

### tabniyth — H8403 (Newly Generated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8403-tabniyth.md`

- **Word:** תַּבְנִית (tabniyth) — pattern, likeness, model
- **Root:** בָּנָה (banah, H1129) — to build
- **Occurrences:** 17 unique verses, 8 books
- **Key verses:** Exo 25:9 ("the pattern of the tabernacle"), Exo 25:40 ("after their pattern, which was shewed thee in the mount"), 1 Chr 28:11,19 (David's temple pattern from God)
- **Semantic distinction:** A buildable structural model, not abstract resemblance. The earthly sanctuary was built according to a tabniyth shown on the mountain — implying a heavenly original from which the pattern derives.
- **LXX:** Primarily ὁμοίωμα (homoioma, G3667), but NT uses τύπος (typos, G5179) in Heb 8:5/Acts 7:44 citations of Exo 25:40 — a deliberate shift emphasizing type/antitype relationship.

### mishkan — H4908 (Newly Generated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4908-mishkan.md`

- **Word:** מִשְׁכָּן (mishkan) — tabernacle, dwelling
- **Root:** שָׁכַן (shakan, H7931) — to dwell
- **Occurrences:** 129 unique verses (145 tokens)
- **Distribution:** Exodus and Numbers = 70% of all uses. "Tabernacle" at ~80% of translations.
- **Key semantic note:** The word emphasizes the dwelling-presence of God. Combined with ohel (tent structure), mishkan names the God-indwelt sanctuary. The LXX overwhelmingly renders it σκηνή (skene, G4633) — the same word used in Heb 8:2 for the "true tabernacle."
- **Distinction from miqdash:** mishkan = the dwelling; miqdash = the consecrated precinct. Dan 8:11 uses miqdash (not mishkan), qualified by mekon — the established consecrated place.

### maon — H4583 (Newly Generated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4583-maown.md`

- **Word:** מָעוֹן (maon) — habitation, den, dwelling place
- **Occurrences:** 17 unique verses
- **Key usage:** The phrase "holy habitation" (maon qodesh) appears 5 times, always referring to heaven (Deu 26:15; 2 Chr 30:27; Jer 25:30; Zec 2:13; Psa 68:5).
- **Distinction from makon:** maon = shelter/refuge (emphasizes protection); makon = established/founded place (emphasizes permanence/stability). Both are used for God's heavenly dwelling, but makon carries the stronger architectural/foundational connotation.
- **Shared LXX rendering:** Both maon and makon map to κατοικητήριον (G2732), collapsing the Hebrew distinction in Greek.

### alethinos — G228
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md`

- **Word:** ἀληθινός — true, genuine, real
- **Occurrences:** 27 tokens (80% in Johannine literature)
- **Key semantic category for this study:** "The Genuine vs. the Copy" — Heb 8:2 ("the true tabernacle"), Heb 9:24 ("figures of the true"), Jhn 1:9 ("the true Light"), Jhn 6:32 ("the true bread")
- **Distinction from alethes (G227):** alethes = factually accurate; alethinos = the real thing vs. a copy/imitation (qualitative authenticity, ontological genuineness)
- **In Hebrews:** Heb 8:2 (true tabernacle), 9:24 (copies of the true things), 10:22 (true heart). Establishes the real/copy ontological contrast central to the sanctuary argument.

### skene — G4633
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4633-skene.md`

- **Word:** σκηνή — tent, tabernacle

### hagion — G39 (Newly Generated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G39-hagion.md`

- **Word:** ἅγιον — holy place, sanctuary (substantivized neuter of G40 ἅγιος)
- **Occurrences:** 10 in NT — ALL in Hebrews (exclusively chapters 8-10, 13)
- **Key for this study:** Heb 8:2 uses τῶν ἁγίων (gen. pl. neut.) — "minister of the sanctuary." This is the Hebrews author's technical term for the tabernacle compartments. The neuter plural follows LXX convention rendering Hebrew qodesh/qodashim.
- **Morphological forms:** Dominant form is τὰ ἅγια (8 of 10 uses). The superlative Ἅγια ἁγίων (Heb 9:3) mirrors Hebrew קֹדֶשׁ הַקֳּדָשִׁים "Holy of Holies."

### leitourgos — G3011
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3011-leitourgos.md`

- **Word:** λειτουργός — minister, public servant
- **Root:** λεῖτος (public) + ἔργον (work)
- **Occurrences:** 5 in NT

### naos — G3485
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3485-naos.md`

- **Word:** ναός — temple, inner sanctuary
- **Occurrences:** 45 in NT (Revelation = 16, 37%)
- **Key naos/hieron distinction:** hieron = temple complex (outer courts, public activity); naos = inner sanctuary where God dwells. Never confused in the NT.
- **Heavenly temple in Revelation:** Rev 11:19 ("temple of God was opened in heaven"); Rev 15:5,8; Rev 7:15; Rev 14:15,17; Rev 16:1,17 — all use naos for the heavenly sanctuary.
- **2 Thess 2:4:** "he as God sitteth in the temple [naos] of God" — the man of sin claims the inner sanctuary space.
- **Absent from Hebrews:** The author of Hebrews uses τὰ ἅγια (G39) rather than naos for the sanctuary concept.

## Word Group Studies Read

### WG-holiness.md
Hebrew root ק-ד-שׁ family (qodesh, qadash, qadowsh, miqdash) + Greek ἁγ- family. LXX collapses noun/adj/place into hagios (G40). The holiness vocabulary operates as a unified semantic field across both testaments.

### WG-tabernacle.md
The skene word family (skene, skenoo, skenoma, skenos, skia) plus Hebrew roots (shakan, mishkan, sukkah). Key distinction: skene (tent/portable) vs. naos (permanent inner sanctuary). Rev 15:5 merges both: "the temple [naos] of the tabernacle [skene] of the testimony."

### WG-temple.md
naos (inner sanctuary) vs. hieron (precinct) vs. oikos (house). naos never appears in the same verse as hieron. The destroy/rebuild pair (katalyo + oikodomeo) exclusively pairs with naos.

### WG-continual-worship.md
tamid (H8548) as the qualifier binding the sacrificial system: olah (burnt offering), minchah (meal offering), nesek (drink offering), chattaah (sin offering), lechem (showbread).

### WG-ofhosts.md
tsaba (organized host), machaneh (camp), chayil (force/valor), milchamah (battle). The Greek dynamis (G1411) bridges both chayil and tsaba, which is why NT "powers of heaven" (Matt 24:29) fuses both ideas.

## Translation Relationship Studies Read

### TR-heykal-naos.md
Hebrew heikal (H1964/H1965) → Greek naos (G3485). Primary LXX bridge for the inner sanctuary/temple building concept. Confirms the inner-sanctuary reading of naos in heavenly-temple passages.

### TR-qodesh-hagios.md
qodesh (H6944) → hagios (G40). One of the tightest Hebrew-Greek mappings (346 of 452 instances). The "holy place" concept transfers seamlessly.

### TR-qadash-hagiazo.md
qadash (H6942) → hagiazo (G37). Near 1:1 verb correspondence for "to sanctify."

### TR-sar-archon.md
sar (H8269) → archon (G758). LXX's primary rendering. The cosmic-ruler sense in Daniel's usage transfers into NT "principalities and powers" language.

### TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md
tsadaq (H6663) → dikaioo (G1344). The forensic "justify/vindicate" bridge. Dan 8:14's nitsdaq maps conceptually to the dikaios/dikaioo word family.

### TR-kohen-hiereus.md
kohen (H3548) → hiereus (G2409). Priest terminology bridge. Heb 8:2's entire argument about Christ as leitourgos builds on this priesthood vocabulary.

## Grammar Reference: Heb 8:1-2 (Newly Generated)
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/heb-8-1-2.md`

Five key grammatical features:
1. **Kephalaion** — discourse-summative marker signaling the thesis of the entire Hebrews argument
2. **ekathisen** — aorist active indicative of kathizo: a completed, punctiliar act of enthronement at the right hand
3. **ton hagion leitourgos** — substantival genitive plural neuter (sanctuary) + predicate nominative (minister)
4. **tes skenes tes alethines** — second attributive position (article-noun-article-adjective): "the tabernacle — the true one"
5. **hen epexen ho Kyrios, ouk anthropos** — NT hapax pegnumi (tent-pitching verb) + contrastive negation: Lord pitched it, not man

The real/copy vocabulary system established: alethinos (heavenly genuine), antitypos (earthly copy), hypodeigma (copy/pattern), skia (shadow), typos (heavenly original pattern), cheiropoietos (handmade).

## Grammar References Read (Dan 8)

### passages/dan-8-11.md
Three coordinate perfect-aspect clauses. Hiphil active (higdil — self-magnification) + two Hophal passives (huram — tamid taken away; hushlak — sanctuary-place cast down). Agent suppressed in passives. The 3ms suffix on miqdasho binds to sar-ha-tsaba (masculine), not qeren (feminine).

### passages/dan-8-11-12-voice-alternation.md
Seven-verb chain across four Hebrew stems in A-B-A chiasm: ACTIVE (1 verb, v.11a) — PASSIVE (3 verbs, vv.11b-12a) — ACTIVE (3 verbs, v.12b-d). Passive zone covers precisely the actions touching the heavenly sanctuary system. Active zones cover the horn's self-exaltation and its earthly attacks.

### passages/dan-8-10-12.md
Full triad parsing. Confirms the voice alternation pattern and the construct chain analysis. The same root shalak appears in both active Hiphil (horn as agent) and passive Hophal (agent suppressed) — proving the author deliberately chose voice.
