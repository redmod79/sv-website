# Reference Gathering: Leviticus 4 and the Blood-Transfer Question

## Question
Does Leviticus 4 teach that individual sin offerings transferred sin into the sanctuary via blood? The text distinguishes four categories: (1) anointed priest (4:3-12) — blood sprinkled before the veil and on the incense altar, (2) whole congregation (4:13-21) — same, (3) ruler (4:22-26) — blood on burnt-offering altar horns only, (4) common person (4:27-35) — burnt-offering altar horns only. For categories 3 and 4, blood never enters the sanctuary building. How does sin reach the sanctuary? Three mechanisms: (a) priestly eating (Lev 6:26, Lev 10:17 — nasa = bearing iniquity), (b) ambient defilement (Lev 15:31, Num 19:13,20 — Piel of tame = causative defilement at distance), (c) DOA scope (Lev 16:16 tumot covers defilement from non-sacrificial channels). Does the system require blood-into-the-building for every sin?

### From CUSTOM-INSTRUCTIONS.md (Integrate list)

**sv-02-biblical-defilement:**
- Question: What does the Bible mean by defilement/uncleanness (tumah H2932, tame H2930)?
- Key finding 1: The two-axis framework (Lev 10:10) — qodesh/chol and tahor/tame are independent axes. A holy thing can be contaminated; both axes must be addressed (Lev 16:19, taher + qadash on the altar).
- Key finding 2: Seven categories of defilement (death contact, bodily emissions, childbirth, skin disease, unclean animals, sexual violations, idolatry/bloodshed). Verb stems distinguish involuntary (Qal), declarative/causative (Piel), and deliberate self-defilement (Hithpael).
- Key finding 3: Three transmission mechanisms — blood application, priestly eating, and ambient contamination at a distance (Lev 15:31, Num 19:13,20). The third fills the tumot category in Lev 16:16.
- Key finding 4: Haggai 2:12-13 asymmetry — impurity transmits by secondary contact; holiness does not. The purity system is inherently defensive.
- Key finding 5: tame→tahor→tsadaq progression established. DOA produces tahor (Lev 16:30), not tsadaq. Vindication (Dan 8:14 nitsdaq) goes further.
- Vocabulary chain: tame (H2930), tumah (H2932), taher (H2891), chaneph (H2610), chalal (H2490)

**sv-03-tamid-doa-connection:**
- Question: What is the connection between the daily tamid and the Day of Atonement?
- Key finding 1: Three mechanisms of sin transfer — (a) blood application (Lev 4:5-7, priest/congregation blood enters Holy Place), (b) priestly eating (Lev 6:26; Lev 10:17 — nasa = bearing iniquity; mutual exclusion rule of Lev 6:30), (c) ambient defilement (Lev 15:31, Num 19:13,20 — Piel of tame).
- Key finding 2: Absolute vocabulary distinction — kaphar+salach (daily, 13x) vs kaphar+taher (annual). salach never in Lev 16; taher never in Lev 4-5.
- Key finding 3: nasa verb connects both phases — priest bears sin IN (Lev 10:17); scapegoat bears sin OUT (Lev 16:22). Same verb, opposite directions.
- Key finding 4: The singular-to-plural shift — daily uses chattato ("his sin"); DOA uses chatotam ("their sins"). Grammatical scope change from individual to corporate.
- Key finding 5: Both EGW and Bohr identify only two transfer mechanisms (blood and flesh). The Bible describes three — ambient defilement is the third.

**sv-04-doa-ritual-sequence:**
- Question: Complete step-by-step ritual sequence of the DOA in Leviticus 16.
- Key finding 1: Chiastic structure — high priest moves inward to Most Holy Place (center), then outward. Blood movement reverses daily direction.
- Key finding 2: Three-term formula in Lev 16:16 — tumot (ritual impurity), pesha (willful rebellion), chattat (sins generally). Broader than daily (which addresses only shegagah).
- Key finding 3: kalah (Lev 16:20) separates blood atonement from scapegoat removal — structural boundary.
- Key finding 4: Lev 16:16 vs 16:21 — first term changes (tumot → avonot). Different vantage points: sanctuary-contamination vs moral-guilt. Same comprehensive scope.
- Key finding 5: DOA as sorting event — Lev 23:29 "afflict your souls" with karat penalty creates binary outcome.

**sanc-06-sin-enters-sanctuary:**
- Question: How does sin transfer from the sinner to the sanctuary?
- Key finding 1: Blood-destination differential — two paths in Lev 4: Path 1 (priest/congregation, blood enters Holy Place via nazah + nathan on incense altar + shaphakh at base), Path 2 (ruler/commoner, nathan on burnt offering altar horns + shaphakh at base).
- Key finding 2: Dual sin-transfer mechanism — Lev 6:30 mutual exclusion rule: blood inside = flesh burned; blood outside = priest eats. Moses confirms in Lev 10:18.
- Key finding 3: Priestly eating as nasa (Lev 10:17) — dual infinitive laset + lekapper = bearing + atoning. Priest's body becomes the vehicle of transfer.
- Key finding 4: Ambient defilement (Lev 15:31, Num 19:13,20) — Piel of tame = causative, sanctuary absorbs community impurity at a distance.
- Key finding 5: Graduated contamination — depth of blood penetration matches offender's position relative to God's covenant presence. Priest (physically serves inside) and congregation (covenantal partner) = deep penetration. Ruler/commoner = courtyard only.

### From Semantic Search (additional)

**atone-01:** (score: 0.527) — "Blood Application in the Chattat Procedure and Graduated Sanctuary Defilement"
- Key finding 1: Three distinct Hebrew verbs — nazah (sprinkle, Hiphil, controlled), nathan (put/place), shaphakh (pour). Not synonyms. Different actions, different functions.
- Key finding 2: nazah appears only in Path 1 (priest/congregation), always seven times, directed liphnei YHWH et-penei paroketh haqqodesh. Function: directional purification-marking toward the Most Holy Place boundary.
- Key finding 3: Graduated procedure does NOT produce graduated forgiveness — same kaphar + nislach formula for all four categories (Lev 4:20, 26, 31, 35). What varies is the depth of sanctuary contamination.
- Key finding 4: Consecration chattat anomaly (Exo 29:10-14; Lev 8:15) — uses courtyard blood procedure but burns flesh outside camp. Inaugural event has its own logic.
- Key finding 5: Lev 4 itself never uses tame or tumah. The defilement-transfer inference comes from Lev 15:31, 16:16, Num 19:13,20.

## Summary for Scoping Agent
- **5 prior studies** found with highly relevant findings. All four named in CUSTOM-INSTRUCTIONS.md plus atone-01.
- **3 external corpus claims** identified for biblical verification — all center on the two-mechanism model (blood + eating) that EGW, Bohr, and Haskell share. None mention the third mechanism (ambient defilement from Lev 15:31, Num 19:13,20).
