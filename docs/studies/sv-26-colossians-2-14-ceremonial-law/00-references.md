# Reference Gathering: Colossians 2:14-17 and the Ceremonial Law at the Cross

## Question
What was "nailed to the cross" in Colossians 2:14? What is the cheirographon tois dogmasin ("handwriting of ordinances")? Does this include the Day of Atonement? If the ceremonial law was abolished at the cross, how can its antitype (the heavenly judgment/DOA) begin in 1844? Examine: (1) What cheirographon means — a handwritten debt document, not the law itself. (2) What "nailed to the cross" destroys — the debt record, not the reality the ceremonies pointed to. (3) The type/antitype distinction: the earthly DOA was a shadow (skia, Col 2:17); the heavenly reality (soma) belongs to Christ. Abolishing the shadow confirms the substance. (4) How Hebrews 9:23 explicitly states the heavenly things require their own action.

**laws-abolished-at-cross** (score: 0.643):
- Question: "Which specific laws were fulfilled/abolished at the cross and which remain in effect?"
- Key finding: Every NT passage describing abolition identifies ceremonial content. Every passage affirming continuation identifies moral content.
- Greek vocabulary chain: cheirographon (G5498, hand-written) + dogma (G1378, decree) = ceremonial system. The Decalogue was written by God's finger (Exo 31:18), not by human hand.
- Col 2:14 analysis: Three terms — cheirographon (hapax, hand-written certificate of debt), tois dogmasin (in/by ordinances), hypenantios (against/contrary). Moses' hand-written law (Deu 31:9,24) matches; God-written Decalogue does not.
- Col 2:16-17: The feast-new moon-sabbath triad follows OT ceremonial calendar pattern (2 Chr 31:3; Eze 45:17; Hos 2:11). Items are "a shadow (skia) of things to come; but the body is of Christ."
- Eph 2:15 parallel: Same dogma vocabulary, same author, same concept — the ceremonial regulatory code abolished.
- Synthesis table: Ceremonial law = abolished at cross; moral law = established, written on hearts.

**colossians-2-16-sabbath** (score: 0.628):
- Question: "What Sabbath is being referred to in Colossians 2:16?"
- Answer: Ceremonial sabbaths (annual rest days of Levitical feast system), not weekly Sabbath.
- Key evidence: (1) cheirographon = hand-written document, Decalogue = God-written — different documents. (2) dogma (G1378) never used for the Decalogue in any NT passage. (3) skia (G4639) exclusively applied to ceremonial system in all 3 theological NT uses. (4) Lev 23:37-38 fourfold millibad separator distinguishes annual feasts from "the sabbaths of the LORD." (5) Col 2:17 shadow language points forward; weekly Sabbath memorial language (zakar) points backward to creation.
- Word studies used: cheirographon G5498, dogma G1378, skia G4639, sabbatismos G4520, zakar H2142, heorte G1859, neomenia G3561.

**law-08-abolished-at-cross** (score: 0.488):
- Question: "Which specific laws were abolished at the cross and which remain?"
- Key finding: All seven primary NT abolition passages (Col 2:14, Eph 2:15, Heb 7:12, Heb 9:10, Heb 10:1-9, 2 Cor 3:7-11, Gal 3:13) each identify something different as abolished — none names the Decalogue.
