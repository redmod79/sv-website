# Word Studies

## From Word Studies Library

### cheirographon — G5498
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5498-cheirographon.md`
- Hapax legomenon — only Col 2:14 in the entire NT
- Compound of cheir (G5495, "hand") + grapho (G1125, "to write")
- Literally: "something hand-written" — a handwritten document
- In Greek legal usage: a signed note of indebtedness (chirograph)
- The three actions applied: exaleipsas ("blotting out"), ērken ek tou mesou ("taken out of the way"), prosēlōsas ("nailing")
- Key distinction: The Decalogue was "written with the finger of God" (Exo 31:18), NOT by human hand. The cheirographon by definition is hand-written. These are different documents.
- The qualifying phrase tois dogmasin (G1378) specifies the content as ordinances/decrees

### dogma — G1378
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1378-dogma.md`
- 5 NT occurrences: Luke 2:1 (Caesar's decree), Acts 16:4 (Jerusalem Council decisions), Acts 17:7 (Caesar's decrees), Eph 2:15 (ordinances abolished), Col 2:14 (ordinances nailed)
- NEVER used for the Decalogue or the moral law in any NT passage
- In Col 2:14 and Eph 2:15: specifies ceremonial regulations as what was abolished
- The consistent usage pattern: civil decrees or ceremonial regulations

### dogmatizo — G1379
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1379-dogmatizo.md`
- Hapax — only Col 2:20
- Verbal form of dogma: "to be subject to ordinances"
- Same root connects v.14 (dogmasin) to v.20 (dogmatizesthe) — forming a vocabulary chain within the passage

### skia — G4639
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4639-skia.md`
- 7 NT occurrences total
- 3 theological uses (shadow of spiritual reality): Col 2:17, Heb 8:5, Heb 10:1
- ALL three theological uses apply exclusively to the ceremonial system
- No NT passage calls the Decalogue, the weekly Sabbath, or any moral law element a "shadow"
- Contrasted with soma (body/substance) in Col 2:17, and with eikōn (image/reality) in Heb 10:1

### soma — G4983
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4983-soma.md`
- 122 unique NT verses, 146 tokens
- Primary meaning: physical body (95% of occurrences)
- In Col 2:17: used metaphorically as "substance/reality" in contrast to skia (shadow)
- The phrase "to de soma tou Christou" = "but the body/substance belongs to Christ"
- This is the only place where soma clearly means "substance" vs. "shadow" in a typological context

### typos — G5179
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5179-typos.md`
- In Heb 8:5: "the pattern (typon) shewed to thee in the mount"
- Moses saw the heavenly original and built the earthly copy according to it
- The type (earthly) is patterned after the antitype (heavenly original)

### antitypon — G499
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G499-antitypon.md`
- In Heb 9:24: the earthly holy places are "antitypa of the true" (heavenly)
- The earthly is the figure/copy; the heavenly is the "true" (alethinos)
- This confirms: the heavenly sanctuary is the ORIGINAL, not a derivative of the earthly

### mello — G3195
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3195-mello.md`
- In Col 2:17: "ton mellonton" = "of the things coming/about to be"
- Present active participle, Gen Pl N — describes things that are in the process of coming
- Also in Heb 10:1: "mellontōn agathōn" = "good things to come"
- The shadow pointed to realities that were still COMING — not just the cross event but the entire fulfillment in Christ's ministry

### katharizo — G2511
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2511-katharizo.md`
- In Heb 9:23: katharizesthai (Present Passive Infinitive) = "to be purified"
- LXX equivalent of Hebrew taher (H2891), the standard Levitical purification verb
- Used in Lev 16:19,30 (LXX) for the DOA cleansing
- The author of Hebrews deliberately uses Levitical purification vocabulary for the heavenly action

### katargeo — G2673
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2673-katargeo.md`
- In Eph 2:15: katargesas = "having abolished" the law of commandments in ordinances
- In Rom 3:31: katargoumen = "do we make void the law?" — emphatic denial (me genoito)
- Same author, same verb: abolishes dogma-qualified ordinances while denying he abolishes the law

### WG-shadow Word Group
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-shadow.md`
- The shadow word group: skia (shadow), typos (type/pattern), antitypon (antitype/figure), hupodeigma (copy/example), parabolē (figure)
- Theological progression: shadow → image → reality
- The shadow is preliminary; the substance is permanent. Abolishing the shadow confirms that the substance has arrived.

### WG-law Word Group
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-law.md`
- Key distinction: dogma (ceremonial decrees) vs. entolē (commandments) vs. nomos (law in general)
- Dogma is NEVER used for the Decalogue; entolē is used for both categories depending on context
- Paul's vocabulary is consistent: dogma-qualified law is abolished; entolē theou (commandments of God) continues

### hypenantios — G5227
**Source:** Fresh lookup (search_strongs.py)
- 2 NT occurrences: Col 2:14, Heb 10:27
- Adjective: "contrary, opposed, adverse"
- In Col 2:14: describes the cheirographon as "contrary to us" (hypenantios hēmin)
- In Heb 10:27: "which shall devour the adversaries" (tous hypenantious)
- LXX background: Isa 26:11 — the fire of God's adversaries
- Significance: The cheirographon is described as actively hostile — "against us" (kath' hēmōn) AND "contrary to us" (hypenantios). The moral law is called "holy, just, and good" (Rom 7:12) — never "against" or "contrary to" believers.

### exaleipho — G1813
**Source:** Fresh lookup (search_strongs.py)
- 5-6 NT occurrences: Acts 3:19, Col 2:14, Rev 3:5, Rev 7:17, Rev 21:4
- Verb: "to smear out, obliterate, erase, wipe away"
- In Col 2:14: Aorist Active Participle — "having blotted out" (completed action)
- In Acts 3:19: "that your sins may be blotted out" — same verb for sin-erasure
- In Rev 3:5: "I will not blot out his name out of the book of life"
- In Rev 7:17; 21:4: "God shall wipe away all tears"
- LXX background: Psa 51:1,9 (blot out transgressions); Isa 43:25 ("I, even I, am he that blotteth out thy transgressions")
- The verb consistently describes the permanent erasure of a written record — whether sins (Acts 3:19), names (Rev 3:5), or the cheirographon (Col 2:14)

### proseloo — G4338
**Source:** Fresh lookup (search_strongs.py)
- Hapax legomenon — only Col 2:14
- Verb: "to peg to, spike fast, nail"
- From pros (G4314, "toward") + hēlos (a nail)
- Aorist Active Participle: "having nailed" — completed action
- The imagery: a Roman legal practice — a cancelled debt or decree was nailed up publicly to signal its annulment. Christ nailed the cheirographon to the cross, publicly cancelling the debt document.

### anankē — G318
**Source:** Fresh lookup (context from Heb 9:23 parsing)
- In Heb 9:23: Nom Sg F — "Necessity" (subject of the sentence)
- The word opens the verse emphatically: "It was NECESSARY" (anankē oun)
- The logical chain: because blood is required for remission (v.22), THEREFORE the heavenly things necessarily require purification with better sacrifices
- This makes the heavenly purification non-optional — it follows by logical necessity from the type/antitype relationship

### hupodeigma — G5262
**Source:** Fresh lookup (from Heb 9:23 and 8:5)
- In Heb 9:23: "the patterns (hupodeigmata) of things in the heavens" = the earthly copies
- In Heb 8:5: "serve unto the example (hupodeigmati) and shadow of heavenly things"
- Meaning: a representation, copy, or model — the earthly sanctuary is a hupodeigma of the heavenly
