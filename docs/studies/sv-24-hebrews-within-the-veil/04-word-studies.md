## From Word Studies Library

### katapetasma (G2665) — veil
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2665-katapetasma.md`

- **Definition:** From kata + petannymi — "something spread down"; a curtain/veil
- **Occurrences:** 6 in NT (4 books: Matt, Mark, Luke, Hebrews)
- **KJV Translation:** "veil" 100% of occurrences
- **Distribution:** 3 in Hebrews (50%), 1 each in Matt, Mark, Luke
- **Two semantic contexts:** (1) Synoptic crucifixion — torn veil (Matt 27:51; Mark 15:38; Luke 23:45); (2) Hebrews veil-theology (Heb 6:19; 9:3; 10:20)
- **Hebrew Source via LXX:** H6532 poreketh (22 co-occurrences, PMI 9.45) = primary; H4539 macak (8 co-occurrences, PMI 8.06) = secondary
- **Critical finding:** The LXX uses katapetasma for BOTH poreketh (inner veil) and macak (outer screen). This means katapetasma in Greek does NOT automatically = inner veil. Heb 9:3's specification "the second veil" (to deuteron katapetasma) implies the author knew the Greek word was ambiguous and needed the ordinal qualifier to specify which veil.
- **Key collocations:** naos (temple) 50%, schizo (rend) 50%, esoteros (within) 17%, enkainizo (consecrate) 17%

### prodromos (G4274) — forerunner
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4274-prodromos.md`

- **Definition:** From pro + dromos (race/course) — "one who runs ahead"
- **Occurrences:** 1 in NT (Heb 6:20 only) — hapax legomenon
- **Morphology:** Nominative Singular Masculine — predicate nominative describing Jesus
- **Context:** "Whither the forerunner is for us entered, even Jesus" — hyper hemon ("on our behalf") emphasizes the representative nature
- **Theological import:** A forerunner implies others will follow. This is fundamentally different from the DOA model where the high priest enters alone (Heb 9:7, "monos") and no one follows into the MHP. Prodromos transforms the access model from solitary to inaugural.
- **Related words:** archegos (G747, "captain/author" in Heb 2:10; 12:2) — both describe Jesus as pioneer, but archegos = leader/originator; prodromos = one who enters ahead so others may follow

### esoteros (G2082) — inner / more inner
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2082-esoteros.md`

- **Definition:** Comparative adjective from eso (G2080, "inside") — "more inner"
- **Occurrences:** 2 in NT (dis legomenon)
  - Acts 16:24 — "inner prison" (ten esoteran phylaken) — attributive, modifies prison
  - Heb 6:19 — "that within the veil" (to esoteron tou katapetasmatos) — substantivized neuter
- **Morphology:** Both accusative; gender matches modified noun (F in Acts = prison; N in Heb = substantivized space)
- **Hebrew source via LXX:** H6442 peniymiyi (21 co-occurrences, PMI 9.10) — standard Hebrew adjective for "inner" in temple/tabernacle architecture
- **Comparative semantics:** Morphologically comparative (-teros suffix), but in Koine the spatial comparatives (esoteros, exoteros, anoteros) function as simple positional adjectives — "inner" rather than "more inner." No positive-degree adjective form exists; the adverb eso serves as the base.
- **Antonym:** G1857 exoteros ("outer") — all 3 NT uses in Matthew's "outer darkness" formula. Inner/outer pair maps a spatial theology: esoteros = divine presence; exoteros = exclusion.

### hagion (G39) — holy place / sanctuary
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G39-hagion.md`

- **Definition:** Substantivized neuter of hagios (G40); a sacred place/sanctuary
- **Occurrences:** 10 in NT — ALL in Hebrews (exclusively Hebrews vocabulary)
- **The ta hagia ambiguity:** The neuter plural ta hagia is used for four different referents:
  1. **Entire sanctuary** — Heb 8:2 ("minister of the sanctuary"); 9:1 ("worldly sanctuary"); 9:24 ("holy places made with hands")
  2. **First compartment (Holy Place)** — Heb 9:2 ("called Hagia/the sanctuary"); 9:6 (priests enter "the first tabernacle")
  3. **Second compartment (Most Holy Place)** — Heb 9:3 ("Hagia hagion" = Holy of Holies); 9:8 ("way into the holiest"); 10:19 ("boldness to enter the holiest")
  4. **Christ's entrance into heavenly sanctuary** — Heb 9:12 ("entered into ta hagia"); 9:25 ("entereth into ta hagia")
- **Critical for this study:** The SAME Greek form (ta hagia) denotes different spaces. Context alone determines the referent. This means Heb 9:12's "entered into ta hagia" could mean the entire sanctuary, the Holy Place, or the Most Holy Place — the grammar alone does not resolve it.

### kathizo (G2523) — sit down
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2523-kathizo.md`

- **Occurrences:** 46 unique verses, 10 books
- **Hebrews occurrences (4):**
  - Heb 1:3 — ekathisen (aorist) — "sat down on the right hand of the Majesty"
  - Heb 8:1 — ekathisen (aorist) — "who is set on the right hand of the throne"
  - Heb 10:12 — ekathisen (aorist) — "sat down on the right hand of God"
  - Heb 12:2 — kekathiken (PERFECT) — "is set down at the right hand of the throne of God"
- **Pattern:** All 4 Hebrews uses describe Christ's enthronement. First 3 are aorist (punctiliar act of sitting), the 4th is perfect (resulting state of being seated). Every instance includes "right hand" (dexios) language. Every instance is connected to completed sacrifice.
- **Louw-Nida domains:** Sit (physical posture), Control/Rule (authority), Assign to Role (enthronement), High Status (rank)

### kathemai (G2521) — be seated
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2521-kathemai.md`

- **Occurrences:** 87 unique verses, 10 books. Highest in Revelation (32, 7.9/100v).
- **Only 1 Hebrews use:** Heb 1:13 — "Sit (kathou, imperative) on my right hand" (Psa 110:1 quotation)
- **Form distribution:** 71% present participle (substantive: "the one sitting")
- **Distinct from kathizo:** kathemai = the state of being seated (deponent); kathizo = the act of sitting down (active). In Hebrews, kathizo (the act) dominates for Christ's enthronement; kathemai appears only in the Psalm quotation.

### entynchano (G1793) — intercede
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1793-entynchano.md`

- **Occurrences:** 5 verses (Acts, Romans, Hebrews)
- **Key use in Heb 7:25:** entynchanein — Present Active Infinitive — "to make intercession" (ongoing, continuous)
- **Tense pattern:** 4 of 5 uses are present tense, emphasizing ONGOING intercession. This directly addresses the sitting-vs-serving question: Christ "sat down" (aorist — completed act) but "ever liveth to make intercession" (present — ongoing activity).
- **Key collocation:** dexios (G1188, "right hand") in Rom 8:34 — "at the right hand of God, who also maketh intercession" — intercession happens FROM the seated position at God's right hand.

### enkainizo (G1457) — inaugurate / consecrate
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1457-enkainizo.md`

- **Occurrences:** 2 in NT — BOTH in Hebrews
- **Heb 9:18:** enkekainistai (Perfect Passive) — first covenant "was dedicated" (resulting state)
- **Heb 10:20:** enekainisen (Aorist Active) — "he hath consecrated" a new way (completed act)

### poreketh (H6532) — veil / inner curtain
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6532-poreketh.md`

- **Definition:** Feminine active participle — "separatrix" (that which separates)
- **Occurrences:** 25 tokens, 23 unique verses (Exo 13, Lev 7, Num 2, 2 Chr 1)
- **KJV Translation:** "vail" 100% — no variation
- **ALL occurrences refer to the inner curtain** dividing Holy Place from Most Holy Place:
  - Construction: Exo 26:31,33; 36:35; 2 Chr 3:14
  - Spatial divider: Exo 26:33 — "the vail shall divide between the holy and the most holy"
  - Access to ark/mercy seat: Exo 30:6; 40:3,21; Lev 16:2,12,15
  - Blood sprinkling before veil: Lev 4:6,17
  - Positional reference ("without the vail"): Exo 27:21; 40:22,26; Lev 24:3
  - Priestly qualification: Lev 21:23; Num 18:7
- **CRITICAL FINDING for Ballenger's claim:** Every OT occurrence of paroketh (H6532) refers to the inner veil separating holy from most holy. The first curtain (at the tabernacle entrance) is never called paroketh — it is always macak (H4539) or described as the "hanging for the door."
- **LXX mapping:** katapetasma (G2665) = 22 of 25 occurrences (PMI 9.45)

### macak (H4539) — screen / hanging / covering
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4539-macak.md` (newly generated)

- **Definition:** From cakak (H5526, "to cover") — a cover, screen, hanging
- **Occurrences:** 25 verses (Exo 16, Num 6, 2 Sam 1, Psa 1, Isa 1)
- **KJV Translation:** "hanging" 68%, "covering" 28%, "curtain" 4%
- **Semantic categories:**
  1. Screen for tabernacle entrance (Exo 26:36; 36:37; 40:28)
  2. Screen for court gate (Exo 27:16; 38:18; 40:8,33)
  3. Covering/shield for the ark in transit (Exo 35:12; 39:34; 40:21; Num 4:5)
  4. Levitical transport responsibility (Num 3:25,26; 4:25)
  5. Non-cultic/figurative (2 Sam 17:19; Psa 105:39; Isa 22:8)
- **LXX mapping:** katapetasma (G2665) = 8 co-occurrences (PMI 8.06); kalyma (G2571) = 5
- **CRITICAL FINDING:** The LXX translates BOTH macak and poreketh as katapetasma. This conflation collapses a distinction the Hebrew preserves. In Hebrew, macak = outer entrance screen; poreketh = inner dividing veil. In Greek, both become katapetasma. This explains why Heb 9:3 must specify "the second katapetasma" — the Greek word alone doesn't distinguish which curtain.

## Grammar Reference

### Greek Comparative Adjective with Definite Article
**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/greek/comparative-adjective-with-article.md`

Key findings for this study:
- **Heb 6:19** — to esoteron (T-ASN + A-ASN-C): article substantivizes the comparative, yielding "the more-inner [place/area]." The genitive tou katapetasmatos specifies what the inner space is relative to.
- **Koine comparative = simple positive:** The -teros spatial comparatives (esoteros, exoteros, anoteros) function as basic positional adjectives in Koine. There is no productive superlative or positive-degree form. Esoteros does NOT force a superlative reading ("innermost of two compartments") — it simply means "interior."
- **Acts 16:24 comparison:** ten esoteran phylaken — attributive position (article-adjective-noun). The comparative distinguishes this cell from outer parts: the "more-inner" cell. Same comparative force as Heb 6:19 but in a non-sacred spatial context.
- **Without article = adverbial:** Luke 14:10 anoteron without article = "higher" (adverbially). The article in Heb 6:19 forces a definite, particularized reference — "THE interior [zone] of the veil."
