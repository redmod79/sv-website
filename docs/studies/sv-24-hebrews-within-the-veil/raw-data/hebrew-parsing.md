## Psalm 110:1
Hebrew: לְדָוִד מִזְמוֹר נְאֻם יְהוָה לַאדֹנִי **שֵׁב** לִימִינִי עַד־אָשִׁית אֹיְבֶיךָ הֲדֹם לְרַגְלֶיךָ

| Word | Lemma | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| שֵׁב | ישׁב | Verb.Qal.**Impv**.2ms | sit |
| לִימִינִי | ימין | Noun.fs.Abs+1us | my right-hand side |
| עַד | עד | Prep | unto |
| אָשִׁית | שׁית | Verb.Qal.**Impf**.1s | I will put |
| אֹיְבֶיךָ | איב | Verb.Qal.Ptcp.mp+2ms | your enemies |

**Key:** shev (ישׁב, Qal Imperative 2ms) = "Sit!" — a command from YHWH to Adonai. The verb yashav means to sit, dwell, remain. As an imperative, it commands the Lord to take His seat — an enthronement command. "Until I make" (ad-ashiyth) sets a temporal boundary: the sitting continues until enemies are subdued.

## Zechariah 6:13
Hebrew: וְהוּא **יִבְנֶה** אֶת־הֵיכַל יְהוָה וְהוּא־**יִשָּׂא** הוֹד וְ**יָשַׁב** וּ**מָשַׁל** עַל־כִּסְאוֹ וְ**הָיָה** **כֹהֵן** עַל־כִּסְאוֹ וַעֲצַת שָׁלוֹם תִּהְיֶה בֵּין שְׁנֵיהֶם

| Word | Lemma | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| יִבְנֶה | בנה | Verb.Qal.**Impf**.3ms | he will build |
| יִשָּׂא | נשׂא | Verb.Qal.**Impf**.3ms | he will bear |
| הוֹד | הוד | Noun.ms.Abs | splendor/glory |
| **יָשַׁב** | **ישׁב** | Verb.Qal.**Perf**.3ms | he sat / will sit |
| **מָשַׁל** | **משׁל** | Verb.Qal.**Perf**.3ms | he ruled / will rule |
| כִּסְאוֹ | כסא | Noun.ms+3ms | his throne |
| **הָיָה** | **היה** | Verb.Qal.**Perf**.3ms | he was / will be |
| **כֹהֵן** | **כהן** | Noun.ms.Abs | **priest** |
| כִּסְאוֹ | כסא | Noun.ms+3ms | his throne |
| עֲצַת | עצה | Noun.fs.Cst | counsel of |
| שָׁלוֹם | שׁלום | Noun.ms.Abs | peace |
| שְׁנֵיהֶם | שׁנים | Noun.d+3mp | the two of them |

**Critical findings:**
1. **yashav** (Qal Perfect 3ms) = "he sat/will sit" — same root as Psa 110:1's shev (imperative). In prophetic perfect, the perfect tense depicts a future event as already accomplished.
2. **mashal** (Qal Perfect 3ms) = "he ruled/will rule" — paired with yashav: "he will sit AND rule upon his throne"
3. **hayah kohen** = "he will be a priest" — also on his throne (al-kis'o repeated twice)
4. The verse explicitly COMBINES sitting-ruling with priesting on the SAME throne for the SAME individual.
5. "The counsel of peace between them both" (atsat shalom bein shneyhem) — peace between the two offices (king and priest) in one person, or between the two persons of the oracle (YHWH and the Branch).

## Leviticus 16:2
Hebrew: וְאַל־**יָבֹא** בְכָל־עֵת אֶל־הַ**קֹּדֶשׁ** **מִבֵּית** **לַפָּרֹכֶת** אֶל־פְּנֵי הַ**כַּפֹּרֶת**

| Word | Lemma | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| יָבֹא | בוא | Verb.Qal.Impf.3ms | he will come |
| קֹּדֶשׁ | קדשׁ | Noun.ms.Abs | holiness/holy place |
| מִבֵּית | מן + בית | Prep + Noun.ms.Cst | from the house of |
| לַפָּרֹכֶת | ל + ה + פרכת | Prep + Art + Noun.fs.Abs | to the curtain |
| כַּפֹּרֶת | כפרת | Noun.fs.Abs | mercy seat/cover |

**Key phrase:** mibbeyth lapparoketh = "from within the curtain" (literally "from the house/interior of the curtain"). This is the exact Hebrew construction behind Heb 6:19's "within the veil." The spatial referent is unambiguous: inside the poreketh = inside the Most Holy Place, before the mercy seat on the ark.

## Exodus 26:33
Hebrew: וְהִבְדִּילָה הַפָּרֹכֶת לָכֶם **בֵּין הַקֹּדֶשׁ** **וּבֵין קֹדֶשׁ הַקֳּדָשִׁים**

| Word | Lemma | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| הִבְדִּילָה | בדל | Verb.**Hiphil**.Perf.3fs | she/it separated |
| הַפָּרֹכֶת | פרכת | Noun.fs.Abs | the curtain |
| בֵּין | בין | Noun.ms.Cst | between |
| הַקֹּדֶשׁ | קדשׁ | Noun.ms.Abs | the holy [place] |
| קֹדֶשׁ | קדשׁ | Noun.ms.Cst | holy of |
| הַקֳּדָשִׁים | קדשׁ | Noun.mp.Abs | the holies |

**Critical:** hivdilah (Hiphil of badal, "separate") — the veil SEPARATES between the holy and the holy of holies. This is the same verb used in Genesis 1 for God's creation-acts of separation (light from darkness, waters from waters). The veil performs a creation-level act of division. The poreketh is "the separatrix" — an active participle functioning as a noun.
