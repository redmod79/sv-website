## Ballenger Claim Verification

**Claim:** Paroketh in the Hebrew OT invariably refers to the inner (MHP) veil, never the first apartment.

**Verification result:** CONFIRMED. All 25 occurrences of H6532 paroketh designate the inner curtain between Holy Place and Most Holy Place. The first curtain/screen at the tabernacle entrance is called H4539 macak ("hanging/screen"), never paroketh.

**However:** The LXX translates BOTH paroketh AND macak as katapetasma (G2665). This means the NT's katapetasma is linguistically ambiguous in Greek — it could refer to either curtain. Heb 9:3's "the second katapetasma" implies the author recognized this ambiguity and specified which veil.

**Uriah Smith's counter-argument:** Smith argues both apartments were closed with a veil. This is TRUE physically — both had fabric coverings. But Smith's argument conflates the physical arrangement with the biblical terminology. The Hebrew text always distinguishes: poreketh = inner divider; macak = outer screen. Smith is reasoning from architecture, not from terminology.
