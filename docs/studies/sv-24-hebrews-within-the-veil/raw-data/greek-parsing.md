## Hebrews 9:2-3
**9:2:** σκηνὴ γὰρ κατεσκευάσθη ἡ πρώτη... ἥτις λέγεται **Ἅγια** (A-NPN = Nom Pl Neuter)
- The first tabernacle (skene... he prote) "is called Hagia" — neuter plural nominative

**9:3:** μετὰ δὲ **τὸ δεύτερον καταπέτασμα** (T-ASN + A-ASN + N-ASN) σκηνὴ ἡ λεγομένη **Ἅγια ἁγίων** (A-NPN + A-GPN)
- "After the second veil" — to deuteron (accusative, ordinal number) katapetasma
- "Called Holy of Holies" — Hagia hagion (superlative Hebrew construction in Greek)

**Critical:** The author numbers the veils: "the second" (to deuteron) implies a first. He knows there are two katapetasmata in the tabernacle.

## Hebrews 9:6-8
**9:6:** εἰς μὲν τὴν **πρώτην σκηνὴν** (A-ASF-S + N-ASF) ... εἰσίασιν οἱ **ἱερεῖς** (V-PAI-3P ... N-NPM)
- Priests enter (present tense — continual) "the first tabernacle"

**9:7:** εἰς δὲ τὴν **δευτέραν** (A-ASF) ἅπαξ τοῦ ἐνιαυτοῦ μόνος ὁ ἀρχιερεύς
- "Into the second [skene implied], once a year, the high priest alone"

**9:8:** τοῦτο δηλοῦντος τοῦ Πνεύματος τοῦ Ἁγίου, μήπω πεφανερῶσθαι (V-RPN = Perf Pass Inf) τὴν τῶν **ἁγίων** (A-GPN) **ὁδὸν** (N-ASF) ἔτι τῆς **πρώτης σκηνῆς** (A-GSF-S + N-GSF) **ἐχούσης στάσιν** (V-PAP-GSF + N-ASF)
- "The way into ton hagion" — genitive plural neuter (the holy places / the holiest)
- "The first tabernacle having standing/status" — genitive absolute. stasis can mean "standing" (physical) or "status" (institutional). This is the key ambiguity: does "first tabernacle" = architectural first compartment (blocking physical access) or institutional first-covenant system (having legal status)?

## Hebrews 9:24
οὐ γὰρ εἰς χειροποίητα **εἰσῆλθεν** (V-2AAI-3S) ἅγια Χριστός, **ἀντίτυπα** (A-APN) τῶν **ἀληθινῶν** (A-GPN), ἀλλ' εἰς αὐτὸν τὸν **οὐρανόν** (N-ASM)
- "Not into hand-made holy places... but into heaven itself"
- antitypa ton alethinon — "copies of the true things" — the earthly sanctuary is the copy
- The destination is equated with "heaven itself" — not a specific compartment but "the presence of God"
- **emphanisthenai** (V-APN, Aor Pass Inf) to prosopo tou Theou — "to appear to the face of God" — face-to-face presence language

## Hebrews 10:11-12
**10:11:** πᾶς μὲν ἱερεὺς **ἕστηκεν** (V-RAI-3S, **Perf** Act Ind) καθ' ἡμέραν **λειτουργῶν** (V-PAP-NSM, Pres Act Ptcp) καὶ ... **προσφέρων** (V-PAP-NSM)
- hesteken = PERFECT tense — "stands" as completed/ongoing state. The perfect indicates standing is the priest's characteristic condition.
- leitourgon = present participle — "ministering" (ongoing)
- prospheron = present participle — "offering" (ongoing)

**10:12:** οὗτος δὲ μίαν ὑπὲρ ἁμαρτιῶν **προσενέγκας** (V-AAP-NSM, **Aor** Act Ptcp) θυσίαν εἰς τὸ διηνεκὲς **ἐκάθισεν** (V-AAI-3S, **Aor** Act Ind) ἐν δεξιᾷ τοῦ Θεοῦ
- prosenkengas = AORIST participle — "having offered" (prior, completed)
- ekathisen = AORIST indicative — "sat down" (completed act)
- Tense contrast: Perfect standing (ongoing condition) vs. Aorist sitting (completed act following completed offering)

## Hebrews 10:19-20
**10:19:** Ἔχοντες (V-PAP-NPM) οὖν... **παρρησίαν** (N-ASF) εἰς τὴν εἴσοδον τῶν **ἁγίων** (A-GPN) ἐν τῷ αἵματι Ἰησοῦ
- parrhesian eis ten eisodon ton hagion — "boldness for the entrance of the holy places"
- ton hagion = genitive plural neuter — same ambiguous form

**10:20:** ἣν **ἐνεκαίνισεν** (V-AAI-3S, Aor Act Ind) ἡμῖν ὁδὸν πρόσφατον καὶ ζῶσαν (V-PAP-ASF) **διὰ** τοῦ **καταπετάσματος** (N-GSN), τοῦτ' ἔστιν τῆς **σαρκὸς** (N-GSF) αὐτοῦ
- enekainisen — aorist: "he inaugurated" (completed act of opening)
- dia tou katapetasmatos — "through the veil"
- tout' estin tes sarkos autou — "that is, his flesh"
- **Gender mismatch:** katapetasmatos is neuter genitive; sarkos is feminine genitive. The apposition crosses genders, suggesting the identification veil = flesh is theological/metaphorical, not grammatical.

## Hebrews 8:1-2
**8:1:** Κεφάλαιον (N-NSN) ... ὃς **ἐκάθισεν** (V-AAI-3S, Aor Act Ind) ἐν δεξιᾷ τοῦ **θρόνου** (N-GSM) τῆς Μεγαλωσύνης ἐν τοῖς οὐρανοῖς
- kephalaion = "the main point / sum" — this is the thesis statement
- ekathisen = aorist: "sat down" at the right hand of the throne in the heavens

**8:2:** τῶν ἁγίων **λειτουργὸς** (N-NSM) καὶ τῆς σκηνῆς τῆς ἀληθινῆς
- leitourgos ton hagion — "minister of the holy places/sanctuary"
- The one who SAT DOWN (8:1) IS a leitourgos (8:2). Sitting and ministering are combined in the same sentence.

## Hebrews 1:3

## Hebrews 12:2
ὃς... ὑπέμεινεν σταυρὸν αἰσχύνης καταφρονήσας, ἐν δεξιᾷ τε τοῦ θρόνου τοῦ Θεοῦ **κεκάθικεν** (V-RAI-3S, **Perf** Act Ind)
- kekathiken = PERFECT tense — "has sat down / is seated" (resulting state, ongoing)
- This is the ONLY Hebrews "sitting" passage in the perfect tense. The others are aorist. Perfect = the sitting has ongoing relevance and continues as a present state.
