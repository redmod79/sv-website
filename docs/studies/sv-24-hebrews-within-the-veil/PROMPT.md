# Bible Study: Hebrews 6:19-20 and the "Within the Veil" Question

## Question
What does Hebrews 6:19-20 mean by "within the veil" (esoteron tou katapetasmatos)? Does katapetasma refer to the first or second veil? How does the LXX use katapetasma? When the author says Jesus entered as "forerunner" (prodromos), does this mean He entered the most holy place at ascension? Compare with Heb 9:3, 9:6-8, 9:12, 9:24, 10:19-20. Two-apartment spatial model or two-covenant temporal model? How does Melchizedek king-priest typology explain sitting while serving?

## Prior Research Summary

**Seven prior studies** directly inform this investigation:

1. **sanc-08-veil-access** — Established that paroketh (H6532) is an active participle meaning "separatrix" with the LXX consistently translating it as katapetasma (G2665, 22/25 occurrences, PMI 9.45). All six NT uses of katapetasma divide into Synoptic veil-rending (Matt 27:51; Mark 15:38; Luke 23:45) and Hebrews veil-theology (Heb 6:19; 9:3; 10:20). Prodromos (G4274) transforms the DOA access model from solitary to inaugural. Esoteros (G2082) designates the space beyond the veil. The rending did NOT abolish the two-compartment structure. **Open question**: whether Heb 6:19's katapetasma refers to the first or second veil — the LXX usage needs systematic analysis.

2. **atone-12** — Covered Hebrews 9:1-10:25 comprehensively. The standing/sitting contrast (10:11-12) is specifically about offering sacrifices. Heb 10:14 has deliberate tense contrast (teteleiooken perfect vs. hagiazomenous present). Heb 9:23 uses katharizo (LXX equivalent of taher from Lev 16). Heb 9:8 is ambiguous between first compartment (architectural) and first-covenant system (covenantal).

3. **sanc-21-hebrews-heavenly-ministry** — Directly addresses the sitting-while-serving tension. Ekathisen (aorist, "sat down") vs. entygchanein (present infinitive, "to intercede") are both in the author's sustained argument. Hebrews 8:1-2 explicitly COMBINES sitting with ministering. Psalm 110 grounds both the sitting (v.1) and the priesting (v.4). Three distinguishable phases: inauguration, intercession, judgment/DOA consummation. Ta hagia (neuter plural) is used 10 times in Hebrews for different referents — context must decide.

4. **sanc-22-heavenly-things-purified** — Heb 9:23 → Dan 8:14 connection is complementary vocabulary: Hebrews uses katharizo (priestly purification); Daniel uses nitsdaq (judicial vindication). Three dimensions of heavenly purification established.

5. **sv-08** — Six independent lines confirm the Dan 8 sanctuary is heavenly. Hebrews 8:1-2 is the kephalaion (thesis statement). Christ is leitourgos of ton hagion.

6. **sv-17** — The Dan 7/Lev 16 parallel is structural, not lexical. The kaphar-to-tsadaq progression through five stages bridges DOA and judgment.

7. **sv-18** — nitsdaq is Niphal of tsadaq ("to be vindicated"). Vindication through three phases: cross (ground), intercession (application), judgment (declaration). The sitting/serving question is directly relevant.

**External corpus claims to investigate:**
- Ballenger argues paroketh in the Hebrew OT invariably refers to the inner (MHP) veil, never the first apartment. Claims "within the veil" always = MHP.
- Ballenger concedes LXX sometimes uses katapetasma for the first curtain but only "in directions for making and moving" with qualifiers. Unqualified katapetasma = inner veil.
- Uriah Smith counter-argues that both apartments were closed with a veil, so "within the veil" could apply to either.
- Bohr argues Aaron defines the FUNCTIONS of Jesus' priesthood but the RIGHT comes from Melchizedek. As priest He stands; as king He sits.
- Bohr and RSS argue Psalm 110:1-4 unifies sitting (v.1) with priesting (v.4). Zechariah 6:12-13 explicitly combines sitting with priesting on the throne.
