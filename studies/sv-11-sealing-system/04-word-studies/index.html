    Word Studies¶

    From Word Studies Library¶

    satham — H5640¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5640-catham.md

    • Original: סָתַם — primitive root
    • Transliteration: satham (saw-tham')
    • POS: Verb — 17 tokens, 14 unique verses, 8 books
    • Core meaning: to stop up; by implication, to repair; figuratively, to keep secret
    • Stem distribution: Qal 10 (77%), Piel 2, Niphal 1

    Semantic categories: 1. Physical blocking of water sources (Gen 26:15,18; 2 Kgs 3:19,25; 2 Chr 32:3,4,30) — filling wells/springs to deny access. This is the literal prototype: you fill the opening so nothing passes through. 2. Repair of breaches (Neh 4:7) — stopping gaps in a wall 3. Prophetic sealing (Dan 8:26; 12:4; 12:9) — shutting up words/visions until end. Always paired with chatham in Daniel. 4. Hiddenness/secrecy (Psa 51:6; Eze 28:3) — concealed knowledge 5. Blocking access to God (Lam 3:8) — prayer shut out

    Key distinction from chatham: satham blocks access to content (the well metaphor — nothing flows through). Chatham secures the container (the signet impression — proves authenticity and prevents tampering). Dan 12:4 uses BOTH because two operations occur on two objects: satham blocks the words (cognitive inaccessibility), chatham seals the book (documentary integrity).

    LXX mapping: σφραγίζω (sphragizo) in Daniel — the LXX collapses the Hebrew distinction between the two verbs.

    chatham — H2856¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md

    • Original: חָתַם — primitive root
    • Transliteration: chatham (khaw-tham')
    • POS: Verb — 26 tokens, 24 unique verses, 11 books
    • Core meaning: to close up; especially to seal
    • Stem distribution: Qal 22, Niphal 2, Piel 1, Hiphil 1

    Semantic categories: 1. Legal and documentary sealing (1 Kgs 21:8; Jer 32:10; Est 8:10; Neh 9:38) — impressing a signet ring to authenticate 2. Divine sealing of hidden things (Deut 32:34; Job 14:17; 9:7; 37:7) — God stores up transgressions, controls stars 3. Prophetic sealing of revelation (Isa 8:16; 29:11; Dan 9:24; 12:4) — preserving vision for fulfillment 4. Closure and enclosure (Lev 15:3; Song 4:12; Ezek 28:12)

    Collocations: sepher (H5612, 33%) — "seal the book"; kathab (H3789, 29%) — "write then seal"; melek (H4428, 17%) — royal authentication.

    Key Daniel uses: - Dan 9:24: la-chatom chazon we-nabi — "to seal up vision and prophecy" — infinitive of purpose; AUTHENTICATION sense (fulfillment validates) - Dan 12:4: wa-chatom ha-sepher — "and seal the book" — imperative; CONCEALMENT sense (document preserved until opener) - Dan 12:9: chatumim — passive participle; STATE report — the sealed condition persists

    chatham (Aramaic) — H2857 / A2857¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A2857-chatham.md (newly generated)

    • Original: חֲתַם — Aramaic cognate of H2856
    • Transliteration: chatham (khath-am')
    • Occurrences: 1 unique verse — Dan 6:17 (hapax in Aramaic)
    • Context: "the king sealed it with his own signet, and with the signet of his lords; that the purpose might not be changed"
    • Significance: Daniel's personal experience of physical sealing. The stone over the lion's den was sealed with multiple signet rings to make it tamper-proof. This creates an experiential bridge: Daniel knew what physical sealing meant before receiving the command to seal prophetic content.

    chowtham — H2368¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2368-chowtham.md (newly generated)

    • Original: חוֹתָם — noun from root חתם
    • Transliteration: chowtham (kho-thawm')
    • POS: Masculine noun — 13 unique verses, 7 books
    • Core meaning: The signet ring itself — the physical instrument used for sealing

    Semantic categories: 1. Standard of engraving (Exo 28:11,21,36; 39:6,14,30) — "engravings of a signet" = finest craftsmanship 2. Personal identity token (Gen 38:18) — Judah's signet as irrefutable proof of identity 3. Instrument of royal authority (1 Kgs 21:8) — Jezebel using Ahab's seal 4. Metaphor for intimacy (Song 8:6; Jer 22:24; Hag 2:23) — set as a seal upon the heart/hand

    Hag 2:23 / Jer 22:24 reversal: God removes Coniah like tearing off a signet (rejection), then restores the image for Zerubbabel (election). The signet-as-person metaphor grounds the seal concept in personal relationship, not merely bureaucratic procedure.

    WG-sealing — Word Group: Sealing¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-sealing.md

    Strong's Word Role Occurrences
    H5640 satham Paired concealment verb 14 OT
    H2856 chatham Primary seal verb 23 OT
    H2368 chowtham Seal instrument (noun) 13 OT
    H2857 chatham (Aram.) Aramaic equivalent 1 OT
    G4972 sphragizo NT seal verb 25 NT
    G4973 sphragis NT seal noun 16 NT
    G2696 katasphragizo Intensive seal verb 1 NT (hapax)

    LXX bridge: H2856 → G4972 (PMI 9.53); H2368 → G4973. The Hebrew two-verb distinction (satham/chatham) collapses into a single Greek verb (sphragizo) in the LXX.

    sphragizo — G4972¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4972-sphragizo.md

    • Occurrences: 25 NT, heavily concentrated in Revelation (72%)
    • Semantic categories: Physical sealing (Mat 27:66, Rev 20:3); Authentication (Jhn 3:33, 6:27); Spirit-sealing of believers (Eph 1:13, 4:30; 2 Cor 1:22); Prophetic sealing (Rev 10:4, 22:10); Protective sealing (Rev 7:3-8)
    • Rev 22:10: μὴ σφραγίσῃς — Aorist Active Subjunctive 2nd Singular = prohibitive subjunctive ("do not seal"). This is the strongest form of negative command in Greek (μή + aorist subjunctive).

    sphragis — G4973¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md

    • Occurrences: 16 NT, 81% in Revelation
    • Three main uses: Authentication (Rom 4:11; 1 Cor 9:2; 2 Tim 2:19); The sealed heavenly scroll (Rev 5:1-2,5,9); Sequential seal-openings (Rev 6:1-8:1)
    • Rev 5:1: "sealed with seven seals" — maximum sealing, using both katasphragizo (verb) and sphragis (noun) in one clause

    katasphragizo — G2696¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2696-katasphragizo.md

    • HAPAX — appears only in Rev 5:1
    • Root: kata (intensive) + sphragizo — to seal COMPLETELY
    • Form: κατεσφραγισμένον — Perfect Passive Participle, Accusative Singular Neuter
    • Force: The perfect aspect shows completed, settled sealing. The kata- prefix intensifies: not merely sealed but thoroughly, completely sealed. Seven seals compound the intensity.
    • LXX parallel: Job 9:7 — God "sealeth up the stars" (katasphragizei)

    anoigo — G455¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G455-anoigo.md

    • Occurrences: 78 tokens, 75 verses, 9 books — concentrated in Revelation (25 uses, 6.2 per 100 verses)
    • Key form in Rev 10:2: ἠνεῳγμένον — Perfect Passive Participle ("having been opened" = permanently in an open state). The perfect tense is crucial: the little book is not being opened; it HAS BEEN opened and REMAINS open.
    • Seal-opening sequence: Rev 6:1-8:1 uses aorist active indicative (completed action by the Lamb as agent)

    WG-opened — Word Group: Opening/Access¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-opened.md

    Key relationships: - anoigo (G455) + sphragis (G4973): 10 shared verses — exclusively Rev 5-8 seal-opening sequence - anoigo (G455) + kleio (G2808): antonym pair — "openeth and no man shutteth; shutteth and no man openeth" (Rev 3:7) - Hebrew source pathach (H6605) → G455 (85x in LXX)

    biyn — H995¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H995-biyn.md

    • Occurrences: 162 verses, highest density in Daniel (5.3 per 100 verses)
    • Stem distribution: Qal 45 (understand), Hiphil 30 (cause to understand), Hithpael 22 (consider carefully)
    • The biyn chain in Daniel: 1:17 → 8:15 → 8:16 → 8:17 → 8:27 → 9:2 → 9:22-23 → 10:1 → 12:8 → 12:10
    • Resolution: Dan 10:1 — "he understood (ubiyn, Qal.Perf.3ms) the thing" — the chain achieves completion

    sakal — H7919¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7919-sakal.md

    • Occurrences: 63 verses, density in Daniel 2.5 per 100 verses
    • Dan 12:3: ha-maskilim — "they that be wise" (Hiphil participle) — the ones who have insight
    • Dan 12:10: "the wise (maskilim) shall understand (yabiynuw)" — sakal + biyn together = the wise who understand, contrasted with the wicked who do not
    • Dan 11:33: "they that understand (maskiley) among the people shall instruct many"

    da'ath — H1847¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1847-daath.md

    • Occurrences: 91 verses, concentrated in Proverbs (43%)
    • Dan 12:4: "knowledge (ha-da'ath) shall be increased" — the article ha- makes it definite: THE knowledge
    • Daniel uses: Dan 1:4 (knowledge and skill), Dan 1:17 (God gave them knowledge), Dan 12:4
    • Context determines scope: In Dan 12:4's context (sealed words + running to and fro to seek + time of the end), the knowledge that increases is most naturally the prophetic knowledge that was sealed. Compare Amos 8:12 where the same verb shuwt (run to and fro) is paired with seeking "the word of the LORD."

    shuwt — H7751¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7751-shuwt.md

    • Occurrences: 13 verses — rare
    • Piel/Polel (intensive): purposeful, searching motion — "run to and fro"
    • Dan 12:4: yeshotetuv (Piel.Impf.3mp) — "many shall run to and fro" — intensive searching
    • Amos 8:12: yeshotetuv (same form) + "to seek the word of the LORD, and shall not find it" — identical verb, opposite outcome. Amos depicts a famine-of-the-word era (searching yields nothing); Dan 12:4 depicts a knowledge-increase era (searching succeeds).
    • Zech 4:10: "the eyes of the LORD... run to and fro (meshotetuv) through the whole earth" — divine comprehensive inspection
    • 2 Sam 24:2: "go to and fro (shutu) through all the tribes" — survey/census motion

    emeth — H571¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H571-emeth.md

    • Occurrences: 125 verses, concentrated in Psalms (37)
    • Root: from aman (H539, "be firm, faithful")
    • Dan 8:26: "emeth hu" — "truth is it" — predicate-noun construction (stronger than adjectival "true")
    • Dan 10:1: "we-emeth ha-dabar" — "and the thing was truth"
    • Attestation chain: Dan 8:26 declares the mar'eh "emeth hu" → Rev 22:6 declares the sayings "pistoi kai alethinoi" (faithful and true)

    alethinos — G228¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md

    • Occurrences: 27 tokens, concentrated in John (10) and Revelation (10)
    • Root: alpha-privative + lanthano ("to escape notice") = "unhidden"
    • Rev 22:6: "These sayings are faithful (pistoi) and true (alethinoi)"
    • Key pairing: pistos + alethinos = "faithful and true" — a Christological title (Rev 19:11) and an authentication formula for prophetic content

    pistos — G4103¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4103-pistos.md

    • Occurrences: 62 verses
    • Rev 22:6: pistos kai alethinos — "faithful and true" — the attestation formula that bookends the canon with Dan 8:26's emeth hu

    WG-truth — Word Group: Truth/True¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-truth.md

    • LXX bridge: H571 emeth → G225 aletheia (83x), G228 alethinos (13x), G227 alethes (5x)
    • Co-occurrences: alethinos + pistos in 5 verses — the "Faithful and True" title
    • The emet→alethinos chain: Dan 8:26 (emeth hu) → Dan 10:1 (emeth ha-dabar) → Rev 22:6 (pistoi kai alethinoi) → Rev 22:10 (seal not) forms a truth-declaration inclusio around the sealed-to-unsealed arc

    anakalypto — G343¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G343-anakalypto.md (newly generated)

    • Root: ana (reversal) + kalypto (to cover/veil) = to unveil
    • Occurrences: 2 (both in 2 Cor 3:14,18) — the veil-of-Moses discourse
    • 2 Cor 3:14: "the same vail untaken away (me anakalyptomenon)" — present passive participle with negative: the veil is NOT being unveiled when reading the old covenant apart from Christ
    • 2 Cor 3:18: "with open (anakekalymmenō) face beholding" — perfect passive participle: the unveiled state is COMPLETE for those in Christ
    • Relevance to sealing: The veil/unveiling concept parallels the seal/unseal concept. Just as Daniel's sealed words await the time of the end to be understood, the old covenant's meaning awaits Christ for its veil to be removed.

    H2858 chothemeth — signet ring (feminine form)¶

    Not found in the library. This is the feminine counterpart to chowtham (H2368). Per Strong's: a seal (used as a signet ring). Appears only in Gen 38:25 — "the signet, and bracelets, and staff" — Judah's identification tokens.

    From Grammar Reference Library¶

    Seal-Command Syntax in Daniel¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/seal-command-syntax-daniel.md

    Four constructions compared: 1. Dan 8:26 — Single imperative (satham only) + definite object (the chazon) + no explicit terminus (duration: yamim rabbim) 2. Dan 12:4 — Dual imperative (satham + chatham) + two distinct objects (words + book) + explicit terminus (ad eth qets) 3. Dan 12:9 — Dual passive participle (setumim + chatumim) + definite subject (the words) + same terminus (ad eth qets) 4. Dan 9:24 — Infinitive of purpose (la-chatom) + double object (chazon + nabi) = authentication, not concealment

    Progression: 8:26 (conceal, command) → 9:24 (authenticate, purpose) → 12:4 (conceal + authenticate, command) → 12:9 (concealed + authenticated, state report). The commands of 8:26 and 12:4 are fulfilled by the state reported in 12:9.

    Dan 8:26 Grammar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-26.md

    Key features: (1) mar'eh ha-erev we-ha-boqer — single construct head with double articled absolute = one compound referent, not two visions; (2) emeth hu — predicate-noun authentication formula; (3) setom he-chazon — Qal.Impv.2ms of satham, concealment command; (4) ki le-yamim rabbim — verbless causal clause, durational but without defined terminus.

    Dan 12:4 Grammar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-12-4-sealing.md

    Key features: (1) Dual imperatives satham/chatham on distinct objects; (2) ad-eth qets as terminus ad quem (state terminates when boundary crossed); (3) Post-terminus coda: yeshotetuv rabbim we-tirbeh ha-da'ath — two yiqtol (imperfective) forms describing progressive activity after the terminus.

    Dan 12:9 Grammar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-12-9-sealing.md

    Key features: (1) Shift from active imperative (12:4) to passive participle (12:9) — the command has been executed, the state now obtains; (2) Dismissal formula lek — "go thy way" = case closed; (3) ki-causal ground: the sealed state IS the reason further inquiry is futile; (4) The state is temporally bounded (ad eth qets) — null at terminus.

    Dan 9:24 Grammar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-9-24-27.md

    Key feature: la-chatom chazon we-nabi — Qal infinitive construct with le, one of six purpose-infinitives under nechtakh. The sealing here is authentication through fulfillment, not concealment. The 70 weeks accomplish the ratification of vision and prophet by bringing predictions to determined realization.

    Asyndetic Noun Pair Grammar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/asyndetic-noun-pair.md

    Dan 8:14's erev boqer — two nouns in asyndetic juxtaposition (no conjunction, no article, no construct). They fuse into a single compound time-unit counted by a single cardinal (2,300). Dan 8:26 recites the same pair with articles and conjunction (ha-erev we-ha-boqer) — shifting from numeric unit to anaphoric title.
