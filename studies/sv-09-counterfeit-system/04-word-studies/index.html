    From Word Studies Library¶

    tamid — H8548¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md

    • Original: תָּמִיד | Transliteration: tamid | POS: Masculine noun
    • Occurrences: 104 in OT | Root: unused root meaning "to stretch" — continuance as indefinite extension
    • Definition: Properly, continuance; used attributively as "constant" or adverbially "constantly"; elliptically "the regular (daily) sacrifice"
    • Translation distribution: continually (53, 50.5%), the continual (17, 16.2%), continual (9, 8.6%), the daily (7, 6.7%), always (6, 5.7%)
    • Key distribution: Psalms 23x, Numbers 20x, Daniel 5x. Two usage domains: ritual law (Pentateuch) and spiritual devotion (Psalms/Prophets). Daniel uses tamid substantively ("the daily").
    • 100% positive across all 104 OT uses — never pagan, never negative. Every use describes God's worship system.
    • Daniel uses: Dan 8:11 (taken away), 8:13 (how long?), 11:31 (taken away + replacement installed), 12:11 (taken away)
    • LXX: Rendered as dia pantos (G1223+G3956, "through all time"), not a single lexeme. In cultic contexts: endelechismos (technical coinage for "the continual offering")
    • Top collocations: olah (burnt offering, 26x), panim (before the LORD, 26x), minchah (meal offering, 16x)

    shiqquwts — H8251¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8251-shiqquwts.md

    • Original: שִׁקּוּץ | Transliteration: shiqquwts | POS: Masculine noun
    • Occurrences: 29 in OT | Root: H8262 shaqats (to be filthy, detest)
    • Definition: Disgusting, filthy; especially idolatrous or concretely an idol
    • Translation: abomination(s) (20, 69%), detestable things (6, 21%), abominable (3, 10%)
    • Distribution: Ezekiel 8x, Jeremiah 5x, Daniel 3x — 55% in prophetic literature
    • Daniel uses (Abomination of Desolation formula): Dan 9:27, 11:31, 12:11 — always paired with shamem (H8074)
    • Always concrete objects/practices — idols placed IN sacred space (Jer 7:30; 32:34; Eze 5:11)
    • LXX: bdelygma (G946, 20x) — carried into NT (Matt 24:15; Mark 13:14)

    shamem — H8074¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8074-shamem.md (newly generated)

    • Original: שָׁמֵם | Transliteration: shamem | POS: Verb (primitive root)
    • Occurrences: 80 verses (103 tokens) | Dual semantic field: physical ruination AND psychological stupefaction
    • Stems: Qal 33 (be desolate, be astonished), Niphal 24 (be laid desolate), Hiphil 17 (make desolate), Piel 4 (make desolate — Daniel's agent noun meshomem)
    • Daniel's formula: shiqquts meshomem — the "abomination that MAKES desolate" (Piel participle = active agent of desolation)
    • Dan 8:13: ha-pesha shomem — "the transgression of desolation" (Qal participle)
    • Dan 9:27, 11:31: shiqquts meshomem — the installed replacement that causes desolation
    • Dan 12:11: shiqquts shomem — the desolating abomination
    • LXX: eremoo (G2049, physical ruin) and eremosis (G2050, noun — used in Matt 24:15 "abomination of desolation")
    • Covenant-curse foundation: Lev 26:32-35 — land lies desolate when Israel breaks covenant

    nathan — H5414¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5414-nathan.md

    • Original: נָתַן | POS: Verb (primitive root) | Occurrences: 2,285 tokens
    • Stems: Qal 1,920 (give, put, set), Niphal 82 (be given, be delivered), Hophal 8 (be given)
    • Key for this study — Niphal = passive "be given/delivered": Carries the "delivered into the hand of" idiom
    • Dan 8:12 tinnaten: Niphal.Impf.3fs of nathan — "was given" / "a host was given against the tamid"
    • The divine passive use: God is the implied agent who gives/permits
    • LXX bridge: didomi (G1325, 1,330 co-occurrences, PMI 3.51) — strongest Hebrew-Greek verb bridge

    emeth — H571¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H571-emeth.md

    • Original: אֶמֶת | POS: Adverb/feminine noun | Occurrences: 131 tokens, 23 books
    • Root: H539 aman (to be firm, believe, be faithful) — stability, certainty, truth, trustworthiness
    • Translation: true (18, 14.4%), and truth (17, 13.6%), in truth (14, 11.2%), truth (12, 9.6%)
    • Semantic category 4 — Instruction, Law, and Divine Word:
    • Psa 119:142 "Thy law is truth"
    • Psa 119:151 "all thy commandments are truth"
    • Psa 119:160 "the beginning of thy word is truth"
    • Mal 2:6 "The law of truth was in his mouth"
    • Daniel uses (Prophetic/Revelatory Certainty):
    • Dan 8:12 "cast down the truth (emeth) to the ground"
    • Dan 8:26 "the vision... is true (emeth)"
    • Dan 10:1 "the thing was true (emeth)"
    • Dan 10:21 "the scripture of truth (emeth)"
    • Dan 11:2 "now will I shew thee the truth (emeth)"
    • Dual Hebrew sense: truth + faithfulness. LXX splits: aletheia (truth pole) vs. eleos/pistis (faithfulness pole)
    • LXX: aletheia G225 (83x), alethinos G228 (13x), dikaiosyne G1343 (23x)

    tsadaq — H6663¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md

    • Original: צָדַק | POS: Verb | Occurrences: 41
    • Stems: Qal 22 (be righteous), Hiphil 12 (declare righteous), Piel 5 (prove right), Niphal 1 (Dan 8:14 — the ONLY Niphal), Hithpael 1
    • Dan 8:14 nitsdaq: Niphal.Perf.3ms — "be vindicated/restored to rightness" — hapax-level construction
    • Dan 12:3 matsdiqey: Hiphil.Ptcp — "they that turn many to righteousness" — same root, causative stem
    • Isa 53:11 yatsdiq: Hiphil — "shall my righteous servant justify many" — Messiah causes righteousness
    • Key contrast: taher (H2891) = ritual/physical purity; tsadaq = moral/forensic rightness. KJV's "cleansed" in Dan 8:14 is a mistranslation.
    • LXX: dikaioo G1344 (21x primary verbal equivalent)

    shna (Aramaic) — A8133¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A8133-shna.md

    • Original: שְׁנָא | POS: Aramaic verb | Occurrences: 21 verses (26 tokens)
    • Stems: Peal 8v (intransitive — be changed), Pael 3v (transitive — change, frustrate), Ithpaal 4v (passive — be changed), Haphel 6v (causative — alter, change times/laws/decrees)
    • Critical contrast:
    • Dan 2:21 — God as subject: "he CHANGETH (mehashne — Haphel PARTICIPLE) the times and seasons" — participle = ongoing, characteristic action; God ACTUALLY changes
    • Dan 7:25 — Horn as subject: "think to CHANGE (lehashnayah — Haphel INFINITIVE) times and laws" — infinitive after yisbar (think/intend) = mere intention, not accomplished fact
    • Grammar encodes the gap: Participle (actuality, ongoing) vs. Infinitive after "think" (intention, attempt). Same verb, same stem, different grammatical forms = sovereignty vs. counterfeit.
    • The irrevocable-law motif (Dan 6): dath (law) + shna = "law that cannot be changed" (Dan 6:8,15). The horn attacks exactly what even Persian kings could not alter.

    zman (Aramaic) — A2166¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A2166-zman.md (newly generated)

    • Original: זְמָן | POS: Aramaic masculine noun | Occurrences: 11 verses (12 tokens)
    • Root: H2163 zaman — to fix (a time), appoint
    • Definition: An appointed occasion — a fixed, designated, or scheduled point in time. Carries inherent sense of pre-arrangement.
    • Dan 7:25 zimnin: The plural "times" that the horn attempts to change — appointed festivals/calendar seasons
    • Distinguished from iddan (H5732): iddan = set time-period/duration (used in "time, times, half a time"); zman = appointed occasion/scheduled moment. Both appear in Dan 7:25 but serve distinct roles.
    • LXX: kairos G2540 (8x, PMI 13.19) — confirms "appointed/opportune time" rather than mere duration (which would favor chronos)

    pesha — H6588¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6588-pesha.md

    • Original: פֶּשַׁע | POS: Masculine noun | Occurrences: 90 verses
    • Root: H6586 pasha — to break away from authority, REBEL, transgress
    • Definition: A revolt (national, moral, or religious) — active defection, not merely moral failure
    • Translation: transgression/transgressions (~68, 76%), rebellion (1, 1%) — the single "rebellion" rendering reveals core sense
    • Dan 8:12 ba-pesha: "by reason of transgression/rebellion" — WHY the host was given against the tamid
    • Dan 8:13 ha-pesha shomem: "the transgression of desolation" — rebellion that desolates
    • Dan 9:24 lekhale ha-pesha: "to finish the transgression" — Messiah's work FINISHES what Dan 8:12 began
    • Connection: 8:12 pesha opens the door → 9:24 pesha is finished → 8:14 nitsdaq declares the result

    cuwr/sur — H5493¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5493-cuwr.md

    • Original: סוּר | POS: Verb | Occurrences: 283 verses (382 tokens)
    • Stems: Qal 158 (turn aside, depart), Hiphil 133 (remove, take away), Hophal 5 (be removed)
    • Dan 11:31 hesirou: Hiphil.Perf.3p — "they shall TAKE AWAY the daily" — active, plural agents, named action
    • Dan 12:11 husar: Hophal.Perf.3ms — "the daily shall be TAKEN AWAY" — passive, agent suppressed
    • The 11:31→12:11 voice shift: Active Hiphil (agents named) → Passive Hophal (agent suppressed). Same verb, same action, different agency encoding.

    dath (Aramaic) — A1882¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A1882-dath.md

    • Original: דָּת | POS: Aramaic feminine noun | Occurrences: 13 verses
    • Three semantic categories: Royal decree (Dan 2:9,13,15), Irrevocable law (Dan 6:8,12,15), Law of God (Dan 6:5; 7:25; Ezr 7:12,14,25)
    • Dan 7:25: "think to change times and LAWS (dath)" — the horn attacks God's law
    • Psa 119:142 emeth = torah: Different vocabulary, same target. Dan 8:12 "truth cast down" = Dan 7:25 "laws changed"
    • Distinguished from torah (H8451): torah = instruction/teaching/covenant law from Moses; dath = administrative decree/statute, used of God's law by Gentile rulers from outside

    tsalach — H6743¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6743-tsalach.md

    • Original: צָלַח | POS: Verb | Occurrences: 64 verses
    • Dan 8:12 hitslichah: Hiphil.Perf.3fs — "and prospered" — the horn's success is genuine but bounded
    • Dan 11:36: "shall prosper TILL the indignation be accomplished" — success bounded by divine decree
    • Ironic tension in Daniel: The verb's normally positive sense is held in tension — the horn genuinely succeeds, but within divine limits

    shalak — H7993¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7993-shalak.md

    • Original: שָׁלַךְ | POS: Verb | Occurrences: 121 verses
    • Exclusively causative stems: Hiphil 112 (throw — agent named), Hophal 13 (be thrown — agent suppressed)
    • Dan 8:11 hushlak: Hophal.Perf.3ms — sanctuary "WAS CAST DOWN" — passive, agent suppressed
    • Dan 8:12 tashlekh: Hiphil.Impf.3fs — horn "CAST DOWN the truth" — active, horn is the agent
    • Same root, both voices in the same passage: This is deliberate. The author knows how to name the horn as agent (Hiphil in v.12) and deliberately does NOT when the sanctuary is the target (Hophal in v.11).

    asah — H6213¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6213-asah.md

    • Dan 8:12 we'astah: Qal.Perf.3fs — "and she (the horn/host) did/acted/practiced"
    • Grammar reference: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/asah-qal-perfect-3fs.md
    • The 3fs subject: Feminine singular agrees with qeren (horn, feminine) — the horn is the actor

    didomi — G1325¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1325-didomi.md

    • Original: δίδωμι | POS: Verb | Occurrences: 377 verses (430 tokens)
    • Passive voice = 21% — divine passive "was given" pattern
    • Rev 13:5 edothe: Aorist Passive Indicative 3S — "there was GIVEN unto him" — God as implied agent
    • Rev 13:7 edothe: Same form, twice — "it was GIVEN unto him to make war... power was GIVEN him"
    • The edothe chain: Dan 8:12 tinnaten (Hebrew Niphal) → Dan 7:25 Aramaic passive → Rev 13:5,7 edothe (Greek aorist passive) — same divine passive pattern across three languages

    ephapax — G2178¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2178-ephapax.md (newly generated)

    • Original: ἐφάπαξ | POS: Adverb | Occurrences: 5 in NT
    • Root: epi (G1909) + hapax (G530) — "upon one occasion" — ONCE FOR ALL
    • All 5 uses:
    • Rom 6:10 — "he died unto sin ONCE"
    • 1 Cor 15:6 — "seen of above five hundred brethren AT ONCE" (different sense — simultaneous)
