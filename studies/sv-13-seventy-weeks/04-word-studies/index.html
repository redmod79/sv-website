    Word Studies¶

    From Word Studies Library¶

    shabuwa (H7620) — "week, sevened period"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7620-shabuwa.md

    • Core: Masculine noun, 17 unique verses, 24 tokens across 9 books. From H7651 sheba (seven) — literally "sevened," a passive participle.
    • Definition: A heptad, a period of seven. The unit (days, years) is determined by context, not by the word itself.
    • Daniel density: 6 occurrences (1.7 per 100 verses) — highest density of any book.
    • Translation: KJV renders as "weeks" (64.7%), "week" (23.5%).
    • Critical evidence for day-year:
    • Gen 29:27-28: shabuwa = a 7-day bridal week, paired with "seven other years"
    • Dan 10:2-3: shabuim YAMIM ("weeks of days") — explicitly adds yamim to specify literal day-weeks
    • Dan 9:24: shabuim WITHOUT yamim — the absence signals these are NOT weeks-of-days
    • Feast of Weeks connection: Exo 34:22, Deu 16:9-10 — shabuwa in the agricultural/festival calendar

    chathak (H2852) — "cut off, determine"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2852-chathak.md

    • Core: Verb, HAPAX LEGOMENON — occurs only in Dan 9:24. Niphal Perfect 3ms.
    • Confirmed hapax: search_strongs.py returns 1 total occurrence, 1 translation ("are determined").
    • Definition: "To cut off" — physical root meaning is severance. Figurative: to decree or determine by cutting a portion from a whole.
    • Niphal form: Passive — "have been cut off/decreed." The seventy weeks were cut off by divine agency.
    • Significance: A unique, otherwise-unused verb deliberately chosen to mark the 70 weeks as a defined segment cut from a larger time-block. The verb's cutting semantics link conceptually (not lexically) to Dan 8:14's time-frame.
    • LXX: Theodotion renders syntemlethēsan (from syntemnō, "cut short"), preserving the cutting metaphor.

    pesha (H6588) — "transgression, rebellion"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6588-pesha.md

    • Core: 90 unique verses, 95 tokens, 17 books. From pasha (H6586) — to break away from authority, rebel.
    • In Dan 9:24: "to finish the transgression" — the 1st of the sin-removal triad.
    • Key distinction: pesha = willful revolt against legitimate authority (not mere mistake). It is the strongest sin-term in the Hebrew triad.
    • DOA connection: Lev 16:16,21 use pesha alongside chattaah and tumot — the only verses combining all three terms (as noted in sv-02).

    chattaah (H2403) — "sin, sin offering"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2403-chattaah.md

    • Core: 270 unique verses, 306 tokens, 25 books. Dual semantic weight: both the act of sinning AND the sacrificial remedy.
    • In Dan 9:24: "to make an end of sins" — the 2nd of the sin-removal triad.
    • Key: The word covers both the problem (sin) and its solution (sin offering). Leviticus 16 uses it 10+ times for the DOA goat.
    • LXX bridge: hamartia (G266) with PMI 31.95 — overwhelmingly the primary Greek equivalent.

    kaphar (H3722) — "atone, cover"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md

    • Core: 102 occurrences. Piel dominates (90%). THE Day of Atonement verb — 16x in Lev 16.
    • In Dan 9:24: "to make reconciliation for iniquity" — the 3rd infinitive, the DOA verb.
    • Collocation pattern: kaphar + chattaah (38x), kaphar + kohen (27x), kaphar + qodesh (16x), kaphar + dam (12x) — a tightly structured ritual pattern.
    • Daily vs. annual formula: Daily = kaphar + salach (atonement → forgiveness). Annual = kaphar + taher (atonement → cleansing). Dan 9:24 elevates: kaphar + tsedeq (atonement → everlasting righteousness).

    avon (H5771) — "iniquity, guilt"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5771-avon.md

    • Core: 213 unique verses, 234 tokens, 26 books. Three senses: the act, the guilt, the punishment.
    • In Dan 9:24: Object of kaphar — "make reconciliation for iniquity."
    • The bearing formula: nasa avon appears 37x — used of priests bearing communal guilt, scapegoat ritual, and the Suffering Servant (Isa 53:6,11).

    tsadaq (H6663) — "be righteous, vindicate"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md

    • Core: 41 occurrences. Job dominates (56%). Five stems: Qal (22), Hiphil (12), Piel (5), Niphal (1), Hithpael (1).
    • In Dan 9:24: The root of tsedeq olamim ("everlasting righteousness") — the 4th infinitive brings in what kaphar produces.
    • In Dan 8:14: nitsdaq — the ONLY Niphal occurrence. The sanctuary "shall be vindicated."
    • In Dan 12:3: matsdiqey — Hiphil participle, "those who turn many to righteousness." Same root, different stem, complementary role.
    • The progression: kaphar (Dan 9:24) → tsedeq (Dan 9:24) → nitsdaq (Dan 8:14). Atonement produces righteousness produces vindication.

    owlam (H5769) — "everlasting, age"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5769-owlam.md

    • Core: 406 unique verses, 432 tokens, 34 books. Core image: a horizon that disappears from sight — time beyond human perception.
    • In Dan 9:24: tsedeq olamim (plural!) — "everlasting righteousness." The plural owlamim intensifies: not just a single age but ages upon ages.
    • In Dan 12:2: "everlasting life" and "everlasting contempt" — the final-judgment uses.
    • LXX: aion (G165) with PMI 31.41 — near-exclusive equivalent.

    chatham (H2856) — "seal"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md

    • Core: 26 tokens, 24 unique verses, 11 books. Four stems: Qal (22), Niphal (2), Piel (1), Hiphil (1).
    • In Dan 9:24: "to seal up the vision and prophecy" — the 5th infinitive.
    • Authentication vs. concealment: Dan 9:24's chatham = authentication (the 70 weeks' fulfillment stamps the prophetic system as genuine). Dan 12:4's chatham = concealment ("seal the book until the time of the end"). Same root, different function — context determines which sense.
    • Legal sealing: 1 Kgs 21:8, Jer 32:10, Est 8:10 — sealing makes documents binding and tamper-evident.
    • WG-sealing.md confirms the two-sense distinction (authentication and concealment) across the sealing word group.

    mashiyach (H4899) — "anointed one, Messiah"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4899-mashiyach.md

    • Core: 38 unique verses, 44 tokens, 10 books. Only 2 occurrences translated "Messiah" (Dan 9:25,26) — all others rendered "anointed."
    • In Dan 9:25: mashiyach nagiyd — "Messiah the Prince." Two absolute-state nouns in apposition.
    • In Dan 9:26: "Messiah shall be cut off" — yikkaret mashiyach (Niphal of karath).
    • LXX: Christos (G5547) with PMI 32.22 — near-exclusive 1:1 mapping.
    • Categories: anointed priest (Lev 4), anointed king (1 Sam), Davidic covenant (2 Sam 22:51, Psa 132), eschatological Messiah (Dan 9:25-26, Isa 55:4).

    nagiyd (H5057) — "prince, leader"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5057-nagiyd.md (Generated Phase 3.5)

    • Core: 44 unique verses, 45 tokens, 14 books. From H5046 nagad — "to stand boldly in front, declare."
    • In Dan 9:25: mashiyach nagiyd — "Messiah the Prince." The one divinely designated to stand at the front.
    • In Dan 9:26: am nagiyd habba — "the people of the prince that shall come." A DIFFERENT nagiyd — the destroying prince.
    • Daniel uses: 3 occurrences (Dan 9:25, 9:26, 11:22) — highest prophetic weight.
    • Key distinction from sar (H8269): nagiyd = divinely designated/conspicuously placed leader. sar = general official at any rank.
    • Collocations: YHWH (36%), Israel (30%), am/people (30%) — the defining formula is "the LORD's nagiyd over his people."
    • LXX: hegeomai/hegoumenos (G2233) with PMI 7.10. Same term appears in Matt 2:6 for the Messiah and Heb 13:7,17,24 for church leaders.

    beriyth (H1285) — "covenant"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1285-beriyth.md

    • Core: 284 occurrences across the OT. From root related to cutting (cf. Gen 15:10,17-18; Jer 34:18-19).
    • In Dan 9:27: "he shall confirm the covenant" — higbiyr beriyth. NOT karath beriyth ("cut a covenant") but gabar beriyth ("strengthen/confirm a covenant"). This implies strengthening an EXISTING covenant, not making a new one.
    • Dan 9:4: Daniel's prayer opens acknowledging God as "keeping the covenant and mercy."

    gabar (H1396) — "be strong, prevail, confirm"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1396-gabar.md

    • Core: 24 unique verses, 31 tokens, 13 books. Four stems: Qal (18), Hithpael (3), Piel (3), Hiphil (2).
    • In Dan 9:27: vehigbiyr beriyth — Hiphil Perfect 3ms with waw-consecutive. "He shall cause [the covenant] to prevail" or "he shall confirm/strengthen [the covenant]."
    • KJV "confirm": Only this one occurrence uses gabar for "confirm." The normal word for covenant-making is karath (H3772, "cut"). Using gabar instead signals that the subject is STRENGTHENING an already-existing covenant, not cutting a new one.
    • Other Hiphil uses: Only 2 total — the other is Psa 12:4 "our lips are our own" (self-strengthening).
    • search_strongs.py --verses H1396 "confirm": Returns 0 — KJV's "confirm" rendering is unique to Dan 9:27.
    • Qal pattern: Most common sense is "prevailed" (Gen 7:18-24 flood waters; Exo 17:11 in battle; 2 Sam 11:23 in warfare).

    karath (H3772) — "cut, cut off"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3772-karath.md

    • Core: 280 unique verses, 360 tokens, 33 books. The dominant "cut off" verb.
    • In Dan 9:26: yikkaret mashiyach — "Messiah shall be cut off" (Niphal Impf 3ms). The covenantal-death formula.
    • Niphal of karath: Standard for judicial/divine cutting off — violent death with covenantal overtones.
    • Contrast with chathak (v. 24): Both mean "cut" but serve different roles. chathak = cutting a TIME PORTION (decree). karath = cutting a PERSON (violent death). The echo is deliberate.
    • Covenant-making formula: karath beriyth ("cut a covenant") = 87 of 360 tokens. Dan 9:27 does NOT use this formula — it uses gabar beriyth instead.

    shabath (H7673) — "cease, rest"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7673-shabath.md (Generated Phase 3.5)

    • Core: 67 unique verses, 112 tokens, 19 books. Hiphil dominates (40 of 71 = 56%).
    • In Dan 9:27: yashbiyt zebach uminchah — Hiphil Impf 3ms, "he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease."
    • NOT the Sabbath noun: The verb shabath is the ROOT of shabbath (H7676, the Sabbath day) but here functions as a causative cessation verb.
    • Prophetic judgment pattern: Ezekiel uses shabath 13x in Hiphil — God "causing to cease" joy, songs, worship as judgment (Eze 26:13; Jer 7:34; Hos 2:11).
    • Daniel's use: Dan 9:27 (sacrifice ceases) and Dan 11:18 (reproach ceases) — eschatological/prophetic.

    charats (H2782) — "determine, decree"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2782-charats.md (Generated Phase 3.5)

    • Core: 14 tokens, 12 unique verses, 8 books. Qal (7), Niphal (5).
    • In Dan 9:26: necheretset shomemot — "desolations are determined" (Niphal Ptcp fs).
    • In Dan 9:27: venecheratsah — "and that determined" (Niphal Ptcp fs). What is decreed shall be poured upon the desolate.
    • In Dan 11:36: "that that is determined shall be done."
    • Near-technical term: The Niphal participle becomes a near-technical term in prophetic literature for God's irrevocable determination — especially of judgment or end-time events. Isaiah 10:22-23, 28:22 use it for "a consumption, even determined."
    • Easily confused with chathak (H2852): Both translated "determined" in Daniel. chathak = cutting off a time allotment. charats = the irrevocable character of decrees WITHIN or AFTER that time.

    charuwts (H2742) — "wall/moat/trench"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2742-charuwts.md (Generated Phase 3.5)

    • Core: 19 tokens, 17 unique verses, 8 books. Passive participle of charats (H2782).
    • In Dan 9:25: rechob wechăruwts — "the street (plaza) and the wall (moat/trench)." KJV renders "wall" but the Hebrew points to a moat or defensive trench (something "cut out").
    • Multiple senses: diligent (Pro 10:4; 12:24), fine gold (Pro 3:14; 8:19), threshing instrument (Isa 28:27; 41:15), sharp (Job 41:30), decision (Joel 3:14), wall/moat (Dan 9:25).
    • The fortification sense: From the root meaning "incised/cut" — a defensive trench around a city. Paired with rechob (open plaza/street) to represent the full urban infrastructure.

    motsa (H4161) — "going forth"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4161-mowtsa.md (Generated Phase 3.5)

    • Core: 31 tokens, 26 unique verses, 13 books. From yatsa (H3318) — to go out, come forth.
    • In Dan 9:25: min motsa dabar — "from the going forth of the commandment/word." The terminus a quo of the prophetic clock.
    • Semantic categories: utterance/decree (Num 30:12; Deu 8:3; Dan 9:25), physical departure (Num 33:2), water source/spring (2 Ki 2:21; Psa 107:33,35), dawn/sunrise (Psa 19:6; Hos 6:3).
    • Decree sense: When motsa governs dabar, it = an authoritative pronouncement going forth from power. The decree "goes forth" from the Persian court.

    From Word Studies Library — Greek Terms¶

    pleroo (G4137) — "fulfill, complete"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4137-pleroo.md

    • In Mark 1:15: Peplerōtai (Perfect Passive Indicative 3S) — "The time HAS BEEN fulfilled." The perfect tense marks a completed state with present relevance. The prophetic clock has arrived.
    • 90 NT occurrences. Passive dominates (~78%) — prophecy is FULFILLED by events, not by human agency.
    • Two semantic domains: fullness (filling up) and completion (finishing a task/period/prophecy).

    kairos (G2540) — "time, appointed season"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2540-kairos.md

    • In Mark 1:15: ho kairos — "THE time" (with article). Not chronos (duration) but kairos (appointed moment). Jesus declares that the divinely appointed moment has arrived.
    • LXX background: Translates H4150 moed (appointed time/meeting) and H6256 eth (time/season).

    H2852 chathak — Hapax Verification¶

    Source: search_strongs.py --lookup H2852 Result: 1 total occurrence, 1 translation ("are determined"). Hapax status confirmed.

    H1396 gabar — "confirm" Translation¶

    day-year-formula.md¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/day-year-formula.md

    • yom lashshanah = distributive lamed + definite article = "each day for the year"
    • Occurs verbatim ONLY in Num 14:34 and Ezek 4:6
    • Framing cardinals (40 days / 40 years) make the 1:1 ratio explicit
    • Performative verbs (natatti, tis'u) frame the conversion as divine decree

    distributive-lamed-ratio.md¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/distributive-lamed-ratio.md

    • The day-year formula is a specific instance of the broader distributive-lamed construction
    • The grammar encodes the RATIO OPERATOR; context encodes the RATIO VALUE
    • The construction is productive — could theoretically write "erev boqer lashshanah" and it would be well-formed
    • But the MT does NOT do this. Daniel's time prophecies use bare day-counts without the la-shanah rider
    • Whether interpreters may APPLY the formula to Daniel is hermeneutical, not grammatical
