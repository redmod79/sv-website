    What Is the 70 Weeks Prophecy?¶

    Question¶

    What is the 70 weeks prophecy? Starting point, day-year principle, calculation, 70th week events, verification key function.

    Summary Answer¶

    The 70 weeks of Daniel 9:24-27 are a Messianic time prophecy that spans 490 years from Artaxerxes' decree in 457 BC to AD 34. The prophecy names the exact year of Jesus' baptism (AD 27), places His death "in the midst" of the final week, and extends the gospel beyond Israel by the period's end. Because the 70 weeks are "cut off" (chathak, H2852) from the 2300 evening-mornings of Daniel 8:14, their precise fulfillment authenticates the day-year principle and verifies the starting point for the longer period.

    Key Verses¶

    Daniel 9:24 "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city, to finish the transgression, and to make an end of sins, and to make reconciliation for iniquity, and to bring in everlasting righteousness, and to seal up the vision and prophecy, and to anoint the most Holy."

    Daniel 9:25 "Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks, and threescore and two weeks: the street shall be built again, and the wall, even in troublous times."

    Daniel 9:26 "And after threescore and two weeks shall Messiah be cut off, but not for himself: and the people of the prince that shall come shall destroy the city and the sanctuary; and the end thereof shall be with a flood, and unto the end of the war desolations are determined."

    Daniel 9:27 "And he shall confirm the covenant with many for one week: and in the midst of the week he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease, and for the overspreading of abominations he shall make it desolate, even until the consummation, and that determined shall be poured upon the desolate."

    Numbers 14:34 "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, even forty days, each day for a year, shall ye bear your iniquities, even forty years, and ye shall know my breach of promise."

    Ezekiel 4:6 "And when thou hast accomplished them, lie again on thy right side, and thou shalt bear the iniquity of the house of Judah forty days: I have appointed thee each day for a year."

    Mark 1:15 "And saying, The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand: repent ye, and believe the gospel."

    Ezra 7:25-26 "And thou, Ezra, after the wisdom of thy God, that is in thine hand, set magistrates and judges, which may judge all the people that are beyond the river, all such as know the laws of thy God; and teach ye them that know them not. And whosoever will not do the law of thy God, and the law of the king, let judgment be executed speedily upon him, whether it be unto death, or to banishment, or to confiscation of goods, or to imprisonment."

    Galatians 4:4 "But when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law."

    Analysis¶

    1. The Setting: Daniel Reads Jeremiah and Prays¶

    The 70 weeks prophecy does not appear in a vacuum. Daniel 9:2 tells us that Daniel was reading Jeremiah's prophecy about 70 years of exile (Jer 25:11-12; 29:10). He understood (biyn, H995) that the 70 years were nearly over, and he began praying — a corporate confession covering Israel's sin, covenant-breaking, and the desolation of Jerusalem.

    This prayer matters because Gabriel's answer responds to it directly. Daniel confesses transgression (pesha), sin (chattaah), and iniquity (avon). Gabriel answers with a plan to "finish the transgression, make an end of sins, and make reconciliation for iniquity" — the same three terms Daniel used, now targeted for resolution (Dan 9:24).

    The backdrop is also numeric. Second Chronicles 36:21 explains that the 70-year exile compensated for missed sabbatical years. The land "kept sabbath" for 70 years because Israel had neglected approximately 490 years of sabbatical rests (Lev 26:34-35). Gabriel then announces 70 weeks — which equal 490 years. The punishment (490 years of sabbatical neglect → 70 years exile) is answered by the remedy (70 weeks = 490 years → Messianic resolution). The numbers lock together: 490 caused the problem, 490 resolves it.

    Gabriel arrives "about the time of the evening oblation" (Dan 9:21) — the tamid service. He identifies himself as the same angel from Daniel 8 ("whom I had seen in the vision at the beginning") and tells Daniel to "understand the matter, and consider the vision (mar'eh)" (Dan 9:23). This is not a new topic. Gabriel is resuming the mission from Daniel 8:16 — to explain the mar'eh, the time prophecy. sv-12 proved this connection through six independent lines of evidence: the back-reference, the identical commission formula, the biyn failure-resolution arc, and the shared vocabulary.

    2. The Day-Year Principle: Declared, Signaled, Verified¶

    The 70 weeks are measured in "weeks" (shabuim, H7620). But what kind of weeks? Literal 7-day periods would yield only 490 days — about 16 months. No Persian decree 16 months before Christ's appearance exists. The prophecy requires a longer unit: year-weeks, where each "day" in the week represents a year.

    The day-year principle rests on three pillars.

    First, it is declared by God Himself. Numbers 14:34 and Ezekiel 4:6 both use the identical Hebrew formula: yom lashshanah — "each day for a year." The distributive lamed (le-) with the definite article creates a ratio operator. God commands 40 days → 40 years in Numbers 14 and 390 days + 40 days → 390 years + 40 years in Ezekiel 4. These are not metaphors. They are divinely decreed conversion ratios applied in judgment contexts. The grammar library (day-year-formula.md, distributive-lamed-ratio.md) confirms these as the only two OT verses with this exact construction.

    Second, it is signaled within Daniel. The book itself tells us these are not ordinary time units. Daniel 10:2-3 uses shabuim yamim — "weeks of DAYS" — adding the word yamim to clarify literal day-weeks. Daniel 9:24 uses shabuim WITHOUT yamim. The same author, in the same book, uses two different constructions. If shabuim always meant literal 7-day weeks, Daniel would have no reason to add yamim in chapter 10. The addition proves he knew the word was ambiguous. When he left yamim out in 9:24, he intended a different kind of "sevened period."

    This is supported by Genesis 29:27-28, where shabuwa refers to a 7-day bridal week but is paired with "seven other years." The word's fundamental meaning (from sheba, "seven") is "a sevened period." Context determines the unit. The H7620-shabuwa canonical word study confirms this flexibility.

    Jeremiah's 70 years (Jer 25:11-12) serve as a control case. That prophecy uses plain shanah ("year") in a non-apocalyptic genre with no seal command and no unusual time unit. Daniel reads it literally (Dan 9:2). The day-year principle is genre-triggered, not universally applied. When apocalyptic signals are absent, time prophecies mean what they say. When signals are present — unusual units, sealing commands, apocalyptic context — the declared day-year ratio applies.

    Third, it is verified by history. If 70 weeks = 490 years, and the starting point is 457 BC, then 69 weeks (483 years) reach AD 27. Jesus begins His ministry in AD 27 and declares: "The time is fulfilled" (Mark 1:15). The Greek is precise: peplerōtai ho kairos — "THE appointed time HAS BEEN fulfilled." Perfect tense, passive voice. The prophetic clock has already reached its destination. Galatians 4:4 says the same in different words: "when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son." Both passages treat prophetic time as something that fills up to a divinely set measure — and it arrived at the calculated moment.

    3. The Starting Point: Why 457 BC¶

    Daniel 9:25 says the prophetic clock begins "from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem." The starting point must be a decree that authorizes both RESTORATION (civil/judicial function) and BUILDING (physical construction).

    Three Persian decrees relate to Jerusalem:

    Cyrus, 538 BC (Ezra 1:1-4): "Build him an house at Jerusalem." Temple only. No mention of restoring civil government, appointing judges, or rebuilding the city's infrastructure.

    Darius, 520 BC (Ezra 6:1-12): Confirms Cyrus' decree. Still temple-focused.

    Artaxerxes, 457 BC (Ezra 7:11-26): This decree stands apart. Beyond the temple, it authorizes Ezra to "set magistrates and judges, which may judge all the people that are beyond the river" (Ezra 7:25). It grants judicial authority with enforcement power: "whether it be unto death, or to banishment, or to confiscation of goods, or to imprisonment" (Ezra 7:26). This is the RESTORE component. Only this decree matches "restore AND build."

    Ezra 6:14 groups all three kings under one "commandment" (ta'am): "according to the commandment of Cyrus, and Darius, and Artaxerxes." Whether we view this as three phases of one divine purpose or as three separate decrees, the critical date remains 457 BC — that is when the restoration of governmental authority was added to the existing temple authorization.

    Nehemiah's request in 444 BC (Neh 2:1-8) involved walls and gates only — no judicial authority, no civil restoration. And 444 BC + 483 years does not reach AD 27 without non-standard calendar adjustments (such as Anderson's 360-day "prophetic year," which has no basis in the Hebrew text).

    The starting point is 457 BC. It is the only date that satisfies the textual requirements ("restore and build") AND produces the correct mathematical result.

    4. The Calculation¶

    The arithmetic is straightforward:

    • 7 weeks + 62 weeks = 69 weeks = 483 years (Dan 9:25)
    • 457 BC + 483 = AD 27 (accounting for no year zero: 457 + 483 - 1 = 27, or simply, the 483rd year from 457 BC lands in AD 27)

    The first 7 weeks (49 years) cover the period of rebuilding — "the street shall be built again, and the wall, even in troublous times" (Dan 9:25). From 457 BC to 408 BC = 49 years, covering the difficulties described in Ezra and Nehemiah.

    The next 62 weeks (434 years) extend from 408 BC to AD 27 — "unto the Messiah the Prince."

    Luke 3:1-3 provides historical verification. Luke names six overlapping officials to date John the Baptist's ministry (and by extension, Jesus' baptism): Tiberius Caesar (15th year), Pontius Pilate, Herod Antipas, Philip, Lysanias, and Annas/Caiaphas. Tiberius' 15th year = AD 27 by inclusive reckoning from his co-regency with Augustus. Jesus was "about thirty years of age" at baptism (Luke 3:23), consistent with a birth around 4 BC and a baptism in AD 27.

    Acts 10:38 confirms that "God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Ghost and with power" — the baptism is the anointing event that makes Jesus the mashiyach (anointed one) of Dan 9:25.

    5. The Six Infinitives: What the 70 Weeks Accomplish¶

    Daniel 9:24 announces six purposes for the 70-week period. They divide into two triads — three dealing with sin-removal and three dealing with positive establishment.

    Sin-removal triad: 1. "To finish the transgression" (lekalle happesha, Piel of kalah + pesha). Transgression — willful rebellion against God's authority — will be brought to completion. The Messiah's death addresses rebellion definitively. 2. "To make an end of sins" (ulehatem chatta'ot, Hiphil of tamam + chattaah). Sin and its effects will be sealed up, completed, brought to full resolution. 3. "To make reconciliation for iniquity" (ulekapper avon, Piel of kaphar + avon). The DOA verb kaphar — the same verb used 16 times in Leviticus 16 — appears here with avon (iniquity). What the Day of Atonement did ritually each year, the Messiah's work does permanently.

    This sin-removal triad (pesha, chattaah, avon) matches the three terms in Leviticus 16:16,21 — the only verses that combine all three. Cross-testament parallels confirm this: Dan 9:24 shares kaphar + qodesh + avon + pesha with Lev 16:16 (score 0.427). The 70 weeks are saturated with Day of Atonement vocabulary (sv-02, sv-04).

    Positive establishment triad: 4. "To bring in everlasting righteousness" (ulehabi tsedeq olamim). The same tsadaq root as Dan 8:14's nitsdaq (vindication) and Dan 12:3's matsdiqey (the wise who turn many to righteousness). The kaphar-to-tsedeq progression: atonement produces righteousness. What sv-01 showed in concept, Dan 9:24 states explicitly. 5. "To seal up the vision and prophecy" (velachtom chazon venabi). chatham (H2856) in the authentication sense (sv-11). The 70 weeks' fulfillment stamps the prophetic time system as genuine — like a notary's seal that authenticates a document. This is not concealment (as in Dan 12:4) but validation. 6. "To anoint the most Holy" (velimshoch qodesh qodashim). qodesh qodashim never refers to a person in the OT — it always designates the Most Holy Place or the most sacred objects (sv-08). mashach + qodesh qodashim points to the inauguration of the heavenly sanctuary, paralleling Exodus 40:9-11 where Moses anointed the earthly tabernacle.

    These six purposes reveal that the 70 weeks are not just a chronological marker — they describe the full scope of what the Messiah's coming accomplishes.

    6. The 70th Week: Covenant, Cross, and Transition¶

    The final week (AD 27-34) is the most event-dense period in the prophecy.

    "He shall confirm the covenant with many for one week" (Dan 9:27). The Hebrew is vehigbiyr beriyth — Hiphil of gabar (H1396), meaning "cause the covenant to prevail" or "strengthen the covenant." This is NOT karath beriyth ("cut a new covenant"), which is the standard formula for covenant-making (87 of karath's 360 occurrences). The distinction matters. gabar means strengthening something that already exists. The Messiah does not introduce a foreign treaty — He fulfills and strengthens the Abrahamic and Davidic covenant promises. Isaiah describes the Messiah as given "for a covenant of the people, for a light of the Gentiles" (Isa 42:6; 49:8). Christ IS the covenant embodied.

    The KJV rendering "confirm" for gabar appears nowhere else in the English Bible. The canonical word study (H1396-gabar.md) confirms: gabar's normal senses are "prevailed" (8x), "strengthened" (3x), "great" (2x). The "confirm" rendering is unique to Dan 9:27, chosen because of the covenant context.

    "In the midst of the week he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease" (Dan 9:27). chatsi hashabuwa — the midpoint of the 7-year week. The Hiphil of shabath (H7673) means "cause to cease." zebach + minchah (sacrifice + grain offering) represent the entire sacrificial system.

    The midpoint of the 70th week = 3.5 years after AD 27 = spring AD 31. This aligns with the crucifixion. At the moment of Jesus' death, "the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom" (Matt 27:50-51) — God's visible declaration that the sacrificial system had reached its fulfillment. Hebrews 10:11-12 explains: "Every priest standeth daily ministering... which can never take away sins: But this man, after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down." The standing priesthood is replaced by the seated Christ. The sacrifice ceases not because it was destroyed but because it was fulfilled.

    The remaining 3.5 years (AD 31-34) extend the "confirming" of the covenant through the apostolic preaching to Israel. The 70 weeks were "upon thy people and upon thy holy city" (Dan 9:24). During this final half-week, the gospel was preached primarily to Jews — the Book of Acts records this Jewish-first focus. Around AD 34, the transition begins: Stephen is martyred (Acts 7:54-60), believers scatter and preach everywhere (Acts 8:1-4), Peter preaches to Cornelius (Acts 10), and Paul declares "we turn to the Gentiles" (Acts 13:46). The exclusive Jewish focus of the 70-week period ends.

    7. The Verification Key: How the 70 Weeks Authenticate the 2300¶

    This is the series' distinctive contribution, drawn from Insight #4 in the series insights document.

    The word nechtak in Daniel 9:24 is a hapax legomenon — it occurs only here in the entire Old Testament. Its root chathak (H2852) means "to cut off" in the physical sense. The Niphal (passive) form means the 70 weeks "have been cut off" — severed from something larger. Since Gabriel's mission is to explain the mar'eh (the 2300 evening-mornings of Dan 8:14, as established in sv-05 and sv-12), the larger period from which the 70 weeks are cut is the 2300.

    This creates a shared starting point. If the 70 weeks begin in 457 BC, the 2300 also begins in 457 BC.

    Now the verification logic unfolds:

    1. The day-year principle is declared (Num 14:34, Ezek 4:6).
    2. The 70 weeks are "cut off" from the 2300 (Dan 9:24, chathak).
    3. The 70 weeks, converted by the day-year principle, yield 490 years.
    4. 457 BC + 483 = AD 27. Jesus declares "the time is fulfilled" (Mark 1:15).
    5. The math works on the testable subset.
    6. Therefore, the same conversion applied to the parent period is strongly supported: 457 BC + 2300 = 1844.

    The logic is inductive, not deductive. Strictly speaking, a subset's fulfillment does not guarantee the parent period's fulfillment with mathematical certainty. But the evidence is stronger than analogy. The 70 weeks are not merely similar to the 2300 — they are CUT FROM it (chathak), sharing the same starting point, the same day-year conversion, and the same prophetic system. When a prophecy's own internal subset verifies the starting point AND the conversion method, the remaining calculation carries the weight of that verification. The 70 weeks function as both a prophecy and a verification key. They predict specific Messianic events (baptism, death, covenant-strengthening). And by doing so, they validate the mathematical system that extends to 1844.

    Daniel 9:24's fifth infinitive — "to seal up (chatham) the vision and prophecy" — uses chatham in the authentication sense. sv-11 distinguished two senses of this verb: concealment (Dan 12:4, "seal the book until the time of the end") and authentication (like a notary's seal that stamps a document as genuine). The 70 weeks' fulfillment is the authentication seal. When Jesus declared "the time is fulfilled," He was stamping the prophetic time system as genuine.

    This means the 70 weeks serve a double function in the series' framework: - For the Messiah: They predict His arrival, death, and work with mathematical precision. - For the 2300: They validate the starting point and the day-year encoding. The subset's fulfillment strongly supports the parent period's calculation.

    The Messiah's death both authenticates the prophecy (Dan 9:26) and qualifies the Lamb to unseal it (Rev 5:5-9). Authentication and unsealing are linked through the cross (Series Insight #6).

    8. The Cutting Motif Across Daniel 9¶

    Three "cutting" verbs run through the prophecy, each performing a different kind of severance:

    • chathak (v.24) — cuts TIME. The 70 weeks are severed from the 2300. Hapax legomenon, Niphal Perfect.
    • karath (v.26) — cuts a PERSON. The Messiah is violently killed. Niphal Imperfect.
    • charats (vv.26-27) — cuts FATE. Desolations are irrevocably determined. Niphal Participle.

    All three are passive forms — the agent behind each cutting act is God. The progression moves from temporal decree to personal sacrifice to historical consequence. The same God who decreed the time period (chathak) ordained the Messiah's death within it (karath) and determined the aftermath (charats).

    This is not casual vocabulary. Daniel uses three different cutting verbs where one would have sufficed. Each carries its own weight: chathak is a hapax chosen precisely because no other word captures "cutting off a time segment." karath is the standard verb for covenant-death. charats in the Niphal participle becomes a near-technical term for irrevocable divine decree (also appearing in Isa 10:22-23, 28:22).

    Word Studies¶

    Three Hebrew words reshape the English reading of Daniel 9:

    chathak (H2852): A hapax legomenon occurring only in Dan 9:24, rendered "are determined" in the KJV. The root meaning is physical cutting/severance. The Niphal passive means "have been cut off." English readers miss the cutting metaphor entirely. This single word is the bridge between the 70 weeks and the 2300 — without the Hebrew, the connection is invisible. The canonical word study confirms the hapax status and the Akkadian cognate background supporting the severance meaning.

    gabar (H1396): Rendered "confirm" in Dan 9:27, but the word means "to be strong, to prevail." The Hiphil ("cause to prevail" or "strengthen") appears only here and in Psalm 12:4. It is NOT karath ("cut a covenant," used 87 times). English readers see "confirm the covenant" and may think of ratifying a new agreement. The Hebrew says the opposite — strengthening an existing one. This eliminates the reading that a future prince makes a new treaty; the subject is strengthening the covenant God already made.

    peplerōtai (G4137): Jesus' declaration in Mark 1:15 uses the Perfect Passive Indicative of plēroō. The perfect tense means "has been fulfilled" — completed action with present relevance. kairos with the article means "THE appointed time." This is not a vague readiness statement; it is a declaration that a specific prophetic period has reached its end. The G4137-pleroo canonical word study shows passive forms dominate (~78% of NT occurrences) — prophecy is fulfilled by events, not by human agency.

    Difficult Passages¶

    The Antecedent of "He" in Daniel 9:27¶

    The 3ms subject of "he shall confirm the covenant" is grammatically ambiguous. It could be the Messiah (from vv.25-26) or the "prince that shall come" (v.26). Dispensationalist interpreters take "he" as a future Antichrist who makes a 7-year treaty with Israel, inserting a gap between the 69th and 70th weeks.

    The Messianic reading is stronger on multiple grounds: (1) gabar beriyth implies strengthening an existing covenant, not making a new treaty — this fits the Messiah, not a political leader; (2) the sustained Messianic subject from vv.25-26 is the natural antecedent; (3) "cause the sacrifice and oblation to cease" fits Christ's work at the cross, not a treaty-breaker's action; (4) the NT treats the prophetic timeline as complete (Mark 1:15, Gal 4:4), not as having a multi-thousand-year gap. But the grammatical ambiguity is real. The text does not resolve it by explicitly naming the subject.

    The Crucifixion Date¶

    "In the midst of the week" places the crucifixion approximately 3.5 years after the 69th week's terminus. From autumn AD 27, 3.5 years forward = spring AD 31. But proposed crucifixion dates range from AD 30 to AD 33 based on Passover calculations and Pilate chronology.

    This does not damage the 70-weeks framework — all proposed dates fall within the 70th week (AD 27-34). The prophecy says "in the midst," not "in the exact mathematical center." The AD 31 date produces the tightest alignment, but AD 30 or AD 33 would still fall within the prophetic week.

    The Tiberius Co-Regency¶

    Luke 3:1 dates John's ministry to "the fifteenth year of the reign of Tiberius." If counted from sole reign (August AD 14), the 15th year = AD 28/29. If counted from co-regency with Augustus (c. AD 12), the 15th year = AD 27. The co-regency dating fits the 457 BC calculation; the sole-reign dating creates a 1-2 year offset.

    The co-regency is attested in Roman history but debated. The calculation does not depend solely on Luke — the 457 BC starting point stands on Ezra 7's independent evidence.

    The Gradual End of the 70th Week¶

    No single event in AD 34 is explicitly named as the terminus. Stephen's death (Acts 7), the scattering (Acts 8:1-4), Peter and Cornelius (Acts 10), and Paul's turn to the Gentiles (Acts 13:46) all occur in this transition period. The shift from Jewish exclusivity to Gentile inclusion is a process, not a single moment.

    This is consistent with Daniel 9:24's framing: the 70 weeks are "upon thy people and upon thy holy city." The period marks a probationary window for Israel's response to the Messiah. When it expires, the gospel expands — not because God rejects Israel, but because the exclusively Jewish phase ends.

    Conclusion¶

    The 70 weeks of Daniel 9:24-27 are one of the most precisely fulfilled prophecies in Scripture.

    The starting point is Artaxerxes' decree in Ezra 7 (457 BC), the only decree authorizing both the restoration of judicial autonomy ("set magistrates and judges," Ezra 7:25) and the rebuilding of the city — matching Daniel 9:25's "restore and to build Jerusalem."

    The day-year principle is not a human invention. It is declared by God in Numbers 14:34 and Ezekiel 4:6, signaled by Daniel's own grammatical distinction (shabuim with yamim vs. shabuim without yamim), and confirmed by historical fulfillment.

    The calculation works: 457 BC + 483 = AD 27. Jesus declares "the time is fulfilled" (Mark 1:15, peplerōtai ho kairos). Paul says "the fulness of the time was come" (Gal 4:4). The prophetic clock arrived at its destination.

    The 70th week unfolds exactly as predicted. The Messiah strengthens (gabar) the existing covenant with many for one week. In the midst of that week, He causes the sacrifice to cease — not by force but by fulfillment. The veil tears (Matt 27:51). Christ sits down (Heb 10:12). The earthly type yields to the heavenly reality. By the week's end (c. AD 34), the gospel extends beyond Israel to the Gentiles (Acts 8-13).

    The verification key function is what sets this study apart within the series. The 70 weeks are "cut off" (chathak, H2852 — a hapax legomenon meaning physical severance) from the 2300 evening-mornings. They share a starting point. The 70 weeks are the testable subset of the larger period — historically verifiable because the events they predict (Messiah's baptism, death, covenant-strengthening) can be checked against the record. Since the math works on the testable 490-year subset, the same day-year conversion applied to the parent 2300-year period is authenticated. The seal proves itself through predictive math.

    Daniel 9:24's fifth infinitive — "to seal up the vision and prophecy" — uses chatham in its authentication sense (as distinguished from its concealment sense in Dan 12:4 — see sv-11). The 70 weeks authenticate the prophetic time system. When Jesus said "the time is fulfilled," He was stamping the system as genuine. And the same event that authenticates the prophecy (the Messiah's death, Dan 9:26) qualifies the Lamb to unseal it (Rev 5:5-9).

    The 70 weeks are both a Messianic prophecy and a mathematical proof. They predict the Messiah's arrival with precision no human calendar could manufacture. And by doing so, they verify the starting point, the day-year principle, and the entire time-prophetic framework that stretches to the 2300 evening-mornings and the vindication of the sanctuary.
