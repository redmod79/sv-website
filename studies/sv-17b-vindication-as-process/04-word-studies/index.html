    04-word-studies.md — Word Study and Grammar Data for sv-17b¶

    Library Entries Referenced¶

    H6663 — tsadaq (צָדַק)¶

    • Type: Verb, primitive root (צדק family)
    • Occurrences: 41 in OT (54 KJV translation entries)
    • Stem distribution: Qal (22), Hiphil (12), Piel (5), Niphal (1), Hithpael (1)
    • Niphal (1 occurrence): Dan 8:14 — "be vindicated, be restored to rightness." This is the ONLY Niphal occurrence in the entire OT.
    • KJV translations: justify/justified (~20), righteous/be righteous (~11), just/be just (~8), justice/do justice (~3), clear/cleansed (~3, including Dan 8:14), to righteousness (1, Dan 12:3)
    • Key distinction: Root means "to be righteous/just/vindicated" — NOT ritual cleansing. KJV "cleansed" in Dan 8:14 is a functional translation, not a lexical one. The ritual cleansing word is taher (H2891).
    • Source: word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md

    G2064 — erchomai (ἔρχομαι)¶

    • Type: Verb (deponent; suppletive paradigm: present ἔρχομαι, aorist ἦλθον, perfect ἐλήλυθα)
    • Occurrences: 604 unique verses (664 tokens) across 24 NT books
    • Semantic domains: Come/Arrive (15.006), Happen (13.004)
    • KJV translations: came (~196, 32%), come (~175, 29%), plus additional forms
    • Key form for this study: ἦλθεν (elthen) — Aorist Active Indicative 3rd Sg — marks arrival as accomplished fact
    • Rev 14:7: ἦλθεν ἡ ὥρα τῆς κρίσεως αὐτοῦ — "the hour of his judgment has arrived/come"
    • Source: word-studies/G2064-erchomai.md

    H4941 — mishpat (מִשְׁפָּט)¶

    • Type: Masculine noun, from shaphat (H8199, to judge)
    • Occurrences: 406 unique verses (448 tokens) across 31 books
    • Definition: Properly a verdict (favorable or unfavorable) pronounced judicially — the act, the place, the suit, the crime, and the penalty. Abstractly: justice.
    • KJV translations: judgment/judgments (~291, 72%), manner/custom (~34, 8%), right/lawful (~23, 6%), ordinance/order (~10, 2%), cause/sentence (~9, 2%)
    • Source: word-studies/H4941-mishpat.md

    Grammar Reference Entries Referenced¶

    dan-8-14 (Passage Analysis)¶

    • venitsdaq: Verb.Niphal.Perf.3ms of tsadaq — the ONLY Niphal of tsadaq in the OT
    • Clause type: WQtX (weqatal-X) in quotation domain
    • Prophetic perfect: Perfect with waw in prophetic context = future certainty
    • The sanctuary receives vindication passively (Niphal = subject receives the action)
    • qodesh (holiness/holy place): Noun.ms.Abs, subject of Niphal verb, anarthrous but definite from context
    • ereb boqer (evening-morning): Asyndetic noun pair = compound time unit
    • Clause 2 is elliptical — no explicit verb; the time phrase serves as temporal framework
    • Source: grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-14.md

    dan-8-13-14-dialogue (Passage Analysis)¶

    Five key grammatical features: 1. Two-speaker narrative frame — v.13 has Daniel overhearing; v.14 answer redirected to Daniel (אֵלַי "to me") 2. The question (v.13) — verbless interrogative nominal clause (NmCl) with הֶחָזוֹן as surface subject 3. The answer (v.14) — elliptical (Ellp) time-phrase; עַד mirrors the עַד of the question = syntactic lock-and-key 4. The numeral — dual + construct + absolute = 2,300 units, unit supplied by ereb boqer 5. The verdict clause — weqatal-X (Niphal.Perf.3ms + subject) with the only finite verb in the answer - Source: grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-13-14-dialogue.md

    dan-7-9-10 (Passage Analysis — Aramaic)¶

    • Four tense-phase structure identified in sv-17:
    • Phase 1: Completed actions (Peil.Perf — thrones placed, Ancient of Days sat)
    • Phase 2: Static description (Nominal clauses — garment, hair, throne)
    • Phase 3: Ongoing service (Imperfect — ministered, stood)
    • Phase 4: Judicial acts (Perf — judgment set, books opened)
    • Sequential verbal progression from scene-setting to judicial action
    • Source: grammar-reference/passages/dan-7-9-10.md

    dan-7-13 (Passage Analysis — Aramaic)¶

    • Son of Man (כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ): Noun.ms.Cst + Noun.ms.Abs with simile preposition כְּ
    • Coming (אָתֵ֣ה הֲוָ֑ה): Peal.Ptcp + Peal.Perf = periphrastic imperfect ("was coming") — ongoing movement
    • Approach (מְטָ֔ה): Peal.Perf.3ms — "arrived" at the Ancient of Days
    • Brought near (הַקְרְבֽוּהִי): Haf.Perf.3mp+3ms — presented before him
    • Direction is TOWARD God (to the Ancient of Days), not AWAY from God
    • Source: grammar-reference/passages/dan-7-13.md

    stem-niphal (Grammar Concept)¶

    • Definition: N-stem formed by prefixing nun to the root. Expresses simple passive, reflexive, or tolerative/middle
    • Perfect form: נִ prefix — נִקְטַל (niqtal)
    • Dan 8:14 (נִצְדַּק) listed as canonical example of Niphal Perfect
    • Tense distribution (500 sampled): Imperfect 35%, Perfect 29%, Participle 13%, Wayyiqtol 13%, etc.
    • Source: grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-niphal.md

    ad-matay-interrogative (Grammar Concept)¶

    • Definition: Fixed compound interrogative עַד "until" + מָתַי "when" = "until when? / how long?"
    • Function: Asks for endurance-limit / terminus ad quem of an ongoing trial
    • Three usage categories: (1) Lament/complaint, (2) Impatience/rebuke, (3) Oracular/angelic query
    • Dan 8:13 is the paradigm of Function 3 (oracular query triggering bounded time-answer)
    • Lock-and-key syntax: ad-matay question opens a lock; YHWH's subsequent ad-X answer is the key
    • Isa 6:11 parallel: Same pattern — "How long?" → "Until [ad asher] the cities be wasted..."
    • Source: grammar-reference/hebrew/ad-matay-interrogative.md

    וָאֶשְׁמְעָה אֶחָד־קָדֹושׁ מְדַבֵּר וַיֹּאמֶר אֶחָד קָדֹושׁ לַפַּלְמֹונִי הַמְדַבֵּר
עַד־מָתַי הֶחָזוֹן הַתָּמִיד וְהַפֶּשַׁע שֹׁמֵם תֵּת וְקֹדֶשׁ וְצָבָא מִרְמָס

- וָאֶשְׁמְעָה (שׁמע) — Verb.Qal.Wayq.1s — "and I heard"
- מְדַבֵּר (דבר) — Verb.Piel.Ptcp.ms.Abs — "speaking"
- וַיֹּאמֶר (אמר) — Verb.Qal.Wayq.3ms — "and he said"
- פַּלְמֹונִי — Noun.ms.Abs — "a certain one" (palmoni)
- עַד (עד) — Prep — "unto/until"
- מָתַי (מתי) — Introg — "when" (together = "how long")
- הֶחָזוֹן (חזון) — Noun.ms.Abs with Art — "the vision"
- הַתָּמִיד (תמיד) — Noun.ms.Abs with Art — "the continuity/daily"
- הַפֶּשַׁע (פשׁע) — Noun.ms.Abs with Art — "the rebellion/transgression"
- שֹׁמֵם (שׁמם) — Verb.Qal.Ptcp.ms.Abs — "making desolate"
- תֵּת (נתן) — Verb.Qal.InfCon — "to give"
- וְקֹדֶשׁ (קדשׁ) — Noun.ms.Abs — "and holiness/sanctuary"
- וְצָבָא (צבא) — Noun.ms.Abs — "and host/service"
- מִרְמָס (מרמס) — Noun.ms.Abs — "trampling"

    Daniel 8:14 — Full Parsing¶

    וַיֹּאמֶר אֵלַי עַד עֶרֶב בֹּקֶר אַלְפַּיִם וּשְׁלֹשׁ מֵאוֹת וְנִצְדַּק קֹדֶשׁ

Key morphological data:
- וַיֹּאמֶר (אמר) — Verb.Qal.Wayq.3ms — "and he said"
- אֵלַי (אל) — Prep.+1cs — "to me" (answer redirected to Daniel)
- עַד (עד) — Prep — "unto/until"
- עֶרֶב (ערב) — Noun.ms.Abs — "evening"
- בֹּקֶר (בקר) — Noun.ms.Abs — "morning" (asyndetic pair = compound unit)
- אַלְפַּיִם (אלף) — Noun.dual.Abs — "two thousand"
- שְׁלֹשׁ (שׁלשׁ) — Noun.s.Cst — "three"
- מֵאוֹת (מאה) — Noun.fp.Abs — "hundred" (= 2,300)
- וְנִצְדַּק (צדק) — **Verb.Niphal.Perf.3ms** — "be just/vindicated" (ONLY Niphal of tsadaq in OT)
- קֹדֶשׁ (קדשׁ) — Noun.ms.Abs — "holiness/sanctuary" (subject of passive verb)

    Daniel 7:9 — Full Parsing (Aramaic)¶

    חָזֵה הֲוֵית עַד דִּי כָרְסָוָן רְמִיו וְעַתִּיק יֹומִין יְתִב...

Key morphological data:
- חָזֵה (חזה) — Verb.Peal.Ptcp.ms.Abs — "seeing" (periphrastic with הֲוֵית)
- הֲוֵית (הוה) — Verb.Peal.Perf.1s — "I was"
- עַד דִּי — Prep + Relative — "until" (temporal)
- כָרְסָוָן (כרסא) — Noun.mp.Abs — "thrones"
- רְמִיו (רמה) — Verb.Peil.Perf.3mp — "were placed/set up" (passive)
- עַתִּיק (עתיק) — Adj.ms.Cst — "ancient (of)"
- יֹומִין (יום) — Noun.mp.Abs — "days"
- יְתִב (יתב) — Verb.Peal.Perf.3ms — "sat (down)"
- כָּרְסְיֵהּ (כרסא) — Noun.ms.Abs.+3ms — "his throne"
- שְׁבִיבִין (שׁביב) — Noun.mp.Abs — "flames"
- דָּלִק (דלק) — Verb.Peal.Ptcp.ms.Abs — "burning"

    Daniel 7:10 — Full Parsing (Aramaic)¶

    נְהַר דִּי־נוּר נָגֵד וְנָפֵק מִן־קֳדָמוֹהִי אֶלֶף אַלְפִין יְשַׁמְּשׁוּנֵּהּ
וְרִבֹּו רִבְבָן קָדָמוֹהִי יְקוּמוּן דִּינָא יְתִב וְסִפְרִין פְּתִיחוּ
