    Bible Study: The Maskilim — The Wise Who Understand¶

    Question¶

    Who are the maskilim of Daniel 11:33, 11:35, 12:3, and 12:10? What does sakal (H7919, insight leading to action) mean vs biyn (H995, discernment/comprehension)? The maskilim "instruct many" (11:33), endure persecution and "refinement" (11:35), "shine as the brightness of the firmament" (12:3), and "turn many to righteousness" (matsdiqey, 12:3 — same tsadaq root as nitsdaq in 8:14). How does Dan 12:10's promise ("the wise shall understand, yavinu") complete the biyn chain from 8:16? What characterizes this community — and how do they participate in vindication?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Prior Series Studies (sv-01 through sv-21)¶

    Seven prior series studies contain directly relevant findings:

    • sv-11 (sealing system) — Identified four unsealing mechanisms. The moral/spiritual mechanism DEFINES the maskilim: "Dan 12:10 is the clearest statement — even after the temporal seal expires, understanding is discriminating. Character, not intellect, determines access." The maskilim have been "purified, and made white, and tried" (three purification verbs: barar, laban, tsaraph).
    • sv-16 (Dan 10-12 resolution) — Traced the full biyn chain (19 occurrences of H995 across Dan 8-12). The chain resolves at 10:1 for Daniel but extends to the maskilim at 12:10. Dan 12:10 uses BOTH sakal and biyn in one verse (maskilim yavinu). The purification triad (barar, laban, tsaraph) appears as a set ONLY in Dan 11:35 and 12:10 — exclusive pairing. Stem shift from Hiphil/Piel (11:35) to Hithpael/Niphal (12:10) shows maturation.
    • sv-18 (vindication meaning) — Established the matsdiqey-nitsdaq connection. Dan 12:3's matsdiqey (Hiphil participle of tsadaq) shares the root with nitsdaq (Dan 8:14). Sanctuary receives vindication (Niphal passive); the wise cause many to be righteous (Hiphil causative). Five beneficiaries of vindication identified.
    • sv-20 (Rev 10 unsealing) — The recommission (Rev 10:11) is the vehicle by which the maskilim's understanding reaches the world. The sweet-then-bitter experience leads to broader commission.
    • sv-21 (three angels) — The nitsdaq-matsdiqey-terountes chain: Dan 8:14 → Dan 12:3 → Rev 14:12. The first angel's creation language directly counters the horn's attack on "times and laws."
    • sv-01 (biblical righteousness) — Three dimensions of tsadaq: forensic, relational, character. The Hiphil of tsadaq appears in Isa 53:11 and Dan 12:3.
    • sv-12 (Gabriel's return) — The biyn chain through Dan 8:16 → 8:27 → 9:22-23. Gabriel's commission specifically targets the mar'eh.

    One additional study (dan-18, Daniel 11 power against covenant) covers the historical context for Dan 11:32-35 where the maskilim first appear.

    External Corpus Leads¶

    EGW identifies the "remnant people of God, who keep His commandments" as the Dan 12:10 maskilim (TDG 84.2). Links the maskilim's instructing ministry (11:33) to present-day warning of the deceived (Ms 174, 1903). Connects maskilim shining with "accumulated light of centuries" (4BC 1169.4). Bohr connects Dan 12:3 to Matt 13:43 (ETDP p. 49). Pioneer writers (Miller, Smith) identify the maskilim of 11:33 as "private brethren among the people" who maintained truth through persecution. These require biblical verification.

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The sakal-biyn distinction in Daniel 12:10: Dan 12:10 uses BOTH words in one verse: ha-maskilim (Hiphil participle of sakal) yavinu (Qal imperfect of biyn). Tool discoveries show sakal has 70 occurrences spanning "wise, prosper, prudent, instruct" while biyn has a much larger range focused on "discernment, consideration, understanding." Why does Daniel use both? The research agent should READ D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7919-sakal.md and D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H995-biyn.md, then run hebrew_parser.py on Dan 11:33, 11:35, 12:3, and 12:10 to verify the exact morphological forms (participle, imperfect, etc.) and how each word functions grammatically.

    2. The purification triad (barar-laban-tsaraph) as identity marker: The exclusive pairing of all three verbs in Dan 11:35 and 12:10 — appearing nowhere else as a set in the OT — defines the maskilim's formation process. Sv-16 established the stem shift (Hiphil/Piel in 11:35 → Hithpael/Niphal in 12:10). The research agent should READ D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1305-barar.md, D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3835-laban.md, D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6884-tsaraph.md, and D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-refining.md. Run hebrew_parser.py on Dan 11:35 and Dan 12:10 to verify the stem forms. Also retrieve Mal 3:2-3 (refiner's fire imagery) and Zec 13:9 (refining language) to check whether the same three verbs or similar vocabulary appears.

    3. The matsdiqey-nitsdaq connection as participatory vindication: Dan 12:3's matsdiqey (Hiphil participle construct of tsadaq) mirrors Dan 8:14's nitsdaq (Niphal perfect). Series insights #20 and sv-18 established the complementary stems (Niphal passive = sanctuary receives vindication; Hiphil causative = the wise cause many to be righteous). The research agent should READ D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md and D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md, READ grammar entry D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/passages/dan-8-14.md and D:/bible/bible-studies/grammar-reference/hebrew/stem-hiphil.md. Run hebrew_parser.py on Dan 12:3 to verify the matsdiqey parsing. Cross-reference Isa 53:11 (yatsdiq, Hiphil imperfect of tsadaq — "my righteous servant shall justify many") — the SAME Hiphil stem and a conceptual parallel (one who causes others to be righteous).

    4. The H2094 zahar dual meaning (warn + shine) in Dan 12:3: Tool discovery shows H2094 zahar has 29 occurrences, 28 meaning "warn/admonish" and exactly ONE meaning "shall shine" — Dan 12:3. The semantic_strongs search ranked it at 0.272 under "wise/prudent/insight/instruction" — it is an instruction word in 28/29 uses.     5. The biyn chain's termination at 12:10 — from Daniel to the maskilim: The biyn chain (19 occurrences across Dan 8-12) begins with Gabriel's Hiphil imperative at 8:16 ("cause this man to understand"), fails at 8:27 ("none understood"), resolves for Daniel at 10:1, fails again at 12:8 ("I understood not"), and terminates at 12:10 (the maskilim "shall understand").     6.     7. The maskilim's instructing ministry — Dan 11:33 in historical and eschatological scope: Dan 11:33 says "they that understand among the people shall instruct many" (yaviynuw ... yavinu). The "many days" (yamim rabbim) duration of persecution, combined with the "to the time of the end" phrase in 11:35 (ad eth qets), suggest the maskilim span from the historical persecuted community to the eschatological unsealing community. The research agent should retrieve Dan 11:32-35 with full chapter context. Run hebrew_parser.py on Dan 11:33 to verify the construct (ha-maskilim + am, participial relationship).     8. The "eth qets" (time of the end) temporal boundary: Dan 11:35 includes "even to the time of the end" (ad eth qets) — the same phrase as Dan 12:4 and 12:9.     9. The wicked-wise moral binary (Dan 12:10): Dan 12:10 creates a stark binary: "the wicked shall do wickedly and none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand." The Nave's RIGHTEOUS topic (DAN 12:1-3) and PRUDENCE (DAN 11:32,33) both index this passage.     10. The nitsdaq-matsdiqey-terountes chain — vindication from heaven to earth: Sv-21 established the chain: Dan 8:14 (nitsdaq, sanctuary vindicated) → Dan 12:3 (matsdiqey, the wise turn many to righteousness) → Rev 14:12 (terountes, the saints keep commandments and faith of Jesus).
