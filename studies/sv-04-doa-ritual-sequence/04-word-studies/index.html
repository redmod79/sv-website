    Word Studies¶

    From Word Studies Library¶

    kaphar (H3722) — cover / make atonement¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md

    • Occurrences: 102 OT (67 in Leviticus alone — 66% of all uses)
    • Stems: Piel 92x (dominant), Pual 7x, Qal 1x
    • Piel function: Factitive — "make atonement, expiate, purge" (see grammar-reference stem-piel.md)
    • KJV: "make atonement" ~73x (72%), "purge" 8x, "reconcile" 7x, "forgive/pardon" 5x
    • Key collocations: chattaah (38x), kohen (27x), mizbeach (17x), olah (17x), dam (12x), salach (12x), taher (12x)
    • In Lev 16: kaphar appears 16 times — vv.6,10,11,16,17(x2),18,20,24,27,30,32,33(x3),34
    • LXX: hilaskomai G2433 (highest PMI 6.47), aphiemi G863 (14x), katharizo G2511 (14x)
    • Critical distinction: kaphar+salach = daily service pattern (13x); kaphar+taher = annual DOA pattern. Salach NEVER appears in Lev 16 (verified).

    taher (H2891) — be clean / purify¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2891-taher.md

    • Occurrences: 94 OT (53 in Leviticus — 46%)
    • Stems: Piel 37x (declare/pronounce clean, actively cleanse), Qal 34x (be clean — stative), Hithpael 18x (cleanse oneself)
    • In Lev 16:19: Piel — actively cleanses the altar ("and cleanse it" — wetiharo)
    • In Lev 16:30: Three-verb chain: yekhapper (Piel kaphar) → letaher (Piel taher infinitive construct = "to cleanse you") → titharu (Qal taher = "you shall be clean")
    • Piel vs Qal distinction (from stem-piel.md): Piel taher = "declare/pronounce clean" (declarative-estimative) or "actively cleanse" (factitive). Qal taher = "be clean" (resultant state). In v.30 the shift from Piel to Qal shows the process: God atones → God cleanses → you are clean.
    • LXX: katharizo G2511 (61x primary), katharos G2513 (23x)

    tumah (H2932) — uncleanness¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2932-tumah.md

    • Occurrences: 31 unique verses (14 in Leviticus — 45%)
    • In Lev 16:16: mittumot — "because of the uncleannesses" (from-uncleannesses, plural construct). First term in the three-term formula.
    • In Lev 16:19: mittumot bene yisrael — "from the uncleanness of the children of Israel"
    • Semantic range: bodily discharge impurity (Lev 15), contact/contagion (Lev 5:3; 7:20-21), sanctuary pollution (Lev 16:16,19), national/moral (Ezk 36:17,25)
    • LXX: akatharsia G167 (26x, PMI 28.97 — primary bridge)
    • Key collocation: karath (H3772) 5x — "cut off" penalty formula for uncleanness

    pesha (H6588) — transgression / rebellion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6588-pesha.md

    • Occurrences: 90 unique verses (95 tokens)
    • Root meaning: From pasha H6586 — to break away from authority, rebel. Pesha = the act of willful defection.
    • In Lev 16:16: umipish'eihem — "and because of their transgressions" (second term in three-term formula)
    • In Lev 16:21: kol-pish'eihem — "all their transgressions" (second term again)
    • Key distinction: pesha is willful rebellion, not inadvertent sin. The daily sin offering (Lev 4) covers only inadvertent sins (shegagah). Pesha has no daily provision. The DOA covers what the daily cannot.
    • LXX: distributed across asebeia G763, anomia G458, hamartia G266 — no single Greek equivalent captures "revolt against authority"
    • Classic triad: avon + pesha + chattaah (Exo 34:7; Psa 32:1-2; 51:1-3; Lev 16:21)

    chattaah (H2403) — sin / sin offering¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2403-chattaah.md

    • Occurrences: 270 unique verses (66 in Leviticus — 24%)
    • Dual semantic weight: Both the act of sinning AND the sacrificial remedy. Context distinguishes.
    • In Lev 16:5: "two kids of the goats for a sin offering (chattat)" — singular! Both goats together = one chattat.
    • In Lev 16:16: lekhol-chatto'tam — "in all their sins" (third term in three-term formula, plural)
    • KJV: "sin offering" ~43%, "sin/sins" ~42%
    • LXX: hamartia G266 (242x, PMI 31.95 — overwhelming primary equivalent)

    avon (H5771) — iniquity / guilt¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5771-avon.md

    • Occurrences: 213 unique verses
    • Three BDB senses: (1) the wrongful act (moral perversity), (2) guilt of iniquity (culpable state), (3) punishment/consequence
    • In Lev 16:21: kol-avonot — "all the iniquities" (first term in v.21 formula — replaces tumot from v.16)
    • In Lev 16:22: avonot — scapegoat "bears all their iniquities"
    • Shift v.16 → v.21: tumot (ritual uncleanness, sanctuary perspective) becomes avonot (moral guilt, people perspective). The formula shifts from what defiles the sanctuary to what burdens the people.
    • LXX: adikia G93 (78x), anomia G458 (70x), hamartia G266 (93x)

    nasa (H5375) — bear / carry / lift¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5375-nasa.md

    • Occurrences: 607 unique verses (759 tokens — massive semantic range)
    • In Lev 16:22: "the goat shall bear (nasa) upon him all their iniquities" — Qal perfect 3ms
    • In Lev 10:17: "God hath given it you to bear (nasa) the iniquity of the congregation" — priest bears sin IN via eating
    • Dual direction: Priest bears sin INTO the sanctuary (daily), scapegoat bears sin OUT of the sanctuary (annual). Same verb, opposite directions.
    • In Exo 34:7: nose avon vapesha vechattat — "forgiving (bearing/lifting) iniquity and transgression and sin" — God as subject = forgiveness
    • LXX: airo G142 (164x primary)

    kalah (H3615) — complete / finish¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3615-kalah.md

    • Occurrences: 199 unique verses
    • Stems: Piel 141x (transitive — bring to an end), Qal 64x (intransitive — come to an end)
    • In Lev 16:20: vekhillah mikapper — "and when he hath made an end (kalah Piel) of reconciling (kaphar Piel infinitive construct)" — Piel kalah marks structural boundary
    • Structural function: The scapegoat ceremony (vv.20-22) begins AFTER kalah — after blood atonement is complete. This verb separates the two phases of the DOA.

    nazah (H5137) — sprinkle¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5137-nazah.md

    • Occurrences: 22 unique verses (13 in Leviticus)
    • Stems: Hiphil 20x (standard priestly sprinkling), Qal 4x (natural spattering)
    • In Lev 16:14: Hiphil — hizzah (once on kapporeth surface) then yazzeh (seven times before it). Same verb, two applications.
    • In Lev 16:15: Same pattern with LORD's goat blood.
    • In Lev 16:19: Seven times on the altar — cleansing moves outward.
    • Key distinction from zaraq (H2236): nazah = fine finger-sprinkling; zaraq = forceful dashing/throwing at altar base. DOA uses nazah exclusively.
    • Pattern: 1x on + 7x before (MHP) → 7x on (altar) — consistent "seven times" motif
    • Collocations: dam (12x, 55%), sheba paam (10x, 45%), etsba (7x, 32%)

    karath (H3772) — cut off / make covenant¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3772-karath.md

    • Occurrences: 280 unique verses
    • In Lev 23:29: Niphal — venikhretah — "shall be cut off" (passive: divine agency). The soul that is NOT afflicted shall be cut off from her people.
    • Niphal stem (from stem-niphal.md): Passive — the agent performing the cutting off is God, not human courts.
    • Karat penalty scope: Covenant violation formula. Appears ~30x in Leviticus-Numbers.

    azazel (H5799) — scapegoat¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5799-azazel.md

    • Occurrences: 4 tokens in 3 verses — all in Lev 16 (16:8 x2, 16:10, 16:26)
    • Etymology: From ez (goat, H5795) + azal (go away/disappear, H235) = "goat of departure"
    • Syntactic parallel: la-YHWH / la-azazel (Lev 16:8) — identical grammatical structure (preposition lamed + proper noun/name)
    • BHSA classification: Grouped with proper-name domain [003001012] Names of Supernatural Beings
    • LXX: apopompaios ("one sent away") — descriptive, not a name
    • Structural markers of scapegoat: (1) alive — not killed, (2) after kalah — comes after blood atonement is complete, (3) sent to desolation, (4) by ish itti — "fit man" (hapax), (5) handler becomes ritually unclean (v.26)

    samak (H5564) — lay hands on¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5564-camak.md

    • Occurrences: 47 unique verses (14 in Leviticus — 30%)
    • In Lev 16:21: Qal — vesamakh Aharon et-shtei yadav — "Aaron shall lay BOTH his hands." Distinctive: all other sacrificial hand-layings use one hand. Only the scapegoat receives both hands.
    • Function: Identification and transfer. In regular sacrifices (Lev 1:4; 4:29): one hand on head of animal before slaughter. On scapegoat: two hands + verbal confession = transfer of sins.

    yadah (H3034) — confess / praise¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3034-yadah.md

    • Occurrences: 111 unique verses
    • In Lev 16:21: Hithpael — vehitvaddah — "and confess" (reflexive of Piel). The ONLY reflexive verb in Lev 16.
    • Hithpael confession function (from stem-hithpael.md): The Hithpael of yadah = to confess sins (not praise). Reflexive: the subject confesses for/over itself. The same stem appears in Lev 5:5; 26:40; Num 5:7; Dan 9:4; Neh 1:6; 9:2.
    • Hiphil vs Hithpael: Hiphil = praise/give thanks (dominant, ~113x). Hithpael = confess sins (~10x). Completely different semantic functions by stem.

    shalach (H7971) — send / send away¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7971-shalach.md

    • Occurrences: 790 unique verses
    • In Lev 16:21: Piel — veshillach — "and shall send away." Piel = intensive/frequentative "send away, let go, release, dismiss."
    • In Lev 16:22: "he shall let go (shalach Piel) the goat in the wilderness"
    • Piel note from stem-piel.md: shalach Piel = "send forth/away" vs. Qal "stretch out." The same Piel form is used for Pharaoh "letting go" Israel in Exodus.

    bad (H906) — linen¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H906-bad.md

    • Occurrences: 19 unique verses (4 in Leviticus, 6 in Ezekiel, 3 in Daniel)
    • In Lev 16:4: Four items all made of bad: kutonet bad (linen tunic), mikhnesei bad (linen breeches), avnet bad (linen girdle), mitsnefet bad (linen mitre). The word bad appears 4x in this one verse.
    • Designation: bigdei-qodesh hem — "garments of holiness they [are]" (nominal clause, v.4 end)
    • Judgment figures in bad: Ezk 9:2 (man with inkhorn — marking for judgment), Dan 10:5 (heavenly man — eschatological revelation), Dan 12:6-7 (the same figure over the waters)
    • Rev 15:6 parallel: Angels "clothed in pure and white linen" — Greek linon, judgment context

    Other Library Entries Read (summary data)¶

    kapporeth (H3727): 22 verses, all "mercy seat." In Lev 16: blood applied upon the kapporeth surface (v.14,15). 7x in vv.2-15, zero in vv.16-34. LXX hilasterion G2435 → Rom 3:25 (Christ as hilasterion). Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3727-kapporeth.md

    kippur (H3725): "Atonement" — only plural kippurim. "Yom hakkippurim" = Day of Atonement (Lev 23:27). Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3725-kippur.md

    tame (H2930): "Be unclean/defile" — root verb of tumah. ~160 verses. Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2930-tame.md

    qadash (H6942): "Be holy / consecrate" — Piel factitive "make holy." In Lev 16:19: veqiddesho — "and hallow it" (Piel, factitive — make the altar holy again). Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6942-qadash.md

    qodesh (H6944): "Holiness / holy thing" — the object kaphar acts upon in Lev 16. Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md

    saiyr (H8163): "He-goat / hairy" — the goat animals. Collocates with chattaah 41x. Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8163-saiyr.md

    tamid (H8548): "Continual" — the daily offering that never stops, even on DOA (Num 29:11). Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md

    salach (H5545): "Forgive" — VERIFIED ABSENT from Lev 16. 49 total occurrences.     anah (H6031) — afflict / humble / be lowly¶

    Source: Fresh tool queries (library entry was not generated)

    • Occurrences: 84 (BLB) / 92 tokens (search_strongs)
    • Transliteration: anah (ʻânâh)
    • Definition: A primitive root; to depress literally or figuratively, transitive or intransitive — abase self, afflict(-ion, self), chasten self, deal hardly with, defile, exercise, force, gentleness, humble (self), hurt, ravish, submit self, weaken

    Semantic range (from KJV translations): - "afflict" — 5x; "afflicted" — 2x; "ye shall afflict" — 4x; "was afflicted" — 3x - "humble" — 5x (Deut 8:2,3,16; 2 Sam 7:10) - "forced/force" — 5x (sexual violence: Gen 34:2; 2 Sam 13:12,14,22,32) - "exercised" — 2x (Ecc 1:13; 3:10)

    Stem analysis from hebrew_parser.py: - Lev 16:29,31: Piel Perfect 2mp — veinnitem et-nafshoteikhem — "you shall afflict your souls" (active, intensive) - Lev 23:27: Piel Perfect 2mp — veinnitem — same form - Lev 23:29: Pual Imperfect 3fs — lo te'unneh — "shall not be afflicted" (passive). The shift from Piel (active "you shall afflict") to Pual (passive "shall not be afflicted") in the penalty clause is significant — the Pual implies divine agency or external process. - Lev 23:32: Piel Perfect 2mp — veinnitem — active command again - Num 29:7: Same Piel form

    "Afflict your souls" cross-references verified: - Isa 58:3 — "we afflicted our soul" (Piel) — Israel asking why their affliction wasn't noticed. God responds: true affliction = loosing bands of wickedness, freeing the oppressed, feeding the hungry. - Isa 58:5 — "a day for a man to afflict his soul" (Piel) — God questions whether outward fasting IS affliction. - Psa 35:13 — "I humbled (anah, Piel) my soul with fasting" — connects affliction with fasting. - Ezra 8:21 — "afflict ourselves (Hithpael) before our God" — communal fasting and prayer. - Dan 10:12 — "chasten thyself" (anah Hithpael) — Daniel's self-humbling through fasting (Dan 10:2-3).

    Key observations: 1. The DOA anah is Piel — intensive/factitive: actively bring about the state of lowliness/affliction upon one's nephesh (soul). 2. The penalty clause (Lev 23:29) shifts to Pual — the soul that is NOT passively afflicted (or: does not find itself in the state of affliction) is cut off. 3. Cross-references consistently link anah with fasting (Psa 35:13; Isa 58:3,5; Dan 10:2-3,12; Ezra 8:21), prayer, and self-humbling before God. 4. The karat penalty (Niphal karath — divine passive) for not afflicting = the DOA creates a binary: those who humble themselves (participate) and those who do not (cut off). This is a sorting/judgment function.
