    Bible Study: Biblical Righteousness — Vocabulary Foundation for the Vindication Verdict¶

    Question¶

    What does the Bible mean by righteousness? What are the Hebrew words (tsadaq H6663, tsedeq H6664, tsedaqah H6666, tsaddiyq H6662) and Greek words (dikaios G1342, dikaiosyne G1343, dikaioo G1344, dikaiosis G1347)? How is righteousness received, maintained, and lost? Cover forensic (declared), relational (covenant faithfulness), and character (conduct) dimensions. This study establishes the vocabulary that culminates in Daniel 8:14's nitsdaq — the learner must understand that tsadaq is courtroom language before encountering it in the prophetic verdict.

    Series Context¶

    This is Study 1 of 23 in the "Daniel 8:14 and the Sanctuary Vindication" series. It establishes the tsadaq endpoint of the progression tame → tahor → tsadaq (defiled → cleansed → vindicated). The learner must grasp that tsadaq is judicial vocabulary BEFORE encountering it in Dan 8:14. No prior series studies exist — this is the foundation.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    From prior study (biblical-righteousness): - The tsadaq root produces four major words: tsaddiyq (H6662, 210x), tsedaqah (H6666, 165x), tsedeq (H6664, 119x), tsadaq verb (H6663, 41x) — over 530 OT occurrences total - Verb stem variations encode dimensions: Qal (be righteous, 22x), Hiphil (declare righteous/causative, 12x — includes Isa 53:11 and Exo 23:7), Piel (comparative, 5x), Niphal (Dan 8:14 only — passive verdict), Hithpael (Gen 44:16, justify oneself) - Greek dik- family: dikaios (G1342, 81x), dikaiosyne (G1343, 92x), dikaioo (G1344, 40x), dikaioma (G1345, 10x), dikaiosis (G1347, 2x), dikaiokrisia (G1341, hapax Rom 2:5) - LXX bridge: tsaddiyq → dikaios PMI 32.40 (extremely tight correspondence) - dikaioo voice distribution: ~30 passive (divine passive), ~9 active — justification is received, not achieved - Righteousness obtained by faith through imputation (Gen 15:6 → Rom 4:3). Maintained by walking in the Spirit (Rom 8:1-4), confession (1 John 1:7-9), faith working through love (Gal 5:6). Lost through deliberate, sustained turning (Eze 18:24, Heb 10:26-29, 2 Pet 2:20-22). - Sanctuary bridge: Lev 16 = kaphar → taher; Dan 9:24 = kaphar → tsedeq olamim; Zech 3 = filthy garments removed + festival robes given; Rom 3:25-26 = hilasterion → dikaios kai dikaiunta - Garment imagery: Job 29:14 (conduct), Isa 61:10 (God provides), Zech 3:3-5 (cleansing + investiture), Matt 22:11-14 (required), Rev 7:14 (washed), Rev 19:8 (dikaiomata = righteous acts, granted)

    From sanc-19 (vindication-courtroom): - Dan 8:14's nitsdaq uses courtroom vocabulary (tsadaq), not ritual vocabulary (taher/kaphar) — THE foundational lexical observation - Four dimensions of vindication: God's Law, God's People, God's Plan, God's Character - The riyb (H7378, lawsuit) pattern: God initiates His own trial (Mic 6:1-2), invites cross-examination (Isa 43:26), asks to be evaluated (Isa 5:3-4) - Isa 53:11: three tsadaq forms converge — righteous servant IS righteous, DECLARES many righteous, BY BEARING iniquities

    From sanc-25 (Daniel 8:14 vindicated): - nitsdaq = Niphal Perfect of tsadaq (H6663) — the ONLY Niphal in the entire OT - KJV's "cleansed" is unique among 54 occurrences — anomaly from Vulgate influence - OG LXX uses dikaiothesatai (justified); Theodotion uses katharisthesetai (cleansed) - kaphar → tsadaq progression across four texts: Lev 16:30 → Dan 9:24 → Isa 53:11 → Rom 3:25-26 → Dan 8:14

    From atone-16 (justification by faith): - dikaioo is courtroom vocabulary, Louw-Nida domain 56.005 — Courts: Judge, Condemn, Acquit - Antonym pair katakrima/dikaiosis (G2631/G1347) in Rom 5:18 - Four atonement frameworks each contribute a dimension to justification

    External corpus leads (EGW, Bohr) — for biblical verification: - EGW claims imputed righteousness "means holiness, uprightness, purity" (TSB 134.1) and the righteousness garment = actual character, not just legal covering - EGW connects Dan 9:24 "everlasting righteousness" to Christ's imputed righteousness clothing the believer (1SM 396.1) - EGW distinguishes "active obedience of Christ" clothing the believer with righteousness meeting law's demands (SD 240.4) - Bohr claims tsadaq is "not connected with the ritual of the sanctuary but rather is a legal term used in the context of a court of law" (GPOT2V1 p. 442) - Bohr argues Dan 7:9-14 (judicial) and Dan 8:14 (sanctuary) describe the same event from different angles

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The tsadaq word family: four Hebrew words, one root
    2. WHAT: Map all four Hebrew words (H6663 tsadaq verb, H6664 tsedeq, H6666 tsedaqah, H6662 tsaddiyq) with their distinct usages, occurrence counts, and semantic ranges.
    3. WHY: Tool discoveries confirm 530+ OT occurrences across the family. The verb's stem variations (Qal, Niphal, Hiphil, Piel, Hithpael) encode different dimensions of righteousness — and the Niphal occurs only in Dan 8:14. Prior studies emphasize this as THE foundational lexical observation for the series.

    4. HOW: READ the four library entries: H6663-tsadaq.md, H6664-tsedeq.md, H6666-tsedaqah.md, H6662-tsaddiyq.md. Also READ WG-hebrew-righteousness.md for the word group overview. Run search_strongs.py --lookup H6663 for fresh data on stem distributions if needed. Retrieve Gen 15:6, Exo 23:7, Deu 25:1, Isa 53:11, Psa 51:4, Dan 8:14 with full chapter context.

    5. The Greek dik- family: seven NT words mapping to tsadaq

    6. WHAT: Map all Greek dik- words (G1342 dikaios, G1343 dikaiosyne, G1344 dikaioo, G1345 dikaioma, G1346 dikaios adverb, G1347 dikaiosis, G1341 dikaiokrisia) with their usage patterns and voice distributions.
    7. WHY: Nave's JUSTIFICATION entry surfaced 40+ verses in Romans and Galatians using dik- vocabulary. The dikaioo voice distribution (~30 passive, ~9 active) reveals that justification is divine action, not human achievement. dikaiosis appears only twice (Rom 4:25, 5:18), making each occurrence critical.

    8. HOW: READ the seven library entries: G1342-dikaios.md, G1343-dikaiosyne.md, G1344-dikaioo.md, G1345-dikaioma.md, G1346-dikaios.md, G1347-dikaiosis.md, G1341-dikaiokrisia.md.     9. The LXX bridge: tsadaq → dikaioo and the cross-linguistic unity

    10. WHAT: Document how the LXX translators rendered the Hebrew tsadaq family into Greek, establishing the continuity between OT and NT righteousness vocabulary.
    11. WHY: The translation relationship TR-tsadaq-dikaioo shows PMI 26.99 (or higher), one of the tightest Hebrew-Greek mappings in the LXX. This proves that when Paul writes dikaioo, he means exactly what Moses and Isaiah meant by tsadaq. Tool discoveries confirm dikaioo's Hebrew sources include tsadaq (26.99), shaphat (12.26), zakah (11.43), riyb (9.34) — the entire orbit is legal.
