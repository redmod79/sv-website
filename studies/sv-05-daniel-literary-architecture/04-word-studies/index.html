    Word Studies¶

    From Word Studies Library¶

    chazon — H2377 (Vision/Panoramic Revelation)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2377-chazon.md

    • Word: חָזוֹן | Transliteration: chazon | POS: Masculine noun
    • Root: H2372 chazah (to gaze, perceive in vision)
    • Occurrences: 37 in OT; Daniel = 12 (32% of all OT occurrences)
    • Semantic domain: Prophetic/apocalyptic revelation — the divinely revealed content
    • Key distinction from mar'eh: chazon = the revelatory CONTENT; mar'eh = the visual APPEARANCE
    • LXX primary equivalent: ὅρασις (horasis) G3706 (28x, ~76%)
    • Daniel occurrences as "the vision" (H2377): Dan 8:13, 8:15, 8:17, 8:26, 9:24, 10:14, 11:14
    • All Daniel chazon occurrences (12x): Dan 1:17, 8:1, 8:2, 8:13, 8:15, 8:17, 8:26, 9:21, 9:24, 10:14, 11:14 + one in Aramaic section as chezev (Dan 7)
    • Collocations with biyn: 3x — understanding the vision (Daniel specifically)

    mar'eh — H4758 (Appearance/Sight/Vision)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md

    • Word: מַרְאֶה | Transliteration: mar'eh | POS: Masculine noun
    • Root: H7200 ra'ah (to see — primitive root)
    • Occurrences: 110 tokens across 82 verses; Daniel = 11 (3.1 per 100 verses)
    • Semantic range: Physical attractiveness, facial countenance, clinical inspection (Lev 13), theophanic appearances, prophetic visions
    • Key distinction from chazon: mar'eh = what is SEEN (the visual form/appearance); chazon = the revealed CONTENT
    • LXX primary equivalent: ὄψις (opsis) G3799 for appearance; ὅρασις (horasis) G3706 for vision-sense
    • Daniel as "the vision" (H4758): Dan 8:16, 8:27, 9:23 (+ Dan 10:1 as "the vision")
    • Critical LXX note: LXX erases the Hebrew chazon/mar'eh distinction — both rendered ὅρασις in prophetic contexts

    The chazon/mar'eh distribution in Daniel 8: | Verse | Term | Context | |-------|------|---------| | 8:1 | chazon | "a vision appeared unto me" | | 8:2 | chazon | "I saw in a vision" | | 8:5 | — | Daniel "considering" (biyn) | | 8:13 | chazon | "how long the vision concerning the tamid" | | 8:15 | chazon + mar'eh | "had seen the chazon... as the mar'eh of a man" | | 8:16 | mar'eh | Gabriel commanded: "make him understand the MAR'EH" | | 8:17 | chazon | "at the time of the end shall be the chazon" | | 8:26 | mar'eh + chazon | "the MAR'EH of the evening and morning is true... seal the CHAZON" | | 8:27 | mar'eh | "astonished at the MAR'EH, and none understood" |

    Key finding: The shift at v.16 is not absolute — chazon still appears at v.17. But the object of Gabriel's commission and Daniel's non-understanding is consistently mar'eh (8:16, 8:27, 9:23, 10:1).

    biyn — H995 (Understand/Discern)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H995-biyn.md

    • Word: בִּין | Transliteration: biyn | POS: Verb (primitive root)
    • Root: Primitive — "to separate mentally, distinguish"
    • Occurrences: 162 verses (189 tokens); Daniel = 19 (5.3 per 100 verses — HIGHEST of any book)
    • Stem distribution: Qal 45, Hiphil 30, Hithpael 22, Niphal 21, Piel 1
    • LXX primary equivalent: συνίημι (syniemi) G4920 (63 co-occurrences)

    The biyn chain in Daniel (8 stages, verified by parsing):

    Stage Verse Hebrew Form Stem Meaning
    1a Dan 1:4 mebiyney Hiphil.Ptcp.mp "understanding science" — selection criteria
    1b Dan 1:17 hebiyn Hiphil.Perf.3ms "Daniel had understanding in all visions" — God-given gift
    2 Dan 8:16 habiyn Hiphil.Impv.2ms "make this man UNDERSTAND the mar'eh" — command to Gabriel
    3 Dan 8:27 mebiyn Hiphil.Ptcp.ms + ein "none understood" — failure
    4 Dan 9:2 biynoti Qal.Perf.1cs "I understood by books" — Daniel reads Jeremiah
    5a Dan 9:22 yabiyn Hiphil.Wayq.3ms "he informed me" — Gabriel returns
    5b Dan 9:22 biynah Noun (goal) "to give thee skill and understanding"
    5c Dan 9:23 biyn Qal.Impv.2ms "understand the matter"
    5d Dan 9:23 habiyn Hiphil.Impv.2ms "consider the mar'eh"
    6a Dan 10:1 biyn Qal.Perf.3ms "he understood the thing" — RESOLUTION
    6b Dan 10:1 biynah Noun (achieved) "and had understanding of the mar'eh"
    7 Dan 12:8 abiyn Qal.Impf.1cs "I heard, but I understood NOT" — reversal
    8 Dan 12:10 yabiyn (2x) Qal.Impf.3mp "none of wicked shall understand; the wise SHALL understand"

    biynah — H998 (Understanding)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H998-biynah.md

    • Word: בִּינָה | Transliteration: biynah | POS: Feminine noun
    • Root: H995 biyn
    • Occurrences: 38 verses; Daniel = 4 (1.1 per 100 verses)
    • Daniel occurrences: Dan 1:20 (understanding), Dan 8:15 (sought biynah), Dan 9:22 (give biynah), Dan 10:1 (biynah of the mar'eh)
    • Nominal form of biyn — the faculty/result of understanding

    sakal — H7919 (Be Wise/Prosper/Understand)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7919-sakal.md

    • Word: שָׂכַל | Transliteration: sakal | POS: Verb (primitive root)
    • Occurrences: 63 verses; Hiphil dominant (~60/63)
    • Key distinction from biyn: sakal = prudent ACTION and its successful outcome; biyn = mental PERCEPTION and discernment
    • Daniel pairing: Dan 1:4 (maskilim — wise), Dan 1:17 (haskel — skill), Dan 9:22 (lehaskilkha — give skill), Dan 9:25 (know and understand), Dan 11:33 (maskilim — they that understand), Dan 11:35 (maskilim — of understanding), Dan 12:3 (maskilim — the wise), Dan 12:10 (maskilim — the wise)
    • LXX equivalent: Also maps to syniemi G4920 — see TR-sakal-syniemi.md

    chatham — H2856 (Seal)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md

    • Word: חָתַם | Transliteration: chatham | POS: Verb (primitive root)
    • Occurrences: 26 tokens, 24 verses; Daniel = 3 (Dan 9:24, 12:4, 12:9)
    • Stem distribution: Qal 22 (basic sealing action), Niphal 2, Piel 1, Hiphil 1
    • Semantic categories: Legal/documentary sealing, divine sealing of hidden things, prophetic sealing of revelation, physical closure
    • LXX: σφραγίζω (sphragizō) G4972 — 15x primary equivalent
    • Daniel sealed content (per Insight #2): The temporal element (2300 erev boqer) is sealed, not the event (vindication)

    qets — H7093 (End)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7093-qets.md

    • Word: קֵץ | Transliteration: qets | POS: Masculine noun
    • Root: qatsats (to cut off)
    • Occurrences: 71 in OT; Daniel = 16 (4.5 per 100 verses — HIGHEST of any book)
    • The eth qets formula: Unique to Daniel — 5 occurrences (8:17, 11:35, 11:40, 12:4, 12:9)
    • LXX split: πέρας (peras) for spatial/absolute end; καιρός (kairos) for appointed-time sense

    acharith — H319 (Latter End/Future)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H319-achariyth.md

    • Word: אַחֲרִית | Transliteration: acharith | POS: Feminine noun
    • Occurrences: 60 verses; Daniel = 5
    • The be'acharith hayyamim formula: "In the latter days" — prophetic formula (Gen 49:1; Deut 4:30; Isa 2:2; Dan 10:14; Mic 4:1)
    • Daniel inclusio: Dan 2:28 (Aramaic acharith) → Dan 10:14 (Hebrew acharith) — frames ALL four vision cycles
    • LXX: ἔσχατος (eschatos) G2078 — dominates; continues as NT "last days"
    • Key distinction from qets: acharith = what comes AFTER (the aftermath/resulting state); qets = the CUT-OFF POINT (terminus)

    tamid — H8548 (Continual)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md

    • Word: תָּמִיד | Transliteration: tamid | POS: Masculine noun (attributive/adverbial)
    • Occurrences: 104 in OT; Daniel = 5 (Dan 8:11, 8:12, 8:13, 11:31, 12:11)
    • 100% positive across 104 OT occurrences (established by sv-03 study)
    • Daniel's unique usage: Used substantively as "the tamid" (the continual [service]) — no qualifying noun
    • Aramaic equivalent: tedira (H8411) — used in Dan 6:16,20 for "continually"

    shiqquts — H8251 (Abomination/Detestable Thing)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8251-shiqquwts.md

    • Word: שִׁקּוּץ | Transliteration: shiqquts | POS: Masculine noun
    • Root: H8262 shaqats (to be filthy, detest)
    • Occurrences: 29 in OT; Daniel = 3 (Dan 9:27, 11:31, 12:11)
    • LXX: βδέλυγμα (bdelygma) G946 (~70%) — carried into NT (Matt 24:15, Mark 13:14)
    • Daniel pairing with tamid: Dan 11:31, 12:11 — the tamid is taken away and the shiqquts is set up

    tsadaq — H6663 (Be Righteous/Vindicate)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md

    • Word: צָדַק | Transliteration: tsadaq | POS: Verb (primitive root)
    • Occurrences: 41 in OT; Daniel = 4 (Dan 8:14, 9:7, 9:14, 12:3)
    • Niphal (1 occurrence): Dan 8:14 nitsdaq — "the sanctuary shall be vindicated/restored to rightness"
    • The destination of the literary structure: The biyn chain drives toward understanding; the endpoint of that understanding is the nitsdaq verdict of Dan 8:14

    chazah — H2372 (Gaze/Behold/Prophesy)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2372-chazah.md

    • Root verb of the chazon word family
    • 51 OT occurrences; specialized prophetic seeing (not ordinary sight)

    chozeh — H2374 (Seer)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2374-chozeh.md (newly generated)

    • Word: חֹזֶה | Transliteration: chozeh | POS: Masculine noun (active participle of chazah)
    • Occurrences: 21 in OT
    • Primary rendering: "seer" (76% of uses)
    • Concentrated in Chronicles (10/21)
    • Distinguished from nabiy (prophet): chozeh = visionary reception mode; nabiy = proclamation function
    • Distinguished from roeh (H7203): roeh from common ra'ah "to see"; chozeh from specialized chazah "to gaze in vision"
    • LXX: prophetes G4396 (14x) — collapses the Hebrew seer/prophet distinction

    raah — H7200 (See)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7200-raah.md

    • Root verb of the mar'eh word family
    • 1,300+ OT occurrences; ordinary physical sight + divine seeing
    • Dan 8:15 uses ra'ah (infinitive construct) for Daniel's perception of the chazon

    moed — H4150 (Appointed Time/Meeting)¶
