    Bible Study: Daniel 7:9-10 as the Antitypical Day of Atonement¶

    Question¶

    How does Dan 7:9-10 correspond to Lev 16? Five thematic parallels. Structural or lexical? What do books contain? kaphar-to-tsadaq progression as linguistic bridge.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    From Series Studies (sv-01 through sv-16)¶

    Sixteen prior series studies provide extensive foundation: - sv-01 established the tsadaq word family (530+ OT uses, dik- family 234+ NT uses); nitsdaq is the ONLY Niphal of tsadaq in the OT; three dimensions of righteousness (forensic, relational, character) - sv-02 mapped two-axis defilement framework (qodesh/chol, tahor/tame); three mechanisms of sanctuary defilement; DOA three-category formula (tumot, pesha, chattat in Lev 16:16) - sv-03 proved daily and annual are two halves of one system; absolute vocabulary distinction kaphar+salach (daily) vs kaphar+taher (annual); nasa connects both halves - sv-04 mapped the 12-step DOA ritual sequence; chiastic structure with five grammatical markers; Lev 16:17 "no man in the tabernacle" as grammatical center - sv-07 proved Dan 7 and Dan 8 horns are the same entity; Dan 7 provides the courtroom scene, Dan 8 provides the sanctuary context - sv-14 established Dan 9:24 as the ONLY verse where kaphar and tsedeq appear together; six infinitival purposes in two triads; sin-triad matches Lev 16:21 exactly (pesha, chattat, avon) - sv-15 calculated 2300 = 1844; event at terminus = nitsdaq qodesh = beginning of antitypical DOA = pre-advent judgment of Dan 7:9-10

    From Non-Series Studies¶

    • dan-7-judgment-antitypical-doa (score: 0.767) — directly on point, earlier study on same question. Found DOA-to-Dan 7 parallel holds at structural/functional level, not lexical. Five-element correspondence tested: judicial examination strongest, cloud weakest. kaphar-to-tsadaq progression = strongest linguistic bridge. Books contain "works" (Rev 20:12), broader than tamid-transferred sins.
    • sanc-24-daniel-7-court (score: 0.683) — mapped Aramaic judicial vocabulary: dina yetib, rmah Peil, sephar with Persian court-archive associations. Three-phase judgment structure.
    • day-of-atonement-revelation-chiasm (score: 0.558) — DOA as liturgical template for Revelation's chiastic architecture. Lev 16:17 / Rev 15:8 parallel. Scapegoat = Satan's binding.

    External Corpus Claims (to verify)¶

    1. EGW (4SP 266.1): Sins "in fact, transferred to the heavenly sanctuary by the blood of Christ"; cleansing = "removal, or blotting out, of the sins which are there recorded"; necessitates "investigative judgment"
    2. EGW (1BC 1111.5): Direct typological line from DOA high priest's white linen garments to Christ's ministry
    3. Bohr (GPOT2V1 p.451): Dan 8 / Lev 1-16 thematic sequence runs parallel: daily ministration chapters → annual cleansing chapter

    Focus Areas¶

    1. Five thematic parallels — systematic element-by-element test: The earlier study (dan-7-judgment-antitypical-doa) tested all five parallels and found judicial examination strongest, cloud weakest. This study must re-examine each with the series vocabulary now available (sv-01 through sv-16).
