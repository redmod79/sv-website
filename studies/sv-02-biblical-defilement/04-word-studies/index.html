    Core Defilement Vocabulary¶

    tame (verb) — H2930¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2930-tame.md

    • Word: טָמֵא (tame), primitive root verb
    • Occurrences: 142 unique verses (194 tokens) across 13 books
    • Definition: To be foul, especially in a ceremonial or moral sense; defile (self), pollute (self), be/make/pronounce unclean
    • Stem Distribution:
    • Qal (72) — be/become unclean (involuntary state change)
    • Piel (43) — defile, make unclean, pronounce unclean (causative/declarative)
    • Niphal (16) — be defiled (passive)
    • Hithpael (12) — defile oneself (reflexive — voluntary self-contamination)
    • Hotpael (1) — be made unclean (Deut 24:4 only)
    • Pual (1) — be defiled (Ezek 4:14 only)
    • Distribution: Leviticus 71 (50%), Ezekiel 28 (20%), Numbers 21
    • LXX: μιαίνω G3392 (86x, primary verb), ἀκάθαρτος G169 (53x, adjective)
    • Semantic categories: (1) Ritual impurity from contact, (2) Bodily discharges/skin conditions, (3) Priestly declaration (Piel), (4) Sexual defilement, (5) Idolatry/self-defilement (Hithpael), (6) Defilement of sacred space/land, (7) Josiah's deliberate desecration (Piel)
    • Key insight: Stem distribution maps directly onto the ritual/moral distinction: Qal = involuntary, Piel = causative/declarative, Hithpael = deliberate self-defilement (almost exclusively idolatry in Ezekiel)

    tame (adjective) — H2931¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2931-tame.md

    • Word: טָמֵא (tame), adjective
    • Occurrences: 78 unique verses across 15 books
    • Definition: Foul in a religious sense — defiled, infamous, polluted, unclean
    • Distribution: Leviticus 38 (49%), Numbers 11, Deuteronomy 8
    • LXX: ἀκάθαρτος G169 (69x, primary), ἀκαθαρσία G167 (8x)
    • Semantic categories: (1) Unclean animals, (2) Disease/leprosy, (3) Contact with death, (4) Bodily discharges, (5) The clean/unclean binary (Lev 10:10; 11:47; Ezek 44:23), (6) Moral/spiritual uncleanness (prophetic extension), (7) Unclean land
    • Key collocations: tahor H2889 (13x — the governing binary), kohen H3548 (10x — priest pronouncing), naga H5060 (14x — contact transmission)

    tumah (noun) — H2932¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2932-tumah.md

    • Word: טֻמְאָה (tumah), feminine noun
    • Occurrences: 31 unique verses (36 tokens) across 9 books
    • Definition: Religious impurity: filthiness, unclean(-ness)
    • Translation: "uncleanness" (77%), "filthiness" (19%), "unclean" (6%)
    • Distribution: Leviticus 14 (45%), Ezekiel 7 (23%)
    • LXX: ἀκαθαρσία G167 (26x, PMI 28.97 — near-exclusive rendering)
    • Semantic categories: (1) Bodily discharge impurity, (2) Contact/contagion, (3) Sanctuary pollution requiring DOA, (4) Nazirite/dietary separation, (5) National/moral uncleanness (prophetic), (6) Land pollution, (7) Persistent moral corruption
    • Key verses: Lev 16:16 (DOA addresses tumot), Lev 15:31 (ambient defilement clause), Lev 16:19 (cleanse altar from tumah)
    • Key collocations: kol H3605 "all" (14x, 45%), taher H2891 (5x — antonymic action), karath H3772 "cut off" (5x — penalty)

    Core Cleanness Vocabulary¶

    tahor (adjective) — H2889¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2889-tahowr.md

    • Word: טָהוֹר (tahor), adjective
    • Occurrences: 86 unique verses across 17 books
    • Definition: Pure (physical, chemical, ceremonial, or moral sense): clean, fair, pure(-ness)
    • Translation: "pure" (44%), "clean" (36%)
    • Distribution: Exodus 28 (tabernacle "pure gold"), Leviticus 20
    • LXX: καθαρός G2513 (65x, PMI 7.06 — primary)
    • Semantic categories: (1) Pure gold/material purity, (2) Ceremonially clean persons, (3) Clean animals, (4) Skin disease verdicts, (5) Moral/inner purity, (6) Sacred distinction (Lev 10:10), (7) Pure worship
    • Antonym: tame H2931 (13x contrastive pair)

    taher (verb) — H2891¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2891-taher.md

    • Word: טָהֵר (taher), verb
    • Occurrences: 94 across 80 unique verses
    • Definition: To be bright; to be pure, clean; to cleanse, purify, pronounce clean
    • Stem Distribution:
    • Piel (37) — declare/pronounce clean; actively cleanse
    • Qal (34) — be clean (stative)
    • Hithpael (18) — cleanse oneself, purify oneself
    • Pual (1) — be cleansed
    • Distribution: Leviticus 53 (46%), Ezekiel 15
    • LXX: καθαρίζω G2511 (61x, primary)
    • Key pairing: kaphar H3722 (12x — "make atonement... and he shall be clean")
    • Key insight for DOA: Lev 16:30 uses kaphar + taher — atonement produces cleansing. This is the tahor step in the tame→tahor→tsadaq progression. salach (forgiveness) belongs to daily; taher (cleansing) belongs to annual.

    Two-Axis Framework Vocabulary¶

    qodesh (noun) — H6944¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md

    • Word: קֹדֶשׁ (qodesh), masculine noun
    • Occurrences: 452 in OT across 31 books
    • Definition: Sacred place or thing; sanctity. "Holy, holiness, sanctuary, most holy"
    • Distribution: Leviticus 84, Exodus 70, Numbers 58, Ezekiel 51
    • LXX: ἅγιος G40 (346x, ~77% — overwhelming dominance)
    • Semantic categories: Sacred quality, sacred space (sanctuary), most holy (qodesh qodashim), abstract holiness, holy things, consecrated status
    • Key for this study: Qodesh is one pole of the first axis (qodesh/chol). It is fundamentally a noun of separation — what is withdrawn from common use for God.

    chol (adjective/noun) — H2455¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2455-chol.md

    • Word: חֹל (chol), masculine noun
    • Occurrences: 7 unique verses across 3 books (Leviticus, 1 Samuel, Ezekiel)
    • Definition: Properly "exposed"; profane, common, unholy. The opposite of qodesh.
    • Translation: "profane" (43%), "common" (29%), "unholy" (14%)
    • LXX: βέβηλος G952 (5x, PMI 20.42 — primary)
    • Key verses: Lev 10:10 (foundational); Ezek 22:26, 44:23 (priestly failure/restoration)
    • Key insight: The holy/common axis is independent of the clean/unclean axis. Something can be common (chol) and clean, or common and unclean. The dangerous intersection is when something holy (qodesh) becomes unclean (tame).

    chalal (verb) — H2490¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2490-chalal.md

    • Word: חָלַל (chalal), verb
    • Occurrences: 132 unique verses across 27 books
    • Definition: To bore/pierce; to profane, defile, break; to begin
    • Stem distribution: Piel 66 (profane), Hiphil 54 (begin)
    • Distribution: Ezekiel 31 (24%), Leviticus 15
    • LXX: βεβηλόω G953 (61x — "profane" sense)
    • Semantic categories: (1) Profaning God's name, (2) Profaning holy things/sanctuary, (3) Profaning Sabbath, (4) Profaning persons/priesthood, (5) Breaking covenant
    • Key difference from tame: chalal = treating the sacred as common (violating the qodesh/chol boundary); tame = ritual impurity (the tahor/tame axis)

    qadash (verb) — H6942¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6942-qadash.md

    • Word: קָדַשׁ (qadash), verb
    • Occurrences: 153 unique verses across 23 books
    • Definition: To be/make/pronounce holy; consecrate, sanctify
    • Stem distribution: Piel 41, Hiphil 13, Qal 7, Hithpael 4, Niphal 3
    • LXX: ἁγιάζω G37 (119x — near 1:1)
    • First occurrence: Gen 2:3 — God sanctified the seventh day

    qadosh (adjective) — H6918¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6918-qadowsh.md

    • Word: קָדוֹשׁ (qadosh), adjective
    • Occurrences: 108 unique verses across 20 books
    • Definition: Sacred (ceremonially or morally); the Holy One, saint
    • Key use: "The Holy One of Israel" — Isaiah's signature phrase (34 of 108 occurrences in Isaiah)
    • Key verses: Lev 11:44 "ye shall be holy; for I am holy"; Lev 19:2; 20:26

    WG-holiness¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-holiness.md Members: qodesh H6944, qadash H6942, qadosh H6918, miqdash H4720, kohen H3548, har H2022, hagios G40, hagiazo G37, hagiasmos G38, hagiosyne G42, hagiotes G41. Key passages where multiple members appear: Lev 10:10, Ezek 22:26, Ezek 44:23, Exo 29:43-44, Lev 19:2.

    Translation Relationships (LXX Bridges)¶

    TR: tame → akathartos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tame-akathartos.md Chain: H2931 tame (78x OT) → G169 akathartos (69x LXX, PMI 31.22) → G169 akathartos (29x NT) Key shift: OT applies primarily to animals, diseased persons, dead bodies; NT applies primarily to unclean spirits (25 of 29 occurrences). Acts 10-11 explicitly transitions the word's application.

    TR: tame → miaino¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tame-miaino.md Chain: H2930 tame verb (142x OT) → G3392 miaino (86x LXX, PMI 31.76) → G3392 miaino (4x NT) Key shift: OT overwhelmingly bodily/ceremonial impurity; NT 3 of 4 uses are moral/conscience defilement (Titus 1:15, Heb 12:15, Jude 1:8). Only John 18:28 preserves ritual sense. Perfect tenses in Titus 1:15 suggest settled state, not temporary condition.

    TR: tumah → akatharsia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tumah-akatharsia.md Chain: H2932 tumah (31x OT) → G167 akatharsia (26x LXX, PMI 28.97) → G167 akatharsia (10x NT) Key shift: OT overwhelmingly ritual-cultic; NT overwhelmingly moral-ethical (sexual sin, greed, hypocrisy). Ezekiel 36:17,25 forms the transitional bridge. Matt 23:27 applies classic corpse-contact impurity imagery to moral hypocrisy. 1 Thess 4:7: "God hath not called us unto uncleanness, but unto holiness" — akatharsia opposes hagiasmos.

    TR: taher → katharizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-taher-katharizo.md Chain: H2891 taher (94x OT) → G2511 katharizo (61x LXX) → G2511 katharizo (30x NT) Key continuity: Blood remains primary instrument (Lev 16 → Heb 9); God remains ultimate agent (Ezek 36:25 → 1 John 1:9); priestly declaration pattern persists (Lev 13 → Jesus in Matt 8). Key shift: From external ritual to internal spiritual; from priest-mediated to direct (Jesus declares "be thou clean"); from repeated to once-for-all (Heb 10:2).

    TR: qodesh → hagios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-qodesh-hagios.md Chain: H6944 qodesh (452x OT) → G40 hagios (346x LXX) → G40 hagios (219x NT) Remarkably little shift. The command "be ye holy, for I am holy" transfers directly (Lev 19:2 → 1 Pet 1:16). The spatial holiness gradient preserved in Heb 9. "Saints" (hagioi) becomes standard term for believers.

    TR: chol → bebelos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-chol-bebelos.md Chain: H2455 chol (7x OT) → G952 bebelos (5x LXX, PMI 20.42) → G952 bebelos (5x NT) The Lev 10:10 framework persists into NT, with both bebelos and koinos occupying the "common" slot. Key NT passage: Heb 12:16 — Esau as paradigm bebelos person (treating the sacred as common). Parallel axis: NT develops koinos G2839 as a second, less formal word for "common" — used in Acts 10-11 Gentile inclusion debate.

    DOA/Sin Vocabulary¶

    kaphar (verb) — H3722¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md

    • Occurrences: 102 in OT
    • Stem: Piel 92 (90%), Pual 7
    • Translation: "make atonement" (72%), "purge" (8%), "reconcile" (7%)
    • Distribution: Leviticus 67 (66%), Numbers 19
    • Key verse for this study: Lev 16:16 (kaphar on the qodesh because of tumot, peshaim, chattat); Lev 16:30 (kaphar to taher)
    • Key pairing: kaphar + taher in Lev 16:30 — atonement produces cleansing. kaphar + salach in daily passages (Lev 4:26,31,35) — atonement produces forgiveness.

    pesha (noun) — H6588¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6588-pesha.md

    • Occurrences: 90 unique verses across 17 books
    • Definition: Revolt (national, moral, religious): rebellion, transgression
    • Translation: "transgression" (76%)
    • Key for DOA: Lev 16:16 lists peshaim as the second category the DOA addresses — willful rebellion, distinct from tumot (ritual impurities) and chattat (sins).
    • The root pasha (H6586) carries the sense of breaking away from just authority — active defection.

    chattaah (noun) — H2403¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2403-chattaah.md - Sin/sin offering. Third category in Lev 16:16. The daily sin offering transfers sin to the sanctuary via blood and priestly eating; the DOA addresses the accumulated chattat.

    salach (verb) — H5545¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5545-calach.md - "To forgive." Paired with kaphar in daily sin-offering passages (Lev 4:26,31,35: "the priest shall make atonement... and it shall be forgiven"). Absent from Lev 16 — the DOA uses taher, not salach. This vocabulary distinction confirms the daily/annual complementary system.

    Abomination Vocabulary¶

    shiqquts — H8251¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8251-shiqquwts.md - Abomination/detestable thing. Used for idolatrous objects and practices. Ezek 5:11 "thou hast defiled my sanctuary with all thy detestable things."

    sheqets — H8263¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8263-sheqets.md - Filth, abominable thing. Applied specifically to unclean animals (Lev 11:10-13,20,23,41,42).

    Additional Hebrew Vocabulary¶

    niddah (noun) — H5079¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5079-niddah.md - Impurity/menstrual separation. In Num 19:9,13,20,21, "water of niddah" (separation water) is the purification agent for corpse contamination. The term links bodily impurity with the purification system.

    chaneph (verb) — H2610¶

    Hebrew/Greek Parsing Results (Summary)¶

    Lev 10:10 — The Two-Axis Framework¶

    Structure: ule-havdil beyn haqqodesh uveyn hachol uveyn hattame uveyn hattahor - havdil = Hiphil infinitive construct of badal "to separate/distinguish" - Four terms: haqqodesh (the holy), hachol (the profane), hattame (the unclean), hattahor (the pure) - Two independent pairs joined by repeated beyn...uveyn construction - Confirms two axes, not one spectrum

    Lev 15:31 — Ambient Defilement Clause¶

    • wehizzartem (Hiphil perf 2mp of nazar "dedicate/separate") — separate the children of Israel
    • mittum'atam (from their tumah) — from their uncleanness
    • betam'am (Piel infinitive construct + 3mp of tame) — "when they defile" (causative: their defilement pollutes)
    • et-mishkani (my dwelling-place) — the object of defilement is God's dwelling
    • Critical finding: The Piel infinitive construct of tame shows causative action — the uncleanness actively defiles the tabernacle at a distance

    Lev 16:16 — The Three-Category Formula¶

    • vekhipper (Piel perfect 3ms of kaphar) — "and he shall atone"
    • al-haqqodesh (upon the holy place) — the object of atonement is the sanctuary itself
    • mittum'ot (from the tumot — plural of tumah) — first category: uncleannesses
    • umipish'eihem (and from their peshaim) — second category: rebellions/transgressions
    • lekhol-chatto'tam (for all their chattat) — third category: sins
    • Structure: Three min-prepositions (mi-/u-mi-/le-) introduce three distinct categories
    • Final clause: hashshokhen ittam betokh tum'otam — "that dwells with them in the midst of their uncleannesses"

    Lev 16:30 — The kaphar-taher Pairing¶

    • yekhapper (Piel imperfect 3ms of kaphar) — atonement
    • letaher (Piel infinitive construct of taher) — purpose: "to cleanse you"
    • mikol chatto'teikhem (from all your sins)
    • titharu (Qal imperfect 2mp of taher) — "you shall be clean"
    • Key: kaphar produces taher, not tsadaq. The DOA moves from tame to tahor, not directly to tsadaq.

    Num 19:13 — Ambient Defilement Penalty¶

    • tame (Piel perfect 3ms of tame) — "he has defiled" — causative: the unpurified person actively defiles
    • et-mishkan YHWH — "the dwelling-place of the LORD" — same object as Lev 15:31
    • venikhretah (Niphal perfect 3fs of karat) — "shall be cut off" — capital/covenantal penalty
    • mei niddah lo-zoraq alav — "water of separation was not sprinkled upon him"
    • Confirms ambient mechanism: The person need not physically enter the tabernacle. Failing to purify = defiling the sanctuary at a distance.

    Num 35:33 — Land Defilement (chaneph root)¶

    • tachaniphu (Hiphil imperfect 2mp of chaneph) — "do not pollute" — different root from tame
    • yachanif (Hiphil imperfect 3ms of chaneph) — "blood pollutes the land"
    • lo-yekhuppar (Pual imperfect 3ms of kaphar) — "cannot be atoned for" except by blood of the shedder
    • Confirms distinct vocabulary: chaneph (Hiphil) for land-pollution by bloodshed, tame for ritual/moral impurity

    Hag 2:12-13 — The Asymmetry Test¶

    • v.12: If holy flesh (besar-qodesh) touches food via garment — hayiqdash? (Qal impf 3ms: "shall it be holy?") — Answer: No
    • v.13: If one unclean by a dead body (teme-nephesh) touches food — hayitma? (Qal impf 3ms: "shall it be unclean?") — Answer: Yes (yitma)
    • Asymmetry confirmed: Holiness does not transfer by secondary contact; uncleanness does. The purity system is inherently defensive.

    Ezek 22:26 — Priestly Failure¶

    • yechalelu (Piel wayyiqtol 3mp of chalal) — "they profaned" (my holy things)
    • lo hivdilu (Hiphil perfect 3mp of badal) — "they did not distinguish" (between holy and profane)
    • lo hodi'u (Hiphil perfect 3mp of yada) — "they did not make known" (between unclean and clean)
    • va'echal (Niphal wayyiqtol 1cs of chalal) — "and I am profaned" among them
    • Two failures: (1) Not distinguishing qodesh from chol, (2) Not teaching tame from tahor

    Lev 18:24 — Moral Defilement (Hithpael)¶

    • tittamme'u (Hithpael imperfect 2mp of tame) — "do not defile yourselves" — reflexive: voluntary self-defilement
    • nitme'u (Niphal perfect 3p of tame) — "the nations were defiled" — passive: they were defiled by these practices
    • Confirms: Hithpael tame in moral/sexual context, not ritual

    Mark 7:15 — Greek Parsing¶

    • koinosai (Aorist Active Infinitive of koinoo G2840) — "to defile/make common"
    • koinounta (Present Active Participle of koinoo G2840) — "the things that defile"
    • Jesus uses koinoo, not miaino. He addresses the holy/common distinction (the qodesh/chol axis), not the clean/unclean distinction (the tahor/tame axis). The word choice is significant.

    Heb 9:13-14 — Blood Purification¶

    • v.13: kekoinomenous (Perfect Middle/Passive Participle of koinoo G2840) — "those who have been defiled" — the settled state of defilement
    • hagiazo (Present Active Indicative of G37) — "sanctifies" — the Levitical blood sanctifies toward fleshly purity (sarkos katharoteta)
    • v.14: kathariei (Future Active Indicative of katharizo G2511) — "will cleanse" — Christ's blood cleanses the conscience (syneidesis)
    • Key contrast: Levitical system → purity of flesh; Christ's blood → purity of conscience. The NT internalizes the defilement/cleansing dynamic.
