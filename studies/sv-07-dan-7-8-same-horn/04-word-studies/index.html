    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    Are the little horns of Daniel 7 and Daniel 8 the same entity? Vocabulary comparison across the two chapters.

    From Word Studies Library¶

    Horn Vocabulary¶

    qeren (H7161) — Hebrew Horn¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7161-qeren.md

    • 69 unique verses, 85 tokens across 21 books
    • 97% rendered "horn/horns" in KJV
    • Highest density in Daniel (2.2/100v)
    • Used in Dan 8 (Hebrew section): Dan 8:3,5,6,7,8,9,20,21
    • Semantic categories: physical horn, altar horn, oil flask, figurative power, prophetic/apocalyptic symbol, peak/ray
    • Prophetic usage: Zech 1:18-21 (four horns = scattering powers), Dan 8:3-9,20-21 (ram/goat/little horn)
    • LXX: κέρας (keras, G2768) — 60 co-occurrences, PMI 8.23

    qeren (A7162) — Aramaic Horn¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A7162-qeren.md

    • 10 unique verses, 21 tokens — Daniel only (chapters 3 and 7)
    • Used in Dan 7 (Aramaic section): Dan 7:7,8,11,20,21,24
    • Dan 7:8 alone has 4 tokens of qeren
    • Co-occurs with rabrab (H7260) at 40% — "great words" of the little horn's boasting
    • LXX: κέρας (keras, G2768) — same Greek rendering as H7161
    • Key distinction: Dan 7 uses A7162 (Aramaic), Dan 8 uses H7161 (Hebrew). Same word, different language register. The language shift from Aramaic to Hebrew between chapters 7 and 8 is the structural divide, not a difference in referent.

    Greatness / Magnification Vocabulary¶

    gadal (H1431) — "to be great, magnify"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1431-gadal.md

    • 111 unique verses, 153 tokens across 31 books
    • Daniel has 9 occurrences at 2.5/100v — striking concentration
    • Verb stem distribution: Qal 54, Hiphil 33, Piel 25, Hithpael 4
    • In Daniel 8: Dan 8:4 (ram "became great" — Hiphil), 8:8 (goat "waxed very great" — Hiphil), 8:9 (horn "waxed exceeding great" — Qal), 8:10 (horn "waxed great" — Qal), 8:11 (horn "magnified himself" — Hiphil), 8:25 (horn "magnify himself in his heart" — Hiphil)
    • In Daniel 11: 11:36-37 — Hithpael yithgaddel ("shall magnify himself")
    • Self-exaltation uses always condemned: Isa 10:15, Jer 48:26,42, Zeph 2:8,10
    • LXX: megaluno (G3170) — 56 co-occurrences

    Dan 8 gadal progression (from sv-06): 1. Ram "became great" (higdil, 8:4) 2. Goat "waxed very great" (higdil ad-meod, 8:8) 3. Horn "waxed exceeding great" (tigdal-yether, 8:9) — surpasses BOTH named empires

    yether (H3499) — "remainder, excess, exceedingly"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3499-yether.md

    • 95 unique verses across 26 books
    • 65% = "the rest of" (regnal archive formula)
    • 5% = "excellency / exceeding" — rare but early (Gen 49:3)
    • Dan 8:9 — "which waxed exceeding great" (tigdal-yether). The noun yether functions adverbially to intensify the verb gadal. This is the only prophetic use of yether as "exceedingly."
    • The yether requirement: the horn must grow BEYOND the greatness of both the ram (Medo-Persia) and the goat (Greece). Only Rome satisfies this — Antiochus Epiphanes was a minor Seleucid king who never exceeded either empire.

    mits'eirah (H4704) — "littleness" (HAPAX)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4704-mitstsiyrah.md

    • HAPAX LEGOMENON — appears only in Dan 8:9
    • Feminine of H4705 (mits'ar), compound: min + tsa'ir (small/young/least)
    • Marks "from smallness" — the starting point of something that expands dramatically
    • Contrast with tigdal-yether is the whole point: from utter insignificance to surpassing greatness
    • Compare Dan 7:8: ze'irah (A2191) = "small" (Aramaic adjective). Dan 7 uses a common Aramaic adjective. Dan 8 uses a Hebrew hapax noun emphasizing origin from smallness. Both describe the same initial condition but Dan 8 uses a rarer, more emphatic form.

    rabrab (H7260) — "very great" (Aramaic)¶

    Source: Fresh lookup (no library entry)

    • 8 occurrences, all in Daniel's Aramaic sections (Dan 2-7)
    • Reduplicative form of rab: rab + rab = "very great, huge"
    • 75% rendered "great," 12.5% "great things," 12.5% "very great things"
    • Dan 7:7 "great iron teeth" (rabr'van) — the fourth beast
    • Dan 7:8 "a mouth speaking great things" (pum memalil rabrevan)
    • Dan 7:11 "the voice of the great words which the horn spake" (rabrevan)
    • Dan 7:20 "a mouth that spake very great things" (rabrevan)
    • Dan 7:3 "four great beasts" — describes the beasts themselves
    • This is the Aramaic greatness vocabulary that parallels Hebrew gadal in Dan 8. The horn in Dan 7 SPEAKS great things (rabrab); the horn in Dan 8 DOES/BECOMES great things (gadal). Same concept, different language.
    • Co-occurs with A7162 qeren at 40% — directly linked to the horn imagery.

    Dan 8 Unique Targets (Absent from Dan 7)¶

    tamid (H8548) — "continual, daily"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md

    • 104 occurrences. Fundamentally a temple/worship term.
    • In Daniel: 5 occurrences (Dan 8:11, 8:13, 11:31, 12:11 — plus Dan 8:12 if counted)
    • Used substantively as "the tamid" (the continual service) — unique to Daniel's apocalyptic usage
    • 100% positive across all 104 OT occurrences — the tamid is always presented as something good that should continue
    • Key point: tamid is ENTIRELY ABSENT from Dan 7. The horn's attack on the continual service is a Dan 8 innovation. This is the core of what Dan 8 ADDS to Dan 7.
    • Top collocations: olah (burnt offering) 26x, panim (before the LORD) 26x, minchah (meal offering) 16x

    qodesh (H6944) — "holiness, sanctuary"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md

    • 452 occurrences. Heavily concentrated in Pentateuch (47%) and Ezekiel (51).
    • Daniel has 12 occurrences at 3.4/100v
    • Dan 8:13 — "to give both the sanctuary (qodesh) and the host to be trodden under foot"
    • Dan 8:14 — "then shall the sanctuary (qodesh) be cleansed (nitsdaq)"
    • Key point: qodesh is ABSENT from Dan 7. The sanctuary as a target of attack and the object of vindication is unique to Dan 8.
    • LXX: hagios (G40) — 346 co-occurrences (77%), one of the tightest Hebrew-Greek mappings

    emeth (H571) — "truth, faithfulness"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H571-emeth.md

    • 125 unique verses across 23 books
    • Daniel: 6 occurrences at 1.7/100v
    • Dan 8:12 — "it cast down the truth (emeth) to the ground" — truth as a target of the horn
    • Dan 8:26 — "the vision of the evening and the morning which was told is true (emeth)"
    • Dan 10:1, 10:21, 11:2 — in the prophetic certainty sense
    • Key point: emeth as a TARGET of horn attack is unique to Dan 8. Dan 7 does not mention truth being attacked. This is a progressive revelation addition.
    • Dual sense: truth (cognitive/propositional) + faithfulness (relational/covenantal)

    pasha (H6586) — "to rebel, transgress"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6586-pasha.md

    • 50 tokens in 37 unique verses across 14 books
    • Core idea: breach against rightful rule
    • Dan 8:12 — "by reason of transgression" (be-pesha) — the mechanism enabling the horn's attack
    • Dan 8:13 — "the transgression of desolation" (ha-pesha shomem) — the desolating transgression
    • Dan 8:23 — "when the transgressors are come to the full" (ke-hatem ha-posh'im)
    • Key point: pesha/pasha vocabulary is ABSENT from Dan 7. The concept of transgression as a mechanism/cause of the horn's power is unique to Dan 8.

    tsadaq (H6663) — "to be righteous, vindicate"¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md

    • 41 occurrences. Job dominates with 56%.
    • Verb stem distribution: Qal 22, Hiphil 12, Piel 5, Niphal 1, Hithpael 1
    • The sole Niphal: Dan 8:14 — ve-nitsdaq qodesh ("then shall the sanctuary be cleansed/vindicated")
    • This is the ONLY Niphal of tsadaq in the entire OT — a unique form for a unique event
    • Forensic/judicial vocabulary: collocates with mishpat (8x), shaphat (8x), tsaddiq (7x), rasha (6x)
    • Key point: tsadaq is ENTIRELY ABSENT from Dan 7. The verdict (nitsdaq) is unique to Dan 8. Dan 7 provides the judicial PROCESS (diyn, A1780 — thrones, books, court); Dan 8 provides the judicial VERDICT (nitsdaq). They are complementary.

    Dan 7 Unique Vocabulary (Absent from Dan 8)¶

    diyn (A1780) — "judgment" (Aramaic)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A1780-diyn.md

    • 5 unique verses: Dan 7:10, 7:22, 7:26 (+ Dan 4:37, Ezra 7:26)
    • All 5 rendered "judgment" in KJV
    • Dan 7:10 — "the judgment was set (diyna yethib), and the books were opened"
    • Dan 7:22 — "judgment was given to the saints of the most High"
    • Dan 7:26 — "the judgment shall sit, and they shall take away his dominion"
    • Three stages: judgment SET (7:10) -> judgment GIVEN to saints (7:22) -> judgment ACTS against dominion (7:26)
    • Key point: diyn is ABSENT from Dan 8. Dan 7 provides the full courtroom drama (thrones, Ancient of Days, books, river of fire, attendant hosts). Dan 8 provides the verdict (nitsdaq) without the courtroom scene.

    bla (A1080) — "wear out" (Aramaic HAPAX)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A1080-bla.md

    • HAPAX LEGOMENON — appears only in Dan 7:25
    • Pael stem (intensive active): yeballe = "he shall wear out"
    • Hebrew cognate H1086 balah = physical decay (garments, body). Aramaic narrows to mental/spiritual attrition.
    • "Shall wear out the saints" — prolonged, relentless harassment over time
    • Theodotion LXX: katatripsei (from katatriboo — to wear away by rubbing)
    • Key point: This specific verb for prolonged attrition is unique to Dan 7. Dan 8:24 describes destruction (yashchit, H7843 Hiphil) and Dan 8:10 describes casting down (tappel, H5307 Hiphil), but neither captures the sustained grinding-down of bla.

    shna (A8133) — "to change, alter" (Aramaic)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A8133-shna.md

    • 21 unique verses (26 tokens), all in Daniel's Aramaic sections + Ezra 6
    • Dan 7:25 Haphel: "think to change (le-hashnayah) times and laws"
    • Dan 2:21 Haphel: "he changeth (me-hashneh) the times and the seasons" — GOD as subject
    • The contrast: God actually changes times (2:21); the horn merely THINKS to change them (7:25 — yisbar, "intend/think")
    • Dan 7:3,7,19,23,24 — "diverse" (Peal) — the fourth beast is categorically different
    • Key point: The "change times and laws" formula is unique to Dan 7:25. Dan 8 does not use shna vocabulary. Instead, Dan 8 describes REMOVAL of the tamid and CASTING DOWN of the sanctuary — different vocabulary for related actions.

    iddan (A5732) — "time" (Aramaic)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/A5732-iddan.md

    • 15 occurrences, all in Aramaic Daniel (Dan 2-7)
    • Dan 7:25 — "a time (iddan) and times (iddanin) and half a time (pelag iddan)" = 3.5 years
    • This is the prophetic time formula. Dan 8:14 gives a different time: "2300 evening-morning"
    • Dan 7:25 parallels Dan 12:7 (Hebrew mo'ed replaces Aramaic iddan)
    • Key point: iddan time formula is unique to Dan 7. Dan 8 uses erev boqer (evening-morning) as its time unit — a different scale, a different framework.

    Word Groups¶

    WG-greatness — Greatness / Magnify¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-greatness.md

    • Members: gadal (H1431), gadol (H1419), gedullah (H1420), rab (H7227), rabah (H7235)

    • LXX bridge: gadal/gadol -> megas/megaluno; rab/rabah -> polys/plethyno

    WG-holiness — Holiness¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/WG-holiness.md

    • qodesh (H6944), qadash (H6942), qadowsh (H6918), miqdash (H4720), qaddish (H6922 Aramaic)
    • Dan 7 uses qaddish (Aramaic: "holy ones" = saints): Dan 7:18,21,22,25,27
    • Dan 8 uses qodesh (Hebrew: "sanctuary/holiness"): Dan 8:13,14
    • Dan 8 also uses qadowsh (Hebrew: "holy" people): Dan 8:24
    • The holiness vocabulary shifts from PERSONS (Dan 7 saints) to PLACE (Dan 8 sanctuary) — progressive revelation of the target
