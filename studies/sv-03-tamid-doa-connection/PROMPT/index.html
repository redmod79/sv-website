    Bible Study: The Daily-to-Annual Sanctuary System — Tamid and Day of Atonement¶

    Question¶

    What is the connection between the daily tamid service and the Day of Atonement in Leviticus? How does the daily service transfer sin INTO the sanctuary (Lev 4:5-7, 10:17, 15:31)? How does the DOA take sin OUT (Lev 16:14-22)? What is the vocabulary distinction — kaphar+salach (daily, forgiveness) vs kaphar+taher (annual, cleansing)? How does the nasa verb connect the daily priest (Lev 10:17) and the annual scapegoat (Lev 16:22)? Why does the tamid never stop, even on the DOA (Num 29:11)?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Series Context¶

    This is Study 3 of 23 in the "Daniel 8:14 and the Sanctuary Vindication" series. Study 1 (sv-01-biblical-righteousness) defined righteousness — the tsadaq word family across forensic, relational, and character dimensions. Study 2 (sv-02-biblical-defilement) defined defilement — the two-axis framework (holy/common × clean/unclean), seven defilement categories, and three transmission mechanisms. This study establishes the sanctuary mechanism that manages the transition from defilement to cleansing through two complementary services.

    Prior Study Findings¶

    Eight prior studies directly inform this research: - sv-01 established the tame → tahor → tsadaq progression. Dan 9:24 is the ONLY verse bridging kaphar and tsedeq. - sv-02 established three sin-transfer mechanisms (blood application, priestly eating, ambient defilement via Lev 15:31). The DOA three-category formula (mittum'ot + umipish'eihem + lekhol-chatto'tam) covers all categories. The vocabulary distinction (kaphar+salach vs kaphar+taher) was identified as absolute. - tamid-day-of-atonement-connection (prior study of same question): Confirmed the tamid as five integrated elements, three channels of sin transfer, the absolute vocabulary distinction, the singular-to-plural shift, the nasa verb connecting priest and scapegoat, and tamid continuation on the DOA (Num 29:11). - sanc-04-daily-service-tamid: tamid (H8548) has 105 OT occurrences in four semantic domains. The "al olat hattamid" formula appears in Num 28-29 for EVERY special occasion. Exo 29:42 makes tamid the meeting point. The Lamb connection: kebes (H3532) → LXX amnos (G286) → John 1:29. - sanc-06-sin-enters-sanctuary: Three mechanisms confirmed with Hebrew verbs — nazah/nathan/shaphakh triad for blood application; Lev 6:30 mutual exclusion; Lev 10:16-20 dual infinitive laset + lekapper. - sanc-09-day-of-atonement-ritual: kaphar appears 16× in Lev 16. Sequence: preparation → priestly self-atonement → congregational atonement → sin removal → concluding. Lev 16:17 total exclusion. kalah closure (v.20) marks structural boundary. Lev 16:30: kaphar → taher (Piel) → titharu (Qal). - atone-17: DOA integrates chattat, priestly sin-bearing, and scapegoat as sequential phases. Exo 34:7's three sin-words match Lev 16:21. salach absent from Lev 16 — theologically significant. - christ-sin-bearer-as-high-priest: nasa (H5375) has three registers — divine, priestly, removal. NT uses anaphero (G399) for Christ in the priestly register.

    External Corpus Claims (to verify biblically)¶

    1. EGW identifies TWO transfer mechanisms (blood sprinkled and flesh eaten, GC 418.1). Prior study found a THIRD (ambient defilement via Lev 15:31, Num 19:13). Verify whether Lev 16:16's tumot category requires this third mechanism.
    2. EGW frames scapegoat as representing Satan with "already atoned-for sins" (BR-ASI9 165.3). Verify from Lev 16:20-22 whether the scapegoat phase is structurally separate from blood atonement (kalah closure verb in v.20).
    3. Froom claims sins were "blotted out" through DOA ministry (PFF4 1231.6). Verify whether machah (blotting out) vocabulary appears in Lev 16 or is imported from other passages (Isa 43:25, Isa 44:22, Acts 3:19).
    4. Bohr claims only sins entering through blood were cleansed (HRC p.180, HWIS p.218). Contradicts Lev 16:16 beginning with mittum'ot (ambient defilement). Verify the three-category formula text.
    5. Bohr identifies only two transfer mechanisms (blood and flesh). Verify Lev 15:31 and Num 19:13,20 as a genuine third mechanism — ambient defilement that enters without blood.
    6. Haskell (CIS 125.3, 183.2) confirms both blood and flesh transfer, details Lev 6:30 mutual exclusion. Verify his claim that sins were "hid from view" in the sanctuary.

    1. The tamid as integrated system (not just one sacrifice): Tool discoveries confirm tamid (H8548) describes an entire system — two lambs, incense, lampstand, showbread, altar fire (sanc-04 findings). The "al olat hattamid" formula appears in Num 28-29 for EVERY special occasion, establishing the tamid as the foundation that never suspends.
