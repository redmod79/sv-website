    Bible Study: The Atonement — Ground, Application, and Declaration¶

    Question¶

    Is the atonement complete at the cross, or does it have ongoing phases? Three-phase model: ground (cross), application (intercession), declaration (judgment). tetelestai meaning. The "time gap" objection. DOA kill-then-apply structure as the answer.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    From Prior Studies (sv/atone/sanc series)¶

    Nine prior studies provide extensive groundwork:

    • sv-03 established the daily-annual vocabulary distinction: daily = kaphar+salach (13x Lev 4-5); annual = kaphar+taher (Lev 16:19,30). salach NEVER in Lev 16; taher NEVER in Lev 4-5. Three mechanisms of sin transfer into sanctuary (blood application, priestly eating, ambient defilement). nasa (H5375) connects both phases: priest bears sin IN (Lev 10:17); scapegoat bears sin OUT (Lev 16:22).
    • sv-04 established the DOA ritual sequence: goat killed in courtyard (Lev 16:15) → blood carried inside and applied to mercy seat (Lev 16:14-15). kalah (H3615) in Lev 16:20 marks the structural boundary. The two goats = ONE sin offering in two phases. The DOA's own internal structure separates KILLING from BLOOD APPLICATION by space and time.
    • sv-14 established Dan 9:24 as the ONLY verse where DOA vocabulary (kaphar, H3722) and vindication vocabulary (tsedeq, H6664) appear together. Six purposes in two triads. The kaphar→tsedeq progression bridges the three-phase model.
    • sv-17 established the judgment as antitypical DOA with three-phase accomplishment: Phase A = pre-advent (Dan 7:9-10); Phase B = millennial review (Rev 20:4); Phase C = post-millennial execution (Rev 20:11-15).
    • sv-18 defined vindication through the three-phase model. nitsdaq = Niphal of tsadaq (unique). Phase 1: cross provides ground. Phase 2: intercession applies benefits. Phase 3: judgment declares result.
    • sv-24 showed Hebrews teaches sitting = completed SACRIFICE, not ceased MINISTRY. Heb 8:1-2 combines sitting with ministering. Heb 7:25 says he "ever liveth to make intercession." Psalm 110:1+110:4 concurrent.
    • atone-11 established five dimensions of intercession (entygchano, emphanizo, parakletos, mesites, leitourgos). Isa 53:12 tense shift: nasa Perfect = completed bearing; yaphgia Imperfect = ongoing intercession. hapax/ephapax ALWAYS modify sacrifice, NEVER intercession.
    • atone-12 covered Hebrews 9:1-10:25 comprehensively. Heb 9:23 katharizesthai = DOA verb for heavenly counterpart. Heb 10:14 "hath perfected" (perfect tense) + "them that are sanctified" (present participle). Standing vs. sitting contrast.
    • sanc-23 confirmed Christ's heavenly ministry follows the three-phase sanctuary pattern: inauguration → ongoing intercession → DOA judgment.

    From it-is-finished study¶

    tetelestai (G5055) = perfect passive indicative of teleo. Root telos (G5056) = goal, designed endpoint. "Finished" means the SACRIFICE reached its designed endpoint, not that all ministry ceased. Dan 9:24 connection: kalah (H3615) whose LXX translation is teleo — the verb in John 19:30.

    External Corpus Leads¶

    1. EGW says "The Atonement is complete" at the cross (7ABC 459.6) — yet the broader framework distinguishes sacrificial atonement (complete at cross) from application of atoning blood (ongoing).
    2. EGW describes DOA sequence: goat killed → blood taken past the veil → sprinkled on mercy seat (GC 419.1).
    3. Pioneer tension: Uriah Smith says "Christ did not make the atonement when he shed his blood upon the cross" (LUJ 237.2) vs. EGW's "The Atonement is complete."
    4. Bohr explicitly distinguishes the "sacrificial aspect" of the DOA (cross) from the "application of the blood" (post-1844) (HRC Lesson 24, p.181).
    5. Bohr says tetelestai means "a perfect life and death available for the penitent" (HWIS p.50).
    6. The claim that blood application "begins at the end of the 2300 days" (Bohr) needs careful examination: does ongoing intercession (Heb 7:25) also constitute blood application throughout the Christian era?

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The DOA's kill-then-apply structure as the typological foundation: The DOA itself separates killing (Lev 16:15, goat slaughtered in courtyard) from blood application (Lev 16:14-15, blood carried inside past the veil and sprinkled on the mercy seat). kalah (H3615) in Lev 16:20 formally marks the completion of the blood-application phase before the scapegoat ceremony begins. This structural separation of killing from application IS the type that the antitype follows across centuries rather than minutes. The research agent should retrieve Lev 16:1-34 with full chapter context, READ the grammar reference entries for Lev 16:14-16, Lev 16:20-22, and Lev 16:30, and READ the word studies for H3722-kaphar, H3615-kalah, H5375-nasa. Run hebrew_parser.py on Lev 16:15 and Lev 16:20 to verify the verb sequence.

    2. tetelestai (John 19:30) — what is "finished"?: The perfect passive indicative of teleo (G5055) from telos (G5056). READ the library entries G5055-teleo.md and G5056-telos.md. The it-is-finished study established that tetelestai means the SACRIFICE reached its designed endpoint. The research agent should retrieve John 19:28-30 with full chapter context, run greek_parser.py on John 19:30, and trace the kalah→teleo LXX bridge (Gen 2:1-2 kalah Pual → LXX synteleo → John 19:30 teleo). READ the grammar reference for Gen 2:1-2 (gen-2-1-2.md) which documents this very bridge. Run cross-testament parallels on John 19:30 both directions.

    3. The kaphar→taher→tsedeq vocabulary progression: This progression traces the three phases: atonement (ground, kaphar) → cleansing (application, taher) → vindication (declaration, tsadaq). Lev 16:30 uses kaphar→taher. Dan 9:24 bridges kaphar→tsedeq. Dan 8:14 uses nitsdaq (Niphal of tsadaq). READ the library entries H3722-kaphar.md, H2891-taher.md, H6663-tsadaq.md, H6664-tsedeq.md, and the translation relationships TR-taher-katharizo.md and TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md. READ the grammar references for Dan 8:14, Dan 9:24-27, and Lev 16:30. The research agent should retrieve Dan 9:24, Dan 8:14, Lev 16:30, Rom 3:25-26, and Isa 53:11 with context and run hebrew_parser.py on Dan 9:24 and Isa 53:11.

    4. The sitting-intercession concurrent model (Hebrews): sv-24 established that sitting = completed sacrifice, not ceased ministry. Heb 8:1-2 explicitly combines sitting (kathizo, G2523) with ministering (leitourgos, G3011). Heb 7:25 says "ever liveth to make intercession" (entygchano, G1793). Heb 10:11-14 contrasts standing (daily priests, never finished) with sitting (Christ, sacrifice finished) but adds "expecting" (v.13). READ the library entries G2523-kathizo.md, G2521-kathemai.md, G1793-entynchano.md, G3011-leitourgos.md. The research agent should retrieve Heb 7:24-28, Heb 8:1-6, Heb 10:11-14, Psa 110:1-4 with full chapter context. Run greek_parser.py on Heb 7:25, Heb 8:1-2, Heb 10:12-13. Run cross-testament parallels on Heb 8:1 both directions.

    5. "Once for all" adverbs — what they modify: hapax (G530) and ephapax (G2178) appear in Heb 7:27, 9:12, 9:26, 9:28, 10:10. atone-11 and atone-12 established that these adverbs ALWAYS modify the sacrifice/offering and NEVER modify the intercession or ministry. The research agent should run search_strongs.py --verses on G530 and G2178 (if no library entry exists, fresh lookups are needed) to verify every occurrence and confirm which verb each adverb modifies. Run greek_parser.py on Heb 9:26-28 and Heb 10:10.

    6. Heb 9:23 — the heavenly things need purification: katharizesthai (from katharizo = taher) is used for the heavenly counterpart. This verse is the single strongest NT evidence that a DOA-type event applies to the heavenly sanctuary AFTER the cross. READ G2511-katharizo.md and G2512-katharismos.md and TR-taher-katharizo.md. The research agent should retrieve Heb 9:18-28 with full chapter context, run greek_parser.py on Heb 9:23, and run cross-testament parallels on Heb 9:23 both directions.

    7. Heb 10:14 — the perfect/present tense combination: "Hath perfected" (perfect tense — completed action with ongoing results) + "them that are sanctified" (present participle — ongoing process). This single verse encodes the two-phase structure grammatically: sacrifice complete, sanctification progressive. The research agent should run greek_parser.py on Heb 10:14 and READ G37-hagiazo.md and G38-hagiasmos.md. Retrieve Heb 10:10-18 with context.

    8. Isaiah 53:12 tense shift as prophetic encoding: nasa (H5375, Perfect — completed bearing) + yaphgia (H6293, Imperfect — ongoing intercession) encodes the two-phase structure at the prophetic level. The bearing is done; the intercession continues. The research agent should run hebrew_parser.py on Isa 53:12 to verify the tense shift. READ H5375-nasa.md. Retrieve Isa 53:10-12 with context. Run cross-testament parallels on Isa 53:12 both directions.

    9. Rom 3:25-26 — hilasterion within the dikaios framework: Christ is set forth as hilasterion (G2435, mercy seat/propitiation), and the purpose is "to declare his righteousness" (eis endeixin tes dikaiosynes autou). This combines DOA vocabulary (hilasterion) with vindication vocabulary (dikaiosyne), echoing the Dan 9:24 kaphar→tsedeq bridge in Greek. READ G2435-hilasterion.md and G1344-dikaioo.md. The research agent should retrieve Rom 3:21-26 with context, run greek_parser.py on Rom 3:25-26, and run cross-testament parallels on Rom 3:25 both directions.

    10. The blood that "speaks" and "cleanses" in present tense: Heb 12:24 — blood "speaks" (present participle); 1 Jn 1:7 — blood "cleanses" (present active indicative). The sacrifice was offered once; its effects continue indefinitely. This ongoing activity of the blood is the application phase. The research agent should retrieve Heb 12:22-24 and 1 Jn 1:5-2:2 with context. Run greek_parser.py on Heb 12:24 and 1 Jn 1:7.

    External Corpus Leads (from 00-references.md)¶

    1. EGW: "The Atonement is complete" at the cross (7ABC 459.6)

    2. Verify: Does the DOA type itself distinguish a "complete" phase from a subsequent phase? Retrieve Lev 16:15 (killing = complete sacrifice) vs. Lev 16:14-15 (application begins after killing). The three-phase model resolves this: the sacrificial ground IS complete; the application is a distinct subsequent phase using the completed sacrifice's blood.

    3. EGW: DOA kill-then-apply sequence (GC 419.1)

    4. Verify: Confirm from Lev 16:15 that the goat is killed in the courtyard, then the blood is carried inside. Run hebrew_parser.py on Lev 16:15. Trace the antitype: cross = killing (courtyard); heavenly ministry = blood application (inside the sanctuary, Heb 9:12, 9:24).

    5. Pioneer tension: Smith vs. EGW on "atonement"

    6. Verify: Does the biblical vocabulary support using "atonement" (kaphar) for both the sacrifice and the blood application? Lev 16:6 and 16:15-16 use kaphar for the blood-application phase, not the killing. If kaphar technically refers to what the blood DOES (covering), then Smith's point has lexical support — the kaphar action happens when blood meets mercy seat, not when the goat dies. But the killing is the necessary prerequisite. Both are parts of one process.

    7. Bohr: sacrificial aspect of DOA at cross vs. blood application post-1844 (HRC L24 p.181)

    8. Verify: Is the application of Christ's blood limited to 1844+? Heb 7:25 describes ongoing intercession throughout the Christian era. 1 Jn 1:7 says blood "cleanses" (present tense) NOW. The daily service also applies blood (Lev 4:5-7). The research agent must distinguish daily-type blood application (ongoing intercession) from DOA-type blood application (judgment phase). READ grammar reference lev-4-5-7.md for the daily blood path.

    9. Bohr: tetelestai = "a perfect life and death available for the penitent" (HWIS p.50)

    10. Verify: Does the teleo/telos word family support the "available for" reading? READ G5055-teleo.md and G5056-telos.md. Check if tetelestai ever carries a "made available" sense or strictly "reached completion."

    11. The claim that blood application "begins at the end of the 2300 days"

    12. Verify: The daily service applies blood throughout the year (Lev 4:5-7). The DOA applies blood on one specific day (Lev 16:14-15). Christ's intercession applies sacrifice benefits throughout the Christian era (Heb 7:25). What begins at the end of the 2300 days is specifically the DOA-type application (judgment/vindication), not blood application generically. The research agent must distinguish these two types of application.
