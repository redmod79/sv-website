    Bible Study: Leviticus 4 and the Blood-Transfer Question¶

    Question¶

    Does Leviticus 4 teach that individual sin offerings transferred sin into the sanctuary via blood? The text distinguishes four categories: (1) anointed priest (4:3-12) — blood sprinkled before the veil and on the incense altar, (2) whole congregation (4:13-21) — same, (3) ruler (4:22-26) — blood on burnt-offering altar horns only, (4) common person (4:27-35) — burnt-offering altar horns only. For categories 3 and 4, blood never enters the sanctuary building. How does sin reach the sanctuary? Three mechanisms: (a) priestly eating (Lev 6:26, Lev 10:17 — nasa = bearing iniquity), (b) ambient defilement (Lev 15:31, Num 19:13,20 — Piel of tame = causative defilement at distance), (c) DOA scope (Lev 16:16 tumot covers defilement from non-sacrificial channels). Does the system require blood-into-the-building for every sin?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Five prior studies provide extensive foundation:

    • sv-02 established the two-axis framework (Lev 10:10: qodesh/chol and tahor/tame independent), seven defilement categories, three transmission mechanisms (blood, eating, ambient), Haggai 2:12-13 asymmetry, and the tame→tahor→tsadaq progression.
    • sv-03 established the absolute vocabulary distinction (kaphar+salach daily vs kaphar+taher annual), the nasa connection (priest bears IN via Lev 10:17, scapegoat bears OUT via Lev 16:22), and the singular/plural shift (chattato→chatotam). Documented that EGW and Bohr identify only two transfer mechanisms while the Bible describes three.
    • sv-04 mapped the complete DOA ritual sequence and chiastic structure, the three-term formula (tumot, pesha, chattat in Lev 16:16), and the DOA as sorting event (Lev 23:29).
    • sanc-06 documented the blood-destination differential (Path 1 vs Path 2), mutual exclusion rule (Lev 6:30), priestly eating as nasa (Lev 10:17 dual infinitive), ambient defilement (Lev 15:31), and graduated contamination based on offender's proximity to God.
    • atone-01 analyzed the three blood verbs (nazah, nathan, shaphakh), graduated sanctuary penetration, and the finding that graduated procedure does NOT produce graduated forgiveness.

    External corpus leads: EGW (GC 418.1) and Bohr (HWIS p.218) both identify exactly two transfer mechanisms (blood sprinkled, priest eating). Neither mentions ambient defilement as a third channel. EGW describes blood being "carried by the priest into the holy place" as a general description that needs verification against the four-category system.

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The four-category blood-destination system in Leviticus 4: WHAT — Map exactly where blood goes for each of the four offerer categories. WHY — Tool discoveries confirm only categories 1-2 send blood inside the building; categories 3-4 blood stays at the courtyard altar. This is the textual fact the study must establish first. HOW — Retrieve full text of Lev 4:1-35 with chapter context. Run hebrew_parser.py on Lev 4:6-7 (Path 1 blood verbs) and Lev 4:25 and 4:30 (Path 2 blood verbs). READ grammar reference passages/lev-4-5-7.md for parsed verb chain.

    2. The mutual exclusion rule and priestly eating as sin-bearing: WHAT — Establish that when blood stays outside (ruler/commoner), the priest MUST eat the flesh, and this eating IS sin-bearing. WHY — Lev 6:30 and Lev 10:17-18 together prove that eating is the alternative transfer mechanism when blood does not enter the building. Moses' anger proves this is not optional. HOW — Retrieve Lev 6:24-30 and Lev 10:16-20 with full context. READ grammar reference passages/lev-10-16-20.md for the dual infinitive chain (laset + lekapper). READ word study H5375-nasa.md for priestly bearing register. Run hebrew_parser.py on Lev 10:17.

    3. Ambient defilement as the third mechanism: WHAT — Establish that the sanctuary absorbs community uncleanness without blood and without priestly eating. WHY — Lev 15:31 and Num 19:13,20 use the Piel of tame (causative) to describe the tabernacle being defiled by people who never enter it and never bring a sacrifice. This fills the tumot category in Lev 16:16 that neither blood nor eating can account for. HOW — Retrieve Lev 15:31 with full chapter context. Retrieve Num 19:11-22. Run hebrew_parser.py on Lev 15:31 (confirm Piel infinitive construct of tame). READ word study H2930-tame.md for Piel stem distribution.

    4. The DOA three-category formula and its scope: WHAT — Show that Lev 16:16 addresses tumot (including ambient defilement) + pesha (willful rebellion with no daily remedy) + chattat (sins generally). WHY — If the DOA addresses tumot that entered without blood, then the system does NOT require blood-into-the-building for every sin to be resolved. HOW — Retrieve Lev 16:14-20 with full chapter context. READ grammar reference passages/lev-16-14-16.md for the three prepositional sin-terms. READ word studies H2932-tumah.md, H6588-pesha.md, H2403-chattaah.md.

    5. The vocabulary distinction — kaphar+salach vs kaphar+taher: WHAT — Verify the absolute partition: salach never in Lev 16; taher never in Lev 4-5. WHY — This proves daily and annual address different things. The daily forgives the sinner. The annual cleanses the sanctuary. Both use kaphar but produce different results. HOW — READ word studies H3722-kaphar.md, H5545-calach.md, H2891-taher.md. READ grammar reference passages/lev-16-30.md for three-verb chain.

    6. The graduated contamination principle: WHAT — Show that all four categories receive identical forgiveness (same kaphar+nislach formula) despite different blood procedures. WHY — This proves the graduation is about depth of sanctuary contamination, not degree of forgiveness. The priest and congregation contaminate deeper because of their covenantal proximity to God. HOW — Compare Lev 4:20, 4:26, 4:31, 4:35 — identical result formulas. READ word study H5545-calach.md for Niphal divine passive.

    7. Shegagah scope limitation and pesha: WHAT — Establish that the daily chattat addresses only inadvertent sin (shegagah, from shagah H7686). Willful rebellion (pesha) has no daily remedy. WHY — The DOA's inclusion of pesha (Lev 16:16) proves it addresses what the daily cannot. HOW — Retrieve Lev 4:2,13,22,27 (shegagah openings). Retrieve Num 15:30-31 ("high hand" sin has no sacrifice). Fresh lookup on H7686 shagah (no library entry).

    8. Hebrews' reading of the chattat system: WHAT — Show how Hebrews identifies Christ's sacrifice with the highest-grade Path 1 chattat and extends the purification principle to heavenly things. WHY — Heb 13:11-12 specifically connects Christ to the Path 1 chattat (bodies burned outside camp). Heb 9:23 uses katharizesthai (LXX taher equivalent) for heavenly purification. HOW — Retrieve Heb 9:7-28 and Heb 13:11-12 with chapter context. Run cross-testament parallels on Lev 4:6 and Lev 10:17.

    External Corpus Leads (from 00-references.md)¶
