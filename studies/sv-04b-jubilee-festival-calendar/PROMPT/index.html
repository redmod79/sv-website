    PROMPT.md — sv-04b: The Jubilee, Isaiah 61, and the Festival Calendar¶

    Question¶

    What happens after the Day of Atonement in the festival calendar? The Jubilee is proclaimed ON the DOA (Lev 25:9-10) — debts cancelled, slaves freed, land returned. The Feast of Tabernacles follows five days later (Tishri 15-22) with commanded rejoicing (Lev 23:40). How does the Tishri sequence (Trumpets → DOA → Jubilee → Tabernacles) map the full arc from alarm to judgment to restoration to celebration? How does Isaiah 61 bridge Jubilee vocabulary (deror, H1865) to vindication vocabulary (tsedeq/tsedaqah, the tsadaq root of nitsdaq in Dan 8:14)? Trace the vocabulary chain: deror (v.1) → ratson + naqam (v.2, matching the DOA binary of Lev 16:30/23:29) → tsedeq (v.3) → mishpat (v.8) → tsedaqah garment (v.10) → tsedaqah before nations (v.11). How does Jer 34:15 (deror proclamation = yashar in God's sight) confirm this connection?

    Series Context¶

    This is Study 4b in the Daniel 8:14 and Sanctuary Vindication series (24 studies). Studies 1-4 established biblical righteousness vocabulary (sv-01), defilement vocabulary (sv-02), the daily-to-annual system (sv-03), and the DOA ritual sequence (sv-04). This study examines what the DOA DECLARES and what FOLLOWS it in the festival calendar. It also traces the Isaiah 61 vocabulary bridge from Jubilee language (deror) to vindication language (tsadaq family), providing a second bridge text alongside Dan 9:24 (which sv-14 will examine later).

    Prior Research Summary¶

    From sv-03 (Daily-to-Annual System)¶

    • The daily and annual services are two phases of one integrated system: daily puts sin IN, annual takes sin OUT.
    • Vocabulary distinction is absolute: kaphar+salach (daily, forgiveness) vs. kaphar+taher (annual, cleansing). salach never appears in Lev 16; taher never appears in Lev 4-5.
    • The nasa verb connects both phases: priest bears sin IN (Lev 10:17); scapegoat bears sin OUT (Lev 16:22).

    From sv-04 (DOA Ritual Sequence)¶

    • The DOA follows a chiastic structure centered on the Most Holy Place.
    • The three-verb chain in Lev 16:30: kaphar (act) → taher Piel (process) → taher Qal (state).
    • "Afflict your souls" (Lev 23:29) makes the DOA a sorting event: cleansed OR cut off (karath Niphal). No other festival carries this penalty.
    • The divine-knowledge function is built into the DOA: only God can verify inner compliance.

    From Prior Library Studies¶

    • sanc-15 (Jubilee): The Jubilee's three components: personal liberty (deror), return to possession (shuv to land), return to family. The deror→aphesis LXX mapping creates a vocabulary bridge to NT forgiveness. Weakness: did not trace the Isa 61 vocabulary chain verse by verse.
    • sanc-16 (Tabernacles): The sukkah→skene LXX bridge. The emotional trajectory: DOA commands "afflict your souls" (Lev 23:27); five days later Tabernacles commands "rejoice before the LORD" (Lev 23:40). Isa 4:4-6 follows the DOA-to-Tabernacles sequence.
    • sanc-12 (Seven Feasts): The seven feasts form a unified prophetic calendar. Tabernacles is the ONLY feast projected into the eschatological age (Zec 14:16).
    • sanc-14 (Trumpets): zikron teruah ("memorial of blowing"). teruah spans three semantic fields: war cry/alarm, acclamation of joy, liturgical trumpet blast.
    • sanc-19 (Vindication/Courtroom): Dan 8:14's nitsdaq uses courtroom language (tsadaq), not ritual language (taher/kaphar). The sanctuary needs a verdict. Zechariah 3:1-4 presents courtroom garment change as vindication.
    • rev-22 (New Jerusalem/Tabernacles): Rev 21:3's double sken- root. The DOA-to-Tabernacles calendar sequence is preserved in Rev 20-21.

    Key Gap This Study Must Fill¶

    No prior study has traced the Isaiah 61 vocabulary chain as a unified progression from Jubilee language (deror) to vindication language (tsadaq family). No prior study has connected Jer 34:15 (deror = yashar) to the righteousness vocabulary. No prior study has presented the Tishri sequence as a single mapped arc.

    Discovered Scope¶

    Topics Found (from Nave's Topical Dictionary)¶

    1. JUBILEE — Called "the acceptable year of the LORD" (Isa 61:2), "the year of liberty" (Ezk 46:17). Laws: Lev 25:8-55; 27:17-24; Num 36:4.
    2. LIBERTY — Proclaimed in the year of Jubilee (Lev 25:10; Jer 34:8,15-17). Figurative: Isa 61:2; 63:4; Luk 4:19; Jhn 8:32,33,36.
    3. TABERNACLES, FEAST OF — Instituted Exo 23:16; 34:22; Lev 23:34-43. Design: Lev 23:42,43. Penalty for not observing: Zec 14:16-19.
    4. TRUMPETS (FEAST OF) — When and how observed: Lev 23:24,25; Num 29:1-6. Celebrated after captivity: Neh 8:2,9-12.
    5. ATONEMENT — For tabernacle and furniture: Lev 16:15-20,33. Day of: Lev 16:2-34; 16:29-31; 16:30,34; 23:27; Num 29:7-11.
    6. RIGHTEOUSNESS — By faith (Gen 15:6; Rom 4). Garment of (Job 29:14; Mat 22:11-14). Fruits of: extensive list.
    7. VENGEANCE — Belongs to God (Psa 94:1; Rom 12:19).
    8. ROBE — Of righteousness: 2 Chr 6:41; Isa 61:10; Rev 6:11; 7:9,13.
    9. GARMENT — Of righteousness: Isa 61:10; Mat 22:11; 2 Cor 5:3; Rev 3:18; 7:14; 16:15; 19:8.
    10. RESTORATION — Of all things: Acts 3:21; Rev 21:1-5.
    11. SABBATIC YEAR — Called "year of release" (Deu 15:9; 31:10). Related to Jubilee cycle.

    Verse References Discovered (Tool-Verified)¶

    Leviticus Festival Calendar: - Lev 23:24-25 (Feast of Trumpets — Tishri 1) - Lev 23:27-32 (Day of Atonement — Tishri 10) - Lev 23:29 (karath penalty for non-compliance) - Lev 23:34-43 (Feast of Tabernacles — Tishri 15-22) - Lev 23:39-40 (commanded rejoicing at Tabernacles) - Lev 23:42-43 (design/purpose of sukkot) - Lev 25:8-10 (Jubilee institution — trumpet on DOA) - Lev 25:23 ("the land is mine") - Lev 25:9 (showphar teruah on Tishri 10) - Lev 16:30 (kaphar→taher three-verb formula) - Lev 16:29-31 (DOA statute)

    Isaiah 61 Vocabulary Chain: - Isa 61:1 (deror — liberty to captives) - Isa 61:2 (ratson — acceptable year + naqam — day of vengeance) - Isa 61:3 (elei ha-tsedeq — oaks of righteousness) - Isa 61:8 (mishpat — I love judgment) - Isa 61:10 (meil tsedaqah — robe of righteousness) - Isa 61:11 (tsedaqah before all nations)

    Jeremiah 34 — Anti-Jubilee: - Jer 34:8 (deror proclamation in Jerusalem) - Jer 34:15 (deror = yashar "right in my sight") - Jer 34:17 (anti-Jubilee: "I proclaim a deror to the sword")

    Zechariah 3 — Post-Judgment Garment Change: - Zech 3:1-5 (full courtroom scene) - Zech 3:4 (machalatsot — festival garments; hiphil of avar — "caused iniquity to pass") - Zech 3:5 (tsaniyph tahor — pure turban)

    Trumpets / Alarm: - Num 10:1-10 (trumpet usage prescribed) - Num 29:1-6 (Trumpets offering requirements) - Neh 8:2,9-12 (post-captivity Trumpets celebration) - Psa 81:3-4 (blow trumpet at new moon / solemn feast)

    Tabernacles / Eschatological: - Exo 23:16; 34:22 (Feast of Ingathering) - Deu 16:11-14 (commanded rejoicing) - Deu 31:10-12 (law read at Tabernacles every seventh year) - Neh 8:14-18 (post-captivity Tabernacles) - Zec 14:16-19 (nations keep Tabernacles after eschatological battle) - Rev 7:9-17 (palm branches + Lamb shelters — Tabernacles imagery) - Rev 21:3 (skene of God with men — double sken- root)

    Isaiah 4 — DOA-to-Tabernacles Parallel: - Isa 4:4-6 (purification by spirit of judgment, THEN God creates sukkah)

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The Tishri Festival Calendar as a Unified Arc¶

    WHAT: Document the complete Tishri sequence from Leviticus 23 and 25: Trumpets (Tishri 1, Lev 23:24-25) → Day of Atonement (Tishri 10, Lev 23:27-32) → Jubilee proclaimed ON the DOA (Lev 25:9-10) → Tabernacles (Tishri 15-22, Lev 23:34-43). WHY: Prior studies treat these feasts individually. This study must present them as one continuous narrative arc: alarm → judgment → restoration → celebration. The five-day gap between DOA and Tabernacles is structurally significant. HOW: Parse the Leviticus 23 calendar entries verse by verse. Use the Hebrew parser on key verses. Cross-reference Num 29:1-6 (Trumpets), Num 29:7-11 (DOA), Lev 25:8-10 (Jubilee). Demonstrate from the text that the calendar encodes a progression.

    2. The Jubilee-on-DOA Connection (Lev 25:9-10)¶

    WHAT: Establish from the text that the Jubilee trumpet sounds specifically on the Day of Atonement. Verify the vocabulary: showphar teruah (Lev 25:9) + deror (Lev 25:10). WHY: The placement of the Jubilee proclamation on the DOA is the hinge of this study. Liberty follows cleansing. The DOA resolves the accumulated defilement (sv-03, sv-04), and only THEN can liberty be proclaimed. HOW: Parse Lev 25:9-10 with the Hebrew parser. Cross-reference the DOA date (Tishri 10) in both Lev 23:27 and Lev 25:9. Verify that deror in Lev 25:10 is the same word as deror in Isa 61:1 (H1865 in both). Compare with Jer 34:8-17 where deror is used in a non-Jubilee context.

    3. The Isaiah 61 Vocabulary Chain (deror → tsedeq → tsedaqah)¶

    WHAT: Trace the vocabulary progression verse by verse through Isaiah 61: deror (v.1) → ratson + naqam (v.2) → tsedeq (v.3) → mishpat (v.8) → tsedaqah garment (v.10) → tsedaqah before nations (v.11). WHY: This is the primary thesis of the study — Isaiah 61 compresses Jubilee vocabulary into vindication vocabulary within a single chapter. The chain demonstrates that deror (Jubilee liberty) and tsedaqah (righteousness/vindication) are not separate concepts but points on a single continuum. The tsadaq root connects directly to nitsdaq in Dan 8:14. HOW: Read each verse in Hebrew using the parser. Verify the Strong's number for each key word. Map the progression: liberation language (v.1) → divine favor/judgment binary (v.2) → righteousness as outcome (v.3) → justice as covenant basis (v.8) → righteousness as garment/identity (v.10) → righteousness as public vindication (v.11). Read the existing word studies for H6663, H6664, H6666, H4941.

    4. The Ratson + Naqam Binary (Isa 61:2) and the DOA Binary (Lev 16:30/23:29)¶

    WHAT: Demonstrate that Isa 61:2's "acceptable year" (ratson) + "day of vengeance" (naqam) maps to the DOA's binary outcome: cleansed (Lev 16:30) or cut off (Lev 23:29). WHY: The DOA is the only festival with a karath penalty for non-participation (sv-04 finding). Isa 61:2 encodes the same binary: favor for those who participate, vengeance for those who refuse. This demonstrates that Isa 61 is using DOA-shaped language. HOW: Parse Isa 61:2 with the Hebrew parser. Read the existing word study for H7522 (ratsown) and verify its usage in sacrificial acceptance contexts (Lev 1:3). Compare with the sv-04 finding about the DOA's sorting function. Look up the naqam word family (H5358/H5359).

    5. The Jer 34:15 Connection (deror = yashar)¶

    WHAT: Examine Jer 34:8-22, focusing on v.15 where deror proclamation is called "yashar in my sight" (right/upright before God). Verify whether yashar and tsadaq overlap semantically. WHY: If deror (Jubilee liberty) is yashar (righteous/right), and the tsadaq family is the vindication vocabulary of Dan 8:14, then Jer 34:15 provides an explicit textual bridge between liberty and righteousness. This is independent of Isaiah 61 and confirms the same connection from a different author. HOW: Parse Jer 34:15 with the Hebrew parser. Read the existing word study for H3477 (yashar) and the TR-yashar-euthys study. Compare yashar's semantic field with tsadaq's. Examine the full Jer 34:8-22 context, especially v.17 where the anti-Jubilee (deror to the sword) reverses the liberty.

    6. The Zech 3:4 / Isa 61:10 Garment Parallel¶

    WHAT: Compare the post-judgment garment change in Zech 3:4 (machalatsot — festival garments) with the tsedaqah garment in Isa 61:10 (meil tsedaqah — robe of righteousness). Check whether both texts describe a post-judgment conferral of righteous standing. WHY: If the garment change in both texts follows judgment (Zech 3: courtroom scene; Isa 61: DOA-shaped deror/naqam language), the parallel strengthens the case that vindication includes investiture — not just a verdict but a visible transformation. HOW: Read the existing grammar entry for Zech 3:4-5. Read the existing word study for H4254 (machalatsah). Parse Isa 61:10 with the Hebrew parser. Verify whether the garment vocabulary overlaps or differs. Note: machalatsot in Zech 3:4 vs. meil tsedaqah in Isa 61:10 — different garment terms, but both in post-judgment contexts.

    7. The Trumpets-to-Tabernacles Emotional Trajectory¶

    WHAT: Document the emotional/experiential progression mandated by the calendar: alarm/warning (Trumpets, teruah) → affliction/self-examination (DOA, anah nephesh) → liberty/restoration (Jubilee, deror) → rejoicing/celebration (Tabernacles, samach). WHY: The Tishri sequence is not merely a ritual calendar but an emotional and theological narrative. Each stage has commanded emotional content: you MUST blow the alarm, you MUST afflict your souls, you MUST rejoice. This progression from fear to joy through judgment to restoration is the arc that Dan 8:14 (nitsdaq) encodes. HOW: Use the existing word studies for H8643 (truwah), H6031 (anah), H5315 (nephesh), H8055 (samach). Parse Lev 23:24 (teruah), Lev 23:29 (anah nephesh), Lev 23:40 (samach) to verify the commanded emotional content. Cross-reference Isa 4:4-6 (purification then sukkah).

    8. The Eschatological Tabernacles Projection¶

    WHAT: Trace the eschatological fulfillment of the DOA-to-Tabernacles sequence: Zec 14:16-19 (nations keep Tabernacles after eschatological battle), Rev 7:9-17 (palm branches + Lamb shelters), Rev 21:3 (skene of God with men). WHY: Tabernacles is the only feast projected into the eschatological age. HOW: Read the existing word studies for G4633 (skene), G4637 (skenoo), H5521 (cukkah). Parse Rev 21:3 noting the double sken- root. Reference the sanc-16 and rev-22 studies for prior vocabulary work.     9. Luke 4:18-21 — Jesus' Jubilee Proclamation¶

    WHAT: Examine Jesus' reading of Isa 61:1-2 in the Nazareth synagogue (Luke 4:18-21), noting that he stops mid-verse — reading "the acceptable year of the Lord" (ratson) but NOT "the day of vengeance" (naqam). WHY: This mid-verse stop distinguishes first-advent Jubilee (proclamation of liberty) from second-advent Jubilee (completion of judgment). It separates the ratson and naqam elements of Isa 61:2, confirming that the DOA binary has a temporal dimension — favor now, vindication later. HOW: Compare Luke 4:18-19 with Isa 61:1-2 word by word. Verify the stopping point from the Greek text. Cross-reference with the LXX of Isa 61:2. Note the aphesis (G859) usage as the LXX bridge from deror.

    10. The Hiphil of Avar Bridge (Lev 25:9 and Zech 3:4)¶

    WHAT: Verify that both Lev 25:9 ("cause the trumpet to pass through the land") and Zech 3:4 ("I have caused your iniquity to pass from you") use the Hiphil of avar (H5674). WHY: If the same verb form connects the Jubilee announcement to the cleansing declaration, it provides additional grammatical evidence that liberty and cleansing are one act, not two separate events. HOW: Parse both verses using the Hebrew parser. Read the grammar entry for Zech 3:4-5 (which documents the Hiphil of avar). Verify the form in Lev 25:9.
