    Word Studies¶

    From Word Studies Library¶

    All entries below are sourced from canonical word studies at D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/. Only key data relevant to Dan 8:8-19 is extracted; full entries contain additional material.

    tsadaq — H6663¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md - Original: צָדַק - Transliteration: tsadaq - POS: Verb - Occurrences: 41x (some sources count 40-42) - Definition: to be just, righteous, vindicated - Stems: Qal (22x, state of being righteous), Hiphil (12x, declarative verdict), Piel (5x, comparison), Hithpael (1x, self-clearing), Niphal (1x — Dan 8:14 ONLY) - Dan 8:14 significance: The only Niphal of tsadaq in the entire OT. The Niphal is a receiving-passive: the sanctuary RECEIVES vindication. This is a judicial term, not a ritual cleansing term (taher H2891 would be the cleansing word; kaphar H3722 would be the atonement word). - KJV translation issue: "cleansed" comes from Theodotion/LXX (katharisthesetai) and the Vulgate (mundabitur), not from the Hebrew root tsadaq. - LXX mapping: tsadaq→dikaioo has PMI 26.99 — one of the tightest Hebrew-Greek mappings.

    TR entry: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-tsadaq-dikaioo.md

    tamid — H8548¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8548-tamid.md - Original: תָּמִיד - POS: Noun (functions as substantive adjective) - Occurrences: 104x in OT - Definition: continual, perpetual, regular (the continual service/offering) - Dan 8 occurrences: v.11 (removed), v.12 (host given against it), v.13 (question includes it) - Key finding (sv-03): 100% positive across all 104 OT occurrences. Never describes paganism. - Significance for Dan 8: The question (v.13) asks about ha-tamid; the answer (v.14) counts in the tamid's own units (erev boqer = one tamid cycle). Restoring the tamid restores the system whose endpoint is the DOA. The chain: horn removes tamid → tamid must be restored → tamid feeds the DOA → nitsdaq declares the system vindicated.

    miqdash — H4720¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4720-miqdash.md - Original: מִקְדָּשׁ - POS: Noun, masculine singular - Definition: sanctuary, holy place (mem-preformative noun of √qdsh, "place of sanctification") - Dan 8:11 usage: mekhon miqdasho — "the established place of his sanctuary" — with 3ms suffix binding to the Prince, not the horn. - Lexical contrast with qodesh (H6944): miqdash is the spatial locus (the consecrated precinct); qodesh is the broader holiness-sphere. Dan 8 shifts from miqdash (v.11, the attacked place) to qodesh (vv.13-14, the vindicated domain). The shift enlarges scope at the verdict.

    makon — H4349¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4349-makon.md - Original: מָכוֹן - POS: Noun, masculine singular - Definition: fixed-place, established-site, foundation, dwelling-base - Occurrences: ~17x, predominantly of YHWH's heavenly/Zion-enthronement site - Key collocations: Exo 15:17 (mekhon le-shivtekha — "place for thy dwelling"), 1Ki 8:13/2Ch 6:2 (mekhon le-shivtekha olamim), Psa 89:15/97:2 (tsedeq u-mishpat mekhon kisekha — "righteousness and judgment are the foundation of thy throne") - Significance: The word NEVER denotes a pagan shrine or an ordinary earthly house. It belongs to the divine-dwelling/throne-base semantic register. Dan 8:11 chose the rarest, most theologically loaded option (not heykal "temple" or bayit "house").

    emeth — H571¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H571-emeth.md - Original: אֱמֶת - Definition: truth, trustworthiness, faithfulness, stability - Dan 8:12 significance: The horn casts "emeth" to the ground. This encompasses God's entire faithful order — covenant reliability, revelation, law, and justice. Not just "factual truth" in a narrow sense. - Triple casting pattern: v.7 ram cast down, v.10 stars cast down, v.12 truth cast down — escalating from political to celestial to theological.

    TR entry: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-emeth-aletheia.md

    chazon — H2377¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2377-chazon.md - Original: חָזוֹן - Definition: vision, prophetic revelation (exclusively prophetic) - Dan 8 distribution: vv.1,2,13,15,17,26 — consistently labels the WHOLE vision-prophecy complex - Distinguished from mareh (H4758): In Dan 8, chazon = macro-revelation; mareh = specific visible element. V.26 proves two referents: mareh authenticated, chazon sealed.

    mareh — H4758¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md - Original: מַרְאֶה - Definition: sight, appearance, vision (broader range: physical beauty, priestly inspection, theophanic appearances, AND prophetic visions) - Dan 8 distribution: vv.15(simile),16(direct object of haven),26a(titular),27(unresolved) - The decisive v.16 usage: Gabriel is commanded to explain ham-mareh (NOT he-chazon). V.26a retrospectively identifies mareh as "the evening-and-morning" oracle. V.27 records Daniel's astonishment at "the mareh" — still unresolved.

    TR entries: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-chazon-horasis.md, TR-mareh-opsis.md

    biyn — H995¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H995-biyn.md - Original: בִּין - Definition: understand, discern, perceive - The biyn chain (sv-05): 8 stages across Daniel — capacity → command → failure → quest → resumption → resolution → reversal → eschatological promise - Dan 8 stages: v.15 Daniel seeks vinah (Piel.Wayq), v.16 voice commands haven (Hiphil.Impv), v.17 Gabriel says haven (Hiphil.Impv to Daniel), v.27 ven mevin ("no understander" — chain FAILS here), Dan 9:22 Gabriel returns to supply understanding.

    TR entry: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-biyn-syniemi.md

    tsaba — H6635¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6635-tsaba.md - Original: צָבָא - Definition: host, army, service (military and cultic) - Dan 8 occurrences: v.10 (tseva ha-shamayim — host of the heavens), v.11 (sar-hats-tsava — Prince of the host), v.12 (tsava tinnaten — host given over), v.13 (qodesh ve-tsava mirmas — sanctuary and host trampled) - The construct chain tseva ha-shamayim (v.10) LOCATES the host in heaven. The ladder: host (v.10) → Prince of the host (v.11) → sanctuary of the Prince (v.11) — all anchored in the heavenly realm.

    sar — H8269¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8269-sar.md - Original: שַׂר - Definition: prince, commander, chief, ruler - Dan 8:11: sar-hats-tsava — "Prince of the host" — heavenly ruler figure - Dan 8:25: sar-sarim — "Prince of princes" (same figure at the interpretation layer) - Intra-Daniel bracket: 8:11,25 → 9:25 (mashiach nagiyd) → 10:13,21;12:1 (Michael sar-gadol)

    TR entry: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-sar-archon.md

    gadal — H1431¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1431-gadal.md - Original: גָּדַל - Definition: be great, grow great, magnify - Dan 8 escalation chain: - v.8: higdil (Hiphil.Perf.3ms) — goat "magnified exceedingly" - v.9: tigdal-yeter (Qal.Wayq.3fs) — horn "grew with surplus" - v.10: tigdal (Qal.Wayq.3fs) — horn "grew up to" the host of heaven - v.11: higdil (Hiphil.Perf.3ms) — horn "magnified himself" up to the Prince - v.25: yagdil (Hiphil.Impf.3ms) — horn "shall magnify himself in his heart" - Stem distinction: Qal = intransitive growth (size-increase). Hiphil without object = reflexive-intensive self-exaltation (morally loaded).

    TR entry: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-gadal-megalyno.md

    naphal — H5307¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5307-naphal.md - Hiphil in Dan 8:10: tappel (Hiphil.Wayq.3fs) — causative "caused to fall" — the horn is the grammatical CAUSER of the host's downward motion. With locative artsah "to the ground."

    TR entry: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-naphal-pipto.md

    ramas — H7429¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7429-ramas.md - Dan 8:10: tirmsem (Qal.Wayq.3fs + 3mp suffix) — "she trampled them" - Dan 8:13: mirmas (H4823, miqtal-pattern result-state noun) — "a trampling/trampled-thing" - LXX bridge: ramas→katapateo (G2662), PMI 16.69

    TR entry: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/TR-ramas-katapateo.md

    mirmac — H4823¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4823-mirmac.md - Definition: trampling, thing trampled (result-state noun in miqtal pattern) - All 7 OT occurrences in prophetic contexts — technical covenant-consequence vocabulary - Dan 8:13: qodesh ve-tsava mirmas — compound direct object ("sanctuary and host — a trampled thing"). Both are trampled; the grammar makes it a sustained condition.

    shalak — H7993¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7993-shalak.md - Definition: throw, cast, hurl - The voice-chiasm root: Appears three times across vv.10-12: - v.10: tappel (Hiphil naphal, synonymous "cast down") — ACTIVE - v.11: hushlak (Hophal.Perf.3ms of shalak) — PASSIVE (agent suppressed) - v.12: tashlekh (Hiphil.Impf.3fs of shalak) — ACTIVE (horn as named agent) - Same root, opposite voice at the hinge of v.11 — the sharpest index of deliberate voice alternation.

    nathan — H5414¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5414-nathan.md - Dan 8:12: tinnaten (Niphal.Impf.3fs) — divine passive "was given over" - Dan 8:13: teth (Qal.InfCon) — "to give" (the giving over of sanctuary and host to be trampled) - The tinnaten/nitsdaq divine-passive pairing (from dan-8-14-vindication-meaning): Both are Niphal forms. What God gave over (tinnaten), God will set right (nitsdaq).

    pesha — H6588¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6588-pesha.md - Definition: transgression, rebellion (willful sin, not accidental) - Dan 8:12: be-pesha — "by reason of transgression" — the instrumental/causal cause for the host being given over - Dan 8:13: ha-pesha shomem — "the transgression of desolation" (with Qal participle attributive) - Connection to Lev 16:16: pesha has no daily provision — ONLY the DOA addresses willful rebellion (sv-04 finding).

    qadowsh — H6918¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6918-qadowsh.md - Dan 8:13: echad-qadosh — "one holy one" (the angelic beings who ask and answer the "how long?" question)

    qodesh — H6944¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md - Dan 8:13: qodesh ve-tsava mirmas — "sanctuary and host — a trampling" - Dan 8:14: we-nitsdaq qodesh — "and [the] sanctuary shall be vindicated" - Lexical shift from miqdash (v.11): The vindication targets the broader holiness-sphere (qodesh), not just the physical locus (miqdash).

    ereb — H6153¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6153-ereb.md - Dan 8:14: erev boqer — asyndetic, anarthrous compound. "Evening" component. - Genesis echo: erev-then-boqer matches Gen 1:5 creation-day order.

    boqer — H1242¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1242-boqer.md - Dan 8:14: "Morning" component of the unique counting unit. - Dan 8:26: ha-erev ve-ha-boqer — restated with articles and conjunction, proving v.14's compact form is deliberate.

    zaam — H2195¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2195-zaam.md - Dan 8:19: acharith hazzaam — "the latter end of the indignation" - Vocabulary bracket: zaam in 8:19 ↔ 11:36 ("prosper till the indignation be accomplished")

    achariyth — H319¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H319-achariyth.md - Definition: latter end, after-part, outcome, posterity - Dan 8:19: Not just "end" but the OUTCOME — what happens when the indignation reaches its terminus.

    eth — H6256¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6256-eth.md - Dan 8:17: le-eth-qets — "at a time of end" — ordinary temporal reference

    moed — H4150¶
