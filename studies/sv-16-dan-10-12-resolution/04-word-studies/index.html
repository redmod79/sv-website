    From Word Studies Library¶

    biyn (understand/discern) — H995¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H995-biyn.md

    • Root: Primitive root — "to separate mentally, distinguish"
    • Occurrences: 162 unique verses (189 tokens), 18 books
    • Highest density: Daniel (5.3 per 100 verses) — nearly double the next book (Proverbs)
    • Stem distribution: Qal (45), Hiphil (30), Hithpael (22), Niphal (21), Piel (1)
    • Key translations: understand/understood (26%), consider (9%), prudent (5%), perceive (3%)
    • LXX primary: G4920 syniemi (63 co-occ, PMI 27.04) — "put together mentally"
    • Key collocations: yada H3045 (35x), chakam H2450 (20x), leb H3820 (18x)

    The biyn Chain in Daniel: | Verse | Form | Parsing | Status | |-------|------|---------|--------| | 8:15 | biynah | Noun.fs.Abs | Daniel seeks understanding | | 8:16 | haven | Hiphil.Impv.2ms | Commission: "make understand the mar'eh" | | 8:27 | mevin | Hiphil.Ptcp.ms (negated) | FAILURE: "none understanding" | | 9:2 | biyn | Qal.Perf.1s | Daniel understood (by books) | | 9:22 | biyn (Hiphil) | Hiphil.Perf.3ms | Gabriel "informed" = caused to understand | | 9:23 | biyn + haven | Qal.Impv.2ms + Hiphil.Impv.2ms | Renewed command: "understand + gain insight" | | 10:1 | biyn + biynah | Qal.Perf.3ms + Noun.fs.Abs | RESOLUTION: "he understood... understanding in the mar'eh" | | 10:11 | haven | Hiphil.Impv.2ms | "understand the words" | | 10:12 | lehavin | Hiphil.InfCon | "set heart to understand" | | 10:14 | lehavinekha | Hiphil.InfCon + 2ms suf | "to make thee understand" | | 12:8 | lo avin | Qal.Impf.1s (negated) | SECOND FAILURE: "I understood not" | | 12:10 | lo yavinu / yavinu | Qal.Impf.3mp (negated / affirmed) | DISCRIMINATING CONCLUSION |

    biynah (understanding) — H998¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H998-biynah.md

    • Occurrences: 38 unique verses, 8 books
    • 78.9% translated "understanding"
    • Daniel occurrences: Dan 1:20; 8:15; 9:22; 10:1 — all tied to Daniel's prophetic gift
    • LXX primary: G4907 synesis (19 co-occ) — "mental putting together"
    • Key: Dan 10:1 "biynah lo ba-mar'eh" = "understanding was to him in the mar'eh" — the stative noun expressing settled possession, answering 8:27's deficiency

    chazon (vision) — H2377¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2377-chazon.md

    • Occurrences: 37 in OT; Daniel = 12 (32%)
    • Always rendered "vision" in KJV (34/37)
    • Root: chazah H2372 — to gaze at, perceive mentally
    • LXX primary: G3706 horasis (28x, 76%)
    • Daniel chazon verses: 8:1, 8:13, 8:17, 8:26, 9:24, 10:14, 11:14
    • Key: 10:14 "the chazon is yet for many days" — the chazon track remains OPEN while the mar'eh track closes at 10:1

    mar'eh (appearance/vision) — H4758¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4758-mareh.md

    • Occurrences: 82 unique verses (110 tokens), 18 books
    • Daniel = 11 (3.1 per 100 verses)
    • Translations: appearance (~33), sight (~16), vision (~12), countenance (~11)
    • LXX split: opsis G3799 (physical) / horasis G3706 (prophetic) / eidos G1491 (form)
    • Key: Dan 8:26 "the mar'eh of the evening-morning" — identifies the specific element (2300 time prophecy) within the broader chazon. Dan 10:1 resolves understanding of the mar'eh; the chazon remains for the maskilim.

    tsaba (host/warfare) — H6635¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6635-tsaba.md

    • Occurrences: 492 tokens, 462 verses, 27 books
    • Translation: "of hosts" (59%), "host" (21%), "war/battle" (10%), "army" (7%)
    • Dan 10:1: tsaba gadol = "great warfare" — characterizes the WEIGHT of what was revealed. KJV renders "the time appointed was long" but the primary semantic field is military/conflict. The content of the revelation (Dan 11) concerns centuries of great conflict.

    sakal (be wise/prudent) — H7919¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7919-sakal.md

    • Occurrences: 63 unique verses (70 tokens), 16 books
    • Daniel density: 2.5 per 100 verses (highest)
    • Stem: Hiphil dominates (~60 of 70)
    • LXX: G4920 syniemi (31x, PMI 23.84) — same Greek word as biyn!
    • The maskilim (Hiphil participle):
    • Dan 11:33 — "they that understand among the people shall instruct many"
    • Dan 11:35 — "some of them of understanding shall fall"
    • Dan 12:3 — "they that be wise shall shine"
    • Dan 12:10 — "the wise shall understand"
    • Key link: Both biyn and sakal map to syniemi in LXX, but Dan 12:10 uses BOTH: maskilim (sakal) yavinu (biyn). The wise act with circumspect insight AND discern/comprehend.

    tsadaq (be righteous/vindicate) — H6663¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md

    • Occurrences: 41 in OT
    • Stem distribution: Qal (22), Hiphil (12), Piel (5), Niphal (1), Hithpael (1)
    • The ONE Niphal: Dan 8:14 nitsdaq — "shall be vindicated/cleansed"
    • Dan 12:3 matsdiqey: Hiphil participle construct plural — "those causing many to be righteous"
    • The connection: nitsdaq (8:14, Niphal) = sanctuary receives vindication; matsdiqey (12:3, Hiphil) = maskilim cause many to be righteous. Same root, complementary stems.
    • LXX: dikaioo G1344 (21x) — "to justify, declare righteous"

    'amad (stand) — H5975¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5975-amad.md

    • Occurrences: 597 tokens, 497 verses, 36 books
    • Daniel density: 10.6 per 100 verses — by far the highest of any book
    • Daniel uses span: standing before (service), political accession ("stand up"), withstanding, taking a stand
    • Key Daniel 12 uses:
    • 12:1 ya'amod — Qal.Impf.3ms: "Michael shall stand up" (political/protective rising)
    • 12:1 ha-'omed — Qal.Ptcp.ms: "which standeth for" (ongoing protective stance)
    • 12:13 ta'amod — Qal.Impf.2ms: "thou shalt stand" (resurrection rising to allotted portion)
    • Note: Same verb, different functions: Michael "stands up" as protector-deliverer; Daniel "shall stand" as resurrected recipient of his inheritance.

    barar (purify/select) — H1305¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1305-barar.md

    • Occurrences: 16 unique verses (19 tokens), 11 books
    • Key: Dan 11:35 and 12:10 — part of the three-verb purification triad
    • Dan 12:10 form: Hithpael.Impf.3mp (yitbararu) — reflexive: "shall purify themselves"
    • LXX: eklektos G1588 (6x) — "chosen, elect" — the refined become the chosen

    tsaraph (refine/test) — H6884¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6884-tsaraph.md

    • Occurrences: 29 unique verses
    • Dan 12:10 form: Niphal.Impf.3mp (yitsarfu) — passive: "shall be tried/refined"
    • Key images: God as refiner (Mal 3:2-3), God's word as refined/pure (Psa 12:6; 119:140)
    • The triad: barar (purify/clarify) + laban (whiten) + tsaraph (refine by fire) — appears in BOTH Dan 11:35 and Dan 12:10

    laban (be white) — H3835¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3835-laban.md

    • Occurrences: 8 unique verses, 6 books
    • Dan 12:10 form: Hithpael.Impf.3mp (yitlabbenu) — "shall be made white"
    • Dan 11:35: Hiphil — "make them white"
    • Key parallel: Isa 1:18 "though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow" — Hiphil same root
    • Collocations with tsaraph and barar: 2x — both in Daniel's purification triad

    rasha (wicked) — H7563¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7563-rasha.md

    • Occurrences: 248 unique verses, 20 books; 96% translated "wicked"
    • Dan 12:10: "the wicked shall do wickedly: and none of the wicked shall understand"
    • Primary antonym: tsaddiyq H6662 (paired 81 times) — the fundamental moral binary
    • Key: In Dan 12:10, the wicked are defined by their inability to understand — wickedness is not just moral failure but cognitive/spiritual failure

    satham (stop up/seal) — H5640¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5640-catham.md

    • 17 tokens, 14 unique verses
    • Dan 12:4: Qal.Impv.2ms setom — "shut up (the words)"
    • Dan 12:9: Qal.Ptcp.passive.mp setumim — "are closed up"
    • Core meaning: physical stopping/blocking (wells in Gen 26), figurative concealment (Daniel)

    chatham (seal) — H2856¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2856-chatham.md

    • 26 tokens, 24 unique verses
    • Dan 12:4: Qal.Impv.2ms chatom — "seal (the book)"
    • Dan 12:9: Qal.Ptcp.passive.mp chatumim — "sealed"
    • The pair: satham (conceals content) + chatham (secures container). Both imperatives in 12:4; both passive participles in 12:9.

    qets (end) — H7093¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7093-qets.md

    • 71 occurrences; Daniel = 16 (4.5 per 100 verses — highest of any book)
    • Dan 12:4,9: 'eth qets — "time of the end"
    • Dan 12:13: la-qets... le-qets ha-yamim — "till the end... at the end of the days"
    • Root meaning: from qatsats — to cut off. The point where something is cut short, the terminus.

    nuach (rest/settle) — H5117¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5117-nuwach.md (newly generated)

    • Occurrences: 67 unique verses (108 tokens), 23 books
    • Stem distribution: Hiphil (73.2%), Qal (23.2%), Hophal (3.5%)
    • Dan 12:13 form: Qal.Impf.2ms tanuach — "thou shalt rest"
    • Semantic categories relevant to Dan 12:13:
    • Personal/existential rest: Job 3:13 "then had I been at rest"; Job 3:17 "there the weary be at rest"
    • God giving rest from enemies: Deu 3:20; Jos 21:44; 2Sa 7:1
    • Sabbath rest: Exo 20:11; 23:12
    • LXX: anapauo G373 (24x) — "to repose, refresh"; katapauo G2664 (18x) — "to settle down, cease"
    • Key collocations: YHWH (29x), erets (16x), ruach (9x)
    • Dan 12:13 significance: tanuach (you shall rest) = death-rest, settling into the sleep of death. Combined with ta'amod (you shall stand) and goralekha (your lot) = death, resurrection, inheritance. The verb carries the Deuteronomic promise of rest fulfilled eschatologically.

    goral (lot/portion) — H1486¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1486-gowral.md (newly generated)

    • Occurrences: 67 unique verses (90 tokens), 18 books
    • Joshua dominates (24/67 = 36%) — lot-based land allotment
    • Dan 12:13 form: goralekha — "thy lot" (with 2ms suffix)
    • Semantic range:
    • Casting lots to determine divine will (Lev 16:8; Jon 1:7; Pro 16:33)
    • Land allotment to tribes (Jos 14:2; 18:10)
    • Figurative: allotted portion/destiny (Psa 16:5; Psa 125:3; Dan 12:13; Isa 17:14)
    • Key collocations: nachalah H5159 "inheritance" (9x) — the lot determines the inheritance
    • LXX: kleros G2819 (56x, PMI 7.36) — preserves full range: literal lot, allotted land, portion/destiny
    • Dan 12:13 significance: Daniel's goral is his divinely allotted portion at the end of days. Combined with 'amad (stand), it means: "you will stand [rise] to receive your allotted inheritance." The word bridges the literal land-lot tradition (Joshua) to eschatological destiny.

    chamad (desire/delight) — H2530¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2530-chamad.md (newly generated)

    • Occurrences: 27 unique verses (33 tokens); 67 total root-family occurrences
    • Daniel density highest: 6 occurrences (all noun forms chamudoth/chemdah)
    • Semantic range:
